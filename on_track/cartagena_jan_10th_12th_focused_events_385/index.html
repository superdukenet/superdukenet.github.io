Just sitting down to some tapas and a well earned beer with the boys.

Had a great on track. One of, if not the most technical track I've been on. You never stop thinking and just when u think u are getting the hang of it it shows u you aren't.

Motoronin's SD is not better after all Poor fecker has got to hire a R6 for the next 2 days. Monty's m8 who is on a very nice RC8 had to spend today just gazing at it and hiring a R6 after leaving the key at home. He won't be making the mistake again but at least he will be on it tomorrow as a m8 drove to the airport and got a passenger to carry the key on a flight out this evening.

Weather is warm-ish and we had a shower just after the track closed but tomorrow is mean to be dry. There is only 30 bikes here so the track is as good as empty Guy Martin is here testing and pulling wheelies as he overtakes and showing everyone how to back it in and power slide an R1 and the BMW 1000RR. Apparantly it takes 10% skill 90% balls

We've all also been overtaken by 2x 14 year olds on 125s Jesus these kids are quick. Going round the outside on corners like u are sat still. Two stroke madness. Although they have both got through a piston and cylinder each today lol.

Wish u were here guys. Enjoy the snow

BASH69

2010-01-13 14:27:00 UTC
Got a few mins now for a little write up. Had a fooking fantastic time guys Weather was fab except for some rain on Sunday night, so we lost and hour to damp track on Monday and the last 2 hours yesterday when it rained. Shame about yesterday as I was really feeling at one with the track and SD on what is the most technical track I've been on. Takes a long time to learn the line and we ended up being shown it by a lad Sy that raced British Super Stock last season. That really helped and although I didn't feel as fast, or perhaps getting the lean angle that I was before my little off at Bedford it's definitely the smoothest I've ever felt on track and it felt like the speed was just round the corner. First time I have used Ractecs and have to say I love them!! Loads of grip, quick steering and fill you with confidence. I'm hooked. I also fitted the G2 .50 throttle cam and absolutely love it!!! You were right Stew it is fantastic on track and makes banging it wide open so much easier. No way that will be coming off the bike

You'll be glad to hear that the 2 SDs (mine and Monty's) ate the R6 and ZX6 of Ronin and Schnauzer for breakfast on more than 1 occasion. Though we all had our arses handed to us by 13 year olds on 125s

The most riders were on day 1 at 38 lol. Yesterday it was just 18 and was open pit lane from the 1st min rather than after a session. More track time than you could ever want but typically now I'm back I wish I'd done more. I'd def be tempted to do another cheap winter trip again on the experience of this one.

Was great to meet Monty from here and his m8 Dan who forgot his RC8 keys They were both nice and quick and a great laugh to hang/ride with. Monty got his knee down and was a very happy boy indeed. Ronin and Schnauzer as always were a pleasure to be around although we decided that Schanuzer's talents are on 2 wheels and he was stripped of car driving duties after taking us airborne over a speed hump at 60mph And then only Ronin screaming stop! at him like a big girl had us avoiding running a red light and T-boning a car I'm not sure Schnauzer was that impressed with Ronin's 50mph handbrake turn in the car park yesterday though. Especially as the car was booked on his credit card

Ok not many pics of me because you know me I'm not really into the whole picturing whoring thing

Yeah right

Vids will hopefully be up in a day or so.
