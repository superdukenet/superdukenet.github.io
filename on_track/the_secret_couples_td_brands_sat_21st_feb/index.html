Ducati Pete

2009-02-23 20:35:00 UTC
Without a doubt clearways is the hardest part of that circuit to master. From the moment you turn the bike left from surtees it's difficult to do well.
It's the only part of the track where the surface is flawed, there are changes of camber, and you're trying to get on the gas for the straight.
It's crucial to a good lap time, and, I must admit it's my weekest corner on the indie circuit.

I actually think it's one of the trickiest sections of any track I've ridden.

I was talking to Sam Middlemass last night who did national Jnr superstocks last year (in the BSB paddock.) He did 49.7 'round there on a Triumph 675, using a pretty radical line through clearways. Mid season he changed to a R6 and found that line didn't work very well!
