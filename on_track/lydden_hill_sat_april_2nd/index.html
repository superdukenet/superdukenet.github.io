Ducati Pete

2011-03-25 18:13:00 UTC

I don't think the track surface has been redone for years.
There are repairs in places but it's not bad, better than Mallory was for sure.

The thing about it is, while the surface rides nice and smooth, it's a heavily pitted tarmac. So when they run rally x etc the dirt sits "IN" the surface. I can remember following stupid Luke on a warm up lap on a nice dry morning and it looked like he was throwing up water - it was dirt from the surface.

Given that the grip can be a little unpredictable - especially off line and in the morning, and there is so little run off I always try and ride with a bit of extra caution. That has gone outta the window a few times, one day we did there in summer 09 being a case - I was on it all day.

Twisted Jester

2011-04-06 17:02:00 UTC

Arrived about 0745 and found the paddock to be fairly empty.
They decided to run 3 not 4 groups and go with 20 minutes sessions.

While I was getting sorted out Bob Collins came up and said hello. I’d not seen Bob in the flesh for a few years although I’d been on the phone to him at FWR tyres a few times. Bob won the National superstock cup a few years ago on a FWR entered GSXR1000, he always loved to show off so I thought the day would be entertaining.

Briefing was brief, and with the sun already out we started promptly with us fast groupers at 0900Hrs.

I had endured another very shitty night’s sleep and didn’t push very hard at all at the start of the day. The pattern for the day was pretty much set in that session. Pass several riders in the first 5 laps or so and then riding round on my own, slowly gaining on guys ahead with Bob and another 2 guys who were really rapid coming past sometime in the session. There were a few guys I was lapping.

Bob came past into the top hairpin backing it in and leaving a huge darkie which made me smile.

Sometime in the morning I managed to fook up big time. During my getting ready process I got distracted and I somehow ended up starting to ride off with my rear tyre warmer! I knew I was tired!

I stopped and removed the item and went straight out. On my return I found the cable had been yanked out the top and there was a small rupture in the fabric at the top of the element. That was thrown in the car and I put my spare on. The damaged one is up at HWW now being repaired.

Although there was a lot of loose shit being thrown up from tyres – especially in the early sessions, grip was pretty good. The few damp patches that started the day were gone completely by the 3rd session. The most annoying thing riding wise was I found I was struggling to turn the throttle right to the stop while still off the right side of the bike coming out from the Drift session of the circuit. (The long right hander at the far end.) I ended up over gripping to try and sort it out.
I think the quick action throttle cam isn’t quick enough!

I’d decided the 6th session at 1500 would be my last of the day. This was stopped after 7 minutes when a black Triumph binned it about half way ‘round the drift. I suspect he’d gone over the crest there cranked over and lost the front – I’m always careful to keep the bike balanced with the throttle over that crest.

After 5 minutes we were back out. The restart lasted only 3 laps though. The red flag was raised as I entered the drift and after backing off I emerged from the other side to find another rider and a mainly yellow triumph on the grass on the outside about half way up to the lefthander.

I’m not 100% sure what happened but did overhear a story that he got caught up in 2 other riders incident. It sounded as though 1 had been on a wide line out of the drift and another had been on a tight line. Of course these lines cross at the exit and they had touched. Triumph man was behind and had to take avoiding action. The bike was wankered.

Earlier in the day I’d been on a tighter line there and realised the rider I was about to drive underneath couldn’t see me. I’d decided to roll off a bit. Seeing the carnage after this crash I was glad I did.

I packed up and was at home for tea nice and early.
I caught the sun on my face and head while relaxing in my comfortable chair.
Altogether a nice little day, apart from the tyre warmer thing that added to the expense.
