Bit of the old question nature of nurture Stew....

I think a lot of racer/riders have started from an early age when your young your obviously take a lot more in, crashing just means a few weeks of school if your break something, ive wathed some kids motocrossing they just have no fear, transfer that to the track hey presto.

It takes someone special to be really fast.

Personally I blame my dad not buying me a motocross bike when I was 4, that why im not the next Rossi

Agree experience and practice can make you quicker doubt now I could ever be as fast as the racers but would still be interested to see how fast I could go.

Always makes me laugh when I hear racres talk about slight adjustments to suspesion or how they can feel the difference in a different swingarm etc, I can tell the difference between the sport settings and road ones on the duke but could not say, "hmmm think I need some more rebound on the rear".

I think the main things to being super fast racer is being started young, having the support of racing young and back up from your dad with thier knowledge, and no fear.
