2011-03-05 23:26:00 UTC

So Luke picks me up at 05:30 am and we get to snett about 07.35, every single garage is rammed so no chance of any electric.

We park up and the first thing I notice are two of the biggest queues I've ever seen for signing on and noise testing, we unload the bikes and head on over to signing on, queue has gone down a little bit but it's fookin freezing while we wait to go inside, Luke makes some excuse about leaving his driving licence in the van but I reckon he just went back to warm up in the van for a few minutes while I hold our place in the queue, ice cold fingers clutching at our paperwork.

I sign on, no problem, 12.75 transfer fee, lovely Jubbly and Barry wants to know what sordid sexual depravity Pete was up to this week to do his back in, I said not sure what he's ben up to only that I only agreed to take his place if I didn't have to wear one of his "outfits", and by that I mean his scott russell leathers as I might quite like his other stuff

So then off to Noise testing, or so we thought, after queing forever the guy comes along and tells us to shut off the bikes and go to briefing, fine we thought, except they then kept us waiting another 10-15 minutes before anyone actually came out to give the briefing, probably still signing people on.

So then it's get changed quick smart to head out for sighting laps, the format was that they do sighting laps for each group before anybody goes out on track, I'm rushing to get all my gear on and get out there only to realise that the order is back to front with fast group going out last instead of first so I have a bit of time and take a look at the noise testing again, only a couple of people there so I get tested.

Last year I repacked my exhausts and got another set of baffles to try and bring my bike down to 101db so I could do Bedford as it was previously measuring 103-4 with baffles, when we were at cadwell in April I noise tested both my sets of baffles and without baffles and got 101 on both baffles and 103 without, I passed at Bedford with 100.5, I get tested at snett and he measures the right hand can as normal, he then walks around to the other side and starts doing the other can, unusual I thought but not really concerned as it's 105 limit anyway, and then he tells me my right can is under but my left is 105.3 and so he tells me it's a fail and I need baffles in, no problem, luckily they're still in my bag from the last time out so I head off to fit a set of the baffles.

By the time I've done that a queue has formed for noise testing again and it's my group next for sighting so I go off and do those and then head straight back to noise testing, right can fine, left can 106.5, WTF!!!, by this point I'm thinking shit, that's me screwed, the other set don't knock off anymore than these and I'm thanking my lucky stars I didn't pay the £85 for the day, so I fit the other set and to my amazement I get 104.1, phew, thank christ for that, game is on again.

Twisted Jester

2011-03-06 00:12:00 UTC

I'd already decided to skip the first session as I needed to swap tyres anyway so after a bacon roll and cup of tea I get those off and leave em with the tyre guy and while I 'm waiting I pull apart my end cans and stick some exhaust sealant on the end caps as the rubber gasket thing that was there when I took them apart and repacked them had disentegrated and so I've been sucking in a bit of air since I did it and it's gradually got worse, while I'm doing this a guy comes over and asks me if I have any wadding, apparently they'd just been kicked off for setting off the drive by noise meters despite a static test of 102 and tell me an RC8 was also flagged, so I'm thinking this is going to be a seriously short session for me.

Get me tyres back and the guy has put the tyre on the front the wrong way round, I get it changed just in time to get it fitted back up and gear up ready for my session although he doesn't appear to have balanced it at all.

It's a mist of rain now and the session is pretty sedate, I'm wondering if they know something I don't as everybody seems to be taking it really easy, I'm still getting into it so I overtake a few people but generally don't get up to a very fast pace really, I seem to have gained a stutter at 8000 revs but wierdly in only one gear, usually the one Im in coming out of the hairpin or onto the back straight at this pace we seem to be doing and pick up from low down is a bit lumpy if I pick a higher gear and my exhaust isn't much better either so will have to check the graphite gasket next, I only just replaced it but don't think the pipe pushed all the way home, looked to be a good seal still but maybe it's not quite so.

It's still fookin cold and Luke borrows my all season gloves for his session to warm his hands up a bit, a little while later Schnauzer pulls in, Luke has binned it, shit, you know all those things you start thinking, how bad was it, how bad is the bike and most importantly, is the fooker gonna get back in time with my gloves before my session starts

Twisted Jester

2011-03-06 00:23:00 UTC

So unfortunately, no gopro footage, i didn't bother putting it on in the morning as I knew I wouldn't be up to pace and then the afternoon obviously never came.

I imagine you want to know what the circuit is like, well it's hard to get the best perspective on it because of the pace and I've never ridden the old layout but I reckon it's gonna be pretty damn good, the long corner coming on to the start finish is gonna catch people out as you coming a long way round still turning right when you then have to get the bike up, brake and setup for the tight left in almost no distance at all, it seems a lot less room in real life than it looks on the map, plenty of nice corners in there, gonna be interesting figuring out the right lines for us, but definitely some opportunity for us Superdukers to make up some ground after the long straights.

I'll definitely go back their again and look forward to riding it properly next time, perhaps with a new exhaust setup though, as I can't be doing with this kind of crap at noise testing.

Schnauzer

2011-03-06 18:55:00 UTC

I arrived around 8.30am, temperature was around 2.5°C, the ground slightly damp, Luke had already texted to say there were no garages, I found the guys and unloaded the bike, only to find a flat battery. Luckily, Luke had an Optimate in his van and I found a garage with an unused plug socket to get some juice in to the battery.

In the briefing FE acknowledged that they had quite a few racers who had booked on and it was a fully booked day, 65 per group.

There's a few of us watching the first session from the pit wall, some videoing the passing bikes on their phones/cameras. This little FE nazi walks down the lane bollocking everyone for videoing! "Its against the law, its illegal, its private property, it could be used for timing". More like Kev protecting the incumbent "official" TD video & photo services! Maybe their revenue is diminishing cus of GoPro's etc and he's looking to keep them sweet.

Luke comes in from his sighting laps with frozen fingers, its cold out there! After 45 mins of charging the battery there's enough juice to get the bike started, time to get kitted up. Having missed the first session and not knowing the new layout I follow Luke for the session. The BT003 on the front and BT016 on the rear, were only slightly warm after the session.

The new layout is a great, it lengthens the circuit nicely and adds an interesting dimension to the two straights, two sweepers and two corners. The circuit handled 65 riders on track at one time, with very little traffic and bunching. It will be interesting to see if this is true when the weather warms up and people are more confident with the new layout.

Second session and starting to get a feel the new corners, lines and the flow through them. There's a little drizzle on the visor but nothing heavy. Combined with the new tarmac, temperature and damp everyone is being being careful, there's no TD hero's here today, for most its the first track day of the season.

Third session, the circuit is drying and there a dry line around the track. Everyone is looks to be trying to push a little harder, I'm around 3 riders behind Luke when he goes down coming out of the corner with the back end coming round on him. He's up immediately, it looks to be a relatively uneventful slow event. Back in the pits and the aftermath is minimal, mainly mud and minor scratches.

After lunch the pits have noticeably thinned, the temperature, FE's slicks ban, surface conditions taking its toll. The mojo has gone and we come to the same conclusion, first TD of the year, live to ride another day. The guys next to us have had all the fun they came for as well.
