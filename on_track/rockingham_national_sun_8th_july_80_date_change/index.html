2012-07-09 13:34:00 UTC
It was shite.

Changed to the sunday on request from another forum member who then didnt even turn up. Had we kept the saturday we'd have had an earlier start, more sessions, and sunny dry track till lunchtime.

As it was. 6.5hr drive down and the clutch master cylinder shat its guts out just before the circuit. Got set up ok, good facilites there. Took a walk round the track but learned nothing apart from it even feels slippy walking on it.

Then all the usual wallopers turn up at 8am and for some unknown reason have to start their bikes to roll it from the van to the garages revving the nuts off it.
Went for noise testing and failed, then turned round to see the guy just holding the mic about 6" from the pipe. Bit of a debate asking him where the correct meter stick was but got no sense out him. Baffles in and it scraped by with said nobber cramming the mic right up the pipe.

1st session got 3 laps to try and work out which way the track goes then drizzle started.

2nd session did 4 laps pootling about and trying to run round getting a feel for the place and the bike with it's new susp. Passed Klink on the brakes but dropped it down to one too many into 2nd and had a wee wiggle. Plenty room from my seat but i think Klinky said after it was closer. Backed off to try and get some clear track to get my head down then saw the red flag.

Then it started raining so spat the dummy and packed up. Didnt want to hit any traffic on way home with no clutch.

So 13hours of driving and a full tank of diesel for 7 laps. Learned zero about the track or bike. 1st trackday really i've never took any steps forward with anything. Total waste of time and money. Still kinda looking forward to racing there. Main reasons being i think they'll drag me up to speed quickly and if its wet at least i'll have something worth crashing for.

Pish day, pish track, pish weather. One to forget.
