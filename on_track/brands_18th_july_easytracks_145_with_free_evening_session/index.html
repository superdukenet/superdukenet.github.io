Colonel_Klinck

2011-07-19 07:08:00 UTC
Well what a fab day. First track day of the year for me and first time at Brands as well.

Went out and did my sighting laps and then sat out the first 2 sessions. Think it was the sensible option because as Bic mentioned it was crash and red flag city on a damp track. By session 3 the track was virtually dry and I headed out to try and learn a line. Damn I was rusty and slow Really struggled to get my head around Clearways and especially Druids. There were some BSB boys out there with us and they were going past me like I was out there on a horse and cart, impossible lean angles and tight lines. Fortunately my day at Lyddenhill prepared me for uber close passing and I managed not to panic as a bikes went past my shoulder and across my front wheel with inches to spare. Damn the talent/bravery gap is fooking huge!!

By the 2nd session of the afternoon I was starting to really enjoy myself. Paddock is a fantastic corner and was dragging my knee around Clearways and even Druids as well. Still so slow compared to the vast majority out there and I would have probably been better placed in Inters but its good to try and learn some stuff from watching the real fast boys up close. I think it's safer in Fast even if sometimes it doesn't feel like it when a fast boy passes you mid corner close enough to reach out and touch you.

We all signed up for the free evening session and looked forward to making up for the lost morning sessions. Smack on 5pm though the heavens opened and the track was wet again. We hung around in the hope it might dry out but it didn't and so just after 6 we packed up and set off home. Was a fab day, Jody was fast as ever and lapped me one session the bastard TJ wasn't hanging around either, neither was Duncan. Great to see you too Bic. Was a nice warm up for Cadders in 3 weeks time, can't wait!!!!

Will definitely be heading back to Brands again as it is such a fun track to ride.

Twisted Jester

2011-07-23 01:55:00 UTC
Had an awesome day at brands although a bit of a comedy of errors in the morning with shade forgetting his boots and me forgetting to put one of the seals back in my original fuel tank after putting it back on the bike and so ending up with petrol pissing out around the fuel cap.

Thanks to Jody lending me his SXV to scoot home on once I realised what I'd done, as it meant I only missed one session and it was a damp one anyway so no big deal.

By the 3rd session the track was not bad but there was a really off putting wet patch on paddock hill just off the racing line which was just begging for some target fixation

Loads of people were going off at druids in the morning, the easy track guy commented to me at the end that they were all copying my riding style from last month

There'd been lots of talk of tyre pressures on here and amongst other guys in the garage so I decided to have a chat with the tyre guy and he recommended
30 front 28 rear straight after removing tyre warmers or then 28-26 cold aiming or 31-32 measured when you come back off track.

Now I never remembered to check when I came back in but felt pretty good out on track and tyre wear seemed good so I shall stick with em.

Felt pretty happy with my riding out there most of the day, there were some real fast guys out there and some BSB riders apparently, some of them were cutting it a bit too close, not just the usual fast group close passing but so much so that you had to adjust your own line to deal with it but it seems a few did complain and so it was mentioned to us all to leave reasonable space on overtakes while we were queuing in pit lane at next session, FE probably would have called everyone in for a whole briefing about it.

It was amazing though watching some of the really fast guys going in and out of traffic round clearways though, under one and outside another.

Last session felt really good and I was secretly hoping I might have improved on last months best lap time but it wasn't to be as I only nearly matched it within a few hundredths with a 56.55 which is at least consistent I suppose and had lots more traffic this time around compared to the empty track last month.

Was gutted it hammered it down, almost immediately that we walked out of the office after collecting our free evening wristbands, I was well up for another 3 sessions.

Was great to see everyone again and seeing shade out on track again and Klincky doing his first time at brands, thanks to Jody for helping me load up and unload in the morning and the loan of the SXV

Will sort out some video soon, got some footage following Jody round but not as good a view on the cam with the wide angle as it was in real time, have to be quite close with these things, it was quite fun to watch as he was swapping places with this guy over a series of a few laps, I was getting closer but then got seperated by traffic
