2010-04-08 06:22:00 UTC

I first fitted the slipper several months ago, I didn't have the correct tool to hold the basket when I tightened everything up but I managed to get by with a couple of tyre levers and my foot on the brake. It ran perfectly for a couple of months but I was concerned about my bodge. So I bought the right tool, and took it all apart to tighten it properly. Somehow during this process I managed to ruin the clutch slave cylinder. A used but working one was purchased and fitted. That's when the clutch started slipping and the bike was very difficult to push when in gear with the clutch lever pulled in. Several strips and refits later I was no closer to finding out what I'd done wrong. Long story short, last week I gave up and took the bike to a bikeshop and explained exactly what had gone on. Yesterday I picked it up and in fairness to the mechanic he did say that the clutch plates had become glazed and that he'd roughed them up best he could but that was one of the problems with using fully synthetic oil. He said I'd have to try it and if it still slipped I'd need new plates. It seemed OK at first but quickly deteriorated.

On another note, all this talk of racetech tyres has got me thinking. I have Mich pilot powers on my SD and have a spare set of wheels with Mich pilot races (that are nearly worn out) should I be off buying slicks? I was planning on using the pilot powers in the morning to learn the track and to finish of the pilot race's in the afternoon !

2010-04-13 11:52:00 UTC

Nice one guys

Have to say it was a fab weekend. Although Dan tried to kill me by sending me out on the death bike Super Duke

Have to say the roads around Cadwell are awesome!!! Pity we didn't get to see you performing those with the mandatory black flags at Cadders m8. Will def have to come back up for a blast again this summer guys as it does look like there is some amazing road riding to be had.

Dan was def host with the most. 30+ bottles of beer in the fridge, bags of munchies and then a fab curry with MotoGP, does it get any better? (have to say though the BBC coverage is shite compared to Eurosport

As Dan said I'm glad I bought the new rear Racetec instead of using his old one. Mine was ruined on the right from Cartagena but it would have been a false economy to use an old one then bin it. The grip levels from Racetecs is truly next level

We head out for 1st session in Inters. Bit rusty but feeling ok and Cadwell always feels fantastic to ride around. There really is something very, very special about this track. First time I've been there and its been completely dry from the off, which is a massive bonus.

2nd session I have the new rear on and take it easy for a lap but then open her up and damn it feels good

We head out for first session in the fast group. I stick with Dan for about 3 laps and then he fooks off I keep him in sight for a bit but by the end of the session he is a good 1/3rd of a lap in front. He is def throwing down the marker for us all to step up too. I spotted some orange and black leathers over the barrier near the Goose Neck and at first thought they were Pete's but it turns out to be Jody that wanted to test the Super Dukes off road ability (I'll be joining him on that one in a bit) He is ok though and thr damage is very fixable. I always carry spares and he's back and ready for next session.

We get one more session in before lunch and although a few big IL4s are screaming past me on the straights I think I am more than holding my own through the corners and am overtaking a few people as well. All is going well, I'd started out in front of everyone and 14 mins into session I'm amazingly still out there. Come tearing down the pit straight and there is a bit of traffic but all seems to be moving quickly. Then as i get to Coppice it isn't moving as quickly as I hoped. I'm left with no choice but to go off-roading, Super Duke may not be as good off road as my EXC but she stays upright and I rejoin the track halfway round Charlie's one. Take a lap to make sure there isn't grass all over my tyres and then a lap later its all over and the chequered flag is out. Jamie get past me on the chequered flag lap at the end of Park Straight but i'm sticking with my story that I was on a warm down lap Wish I'd got into this 20 years ago!

Stew is calling it a day riding wise which is a shame. Poor bike isn't happy and so neither is he. Hope it's an easy fix dude.

First session after lunch feels crap, not on it at all. Feeling lethargic and mind isn't in the zone at all. Didn't have a massive lunch but something isn't right and def not happy with performance. 2nd session is much better and feeling like the pace is back. Last session we head out. Jamie then myself, with Dan and Pete behind. Dan is past me and Jamie on lap 2 and then Pete sneaks past me too. I've got Jamie's GoProHD on board so I'm going to try and stick with Pete and get some good footage. Much to my surprise and joy I stay on Pete's rear for the whole session Shame that we find the camera ran out of memory 3 laps into the session. Oh well next time I guess.

All in all a fantastic day. Great to see Dan, Stew and Pete again. Also me meet Jody and his lovely lady plus Scots Duke for the first time. Jamie for talking my ears off there and back Ginge you were missed.

Can't wait for the 2 day SD.net Cadwell Extravaganza, although I might have to squeeze one more in between now and then
