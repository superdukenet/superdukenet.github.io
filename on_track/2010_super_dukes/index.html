Yeah we have before, and for sure we'll get after runs to compare. Thing is though, our before runs were done on a different dyno - which does not have proper fans to pressurize the new box. It'll be easy enough to switch back to stock though. No stock parts are being altered.

bigash, what we are building replaces the entire top of the existing airbox. Fastens to the lower box just the same as stock (same four bolts). Only there is no top. The stock tank is the top. Plus it extends forward to up under the front tank bolt. Probably increases the airbox volume size by another 50%?

Sometimes projects like this grow farther and farther from your original vision (meaning worse and worse) as you fumble your way through reality. This one is quite the opposite I am happy to report. I'll keep updating. And thanks for all the help!
