2013-05-26 05:13:00 UTC
My one and only high side still haunts me now. I was used to the back tyre moving about and as I did more and more track time those kind of feekings and the feedback I got, ( that is different from years of road riding), become more familiar. But I'd never directly experienced a high side effect, knew about it all in theory but that counts for nothing when it comes to knowing how to react when it happens.

All I remember was that it happened SO FAST! On Clearways at Brands - a corner I'd done countless times. But I was off the racing line and maybe a bit too complacent and suddenly there was a micro second of the feeling of loss of grip but then something new and it all went pear shaped. I still replay the memory in my head and it makes me feel ill! I guess you need to high side a lot to just get used to it. I still don't know what I should have done to try and recover the situation. I can't replay the details well enough to analyse anything. I ended up in hospital for a week with a dodgy back.

Because I'm not a really fast/competitive/frequent rider, it's set me back a lot that incident. I could lie and say a load of macho bollox but actually it's made me loose faith in really running on the edge of my tyres and pushing it and it will be a while I guess before that memory fades.
