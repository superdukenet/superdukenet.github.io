tripoddave

2012-02-18 09:18:00 UTC
I thought I would post up here a thread showing what we're doing and the results we get.
The story so far:
Back ground
My SD is the first brought into the UK by KTM, it was the UK press bike and then it was used as the prototype for the Superduke battle series and used as a guest bike in that series.
I bought it at the end of that season and it has been my track bike ever since.
Its been through many modifications over time but the specification it ran to last year was :
DNA Stage 3 airbox
Akrapovic Evo system
YoYoDyne slipper clutch
Butterflies removed
Rev limit set to 10,500

Towards the end of last year it was clear that the SD was making less and less power.
I took the bike into Kev @ Projex-UK and we stripped the top end of the motor and found this:





As you can see the piston was on the verge of break up and we had lost one of the compression rings completely.
There was light scoring on the bore but apart from that I would say I had been extremely lucky.
Subsequent research has revealed that raising the rev limit on the SD is a direct route to piston failure so - on a positive note it is felt that we know why the motor failed.

Given that I was going to have to rebuild the motor and fit new pistons anyway I decided to go the whole hog and do the job properly:
The heads would be gas flowed and skimmed
High compression pistons would be used
High lift cams would be installed as well (already had these)
Extra crank case breathing would be added
RC8R trumpets would be tested and used if they give any gain.
New bodywork painted in Candy Sun-God Orange: we have gone with GSX-R1000K6 bodywork this year.

Suspension
The forks have got progressively worse so it is time to sort them out.
The forks and shock have been removed and taken to RJ Superbike at Mallory Park. Rob and Ady know all there is to know about race bike preparation, especially suspension and are K-Tec dealers.
The shock is being rebuilt by K-Tec themselves and is being re valved and shimmed at the same time
The forks were going to have a K-Tec kit fitted but they aren't doing a replacement system for the SD any more so we have settled for a complete rebuild and replacing the original needles with tapered ones that will actually make a difference when you move a few clicks!
Certainly we should notice a significant difference because the forks were in a bad state internally with one chamber completely dry and the compression needle jammed on one of the legs.
Suspension should be ready for pickup next Friday (24-2-12)

Pistons
The pistons are CP Carillo items that have been re machined finished and coated and sourced from Tornado wia Sean Wilson racing (Tornado agents in the UK) who was also the source of the high lift cams.
The pistons are gorgeous - engine jewelry:



As you can see the valve cut outs are enormous. These babies increase the compression ratio from 10:1 to 13:1.

The heads and barrels were sent to Bob Farnham for gas flowing and relining respectively.
The heads arrived back this week and the amount of metal removed is, IMO, quite startling. Hopefully you will be able to see in these pictures:
(The white stuff is assembly grease btw and NOT because I lurve them so much)
Inlet:


Exhaust:



The next stage is engine assembly and a decision as to whether to run up with stock cams or to go with the high lift cams right from the go.
We are planning to improve the crank case breathing by disconnecting the breathers that go from the front cam cover to the oil tank, fitting two more just like it to the rear cam cover and routing all four into a catch tank.
The oil tank will be vented to the atmosphere though a little K&N type filter.
This should prove to be cost - effective and, er, effective.
Engine build is next week so I will update this thread as we make progress.

Upon assembly we found that the crankshaft could not be turned through 360deg because the piston skirt fouled the oil jet nozzle that cools the underside of the pistons. This problem was present at both the front and rear cylinders. We cured this problem by machining off 1mm from the nozzles.

MrZ32

2012-05-21 07:42:00 UTC
the price of those is fair, buy them. You need to remove the secondaries again. Mount the new bodies. Open the throttle 100% and check
if there is still a little bit of metall from the intake hose blocking the full flow after the rubber mounting. Mark it with an Edding. Stuff your intakes with cloth and seal it with some silicon or whatever you have on hand. Use your Dremel to fit the intake diameter to the new bodies.
Blast the metall away and you are good to go. Apart from that, the throttle bodies are plug and play.
Next step is to trim your current mapping down by approximatly -5% with TuneEcu. The 52mm bodies have a 14 hole spray so have a higher overall fuel throughput. With this you should be able to ride the bike, it will not be perfect, but it should run decent.
Finally a new Dynorun will be needed, i'm running Autotune with a Motty otherwise all these Dynoruns would have made me bankruped.

i would not go higher than max 9800. By the way, do you do track days ? If the answer is yes, i have two more tips for you :
1. Have a look at the breathing back pressure valve, it sits between the cylinders under your Airbox. There is an alloy valve

housing. This will avoid overheating as the new impellar has a way better design.

2012-07-04 09:53:00 UTC
A few things have changed in your engine and I don't want to slag of a tuner just add a few ideas and some different thinking.

Since the compression has increased you need to have a proper look at the ignition advance, this can add or remove heaps of torque and horsepower.
The trick is to get a steady and safe RPM on the dyno in the region of maximum torque and steady throttle. then slowly add a few degrees of advance and see if the hp/ torque goes up or down.
Try also removing a few degrees, you might have too much advance due to the flame front traveling faster.
If you found the torque increasing, work you way past it till it drops off at both ends and find the sweet spot in the middle. While you do this keep an eye (or ear) out for detonation. It takes time but it is worth it. After you worked a baseline out try to apply this to the rest of the ignition curve and do another run.

What I find also funny is that nobody has done a flow test on the injectors. I had a look at it and I recon that the SD ones are max 130hp, don't know about the SDR ones.
Someone needs to put them on a flow tester and run them at the SD fuel pressure which I believe is 43psi.
The SDR injectors look the same as the RC8 so they should be higher flow maybe up to 175 hp, the interesting question as asked above is how much did you had to adjust the map?
Keep in mind that the max recommended duty cycle for injectors is 80%.

Also did anyone had a look at the cam timing?
