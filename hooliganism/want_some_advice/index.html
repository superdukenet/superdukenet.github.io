Im a hobby photographer but i know a coupleof pro guys/wedding photographers. There are a LOT of people out there who think they can do it but cant. If she is really good, she will need to learn the crafts mentioned above, lighting in particular if she goes int portrait/studio work imho.

The shots show someone who has an understanding of what they want but needs to develop the skills and techniques to get it. Learning how depth of field, aperture, light, exposure all combine to manipulate the image is the starting point. Refinement comes later and a style develops. There a few guys out there who run workshops (for a fee) for budding photogs on their specialist subjects (weddings or rock music or nature or whatever). These can also be good ways to get education, experience and also make contacts...another vital part of working life.

A basic DSLR with a couple of fixed lenses is a good place to start. Doesnt have to be expensive if you buy used kit thats well looked after. It can get very expensive though. Thousands for a decent lens is totally normal....and thats just SLR photography. Larger formats and flashy lighting kits etc really add up. Plus a decent Mac and software (ususally pirated) adds to the fun!
