SuperDan

The biggest difference between fast and not so fast riders are actually not lean angle and corner speed.

90% of the difference in lap times come from the difference in how much speed you carry into the corner before reaching apex. I.e. how late and hard you brake.

Offen slower drivers are earlier and harder on the gas mid-corner than real fast guys because they realise they went into the corner to slow.

I am not sure that you are really slow ? - it depend on who you compare to ?

But my best advice is that you to start to brake early but not so hard and try to maintain a higher speed until you get into the corner. By starting braking softly a little earlier you feel much more in control and chances are that you actually carry more speed into the corner. Corner speed will be slower if you brake to late and to hard !

Dont push you self too hard - it will end in a crash. But push you self a little. As long as you have unused rubber on the rear the bike can handle much more lean angle for sure

Good luck

Dan - don't be fixated on lean angle. It will come as a result of cornering faster.

If you want to get a move on around a race track you will end up trying to minimise your lean angle! Time spent on the edge of your tyre is time when you can't get on the gas hard.

The race school at Donny is fairly ££££, you don't get a lot of track time. That said, the instructors are first class and I know a lot of people who say it help them a lot.

CSS is good, it breaks things down to first principles. It works wonders for some, but others don't get on with the system. I think it would work well for you.

There's no substitute for quality track time.

You could consider doing a spanish event with FE. You can book onto the school for a day while you're there. It's not a great deal of money, and you get 1 instructor between 4 pupils for a whole day.

CSS didn't work for me. I can write line after line and page ater page about it, but CSS is for a certain type of person, and i'm not that type of person. It was broken down into a finite set of manouvers, do this, move that, look here, position there, brake there, turn there...

So much so that i was given CSS LEvel 1 and LEvel 2 Free of charge by Andy Ibbot and i didn't go back for level 2.

How do i go faster... it's the question many many riders ask themselves. For me, it was simple (sort of) GO RACING !

Get yourself an ACU Licence and get to Mallory on a Weds afternoon for cheap tracktime, then get up and do a race. PLenty of classes now for the SD and it's a great bike on circuits like Mallory park. Your male instincts will kick in and you WILL go harder and faster and faster. You will chase someone down and you will let go of the brake a fraction earilier.

Think of it this way... letting off the brakes 1/10th of a second earlier can actually make 3/10ths on that corner... 1 on entry speed, 1 on corner speed and 1 on exit speeds... do that 5 times in a lap and you're 3 seconds a lap quicker. It's only really 2mph in a corner more than you were going into it.

You have the desire to go quicker... you have the male pride.... Get racing... and you WILL find seconds upon seconds.

Track time can't be beaten but it is expensive. Repetition is the key.

At 64 I'm too old for racing and practice my corning skills on a select few corners on my pleasure rides. Find a few low traffic, see throught corners or curves and slowly try to increase your turn entry speed.

Another idea I've seen is to find a large open paved area (parking lot) and start circling slowly and gradually increase your speed and lean angle.

The largest block to lean angle (corning speed) is learning to trust your tyres!!!!!
