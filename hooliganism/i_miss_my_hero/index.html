2011-07-28 02:54:00 UTC
What's up guys.. Been a while. I check in now and again. Want to update you on our "progress" with the RC8R. Also want to clear a few things up. First, I read a post a while back where someone commented on how expensive the Nichols triples kit was - then said as much as they like GoGo, they can't justify the expense. Let me hear myself say this out-loud, please, I have NOTHING to do with the business/profit/sales part of that kit. I love it, I helped develop it, and I tried to tell everyone on a Superduke that I could reach, about it. But that's it. I got one free set, that was my pay for helping develop it. Then I produced that video talking about the kit, because I promised I would, for Nichols and Tri Valley - to help sell them. After that I am a customer just like you.

Next, our Superduke is still dead. I have tried many times to get Mike to sell it to me, as is, so that I can bring it back to life. I get nowhere, yet. But I still have hope. And I still have a Superduke Revival bank account waiting in the wings, funded in part by a very generous member of THIS forum - Michael L. Don't worry, I'll get Mike to sell it to me - if it kills him.. It actually kills ME every time I see that bike in the corner of the shop, lying there wounded beyond repair, and neglected. I did race it again though, at least a part of it anyway. They broke our oil pump drive gear while installing a Yoyodyne slipper clutch in the RC8R a month ago. It was late night, we had to run the next morning, so out it came from our wounded Superduke. Same part number, thankfully...

As for my update on our RC8R program... What's my biggest promise - honesty right? So hold on to your asses - I hate it. I hate our RC8R. I hate it's paint job, I hate how it handles, I hate being passed like I'm painted on the wall while the big twins rocket by us, and in fact it's gotten so bad that I actually hate anyone who stands near it. So don't stand near it!



Truth is something is wrong with ours. Must be. By now I have had some top riders run it around the track, they come back in shock that we go as fast as we do on it. It's pretty violent how it "handles". Really it's more like a hell ride at some demented amusement park built by axe murderers. The last top guy who rode it, after six runs on it - he ended up with our Ohlins steering damper dialed all the way in to the stop, then backed out ONE click. Holy shit you almost didn't need a rear stand to park the bike, it was so tight. The biggest problem we have is, you guessed it, instability. There's a lot we have done (probably to cause it) that I won't get into. Recently we have begun to realise some disadvantages we have by running an older model R - like the rear ride height link. So we're looking into updating that now. Also I think the custom triple clamp set we had made for the bike, is fundamentally wrong in design. So we are looking into changing that as well. Finally I think I'm gonna paint the bike before next round. It's just all wrong. Especially from the front. Total crap. Chris Fillmore's RC8R in recent AMA races is very inspirational. That thing is beautiful. And it didn't look like it wanted to kill him either, until Laguna this past weekend..

Regardless of all the hatred in our pits this year, somehow we are second in the Open Twins points. YEY!! ....No, not yey. second sucks. Especially if you knew who we are second place, to. I don't really want to talk about it now. But he's on a KTM, RC8R, and his bike is definitely NOT trying to kill him like ours is me. AND he is about the least likable guy in American roadracing. WTF, of all the people to be second too...

I created a blog finally, on gotagteam.com. WTF is "Go Tag Team" anyway you might ask? The "Go" should be obvious. The "TAG" is my wife's initials - who has been the core of my racing effort for a decade (although to be honest, she kind of took the last four years off from that - which is aNOTHER whole story in itself...). And the "TEAM" is the theme our effort is bulit around - Together Everyone Achieves More. Oddly, the word team also spells out each member of our small family - Tracy Eric And Matthew. So there you go, that's the gotagteam.com. I know, it sounds like a porn site, or a professional wrestling outfit. What can I say, this is my life.

For the past four years we have kind of been separated, Tracy and I. But also kind of together. Trust me this is total hell. Either divorce, or be married. Purgatory is crap for your spirit. We are actively working things out now (I think?), which is great. But during our darkest times... well a few lassies came a-knockin - one of which was just a few days into walking out on her wedding which was due to take place in less than thirty days time. Wanna know who she was getting married to? No, trust me, you don't. But he races a KTM.

Shut up now, stop trying to instigate more problems for me. I did nothing wrong. But you've got to admit, that deal was straight from the Twilight Zone.

T-shirts. I want to say thanks to all of you who bought the Duke shirts I had made. That was a new experience for me. I'm considering doing it again, only this time with a different design, and different color shirt. What was I thinking when I chose white? What an idiot - mine had an ink stain on it in the first five minutes I wore it. And that Hanes Beefy-T cotton, well it's durable as hell but the cotton is so hairy it kind of came through the print and robbed it of it's vital colors. I hate how that happened in just a few washes. I worked really hard on that design. I called the printer, who is a guy I grew up with on the east coast, and told him next time I see him I'm gonna run his dog over, twice.

Maybe the next shirt will be black. Thinner material. A different cut. And printed by a chick covered in tattoos, with spiked hair and Herman Munster boots. Yeah, that's how I'm rollin next time.


So you guys keep jammin on your Superdukes. Wish I was out there with you.

In time, I will be back.

On the hero

GoGo

Thanks, exactly what I need - help!

Actually stock offset on an 09 RC8R is 28mm. First time we ran the bike we had it at 24mm. All wrong. Then we ran it at 26mm. All wrong. Last time we ran it at 27mm. All wrong.

While it IS true what you say, less offset (smaller number) gives you more trail (which in theory should make the bike more stable), it is actually a double edged sword you start swinging when you change offsets. What I mean is you are affecting more than simply your trail number when you change your offset. Unfortunately you are also moving the front wheel back, which puts more weight on the front (closer to the motor). And you are shortening the wheel base (which actually makes the bike LESS stable). And finally, if you view the bike from above, and picture the bars turning one side to the other side, you can see that the less offset you run, the smaller of an arc your front wheel turns on. Doug Chandler calls a bike with more trail when achieved via shortening it's offset - "Close". And close, in his words, is erratic and unstable.

So yes, I agree with you - more trail is achieved by shortening the offset. This is actually exactly what we did. PRoblem is the bike was horrible. Go figure.

What I have learned, this year especially, is that you can't just apply "numbers" to any given chassis and get predictable results. Just ask Rossi.. Every bike is different, every rider is different, all tyres are different, etc. etc.

I plan to abandon our custom made triple clamps, and go back to stock. That our bike is so consistently unstable with all the different offsets we have tried, suggests we have a bigger problem somewhere else.

We got no help from Ohlins. KTM either for that matter. Not this year anyway. We chose Ohlins R&T forks because they are a LOT less expensive than the White Power race kit for the stock forks. I'm not sure but I think #54 runs White Power forks with Ohlins internals - which are also cheaper. Just guessing though, I know nothing about his bike. For the record, we run nothing on our RC8R because he runs it on his. Every time someone tells me a direction he went, I go the other way. In fact if he told me to run from a burning building, I might have to sit there and burn just to spite myself. I know, I make no sense. But I do poor work...

It took us all season but we figured our RC8R out. That bitch goes dam good now. Turns out our custom triples were designed to not only solve problems, but create a few too. Once we discovered what was wrong with them, EVERYTHING turned around. Last big jump we made was giving our bike to Jerry Piazza - to do with it/to it, anything he wanted. I can't even explain what we got back. I mean, I can, don't get me wrong.. Turns out our frame was bent up front, putting our front tyre out to the right. Then our swingarm was bent too, putting our rear tyre out to the left. We got our bike from KTM/from the Skip Barber school - "Never been down" Thanks guys... Add loose steering head bearings to that (related to our triple clamp design), a rear axle 10mm out of true (related to idiocy), Michelin tyres from 2004 (related to, I'm not sure actually) and a rider who won't take no for an answer (fell on head as an infant, a lot) - what you get is, well, a comedy actually.

For the record, if you have problems like that with your bike, and you fix them, you might not only go faster - you may even get laid more often.

Anyone on an RC8R out there, lower the rear ride height adjuster to the floor. This might feel like you're ass just fell into a bucket while you're riding out there - so raise the rear sub-frame height adjustment to the high setting. That'll feel normal again. If you have an 09, buy the adjuster from a 10 or 11 model. Big difference. You're changing two things - swingarm angle and the angle of your forks (for the love of rake and trail, again...) We have our forks pretty close to the top of the triples. I think our Ohlins R&T forks are the same length as the White Power stockers.

If you want trick triple clamps, don't make them yourself. And don't buy ours. Not for the RC8R anyway, unless you're #54. If you are you can have three free sets. The HMC guys got Attack to make their clamps. Attack told us to get bent until after October when we asked for a set, so we went deeper into finding the problem with ours. Turns out the problem with ours was we had no umbrella girl anymore. WTF! How could that affect our triple clamps!
