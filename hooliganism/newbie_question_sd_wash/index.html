get yourself a microfibre wash cloth and do not be tempted by a sponge, it will just scratch the paintwork/plastics. I use the 2 bucket method. 1st bucket for washing and 2nd for rinsing. Then a hose on low pressure for final rinse. Dry bike and then use Autosol polish on paints and plastic polish on plastics. Finally a thin coat of ACF-50 on engine and metal works. Fantastic protection.

Chain wise, I keep mine spotless and use Profi Dry Lube as a lubricant/protector. You can go after the chain with a toothbrush and chain cleaner with no problems. Avoid pressure washers as they can force water past the X/O ring and into the chain interior. Exhausts come up a treat with WD40, as do most things. This can be applied everywhere if you prefer it to ACF-50.

Stay away from the SD with high pressure hoses and the like. Despite KTM being a dirt bike company the SD doesn'tlike moisture.
Problems with dash, the big grey plug and other electrical bits may arise if you ignore this warning.

As Klinck recommends, that is probably one of the best methods to avoid scratches, rinse and dry after with microfibre cloth and all is good.
