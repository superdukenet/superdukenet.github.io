2009-04-15 09:52:00 UTC
the 748 is pretty titchy, i'm pretty small, and found it pretty comfy, but can't imagine it would if you are very tall.
The bike is glorious though, it actually had a very similar feel to the SD i think. obviously it's not quite as pokey, but it delivers its power in a very similar way, and the general tight, mechanical / agricultural feel is the same to me.

When i first rode it, i think i covered about 2 miles before thinking - oh my god, i'm going to have to sell this things asap, it was so god damn painful. but the more you ride it the better it gets comfort wise. I think your body is contorted to such a degree that it actually has to alter to accommodate the riding position, where as bikes that are just a 'bit' awkward leave you eternally achey, the 748 forces you to conform and i found it to be great in town and on the motorway. I did nearly 20,000 miles on it, mostly in London and loved every single one of them right up until the bearings ground me to a halt in streatham and i got off it for the last time (should never have sold it - especially not for the retched 749! - mine was crap i'm sure others are fine).

I can't speak highly enough about the 748, and while i can't afford to own one as a 2nd bike just yet, i will have another soon i hope, in the mean time i'll talk any bugger into getting one while i wait for my time.

never understood the "stones".... what would it be in kg/lb?
i'm 6'3", ~170 lb before putting on the gear... i found R6 ridicolously small, it felt like one of those mini-bikes that dissapear in your crack as soon as you're on it... having said that, the power is nice, it is a IL4 though, so the delivery is much different then v2. SV's are great bikes, my wife has one, and i ride it every now and then, i find it a bit tight for my long legs - this could also be because the seat was cut out lower to fit the wife.
the SV has few advantages: it is cheeper to buy, & to maintain (then Ducati), it is very easy to work on - i love how simple & logical Suzuki's designs are... tons of parts new and used, OEM or aftermarket... and it crashes quite well
that being said, R6 gives you quite a bit more 'kick'...

i do not like Ducatis, so if you are a fan, please stop reading now.
i find them quite unreliable, they cannot take abuse, break down often, are expensive to fix, or replace broken parts after a crash... not a track tool at all, unless you are sposored and they will pay for complete rebuild after every race. they handle great and have good power, but soooo uncomfortable... only good for 10 min rides and displaying in front of a coffee shop - IMO
