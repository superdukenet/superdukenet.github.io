Ive done about 60000 miles on superdukes. Mileage is no problem.

Some pillions have issues with exhaust fumes swhrling up behind the pillion in an updraught and making them nausious...have her smell for it on test ride. Also, no grab rails or handles....just you to grab on to mate.

Ive been out with an MT01 following my old slower SD amd he proper cooked his brakes with just a single rider. Avoid.!

As for the other two....depends on what you want....

Top speed, heavier with slightly less resale value and reliability (subjectively).

Slower steering, less aggressive looks, British tradition and Ace cafe friendly burger n chips appeal

Or fast, great handling, more unique looks, slightly poorer dealer network than Ape and much worse than Trumpet but an addictive package and smiles per mile.

2011-06-14 19:48:00 UTC
SD really is a great bike - just makes you smile. It's generally well engineered and refined from front to back - form really does follow function. It doesn't look like a frickin hedgehog with bits sticking out of it like an after thought. Engines are strong if looked after. Two up - not too much padding but comfortable enough. My wife is 8.5 stone & 5' 8" & fits well. Yours - if she doesn't know already, will need to use her legs - grip your arse with her knees to stop pushing you into the tank - this will counter no grab rail. Compared to your gixxer it's not as smooth - power pulses and all that, but that'll be the same with any twin....and low speed throttle is on/off so feathering the throttle in town isn't particularly relaxed as you do need to work at it harder than on a four. I now ride with my 1st finger over the brake lever to steady my hand...which isn't a bad thing anyway. You do get used to dealing with it so don't let it put you off. All in all....it's brilliant !!
