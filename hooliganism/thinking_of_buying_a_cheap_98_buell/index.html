2011-06-13 13:00:00 UTC
I put the stock bars back on and adjusted them to feel pretty much exactly like my stock Superduke bars...the only change I ever made to the Superduke bars was bar-risers and after one ride around the block I changed those right back..the stock SD bars feel fine and I guess it makes some sense to have both these bikes fit me more or less the same...the Odom bars will go on my Yamaha SR500 Street Tracker project bike...when that finally gets done (maybe this year)

Bar changing may seem like a waste of time, but I like it...It's normally a quick and easy change to do and it radically changes the feel of any bike...But in general I think the best bar on any bike is the traditional relatively low "European" sport bar...which is more or less what originally came on the SD (and the Buell)...its a good compromise....

My younger brother has always ridden his bikes with clip-ons and he thinks the Superduke is a "dirt bike" ....

One last thing about the Buell...aside from the odd mixture of metric and American nuts+bolts sizes, it is very easy to work on...everything real clear and simple...very owner-maintenance friendly ...so different from the Superduke...a nice break now and then.
