test report from driving SDR battle series bike

fredrik

2008-12-06 17:35:00 UTC
Woke up this morning to a very nice day with sun and dry roads. Figured it was a good time to finally test drive an SDR. So I headed down to Bracken where they have a few of the battle series bikes for sale. The one I tested belonged to Jon Pearson, bike number 23. Here it is from when at brands:


First impression was that it's like sitting on a wooden chair. I drove off and was very surprised to find that first gear geared to speads well above 70mph, strange... a few moments later I realised why, the bike was set up for race with first gear up and the rest down. So what I thought was first gear was in fact second. Good thing I didn't try to shift from "first" to "second"

Once I figured out the gear order I still didn't like the gearing, it was definitely higher geared than my bike, first gear (the real first this time) hit 60mph. This is the first thing that I'd change if I buy it.

I do like that wooden chair seat though, it never felt uncomfortable during the 40minute test drive. Instead it felt very maneuverable. Also the ride height is significantly higher than my bike due to the taller rear suspension. This increased rear height results in very noticeable sharpening of the steering -nice! +At 6'4" I'll take any increase in ride height I can get...

Now on to the engine. It's definitely more powerful than my 07SD, but not as much as I was expecting. There's a bit more snatch but I've never had a problem with mine and don't mind the aggressive throttle response. I would have no problems commuting each day with it. The deep sound of the round akras positively surprised me, despite having the baffles in they sounded almost as much as my evo's without the baffles. Also, the bike popped all the time love those pops on the overrun!

So are the differences that big? Yes and no. Right after I got back on my bike it felt like going from a sports car to a comfy mercedes. But after a couple of miles I was once again smiling and enjoying my bike -which is one hell of a bike! But I do prefer the SDR so I've asked the sales guy to give me a quote for my bike in exchange. Should find out Monday what they can do. If it's decent I'll become more orange shortly
