First ride on Superduke...my impression..

DukeMonster

2011-06-30 07:12:00 UTC
Just picked up my Superduke R with some minor upgrades this Monday and gave it my first real ride on this evening after the clouds cleared up. My first impression...let's just say I was a bit disappointed with the power delivery....but mind you, this is me coming off a Ducati 1098 Superbike (recently sold). I felt the same way though after I first rode the 1098 ..after a few modifications, it was a whole different riding experience, as I hope it will be with the Duke. On the Superduke, I found that power delivery was flat, and did not pull as hard as I expected. I was shifting way to often, as the revs came up very quickly. I'd hate to be shifting that many times on faster curves/turns. Just felt like it was not getting the most out of the motor's torque. I attribute this to the gearing (41t) that came with the bike. The set up is probaly more adaptive to supermoto type courses/roads (tight stuff). I'm geared more towards the fast corners and sweepers. So the top to my list of things to change is the rear sprocket, and to put the baffles in the Yoshi exhaust cans to spruce up the torque curve. I plan on doing a couple of trackdays in the upcoming months on tracks that have some speed (T-hill and Infineon). If anyone has any experience on these tracks, your set-up advise (gearing, tyres, damper setting, etc) would be greatly appreciated.

I finally installed the Motobox...spent the majority or the time trying to get off..and on, the SAS bolt on the front cylinder..install was smooth otherwise. First ride after install was great. Throttle felt more crisp and revved up quickly. Went for a 5 hour ride in the Santa Cruz Hills and it felt great, especially in the tighter stuff..it just squirts off the corner and dives into the next. No hesitation or pops, so I assume the current map is pretty close. I will remap at a later date. Had a little drag race with a Honda CBR 600rr, he got the jump at the start and the Duke was able to keep up, but could not close the gap to pass..Damn..I was to busy shifting..had to shift every 10 meters cause reved up to fast. Sprocket just came in, so I will install and hope for the better. Gonna do a trackday next week, so the sprocket should help stretch the Duke's legs.
