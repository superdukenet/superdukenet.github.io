Well, I was reading Hornet's little story and thought it'd be very interesting and funny (hopefully) to read about other people's. Gruesome I know bwaahaahaa!

So I'll start;

2004, I'm begging my Dad to let me ride his bike (02 VFR v-tec), and he finally relents.
"My beloved son, here are the keys, there's the track. I know you've never ridden a bike before. Don't survive if you crash my bike. Have fun".

5 laps later I hit the v-tec for the first time, and head towards a 90deg right-hander at what feels like triple my previous speeds...

So naturally I grab a full four fingers on the front brake with the strength of a woman giving childbirth, and for some stupid-ass reason I actually remember the linked brakes and stamp on the back too.

I sailed gracefully over the handlebars while the bike did an equally graceful cartwheel behind me. The head marshall was behind me at the time, and says the bike actually did a 360 in the air and landed back on it's wheels (explains the popped fork seal), wobbling a bit and then flopping onto it's left-hand side.

Mild concussion, dislocated left shoulder and broken right foot.
All in all, must've been hilarious and I've only finished paying off my Dad 2 months ago.

Not too bad considering what the marshall explained to us happened!



PS: Didn't like the bike anyway...

2008-06-12 12:32:00 UTC
This happened on a brandy new 2002 gixxer 600 yellow/black, and the only bike I've ever owned with a steering damper. I was on the Garden State Parkway riding at about 100mph when my friend and I decided to slow down a bit while passing an infamous cop speed trap. Needless to say I gave it WAY TOO MUCH front brake, the bike began to violently tank slap. Instead of letting go of the brake and riding it out, I gave it more front brake, and off into the wild blue yonder I took off over the bars. I slid 100 yards without a jacket. The bike a total loss slid 500 yards. I recon i was traveling at 75mph when I hit the pavement.

Every moment I was telling myself this is the moment a car takes my life, but nothing ever hit me. When I stopped tumbling I stood up to sea of white lights. My friend ran up to me, and he looked like he died. He was pale, and breathing heavy, and was really happy to find me alive. I walked onto the ambulance, never went into shock, and went home after 3 hours of "scraping" at the hospital. 30 days later I returned to work, but I took a year off from bikes, trying to gather myself...the rest if history. I learned an important lesson, and I hope someone else see this and NEVER RIDES WITHOUT PROPER GEAR!!!!!!

PS I know I'm wearing a moomoo, but what you cant see is both my hips and sides looked just like my arm, and I couldn't wear anything with a waist band for 3 weeks. Showering was the worst part, the water would be at a slow drip, and it still hurt so bad.
