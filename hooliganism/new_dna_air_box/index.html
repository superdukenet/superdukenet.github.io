bic_bicknell

2011-06-26 07:38:00 UTC
I log on here and see the same discussion by new members about airflow and liberating power over and over again.

When I first discovered Superduke.net I'd been riding about on my '07 bike for nearly two years with only engine mod being a set of slip on Akras and the factory map, (installed from new when I bought it so never experienced the standard exhaust or power delivery.) My first thoughts were, "Wow, I can't believe there is so much debate and experimentation about the fueling and breathing on this bike!" There are threads and threads going back for years - this is what got me into the site in the first place. What I did was to get a bottle of wine and go back to the beginning and read the threads - right back to the days of the "Marbox" and the guy who originally developed a better filter and how eventually Aaron developed the Motobox. You'll aslo get to know the guy called, Dr.F and see why he's honoured with a thread title in the technical section. Anyone who takes the time and effort to do this will appreciate the past time and effort that so many SD enthusiasts have invested and what has gone into getting to the state of play we are now in. It makes for a great read - and there are some great characters and discussions.

Doing this might also help appreciate that the Motobox, (as DRSWade rightly point out) is a product developed, tested and manufactured by genuine fans of the SD and this forum - and that this new DNA product is more or less a blatant copy that capitalises on work done by others. As a product designer by profession, I look at their offering and my intuition says that this is a company who has done very little original R&D of their own to develop this further. If Aaron had a design registration he could probably sue - but he won't have and he's a small player and DNA are bigger, and that's how the world works unfortunately. The best way to discourage blatant copying is to choose to buy the genuine product and send your cash to the people who deserve it more. I would leave that decision up to your conscience.

On the power claims issue. What is very clear to me is that improving airflow is never done in isolation. It would be silly to do any work on aspiration without combining it with exhaust mods of some kind. And that is what everyone does. I've never heard of anyone dyno testing a standard SD engine with a Motobox without also doing one or all of the other mods that also contribute to HP and torque gains. And as others have mentioned, many of the benefits of doing all this work is smoother throttle at low revs, cleaner fueling etc, not just top end power - it's not the holy grail for many. And IMHO the difference in inherent BHP that individual SD engines deliver combined with the massive discrepancy between different dyno equipment means that bragging about extreme dyno figures is pretty much irrelevant.

Also, anyone who has bought a Motobox knows the feeling when you unwrap that CNCd top cover and appreciate the quality and workmanship. They are really lovely.

That DNA product looks really cheap and shit in orange!
