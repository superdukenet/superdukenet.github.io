Hey Paul, still looking for a fast way to bleed the clutch? I did mine with a syringe from the bottom. Worked great. There is a blead valve on the master, thanks KTM who ever. Brake and clutch are two different manufactures, and take two different fludis. Minerial oil only in the clutch. I did a final bleed on the masters( both brake and clutch masters) by leaving a foot lenght of clear tube on the bleed fitting to watch the bubbles bleed off. Sure is nice to see the bubbles in the clear line. Hold the line upright. You can leave the line on over night to re-bleed the next day. Agent Orange

Way cool bike Paul. From your task list the most fustrating thing left to do will be to mount your tank, if its anything like my 08SD. The plastic tank will want to move around ,(as in getting bigger) if its not mounted on the frame. You probly know about the new hinges you can buy which moves the tank towards the seat. I modified my stock hinges with a small chain saw file. Another mod I did was on the front tank mounting screw . I champhered the screw by chucking it up to the drill press and taking a file to it. I counter sunk the tapped hole in the frame. This will make it easer to align the screw when you try to shoe horn the tank past the forks top clamp. I also read in this form if you get the screw started to thread into the frame before you push the tank down on to the frame, you will cuss less.
Next on your list to cuss is the battery box / wires. Pure after thought design. My wires and connections are now covered with road grit and grime with only 3 months and 6k smiles on the clock. But what a fun bike, and yes it is a tall bike.
