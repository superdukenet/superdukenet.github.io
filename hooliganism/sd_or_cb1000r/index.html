2011-02-07 14:43:00 UTC
there are a few minor things depending on bike age to watch for. On 05-06s watch for clutch slave cylinder replacement (original OEM ones sometimes gave up) replacements are under £100 from Oberon if you need to.

Very rare water seals issues have turned oil into Mayo on some i think too.

05/06s are a little sharper handling and faster steering due to angle of forks but thats about it.

07 onwards are good bikes, the Clutch slave was sorted i think. Slightly more sedate ride but not much in it i reckon.

The main thing about them is front brakes, a few reports of seizing under salty winter riding. I've never had a problem in those conditions but I've not run OEM pads and I've cleaned my callipers a lot. KTM advise frequent and regular cleaning on front callipers if you ride in those conditions. If you dont, you can just enjoy awesome braking.

I am one of the highest mileage SD owners on here having done about 50000 miles on my 1st bike. I never had any major issues with it (it was an 06) and it is still running (somewhere).

In all honesty, I have run a Honda a lot before too and I really think build quality is as good with KTM, especialy in terms of corrosion protection, frame etc. KTMs component choices are better IMHO (Brembo/WP etc). BUT remember they are built for a different purpose than Honda. They are not as refined in the Honda way, they are a little rougher round the edges but that's where the character comes from. They are a low volume manufacturer so understand that before you commit. Having said that......I reckon a Honda would get boring after a while....but I'm on my 2nd SD(R) and have no desire to change to anything else!

If you go with an SD, you will most likely want to get louder pipes for it and tinker with induction and fuelling eventually....but thats where the fun lies...and the smiles per mile are massive!

2011-02-08 09:45:00 UTC
Regarding reliability, I've onwed 3 bikes with the LC8 engine, and apart from a few early niggles in 2005 with the starter clutch they've generally proved to be bullet proof (for a non-Jap bike, at least). The only scary issues have been with a few brake pads on KTM's brembos. Fuelling can be very lumpy on some bikes (esp SDRs) - although it seeems to vary bike by bike, but there are loads of remedies, from the cheap; (throttle cam) to the expensive; (Akras, airbox, PCIII).

The only thing you must do is keep an eye on oil levels, as lower mileage bikes burn up to 1litre evey 1000 miles, and they must never be run low. Checking oil levels accurately is a bit tricky, so it's easy to over-fill, or not notice that it's getting low. This gets better as the miles pile on. Mine now burns half that oil, or less.

If you're tempted by an SDR (...aren't they pretty...) you'll find the suspension hard as rock and the seat is a plank of wood. Both can be sorted, but it's quite different from the standard bike so you either need to have a steel arse and ride smooth roads, or spend some money sorting it out.
