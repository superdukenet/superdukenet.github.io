I ran Corsa IIIs on the SD at Big Willow last October. They started to give up the ghost in the afternoon. Both the front and back were sliding around.

You can see in the PIC how the edges started looking greasy.

I did a couple of sessions on my GSXR that afternoon. (I had let a friend run in in the AM and we switched.) The GSXR was shod with Power Ones. This was the second track day on those tyres - the first at Streets.

Those fooking tyres are amazing. They never slip a nanometer.

I'm going to try to squeeze another day out of them this Friday at Fontana. If they suck, or don't pass tech, I plan on throwing a set of Michelin slicks on. I haven't tried slicks yet, so I can't recommend them.

I got this set of Power Ones for $275 off e-bay but have never found a like deal. They are expensive, but you won't be disappointed.

The Q2's should do fine, especially in the spring when it's still cool out. Street tyres don't require as much heat as race tyres to perform well. They WILL fade on a hot day if you're pushing them though. Using race tyres (DOT's, slicks) I feel is unnecessary until you've got tyre warmers, consistently slipping on street tyres, and can afford tyres that are nearly twice as expensive as street tyres. Race tyres require heat to work. Chances are you will not get the required heat if you're in the beginner group and could wind up cold tearing them. Plus the SD doesn't have tyre stretching power (or weight) where you really notice the tyres flex and stretch.

I ran 2 year old BT-016's (dry as hell) at NJMP in November and ran consistent times as I had previously on a CBR1000RR WITHDOT's. The only things slipping were my pucks and toe sliders.

A pair of Q2's just showed up on my porch the other day to use at Jennings GP in Florida next month.

If you're deadset on DOT's, they're all really good. I used Conti's, Pirellis, and Bridgestones last year.
