Stupid Luke

2010-11-03 21:24:00 UTC
I swapped from a standard SD to an SDR and it's more raw, harsher, firmer suspension, but it's all good: An extra 15bhp, a smidge more midrange, sharper turning, pretty orange frame...

I had the single seat already, so that was no surprise, but it does need an aftermarket seat or recover to make it bearable for longer rides.

I also changed the front springs to softer items, as it's too firmly sprung for riders under about 85kg (dry), and it's made the bike much nicer around town, and better on track too, so a worthwhile mod for anyone wincing and weighting the pegs every time they see a pothole coming up.

Go for it.

I'm 235 lbs, 5'11, and I absolutely love my SDR! Yeah, sure it's hard-core, sharp-edge, brutal-looking, etc.--but that was the point. If I wanted soft, i could have bought a very nice Speed Triple (still love 'em--not knocking them at all) or kept my R1100S. The engine is lustful. Let me say that again. The engine makes me want to bed my bike! LOL!!

Seriously, though, the only draw back for me is the mono-posto thing. There are times when I want to throw the sheila on the back and go for a spin to Pink's hotdogs or Tommy's or something, and, well, we can't (especially now that I sold my R1100S...). The Ape is similarly mono-posted, so we have to drive on date night. Oh well. Very much worth it!

I'm ordering up some FMF Apex', thinknig hard about the 'box and secondaries, and definitely going to do the Tune ECU thing. I've "become accustomed" to the throttle snatchiness, but I imagine the Motobox and TuneECU/FMF's will help that a bit.

You can't go wrong either way, but I will repeat myself: we don't buy these bikes to be rational OR responsible...
