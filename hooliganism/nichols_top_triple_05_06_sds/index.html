My SD is the first one into the UK.
It was KTM's press bike and then the prototype for the Battle series.
If the '07 onwards model is slower steering than the '05-'06 bikes why did the Nichols clamp come out for them?
I've watched the video on this trying to find out why Trivalley thought this was such a good idea and I'm still not sure I understand what its for/how you are supposed to use it.
I think its important to understand how and what they used both components for and what effects they were looking for. (triple clamp and ride height adjuster)
E.G. if you push the forks down through the yokes (raising yoke and headstock) this has the effect of not only increasing the the trail but increasing the effective steering head angle. If you then use the ride height adjuster you can reduce the steering head angle and trail back to the original figures but this also has the effect of raising the center of gravity. Is this the only effect? Other than increasing the pitch of the motorcycle and making it even more difficult for short arses like myself to reach the ground is this the only effect? Does raising the CofG improve front end feel?
Obviously adjusting the rear ride height too much can severely effect the amount of traction available.
According to my little suspension book raising the rear ride height steepens the steering making the bike turn in more quickly, reduce stability and increasing front grip whilst also reducing rear grip.
