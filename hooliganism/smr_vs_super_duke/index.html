SMR vs. Super Duke

DukeKook

2007-12-08 03:52:00 UTC
Ok all I now have officially lost it and bought my 4th bike this year! Last Saturday I took delivery of a 2007 SMR.


Wow what a fine ride she is, I cant really give a full review yet as I only have about 300 miles on her so I am still taking it easy. Here is what I can tell you


The SMR is a BLAST to ride, I commuted on her all week and just had a ball. The power is good but not in the same ball park as the Super Duke.

The riding posistion is VERY good, I think better than the Super Duke, I like the seat (most people hate it) but I am only doing an hour or 2 at a time so it has not been an issue. I like the way it invites you to move around it is so easy to hang off.

The handle on this bike is also outstanding. It is not as intuitive as the Super Duke but pretty close.

The bike is a bit taller than the SD, It could be an issue for some, but I dont mind. I like sitting up tall in the saddle looking over the cages.

The gearing is a bit odd, the bike lugs in 6th gear at any speed under about 75.

I love the look of the bike, the black over orange frame is a sweet look. It looks so much better in the flesh than in the photos I have seen.


Ok more info after the first service which will also see
Leo's
16T front sprocket
2 larger on the rear (not sure what it is now)
Jet carbs



Overall I could not be happier, I was looking for something that gave me the same fun factor as the super duke and the SMR delivers there hands down. It has to be one of the easiest bikes to ride fast on the market, very forgiving, very comfortable, and the grin factor is off the charts.

Hello All, Just an observation, I have said all of this many times. DK, you know it most of all, yes, IMHO... the SM has a hair less power but it has a very usable power, just less.
I felt....in full lean angles, that I would have been more comfy on a lower bike, AKA SD? the transition from upright to full lean has a neater feel on the SM, weirdly so ?? a very perplexing thing as to the way the SD VS. SM is? Handling is spot on on the SD, but on the SM is just generally happy, no complaints, but not quite as much feed back, so there is less to think about when riding it fast. The complaint of 75 mph in 6th was one of my own too. the gear box is well thought out other than that top gear to the moon ? Better on the clunkiness on the SM than the SD, for rougher paved roads,,,Sorry to say.....that if I had it all to do over once again, I would have kept my 06 SM, and patiently waited on the 08 SDR,
cos the fun factor on the SM is a touch higher, but it took the actual experience for me to realise this. As usual it all boils down to taste, but the SM or SMR will not disappoint, my corner apex speeds were faster on the SM just cos I knew,,,,,I would not be dragging anything, this is a real mental game for sure !
