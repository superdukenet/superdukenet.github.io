Think about what type of rider you are as well as the tracks. I just sold my 600cc (636cc actually) trackbike as it wasnt that much fun to ride. The litre trackbikes are fun to ride but you are looking at a a set of tyres every weekend if you are racing it and the first highside is likely to be a big one.

What I like about the Superduke is the handling on the brakes into, and the low down friendly drive out of the corners. I know the SD will never rip snort down the straights with an S1000RR but those goalposts move every year and pretty soon a GSXR 1000K8 will be down on power too.

I am too old, slow and lazy to be dancing on the gearlever like you have to on a 600 and hated revving the thing to 14000rpm just to keep up. So the SD suits me and my limited aspirations, At the other end of the scale I saw a good rider (Rob MacNealy) at Lydden once on his 06 SD battle bike and he was passing the latest il4 thous like they were stood still. Lydden is a short track, almost all corners and mostly 3rd gear. I reckon MacNealy just stuck it in top and did the whole lap sideways, being an ex Supermoto racer probably helped. Anyway, until I can ride like that I dont need anymore that the SD has.

jmann

2012-01-16 23:27:00 UTC

As mentioned above depends if your racing or just Trackday riding?

If racing a sd won't really be competitive against 1000cc race bikes.

But if just Trackday riding and starting to ride on track then a sd will be a good starting point. I did first 4 trackdays this year and thought after second one id sell the sd get a crap commuter and a trackbike, but realised the sd is all I'll need on track for quite a while they handle well and are good on the brakes and easy to get out of corners. I ride with a friend that has a 1999 cbr600 with ohlins front and back and we are very evenly matched in terms of skill and bike speed.

both had 1000's on the road but the pace of a sd/older 600 is what we need on track to learn what were doing. If we had 1000's I'm sure we'd have learnt less and probably crashed more learning to ride on track, it's a very different game to road riding and we learnt more in 4 days than last 7/8 years of road riding.

I'm not sure from your original post if your new to track or want to start racing but if new to track either keep Superduke or buy a cheap track prepped 600 and realise that fast road riding counts for very little and you have a lot to learn.

Get better brake pads...probably the most noticeable mod I've ever done was changing to race pads. Effortless stopping, you only need to worry about keeping the rear in line, or somewhat close.

Also look into geometry adjustments with triple clamps. The SD turns in great but doesn't like to stay leaned over. That was my limiting factor last year when I compared my laptimes between a Cbr 600, a Cbr 1000, and the SD. And how I wound up with the sweet avatar. But alas, she was still 4 seconds off the 600 and 7 seconds off the 1000 times. I wasn't holding much back that day but the high speed turns felt unstable and significant time was lost.
