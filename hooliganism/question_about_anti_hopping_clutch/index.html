Question about anti-hopping clutch

drswade

2010-09-23 04:12:00 UTC
I have the KTM/Suter slipper clutch. Last track day it started slipping out of slow turns that required heavy acceleration. I replaced the clutch pack with Barnett performance clutch kit. The thing slipped some more and then just stopped grabbing at all. You can hold it still while in first gear! I eliminated the master and slave cylinders as a problem. The clutch pack seems fine, as did the one I took out. Do you all think that this pressure plate is worn out and causing the problem? I don't remember what it looked like new. I don't understand why the plates ride against steel friction plates but the outside disk rides against an aluminium pressure plate?
Seems like this would wear fast.
Thoughts, suggestion and solutions??? Please!!!


bic_bicknell

2010-09-23 04:37:00 UTC
Hi drswade,

I have the same clutch fitted - did it at the beginning of the year and can remember that the inside of the pressure plate was not worn like that, (it was used when I bought it so not brand new).

I can't really help much but maybe you could contact KTM and ask if there is a tolerance for the thickness of the plate in that area, (like measuring a brake disc). It does look kind of worn down. I had a look in the instructions I got with the clutch and there is no mention of this dimension or of general maintenance as the clutch wears.

What's strange is that one would think that the way a clutch should be designed is for the plates to wear out - easily and cheaply replaced, not this part of the clutch - which will not be cheap to replace.

Keep us posted on this - I'd be interested to know what you find out.

Good luck.
