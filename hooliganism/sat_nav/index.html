The old original post date slips by again, I bought the Zumo 660 ages ago now.

Unlike the 550 the 660 motorcycle mount has all it's ports for headphones, usb, mic and power in one cable from the mount itself, which then splits into 4 cables much further down the cable.

It's good for waterproofing and having the sockets coming out by the seat so you can run your headphones/mic leads down the inside of your jacket and just poke out the bottom so no trailing wires across the tank but it's a pain in the bum if like me you wanted to put a quick connect on the power so you can swap the whole cradle to another bike and weren't planning on using the other connections anyway, I decided to buy a spare cradle for my RC8 but was not the cheapest solution, i could have altered the original but didn't fancy ruining it in case i upgrade in the future and want to ebay it.

I bought the Scala q2 dual headset with it and for the phone and directions only it works great but for music is utter shite so it looks like I might use the hard wired connections after all as you can split the mp3 and directions to the hardwire while still operating your phone through the headset. The reason it's shite is that as it turns out the headset is not stereo it is two mono earpads and so just sounds completely wrong. It seems there are other stereo bluetooth intercoms around but the Scala is so widely marketed and sold at every single online sat nav dealer that I just automatically bought it, my mistake, I usually research everything thoroughly

Anyway, I'm pretty happy with the 660, it looks good, the cradle when empty is also better looking than the 550 and it does what it's supposed to do and gets me to where I want to go, sat nav is worth the money just to be able to custom route yourself away from motorways in my opinion and made my travels much more interesting, it even survived 160 miles of constant pissing down rain at 80-90mph, I stayed on the mway for that one as I just wanted to get home.

Here's some pics, I went for the left bar in the end as although I originally wanted it central I found I much prefer it there, it's high enough that I don't have to look down to see it and it's easily operated with my left hand and doesn't get in the way of the clocks or tank bag

If you're going to go touring around Europe for 3 weeks or more, a dedicated Bike SatNav with all the bells and whistles might well be worth the money, but if you're just "riding around" on day trips or weekend trips, then I believe a Car SatNav should do you just fine. Indeed, the best thing about my Garmin Zumo is the ability to create custom routes so I don't have to sit on the motorway or some straight dual carriageway the whole time, to me that alone made the satnav worth its weight in gold.

Owning an 05 I'm also making good use of the fuel guage, it's only really a trip meter but the good part is that when it gets to my predefined mileage an icon comes up which I can press to direct me to the nearest petrol station without having to search through various menus to get there while driving, it definitely saved my bacon at least once on a long trip as the distance to the nearest petrol station was right on my limit and was not on the main road I was on so I'd have never found it without the satnav.

I also use the phone through it, this is not something I use on spirited rides and I keep it to a minimum on any ride but when turning up for customer installs its been brilliant for getting directions to the right entrance or car park without having to deglove, remove my helmet, undo my jacket etc, especially if it's raining. Also good for teling the mrs to unlock the side gate when I'm getting close to home.

I'm still not convinced by the zip bag idea although lots of people mention it so there must be something in it, I rode 160 miles in the hammering rain at 80-90 mph on the motorway and the Garmin came through it perfectly intact which is more than I can say for the gear I was wearing.
