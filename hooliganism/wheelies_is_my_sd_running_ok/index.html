Wheelies is not only about power, technic is just as important. If you know the technic you can easily do a wheelie on a GS500.....

Anyway if you sit upright, and move back a bit, just pull the handlebars when you 'crack the throttle' it will come up immidiately.

If your goal is to follow a R1 or Gixxer 1000, you might want to change your gearing. Following these bikes during flat out accelration, is nearly impossible, they are extremely powerful.

I am running 16/40 gearing, and the bike wheelies like hell in second (clutch not needed). On the track i can follow the powerbikes better, until around 180 km/h, the i have to give up.....

(Good old 4-stroke dirtbikes, are great for practising wheelies, if that is your goal..... )

I am poor at wheelies, but if I quickly crack the throttle open in first gear on my 07, then the front comes up so fast that I often panic and chop the throttle. I'm 170lb. I can't imagine what would happen if I pulled the handle bars.

It took me a while to develop the confidence to crack it wide open. I try to be relaxed in the upper body and grip the bike with my knees. I exhale when I do it to help make sure my upper body is relaxed. If I flare my elbows up or pull the bars I know I'm not relaxing. I always cover the clutch and rear brake. I always look way down the road, and not down. I'm working on running them out to the rev limiter in first and slowly building height. Not sure I will ever get to changing gears.

It also will wheelie with much less throttle if I crack it open some to extend the forks, shut the throttle to compress the forks and then, as the forks extend, crack the throttle open a little more to bring the front up. It's all timing with this method. This is the method taught at a wheelie school I attended. The idea is that with practice you will need less and less speed/revs/throttle to get it to the balance point. Then you can get the wheel up and keep it there while moving slowly by modulating the throttle and rear brake. I'm not good so I've gone back to good old power wheelies.

I also tried clutching it up too, but I am no good and have much less control with that method so I haven't used it much. Too much poo in my pants.

I know lots of this is obvious, but I hope some of it helps.

Your Superduke would wheelie with half the power in the right hands. It would have to be seriously under-performing to lose that much grunt.

Don't ask your bike to wheelie for you, ask yourself to make the bike wheelie.

I can wheelie a 50bhp KTM LC4a any day of the week, and even my 950SM on a good day (much easier, but the fear factor is my limiter).

...so far I know I "could" wheelie the SD any time,.any day, but I seem to have a personal cut-out switch that stops me doing it... but it's categorically NOT the bike.

One word of caution... Wheelie often enough, and eventually you'll bin-it, so practice with that in mind.
