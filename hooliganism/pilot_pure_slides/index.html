Now this thread has come back I have a bit of a thought.

All the latest jap thous have huge bhp numbers Plus the BMW hits 190 BHP with a couple of easy, cheapish mods but where do they do it?.........................Right at top of the rev range that is where.

What does to SD do to tyres? rips them apart from low down, thats what! So why do we all think the latest development in race tyres will be suitable for our bikes?

Why are we suprised when tyre designed to suit the S1000RR'S etc are worse than the tyre they replace on our bikes? Why dont we see it as another benefit of SD ownership? Tyres designed for superstock racing in 2005 are ample for our bikes. If you need more track grip than Supercorsas or Racetecs can offer or more road grip than 2CT''s you wont find it in Pilot Pures or latest Racetecs. We are asking a light 90 degree Vtwin with a light flywheels to stomp us out of a corner as fast as poss. The tyre companies are desiging these tyres for 800cc Moto GP bikes or 600cc Moto 2 bikes to carry corner speed.

We all like to think the latest tyre will make us faster and give us more grip. But our riding styles hasnt changed ,neither has the bike maybe we should be rejoicing in the cheap tyres available that the Superbike boys think are old hat?

No 2CT'S have been discontinued but you can still get them cheap from many tyre suppliers. Just as trackdayers were still having fun on cheap Rennesports until they couldnt get anymore and had to switch to racetecs.

However, the whole principle of tyres has changed. 5 years ago, when our bikes were new tech, the thinking was softish front (k1) and medium rear (k2) not just for us but for il4 1000cc trackdayers too. Look now and Supersport, superstock, and fashion victim trackdayers are running k1/2 front and k0 rears. Not slow in fast out anymore it is fast in and carry speed out. I dont think those tyres work on Superdukes, if they do, they take away our power out advantages.

Personally, I will always look for a harder rear as I hate it when they start breaking off in chunks. If the price I pay is a bit of looseness on corner exit I can live with that, but Dutch with his Pures and Bic with his M5's are getting slides in corners too and paying 250 quid + for the priveledge

I was told the Pilot Pures were made to work on road or track, and to keep the PSI the same for both. This has been mentioned in lots of places, and was backed up when I spoke to a tyre tech at Brands...


...but to throw more fuel onto the fire, when I spoke to my tyre guy in London (who used to service tyres on trackdays) he thought it pretty hilarious that the average rider thinks themsleves fast enough to genuinely need to drop thier tyre pressures for track day use. He said, some people are that quick, but unless it's a very hot day, in a 20 minute session (esp in the Novice group, and some slower Inters), not many riders will get so much heat in their tyre that dropping PSI in advance to compensate is something that would make a noticeable difference. ...His views, not mine. Feel free to disagree.

However, for the Pilot Pures, everyone agrees; 34/34 for all uses. Apparently.


I also agree totally with StupidLukes comments about modern tyres. Supersports track tyres are made for high revving IL4s that frequently reach over 150mph and weigh less and less... Our bikes deliver big fat torque at relatively low RPM and spend more time between 50 and 110, so we want tyres to suit that purpose.
