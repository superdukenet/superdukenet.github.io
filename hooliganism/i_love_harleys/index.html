I'm no stranger to great handling bikes, I started with a 77 Ducati 900 Supersport as my first vehicle in my name as a youngster and have owned many Ducatis, Laverdas, Aprilias since then, along with my SD's. But I still enjoyed the heck out of my Sporster built into a Street-tracker and my Street Rod. They only built the Street Rod for 2 years and if it weren't for the fact that it was apparently built with a complete disdain for trying to save weight (it's as if they made every component as heavy as possible) I would still have it. That thing flat out handled despite being over 125 pounds heavier than I would like. So not all Harleys are bad handling and some are nice all around bikes.

Now, as for the high percentage of "get a lifers", poseurs, fakers, wannabe "tough guys" who buy a Harley for image alone and put on an attitude, sure, they make me sick. But that does not make the entire line of bikes bad, and the company has tried, and indeed succeeded, in bringing quality a long way up.
