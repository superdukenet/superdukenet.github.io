Tyler,

The SDR does have a catalytic converter from the factory. It is located in the silencer inlet, and the factory silencer is a heavy 1 into 2 design. You will be able to use the stock akra headers and pipe to the silencer connection. I have Akra Evo silencers on my bike, but there are quite a few other great choices available that would likely perform and sound very similar.

Once the cat is gone, you can remap in a variety of ways as you've seen. On my SDR, I had the akra map flashed at my local KTM dealer. It smoothed response a little. Then I installed a Motohooligan airbox, SAS blockoff plates and Motohooligan secondary throttle plates. The last three things were done at the same time and really smoothed things out. I also installed O2 eliminators, although I've read the sensors are disabled by the Akra map. The bike has much smoother throttle response and pulls stronger throughout the rpm range now, not to mention it sounds amazing.

The secondary throttle plates are located at the uppermost point on the throttle bodies. Look for a Youtube video of the Motohooligan Motobox and you will find videos showing how they interact with the primary throttle when the engine is running.

I can't speak much about suspension because I haven't had a chance to do a trackday. I'm certain its a great trackbike, and contrary to what some articles say, I think its a very comfortable street bike. I had a Monster S2R 800 previously, and the SDR ergonomics are spades better. I can't comment on a comparison with the current gen Monster.

Installing all of the above is straight forward. Bic Bicknell, a member here, made an outstanding installation guide fot the Motohooligan airbox and it can be found on this site. Riding the SDR stock is fun, but doing the above mods makes it a better bike and more fun.

I guess I would ask if you are going to be riding on the street or tracking it.
You will not be riding the same on either. This bike doesn't lend to "just taking it a little easier" on the street vs. track.
If you consider yourself a competent racer, you will quickly see what I mean.
The triple is mandatory, IMO, as I have said here ad nauseum. The clevis thing for the shock is needed only because of the raising the front with the Nichols triple. The good news is that with your weight, the stock springs should be good. I was just getting to where I was out riding the shock. Perhaps if you have Elka builds a shock for you, you can have them make it longer so you won't need the clevis? The SDs don't use a link so the shock set up is odd for a modern race bike.
I have posted my "to do" list before but here it is in short:
1. Slip ons- Akras and Leos are the finest products available due to their construction techniques. I have the Leo carbon cans. Lost something like 14 to 18 lbs, up high, where it counts.
2. 520 chain and aluminium sprocket. 4 or more pounds loss.
3. Suter slipper clutch
4. Shorai or similar Li Fe batteries. Stock battery= 3.9 kilos Shorai= 560 GRAMS!!!

If you are tracking it, you will be wound out all the time so the MH butterflies may not be needed?
I didn't list the Nichols parts because I have made it clear how I feel about it.
I love the MH box and highly recommend it as well. As you know, you HAVE to re-map after the intake/exhaust mods.
Seems TuneECU is the way to go and how I will handle mapping in the future.
Stay in touch here and keep up the queries!
