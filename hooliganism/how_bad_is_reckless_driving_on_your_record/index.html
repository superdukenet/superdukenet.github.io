Ok, I'll let you in on the secret - its all about the money. (I actually heard a criminal judge announce that from the bench once. Remarkably, his last name was Law. Not so remarkably, he's no longer a sitting judge. But I digress ....)

The system is designed to penalize you. If you hire a lawyer, that costs you money and that's part of the penalty. Hiring a lawyer also shows the judge you are acting responsibly and taking the charge seriously. I don't know what jurisdiction you are in, I've heard horror stories about certain VA courts, but in CA its unlikely you'd receive ACTUAL jail time for reckless driving with no accident or injuries. It would be more likely that if you receive jail time, it could be served as community service - picking up trash on the freeway or mopping floors in the morgue. But, again, that is something your lawyer can arrange for you.

So, that's my advice. Don't discuss the details on the internet and hire a lawyer. Good luck.
