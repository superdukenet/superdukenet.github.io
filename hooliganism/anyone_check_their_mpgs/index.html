159.7 divided by 3.595
by DribbleDuke » Mon May 10, 2010 1:00 pm

I will be riding through the Nevada desert to get to the SBK Utah round.There is a stretch that requires that I travel 170 miles between petrol stops. I have never done that many miles on the SD without a fill up. Maybe 138 miles was the most, on the ride home from Laguna Seca last year. I decided that it would be a good idea to see if it could be done. I filled up and rode 80 miles out a lonely hi-way and turned around and rode back. I can not tell you why the trip was .3 miles shorter back than forward, but the total miles back to the station was 159.7. I put 3.595 gallons of fuel in. The miles per gallon worked out to 44.422. The low fuel light did not even come on.
I rode the bike in a way that would give me the best mileage possible, without getting rediculously involved. I rode at a pace that would be close to 62 miles an hour. I should have grabbed the GPS but I was only seeing if I needed to carry extra fuel so as not to get stuck in the middle of nowhere. My bike was loaded with all the things that I will be taking on my journey. I am camping so that includes tent,sleeping bag, stove, fuel, water,clothes, alcohol,Gatorade. The bike has aftermarket slip ons and the Akrapovic mapping, which I thought would be very detrimental to the fuel mileage, but does not appear to have drastically reduced it. This brings a considerable peace of mind. I have to admit that it is not any where near the way I normally ride. I will sacrifice three hours of the trip each way for the security of making a landing at the next fuel stop. Just thought I would post this up so others would feel more comfortable pushing the MPG limit a little further.
