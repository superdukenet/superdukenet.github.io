Stupid Luke

Dont do it. I was tempted by the Multistrada at last years bike show. 1198 engine, traction control, go anywhere chassis - what's not to like?

Then a mate bought one, the S Model 14000 quid sterling plus a couple of grand of extras. He had nothing but problems with it and the bike was in dealers more than he had it. He was pretty coy about all the faults at the time after bigging the bike up so much when he got it but from what I remember; it was always cutting out, he dropped it during a low speed manouver when it cut out, not a known Ducati fault. The entire left hand switchgear packed up and hand to be replaced. He had about 3 repairs / replacement to the rear brake and none improved it. He hit a pheasant, hardly the bikes fault, and needed a new mirror - none in the country and no date when there will be 9in the end he got a dealer to take one off a showroom bike. There is no doubt more but he got rid, what is the point of having a tourer that never gets further than 10 miles from the dealers? In one of his numerous conversations with Ducati they admitted the bike had been brought to market too soon and really needed 6 months more testing.

None of these are unknown faults, other owners are getting some or none of them. It just seems he was unlucky to get a bike with all of them. I dont know if the basic model has similar faults or it the more electrical s that has all the problems.

Here is the worst bit, after 4 months of ownership he tried to trade it for anything else. First dealer offered him 6000 sterling!!! eventually he got 11000 but had to leave a lot of his bling on. Still a 5000 loss for 4 months of hassle. Worse thing was the new owner took the bike to London and got stranded there when the immobiliser developed a fault on his first ride.

Big shame as I though it would be a great bike.

Even if you find a good one and p/ex the SD you will need to put in another 6000 quid minimum. I am now looking at a two year old 1050 Triumph Tiger ABS for less than 6 grand so I can do a bit of touring next year and still keep the SDR.

Well say I'm getting old ( and sensible?) but I've alway been a victim of the grass is always greener when it comes to bike ownership!
I think many people buy, thinking they will be faster on a newer bigger HP bike. But the bike is largely irrelevant, and you may not be quicker, happier or more impressed with a new bike unless it is vastly different, and to be used for a different purpose than the previous.

While I had an Aprila RSVR prior to the SD, the duke is so much more fun and comfortable which I like and am happy with it.
If I were to trade it on a Tuono, or jap naked, I would be no faster and probably no happier! I love the SD, it ticks all the boxes as an underdog. Not being over powered means you can wring it's neck, ride it to it's limits and get that pump from getting the best from it. Quite frankly, if you need more it should be on the track!

I have a track bike for that purpose.

On the road the SD is the ultimate for me, a hooligan bike with attitude, capable of embaressing any other road bike in the right hands.

So weigh up carefully before changing. A well sorted SD that you know, and have spend your hard earned cash on. to get it to that point where you trust it,rely on and love it........well say no more!

Best of luck with your descision.

I fully appreciate the urge to change. I recently moved from the SD to an RC8. Is it faster?
