I have toured all over thr Pacific Nortwest on sport bikes with soft luggage, even 2 up. This is one nice set up from what I see. It is a design that I have had rolling around in my gray matter for some time. What I do to protect the body work is first wax any body work that will be under the soft bags. Cut clear shelving / book cover stuff that is self sticking and stick it on. Make dang sure you wax first. I got in a rush one time and did not wax and the clear coat on my bike came off with the cover. Another way to go rather than the stick on covering that I did after I lost my clear coat was to cut panels of felt to place under the soft bags. It's easy to toss the felt in the washer after the trip. I used electrical tape to hold it in place. What is nice about this system is you can remove this fast and have no funky hard bag brackets. I also have a Ducati ST4S that came with hard bags and I removed the brackets. I know I can easly tour on the SD. I put 86K on my 1986 Ninja 600. A lot of it 2 up. I have 90K on my 1992 ZX7. This SD is alot more cozzy than those bike for a long haul. Its fun to camp on Mt. Rainer, set up camp and ride the roads before all the tourestas wake up. Hey this has to be good. I think Its from Oregon, my state.
