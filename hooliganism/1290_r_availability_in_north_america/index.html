I can sense many of you are extremely frustrated to say the least with KTM, dealers and the whole process in general around this bike.

I can't say I blame you the least. I am probably the least patient man here. Big statement I know, but I would be close to the top if not in it.

If you however find a way to get through KTMs supply oversight and make it to the other side without killing your dealer I think you will be very pleased

Despite bike's fit flaws, assembly mistakes and other teething issues we see on most new model bikes it's a really a great piece of engineering.

I did a 600kms ride yesterday on the bike over an 8 hour time slot. I went out to seek all those great back roads where you can go bananas and really enjoy the bike. It was a mix of really tight stuff where bike handling would shine and some long sweepers.

The engine is superb! So much power in the midrange and it just keeps on coming. Just when you think you land yourself in a hot spot and you were charging a corner, you can easily get yourself out of trouble. I was not sore anywhere including my bum over such a ride and I am 6' so bike is comfortable for my hight

I complained as much as anyone else here about some issues that I can't accept for the price of the bike and I will have them rectified don't you worry. But when you put all that into prospective, the real riders amongst us buy this bike for how it rides and what it enables us to do and not how it looks or what accessories we can fit on it.

If you are patient enough and you get your bike, you will soon realise that the pesky little annoyances are nothing comparing to the grin you will have on your face after each ride. I still wake up the next morning with a grin on my face as if I slept with a hanger in my mouth.

Comrades: Ok so the bottom line is that there is a belief that if a register existed where somebody could see the Vin number of your bike it may ultimately lead to it being stolen from your home. This is as opposed to that same person seeing it in the street and just stealing it or checking the vin number (in the street) and working out where you live and stealing it from there. Seems like the long way round to me. Further, when bikes do get stolen there seems to be this great hope that somebody on a forum such as this will recognise it and help get it back. Well the reality is that some of those parts you buy on ebay may very well be another member's broken down bike. Easy access to vin numbers and engine numbers Etc may well interrupt that trade. I wonder if all those folks with bikes on the various registers (Vincents, Vellocettes, Enfields ...) have higher rates of theft from their homes ... Superdan do you know?

Perhaps there is a class of criminal that thinks "Umm like to steal a KTM1290R". Gets on the laptop and checks out the register. "Oh here's one, yes it's located in Brisbane, Australia". Gets the vin and works out the owner's address. "Oh good it's only 35Kms from here. Now I'll get the van (must remember to swap over to my set of stolen plates and I can't just ride it because it'll stand out like dogs balls) and drive over tomorrow when everyone will be at work". Now steal the bike and sell the parts on ebay... " "Umm on second thoughts I'll store the thing for a year or so until those parts come under less scrutiny".

Seems to me to be attributing the average scumbag with way too much cognitive ability. Rather, I'd put it to the comrades that the average sleaze merely sees a bike in the street, breaks the lock and rides off only to find that the cops start to take an interest in "Wonna them new KTM's". Runs the cops and gets away but then thinks "Umm can't take it home better torch it ..." Yes I know i've ignored the immobiliser but that would ruin a good yarn ...

Edit: Indeed I am also a great advocate of privacy however I do feel that one has to think things through and identify the real risks. It always amuses me for example, when ladies ring me and if I need to call them back seem reticent to tell me their name It is, after all, they who called me so they know exactly who I am. What are they thinking?
