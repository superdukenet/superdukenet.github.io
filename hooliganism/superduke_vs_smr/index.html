2011-02-10 09:26:00 UTC
The 990 SM & SMR are superb bikes, with great chassis and handling, and on some super-twisty roads the SMR would whip the SDs arse with it's elbows-up, front end-command that the bike offers. Power and engine are about the same although the upright riding position makes it actually feel faster, so in terms of fun - you are bang on. It's a scream, and lots of dealers and KTM mechanics will tell you it's their favourite KTM to ride.

My main criticism would be scale. It's a very tall, top-heavy bike, so that's great for chucking into turns, but you do feel it's heavier than the SD (it's probably about the same tho), and if youre under 5'9" you might find it a pain manouvring around in parking spots, etc. It's a superb bike for tall city dwellers or people who live at the top of a mountain pass. Other negative would be the tiny fuel tank. I'd be surprised if you see 60 miles to the fuel light.

They are pretty though. If you buy one, can I have your wheels?

No idea about the Hypermotard. They are quite polarising and people love them or hate them. To me they look a little girly and effete next to the SMR, and a maybe bit over designed. They are very small and light though, so if you're under 5'9" it might be a better choice for rider vs bike weight. Engine appears to be from a 1970s tractor?

I've owned a SD and SDR--and a Monster S4. Ridden a Hypermotard, Streetfighter, Speed Triple, Tuono, 750 Aprilia, Brutale (all just a little). I now have a SMR with Akro full system (-20 pounds), Motobox, custom tune (120 RWHP--and more torque than my similiarly set-up SDR until 7600 RPMs).

I knew I was on to something when I got off the Streefighter, which was my dream bike, and went: "Meh." I actually asked the Ducati rep if it had a map control and was it set soft (I rode it in an area where I really couldn't get it out of 3rd gear). It sure didn't feel like it would smoke a SMR--or SDR.

SMR is punchy but controlleable and fun (SDR was too twitchy). When I rode my SMR to the dealer to trade the SDR two weeks after I had the SMR, well--I am treading thin here, but I thought: "Good!--now back to my SMRl".

For fun, comfort, road vision (overlooked benefit), utility, punch and fun (yeah--twice), I'm good with the SMR. it just feels good to me for our roads and how I like to use a motorcycle (30 to 100 MPH, with massive braking). It's the Golden Retriever of Motorcycles.
