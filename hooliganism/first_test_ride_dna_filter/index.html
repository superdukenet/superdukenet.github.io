2011-07-16 15:52:00 UTC
I think we may be over complicating things here a little. The SD can only use so much air. This is dictated by the cylinder charging volume and the diameter of the throttle bodies, etc. As the piston travels to BDC it draws air into the cylinder. Once it has it's full charge of 500cc it can use no more. The stock airbox with it's unbelievably small 3" x 1" inlet restricts the amount of air allowed into the cylinder during this process. The MH (and DNA) box provides enough air to facilitate a small block Chevy V8.

By increasing the filter surface area (via taller filter or additional filter in the top of the box) we are not allowing more air into the engine if the engine is already allowed all the air it can possibly use limited by the factors I stated above. The only way to get more air into the motor is through some form of forced induction (ie super or turbo charging) or larger injectors, larger valves, bigger ports in the cylinder head, required cam lift and duration and an ECU tune (ignition and fuel mapping) capable of making it all work. KTM have done this already in the SDR (albeit limited by the stock airbox).

Hi Dutch,
Tuneecu has access to all the data and parameters in the ECU it is extremely powerful and at the same time extremely dangerous. Uploading and downloading all the ECU data each time a change is made is risky and takes time, not to mention that it must NOT interrupt it during the data transfer.
At the same time the Powercommander is handling only the fueling data and some parameters that can’t harm the engine. It can be adjusted real time with the engine working and the MOST IMPORTANT of all, it can be connected to the Dynojet dyno through a program that is called Tuning Link in order to automatically tune the fueling.
More specific the PC3 or PCV is connected to the Dynojet dyno via USB & Tuning Link. Tuning link is at the same time connected to the air/fuel ratio monitoring system of the dyno, checking the AFR in real time (with a Zero to 1 or 2 seconds delay) and it also takes control of the eddy current brake (load control) of the dyno. All the above work as a closed loop and the dyno operator can accurately define the AFR targets, in low, mid & hi rpm, choose the acceleration rate from low rpm to redline regardless the gear, and at the same time record AFR accurately throughout the range. All the procedure and the routines the operator has to follow are computer controlled, at the and of each run or pass, the optimum data are entered in the correct column of the map automatically!
The above is a brief presentation; the procedure is extremely effective, accurate and fast.
This is why almost everyone prefers Powercommander tuning instead of flashing the ECU.
The only drawback is that you need VERY good cooling and a VERY hard rear tyre and a GOOD tuneecu base map, don’t forget tuneecu controls the ignition timing .

Hope this answers your question.
