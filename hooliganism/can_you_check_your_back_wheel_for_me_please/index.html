My two cents;
When I purchased my bike there were two sets of weights on the rear wheel. One set was at a differant location than the other. The speculation, and let me say I believed the theory, was that KTM balances the wheel and adds weight as neccessary to balance the wheel sans tyre installation. They then mount the tyre and balance again, putting more weights on for the tyre balance. All weights were pulled by the bike shop before they mounted the new tyre as that is just part of their proccess. The new tyre is balanced and was the first and last time that I had two sets of weights on the rear wheel. It is my firm beleif that the out of true castings at the hub are in no way affecting the balance at the out side. You could have any shape between the center axis and outer rim you desired or ended up with with no desireable qualitys as long as center axis is center and outside circumferance is true. Balance is within spec at circumfrance. All other garbage between is subject to no psysical laws except that it needs to be connected between the two, center and circumferrence, with something.
If the hub looks like the home made pottery schlop my sisters would bring home from summer camp and It drives you crasy looking at a wheel Fred Flintstone may not even be proud of ,that is a horse of a differant color and I would demand a new one on looks alone. This is under astetics though not usability.
