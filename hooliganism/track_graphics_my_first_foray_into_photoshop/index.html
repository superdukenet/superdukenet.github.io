Track Graphics - My first foray into Photoshop

Twisted Jester

2010-02-11 12:57:00 UTC

I've been toying around with ideas in my head now about a scheme for my bike, especially after getting the graphics done for my sons RC8 replica balance bike and seeing the quality of those.

Then Dan posted up about his graphics and it had me digging out an old slimmed down copy of Photoshop we had lying around and having a play.

I've never used Photoshop, am generally not artistic and can't draw for shit, my handwriting is not much good either, which is probably why I'm into computers, I was able to do a pretty good line drawing copy of pictures in Turbo Pascal programming software when I was at college though.

So anyways, the first one didn't go too well, I wasn't familiar with layers and so didn't specifically making any, I soon found out that you can't undo very much on photoshop and so my efforts were shut down pretty quickly at the point of this abortion below.



So anyways, I then started using layers and cutting bits off of the black SD pics I found to get what I wanted, and eventually, after much messing with the red jester, I found out the proper way to change the colour and make it orange, after a few attempts at recolouring via paint bucket and majic wand went extremely wrong. I also had to stich together bits of the jester as there were gaps in the original image as it came from an image of a graphics kit for an aTV and in the end I've amazed myself with what I was able to acheive and am pretty happy with the results, it's not the tidiest but it's good enough for me.

So here's the finished result more or less (the swingarm graphics were on the original pic and I didn't get round to removing them)



I added the piece on the seat as I didn't like the way the orange seat intersected the lines I was trying to create using the tanks features but whether or not I go to the trouble of getting a spare seat and getting some vinyl stictched on is another matter.

I wasn't sure if the jester would be too cartoony or something but, I like it and represents me and will be truely unique (unless you own an ATV in the states and have the graphics kit), so truly unique in Orange and in the UK

I then added on the R tail as this may be a future revision and resolves the need for a piece on the seat as although the picture doesn't show a great match I assume in reality the matt black of the SDR seat support will not be far off the matt black of the Black SD tank.
