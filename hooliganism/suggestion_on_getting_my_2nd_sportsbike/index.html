I have had Monsters, Aprilia Tuonos, Superdukes, and have ridden the Speed Triple. I've also owned Supermotos, the KTM 690SMC and Husky 510 (a real SM). My SD made me forget them all.

My first ride on the SD disappointed me in 2 ways, top heavy at slow speeds and lurchy. But, a return a year later when Akra slip ons had been added showed that both problems were due to pipes (stockers weigh a ton, and it's high up, and with Akras you use the Akra map) convinced me that this is the best naked package. This is the first time in over a decade that my favorite ride will be the same for the third summer in a row. Neutrality of handling, torque, great response top to bottom, comfort, there is simply nothing else that temps me. Well, loving the SD as much as I do I have been tempted to get an RC8R, but then I realise I never wish for more power from the SD, when revved out it still puts a smile on my face, I love the ergos.

DukeofCornwall

2011-01-22 19:37:00 UTC
I've had many a bike from race reps to choppers. The SD is the best thing out there. Mine has been on multi day touring trips covering 1000's of miles, weekend backroad blasting, and a few trackdays that it effortlessly laid the competition to waste. I was even able to do the same laptimes as my 1000RR despite the 50hp disadvantage. It's got wonderful suspension out of the box, a well balanced chassis, and a smooth motor. The quirks are negligible and I have been able to fix all myself. A little more topend is about all I could ask for but that is only a matter of pipes and a Power Commander, possibly a slight gearing change too.

As stated before, Monsters are slow and underpowered. Maintenance is much more involved as is the cost of services. Suspension is nowhere near top of the line either.

I can't speak for the Street Triple but I imagine it is as close as any non KTM can get to the SD. If it were me the deciding factor would be OTD price and proximity to the respective dealership.
