bic_bicknell

2012-02-10 20:17:00 UTC
You'll wear them out of legal limits at your next track day. It's riding home that you have a problem if you're pulled up.

But most track really use the outside of the tread more so if it's a twisty track and you want to eek out a it more life then go for it. I have done when I'm broke. It seems a waste to me to throw away good outside tread when I could use it up on one last track session. I know you'll have the purists saying stuff about tyre profile and whatever but in the real world another six sessions at track speeds for free is too much to miss out on for me. It's not like you're racing is it? It's a track day. Also I've found that tracks are quite a good place to get some deals on a new set of tyres if they wear out - if there's a company there fitting tyres you can just use the old ones up and get a new set fitted there.

But the other side of the argument, if you are rich, is to pre-fit a good set of new tyres and then scrub them in during the morning sighting laps. Then have a great day and get rid of all the chicken strips, enjoy the confidence of new rubber and leave with thousands of miles left in the middle that look great and have the edges all scrubbed in. Depends how rich you are.

Either way.

I'm with Super Dan. (Edit- Not to say Bic's wrong, we were just typing at the same time. ) I like to have new / nearly new tyres on my bike when I do track days. Some of the track schools won't even let you on the track without new or new-ish (<500 easy street miles). Their stated policy was in line with what Dan said, its cheaper to put new tyres on than pay to fix a bike or, worse yet, the rider. Track day providers are more lenient, but you still may have issues if you show up with tyres nearing the wear bars. Plus, there is nothing better than getting off your bike after a session and smelling molten rubber.
