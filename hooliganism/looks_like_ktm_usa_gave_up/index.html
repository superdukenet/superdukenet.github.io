What I do find ironic is that the dealers are pushing the 690 Supermoto as the city street bike for beginners instead of the 690 Duke. Maybe the 690 Duke's styling is too aggressive for beginners, which is why the new version is more supermoto looking. I don't know retail tax laws, but my guess is that the dealer makes more money on selling bikes that were delivered new for the year, rather than bikes that have been sitting around in inventory for a couple years.

I think it's just a matter of time for the hipster biker gangs to get sick of their Thruxton Steve McQueen vintage look and upgrade to a more modern street fighter. More and more kids are learning to ride now because of gas prices. 3 friends of mine got their M license this year to save on gas. What I notice is the typical upgrade path. Hipster starts with some kind of over priced beater 1980's "custom" CB750 cafe racer or invests in a new Thruxton/Bonneville to look cool (good price on those bikes). Get's sick of it, then goes Ducati Monster/Street fighter or Speed Triple to have the big muscle bike at the cafe. Many are skipping the cafe racer beaters and going straight for the BMW F800r because they think it's the best quality and it has ABS, but the price is too high for most. None of them have any sort of motocross background or experience so they have no idea what is KTM or KTM quality. When I tell them that KTM won Dakar for the past 10 years, they are in shock because the Long Way Around uses BMW which must mean it is the best. Plus, the bright orange motif is so anti-hipster. When they look at the Super Duke, it just barks hooligan at them and they run in fear.

There is simply not enough north american demand for naked bikes, the sv650 was one lone exception, but that was mainly due to low cost and heavy aftermarket support. Being a middleweight twin, it was also a good beginner bike (the superduke is most certainly not in that category) which further helped sales. Even then, the naked sv650 was phased out.

KTM is going to stick to its base, that is, dirtbikes and adventure bikes.

Will the US see the 2013 1190 superduke? I highly doubt it. It would suffer the same fate, to much money for a naked bike that has little demand. All the kids want an R6 afterall, or a gixxer

KTM won't drop the superduke, but probably will be limited to smaller displacement naked bikes and mostly in APAC and EMEA.

Out here in NorCal, its rare to see the SMC, SMT, SMR, and Dukes. Sad really, but thats reality and KTM is doing the right thing business-wise.

In fact, even Ducati has a hard time moving the streetfighter, and that bike is the devil himself.
