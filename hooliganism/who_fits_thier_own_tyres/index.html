I use an Abba beadbreaker and balance frame. Any good franchise tyre supplies company will supply you with weights, levers, rim protectors. All you need then is a compressor and some washing up liquid and a nice pair of rubber gloves so you dont get your mitts dirty!

I spoke to a guy on the Dunlop stand in the IOM one year and he was of the opinion that most balancing machines in bike shops were well out of sync and you were far better off balancing carefully using a balance frame. I never bother balancing the rear rim. Never bothered balancing either rim on the track but was very careful to balance the front before a road race.

I have been mounting my tyres for 35 years +. The hardest part is breaking the bead. A buddy made a lever bead breaker and gave it to me for christmas that I mounted to the shop wall. It's a angle iron 2 x 2 that pivots on the wall with another 2 x 2 that is bolted and pivots. When you are using it one member is horizional bolted to the wall and the other member is vertical which has a nice oval shape of flat bar that will push the tyre off the wheel bead as you apply pressure to the horizional member. This tool does not need to be made from steel. It could be made of 2 x 4 wood. As for a balancer you can make a stand and use 4 skate board wheel bearings to saddle your axle. Another option is to buy a balance kit from Aerostich SP? The kit will fit in your tool box and has an balance shaft, bearing housing and two tapered cones to trap the wheel on the balance shaft. Tubeless tyres are a piece of cake to change compaired to a dirt bike rear wheel with a tube and rim locks. One day soon I will figure how to post pictures on this site, (along with the spell checker). As you might have figured, I know more about motorcycles than web sites.

Colonel_Klinck

2011-07-13 20:28:00 UTC
I've been doing mine for a couple of years now.

I would recommend buying one of the TYC beadbreakers that someone else posted a link for earlier because honestly thats the hardest part & it saves so much hassle, its totally worth it & it gives you a platform to work from as well. I just had to wrap a few spots with an old inner tube to stop the rim getting rubbed in a couple of places. I see so many people struggling with weird homemade beadbreakers at the track & it just seems like a headache, some of those beads can be really tight.

I also just chuck on and use 4 rim protecters because its easier than trying to slide 2 or 3 around to different places, just put all 4 on & start at 1 then by the time you get to the 4th 1 you can pull the rest off by hand. iI use 1 long tyre lever & 2 shorter spoon type ones.

Takes me about 15mins to remove & refit one once the wheels out of the bike.

On my CRF supermoto & SV650 i don't bother balancing them but i took my SD ones (faster top speed so i thought i better get them done!) to the local dealer & he balances them for free for me.
