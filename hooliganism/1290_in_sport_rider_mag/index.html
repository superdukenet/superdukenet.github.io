Crotchrockety

2014-06-05 09:09:00 UTC
I took the old 990 out last weekend, rode to the BMW dealers and then rode the S1000R.

The BM was easier to ride, more comfortable, had better brakes and suspension and at the very top end was faster but.......

The SD had more punch, sounded better and was without a doubt more fun. Yes you had to kick it without mercy, try harder and accept the head shakes and other wobbles but that all added to the pleasure.

I'm sure the 1290 is a great bike but I doubt I could get down an actual road any faster riding one than I do on my old 990, certainly there was no real difference in average speed between the BM and 990. Certainly it has all the good bits about the BM + a more amusing engine but for me its the idiosyncrasies of the old tractor that make it so engaging and amusing on the road.

Sometimes less is more. I'm looking forward to having a thrash on a 1290 when the chance comes but I bet the old Black 990 remains in my garage for a long time to come.

Of course sometimes more is also more and I do have a ZZR1400 as well, nominally for two up touring but also for occasional dips into my warp speed / max acceleration addiction that although reduced is still in there somewhere
