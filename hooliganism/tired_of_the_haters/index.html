When I first thought of this thread, I thought it may get quickly pushed down the thread list. Awesome responses, here's mine.

Although KTM thought of little quirky things (IE an oil sight tube made of brittle plastic, plastic heel guards ETC) the rest of the bike (the parts that really matter) ROCK! Stump pulling engine, top grade brakes and suspension, and a trellis frame.

oafboy182

2009-04-29 13:04:00 UTC
The bike simply looks the business. It has a pitbull's stance when at rest, and then handles like the bike's in the movie Tron. The light feel of the steering at first took some getting used to, but now it just means I can pop it down into a corner with so much more ease and confidence. Unbaffled Akra's just make it a joy to listen to without being obnoxious or annoying on extended runs. Appearance ? It looks like the devil himself carved it from a solid piece of anthracite with enough oblique angles on it to make a stealth bomber jealous. I love the v- twin's engine braking, confuses the fook out of the 4 bangers as they scramble for their front brake lever trying to keep up entering the twisties. Aaaah, the twisties. This is the SD's world. You can slap it around like a 2 dollar whore. A good day is when I get back from a ride and have to pick off the tiny balls of black rubber from the back tyre, swearing blind that I was almost horizontal on some of the corners. I really feel sorry for these cruiser type riders in their shorts and t-shirts plodding around on their iron horses thinking that they're actually motorcycling, but this is not a "hate" thread so I'll leave it at that



...... I personally have had no issues with the bike whatsoever....touch wood, so no blemishes on the KTM brand at all for me...........

LeDuke

2009-04-29 15:13:00 UTC
I had a Suzuki....I went to a motorcycle show a few years ago and saw the first North American Superduke. I went home, immediately phoned a KTM dealer and ordered a bike. Then I gave the Suzuki to my son. I've been riding legally on the street since 1964 and have tried a few different bikes (everything from Honda to Norton) I like most bikes. They have all got at least one good feature. But I love the Superduke...a bike that really handles with the low end torque of a V-twin that can also rev almost up to 10,000 rpm...whats not to like?

I am a sculptor and make my living teaching the history of 20th c. sculpture, architecture and industrial 3-D design at the university level..and I think the Superduke looks fantastic...

The only think I don't really like about the Superduke is its name...Superduke?..sounds kind of dumb to me... but who cares? Its better than calling it the Uberherzog (German: "Superduke") I guess. Maybe it could be shortened..maybe "Uberhog" is a good name.

speaking of hogs...

I was at a Harley-Buell shop the other day..looking over their new XR1200 "sporter". I'd like one for a second (3rd) "slow bike", but they are priced too high for now. (the nearest KTM dealer is also a Harley dealer, but thats a different shop)...The Harley guys really seem to like the SD. They go out to look at it. The head guy told me that he would definitely take the SD in trade on a Harley (Don't worry, I never suggested it and its not happening). I'm not sure why the Harley types like the Superduke though I suspect that its because of two simple things:

1. Its not Japanese.
2. Its a V-twin
