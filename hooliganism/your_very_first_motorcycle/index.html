Agent Orange

2008-11-25 22:48:00 UTC
I turned 17 in 1983. Most of my mates were a year or two older. They all had RD250's or Superdreams. We werent that fussy then and both bikes were equally cool. Every evening we would hang about outside the KFC and get into fights with the other bikers that hung out at Riverview Park.

I had spent the summer of 1982 digging ditches for a construction company and bailing hay for a local farmer (both cash in hand). After getting pissed every night (one pint of bitter ) I had 400 quid left for my dream machine.

Then some tw*t changed the law. I was restricted to a 125cc.

In 1983 there were no cool 125's. Even if there had been my mates would still have taken the p*ss. Moreover, there was a young lady in my sixth form I hoped to impress and a 125 just wasnt going to do it. Her name was Jane Hobbs and she had massive tits. Do you remember before boob jobs you almost never saw a girl with massive tits who didnt also have a massive arse to go with it? Well Jane Hobbs in 1983 was like one of those Greek girls before they inflate. I dont like to think what she looks like now, probably 30 stone. But in 1983 I was in lurve....

Of course, I had never spoken to her. Couldn't. If she ever spoke to me I couldn't even answer, just stammered and blushed and dribbled I used to talk to her ugly mate when she wasnt around. In the hope that ugly would put a word in for me. Never happened.

But I was convinced, 100% convinced, that if I turned up on a proper bike her knickers would just fall down.

Dont blame me for this, blame my parents, I had been at a single sex school (boys, Toby ) from the age of 11. I knew about Wanking and chinese burns and fighting but I knew nothing about girls.

Now my uncle had a 1977 Honda 400/4 in blue. Which to me, looked like a proper bike. My uncle knew I didnt have a licence so he wouldnt sell to me. Luckily I had a mate with a licence who was willing to pretend it was for him and hand over my 250 quid for it.

My mate, Mark, was even desperate enough to agree that I could ride it and pretend to be him if I got stopped as long as he could use it also.

It got me nowhere with Jane Hobbs. It did get me suspended for riding an unlicenced, untaxed motorcycle across the playground. And after the second time my mate Mark had to produce his documents and get a b*ll*cking for my traffic offences he decided enough was enough. He gave me 250 quid and kept the bike.

I thought a car would enhance my sex appeal and bought a 1976 Vauhall Viva. Which, I also drove around without a licence. Also without much sex.

That would have been the end of me and bikes except for the film Top Gun. A mate turned up at my house on his new, financed, GPZ900 and I was smitten and terrified in equal measure. Those too young to remember the impact the GPZ900 made when it was launched think of the Fireblade launch in 1992 and double it.

This time I would do it right. Hired a CG125 from a training company in Lydd did the part 1 test riding around cones on a playground. Then off to Crawley, In January, for the road bit. At that time the M20 ended at Ashford and the rest was A roads. Once I got there I couldnt use motorways on a learner bike and had to go around to coast road then up the A23. The day started with heavy rain which turned to sleet then snow.

When I finally got to the test centre the examiner asked me to start the bike and the kick start broke off. Those Brazilian made CG125's had crap castings. Test cancelled. Back through all kinds of weather in my borrowed wax cotton Belstaffs. Next test was February in Folkestone. A nicer day so I turned up in borrowed full leathers. I looked a right plumb but was too young to know.

Anyway I passed. So same day I went to EC Bates and signed on the line 2500 quid for a used GPZ900A1 in red and charcoal grey. So at home I start phoning around for insurance.......

Them: What bike?
Me:........... GPZ900
Them:How old?
Me:........... Eighteen
Them: How long have you had a licence?
Me: Er............. About 4 hours.
Them: BRRRRRRRR
Me:............Hello? Hello?, The f*cker has hung up!

Finally I found someone silly enough to insure me and I was away. Gravesend's answer to Tom Cruise in a Randy Mamola Arai Giga. Had bikes on and off ever since and still dont get any sex
