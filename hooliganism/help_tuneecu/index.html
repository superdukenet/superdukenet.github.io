First of all tuneecu does not switch off warning lights!!!
What it does is switching off the functioning of lets say the seconday butterflies if you untick the box. Same for O2 sensors and the SAS, the sensors or a replacing resistor still needs to be in place to avoid the fault light comming on.

In your case of the secondary flies you need to leave the little motor and sensor connected, just remove the shaft.

For all the other issues, you obviously disconnected other stuff that is either not plugged back in or wrongly plugged in. Check your wiring dude! This might be why Tuneecu can't connect neither.

I am also under the impression you use tuneecu wrongly. To make changes like rev limiter or turning things on and off you need to change that in your map in tuneecu and then upload the map back in to the ecu to make the changes there. You don't need to have the bike running for this so disconnection issues shouldn't be an issue.

It pays to read up before using this stuff as it can potentially make things go wrong big time.
PS: No need to go to a PCIII you only add more things that can go wrong.
