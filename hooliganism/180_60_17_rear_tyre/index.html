cant see a problem? just make sure you have clearance between the tyre and fender, also it will affect the geometry of the bike, you are effectively raising the ride height
at the back which will quicken the steering, granted only by 2.5mm.

It depends on the tyre, but as Stratkat said, if nothing else it'll affect the geometry of the bike as well as the "gearing".

Personally, I wouldn't go for a 60, but if you get a good deal, by all means try it out. I mean, either you'll like it or you won't. The bike isn't going to explode just from changing tyre profiles.

Bit of an update, i was able to leave the 180/60 on there but i did put the std 38t sprocket back on and that gave me plenty of clearance once the wheel was moved back to take up the chain slack.

It could maybe have worked with the 40t on there but it was a bit closer than i was comfortable with!

Anyway i've done about 250km on the Supercorsa's & they're just so much better than the Dunlops that were on there, even though its real cold here (4.5 degrees on my last ride around the lake!) the back wheel hasn't lit up once on me yet & i'm being way more agressive with the throttle, the dunlops would light up 2 or 3 times a ride hot or cold- scary!
