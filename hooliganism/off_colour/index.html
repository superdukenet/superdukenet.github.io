That WR looks like it's had a hard life.

They are great trail bikes, If you get a good one you should be pretty happy with it. As with all 4T trail bikes, check it's been serviced regularly and that the valves are good.

How long was it run as a Motard? Supermoto can be hard on gearboxes without a cush drive rear wheel.

The WR450 (and the latter updated models) has constantly been amongst the biggest selling bikes in Australia since it was released.

$2000 for an 06 WR450 is a real good price, even if it has seen a lot of time (so long as its been well maintained). Hopefully he can give you some trustworth maintenance history on it. I think with the WR's if you keep up with the maintenance they are bullet proof. 1000's of miles and hundreds of hours (300 to 500 longer?) without needing a top end rebuild or even needing valves adjusted. But, that depends on how hard it was ridden. Seems owners recommend oil changes around 300 miles and check the valves every 3000 miles even though many don't need adjusting at that time. If it isn't burning oil and starts on the first or second kick power is strong has smooth torque then the engine is probably okay. If he can't say when or hasn't checked the valves in awhile you might want to do that just to be safe.

Joe, is this for trail riding or MX? If it's trails and the bike checks out fine, alls good. MX, look for something else. Dirt riding is fun as fook. Hope it works out.
