thats is a difficult question, normally if you go for such a system you also perform other modifications on the engine.
Usually the KTM's are never lean at higher RPM's, in conclusion you do not gain much top end power if you
run at Lambda 13. You gain 2 - 5 PS across the board and torque figures smooth out very nice.

I have modded the airbox, removed secondaries, bored the butterflies from 48 to 50mm using full Akra with baffels in and
reach about 130PS and 100NM torque with an 06 Engine. The 07 ones are usually a little bit weaker. The nice thing is that the engine is totally smoth you can ride it from 2500 until 9800.

I would assume you can reach that goal also with a Powercommander or Bazzaz unit, but due to time and money not all modifactions come always together, so once you have your mapping with the Motty you can test a modifaction and
check if the Motty adds more fuel, if it does you have a power gain. Air + Fuel = Power.

if somebody wants to buy the Motty i can provide a wiring diagram to help installing it.

here is how to wire the Motty. It is not a plug and play story. You need to know electronic basics and understand the wiring. You will need to translate from German to English, let me know if you have dificulties.

Some important things to do upfront :

1. Sync your throttle bodies !!!
2. Disable with TuneEcu the Lambda probs
3. Remove and disable the complete SLS system, otherwise your Lambda readings are not correct
4. Disable gear restriction and remove secondary butterflies, not mandatory

Check your exhaust system for any leaks ! This will also mess up the Lambda readings if there are any.

Assembly steps :

1. Cut wiring harness into correct lenght, the Motty fits perfectly in the back of the bike
2. Most signals will come from the big grey throttle body connector at the right side of the bike, you need to cut the two injection wires and
tap the signal of the TPS sensor, you can you Posi Locks for testing
3. Tap Temp Signal from the sensor at the left side of the bike between the cylinders
4. Tap RPM and Speed signal behind the Mask
5. Mount the LC1 unit to grap the signal from the second Lambda probe and mount it where the snorkel airintake was sitting there is enough room
6. Calibrate lambda probs at free air and use the original position to fit them back in the bike
7. Mount all cable nicely
8. Fire it up
