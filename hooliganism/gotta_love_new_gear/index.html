Gotta love new gear!

VegasSD

2008-09-20 22:04:00 UTC
Today our local Cycle Gear had a 20% sale on all apparel. So I took advantage of the opportunity to pick myself up a new Dainese "Laguna Seca Pelle" jacket and G2 back protector




I also picked up a pair of Frank Thomas "Rage" gloves.



I had initially tried on the $799 Dainese Delmar but couldnt justify the extra $400 over the Laguna jacket. I'm a first time buyer of Dainese or Frank Thomas products and I have to say that both companies live up to their reputations for quality. I tried on several jackets from other companies but none of them had that perfect fit like Dainese. I also tried on several more expensive gloves but none of them provided the feel and quality of the Kangaroo leather in the Frank Thomas gloves. Now, I know white isnt everyone's first choice for gear but when you have to ride in constant 90-100 degree temps, black doesnt cut it.

I'm going on a nice long ride up through Mt. Charleston tomorrow and will provide some feedback on the new gear. At this point though, I'm very happy with both purchases.

2008-11-02 03:57:00 UTC
Well I have some updates on the new gear.

The white laguna jacket is great. My only complaint thus far is the tailoring on the wrists is a bit different from left to right. I can slide my hand out of the right sleeve without unzipping the cuff. Cant do this on the left side. I thought that was kinda strange but nothing to complain about. Overall, I love the jacket. For those of you who said it was gonna be tough to keep clean, you were absolutely right. I would still buy white in Vegas though.

For those of you who suggested that Frank Thomas gear was sub-standard, you were right as well. I went through two pair of Frank Thomas gloves. Both having different fitment issues.

I'm still waiting on the Cortech Adrenaline gloves. The white/black combo is on back order from the manufacturer. I have to say though that Cortech is a pleasure to do business with. Earlier this year I purchased a set of their Accelerator 2 gloves from Parts 411. They ran me about $45.



These were nice gloves overall but I had problems with the the leather on the palm bunching up and causing blisters. I took a chance and emailed Cortech about the issue. Since then they have taken back the Accelerator gloves and agreed to replace them with the Adrenalines, free of charge. I thought that was pretty cool. They agreed to send me the first pair of white/black that comes in.

I had already spent $100 with Cycle Gear on the Frank Thomas gloves and needed something to last me until the Cortechs arrive. I couldnt continue to live with the Frank Thomas gloves because the armor kept digging into my knuckles. When I brought the gloves into the store today, they returned them without question. They had a pair of Held Ninja gloves left over from a previous shipment. This was one of three pairs left because Cycle Gear doesnt normally carry Held products. Since I had heard good things about Held and didnt care for anything else they had I decided to give the Ninjas a try.


Despite their lack of Kangeroo leather in the palm, the Ninjas have a very good feel at the controls. I've only ridden about 2 hours in them so far but I might end up hanging onto these gloves. It depends on how the Cortechs compare when they arrive.
