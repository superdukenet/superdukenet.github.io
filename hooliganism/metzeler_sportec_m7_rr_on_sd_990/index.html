That's what I've been running on my bike for the last year.

I usually have Racetecs in the Summer for track days and sometimes tried to run them through winter but in the cold and wet they are truly shit and put the fear of God up you on roundabouts etc. After a while you loose all your confidence riding in the rain and I eventually swap over to something else (In the past M5s) and then swap back to Racetecs when it gets warmer.

But I have had these M7s on and they are really confidence inspiring in the wet and just feel really good. In the summer when the roads were dry I never found them less than great and I more or less decided to keep on using these all the time because they wear much better than the Racetecs. I've done about 2000 miles this year on them so far and can't see much signs of wear, at least not the usual flattening off in the centre after a few hundred miles on the motorway. I never got a chance to try them on the track though but as Spring comes I will have no problem going and using them for a trackday. I would expect that for my ability they would be fine.

I paid £124 for the rear and £94 for the front but that included the fitting and balancing too.
