Brains vs actual Performance

Dutch

2011-05-23 10:07:00 UTC
So....

I've been hooning around on my little new Ducati Sports Classic, which is basically a Multistrada/Monster/Hypermotard disguised as a 1970s Cafe Racer with fairly decent suspenders and clip-ons.

Having convinced myself over the last few months that Superdukes are not "snatchy" with harsh fuelling, and that other bikers who struggle with the superior KTM are clearly ham-fisted gaylords, I jump off the 1000cc Desmo and back onto my black'n'orange pride and joy...

...and kangaroo off into the distance like some kinda learner!

After just five days on a Duc my return to the Duke has shown me just how harsh our SDRs are - (and mine is MUCH better than stock). No wonder we get so much grief from journos and potential KTM converts. It's not a bike that makes you look good in the first five minutes of ownership.

A few mins later and I've re-adjusted my senses, and the KTM is now suddenly "smooth" again, but it shows how much handling, performance, and the way a bike feels is goverened more by our brain's ability to adjust, than by the facts.

I also "learned" how to ride a bike with clip-ons this week. Having always wondered how the fook anyone with clip-ons can ride as well round corners as someone with handlebars, I'm now slightly converted to leaning over the front wheel and countersteering from the shoulders. It's different, but it really works.

S'funny how two bikes that seem to sit so well side by side in attitude can be chalk and cheese to ride, and amazing how quickly the brain adjusts.
