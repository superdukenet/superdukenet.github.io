The running guidelines in the manual are on the conservative side IMHO Hours of riding bouncing off the rev limiter will have a long term effect on the life of the engine. Probably not in the life time of your ownership but it will have an impact. So no running at a trackday (a couple of weeks ago a guy ran his new ZX10 in at the track and its not his race bike!). Avoid long runs at a constant speed and rpm. I do not exactly ignore the rev limit mentioned in the manual but under smooth accelaration I often will use 80-90% of the rev range.

My old SP1 engine felt very tight from new and therefore I broke it in quite carefully but the GSXR 750 felt fine and I was more free with the revs. For both bikes I changed the oil and filter after 50kms. With modern engines and oil it is not so important but since it does not cost a great deal (in time or money) why would you not do it.

An ex-mechanical engineer who now spends his day taking care of design software for mechanical design.
