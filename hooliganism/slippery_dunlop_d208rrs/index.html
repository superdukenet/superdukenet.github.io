These tyre debates on forums are relatively pointless.

Unless you are comparable riders then any input on tyres is irrelative.

Say paul has knee down and a track addict while peter just fangs about on weekends and really goes slower than mum in her taxi yet he thinks he is pushing the tyre to the limit? Paul tells peter 'you need 209gpr's as they are the best'. So peter races out and buys a set. He will never get the value out of such a tyre as it is outside of his riding capabilities.

Powers, 015, 016, 209, m3, conti .. all are great tyres, whether our 'brain' likes them is another story, but put a magazine test rider on any of them on a current streetbike and he will be hard to find a 'real' fault with any of them.

To be honest, if you have any chicken strips or can't scrape your pegs then ANY TYRE WILL DO. You may think you are having issues with the tyre, but the issue is almost certaintly not with the tyre, the issue will be the rider.

There is no point in me saying I get xxxx klms out of a set because that means nothing if you can't ride exactly the same pace as I can. Even in saying that if I spin the rear up more on exits and my mate does not then he is going to get better tyre wear on the rear than me and thats at exactly the same lap times. So comparing who gets the lowest milage from tyres is a pointless exersize other than showing whos got the biggest penis to other bikers.

Just buy any set and ride it. It's a Superduke..it will ride around anything we can throw against it

Mr KTM's got a point. However, I'd like to point out that it's not tyre-life, tyre-pattern, tyre-colour (thanks for using my pic, btw ), or brand. It's the feeling that's important. If the tyre inspires confidence in the rider, then that's a good tyre for that rider.

On the aforementioned, my chicadee hated her BT-14s on the GSX-R600. She found the tyres just fine. I found them (felt hard and very uninspiring). We both loved the , both on the GSX-R600 (6,000 miles and counting!) and the K1200R. However, I found them starting to slide after 3 back-to-back laps on the 'Ring. Overheating, a bit, methinks. I used the Pirelli Corsa III on the K1200R last, and I found them pretty much the same as the Sportec M3 (they are sister tyres after all). However, the SDR came with Corsa IIIs and I find them hard as a rock (but I haven't double checked the tyre pressures).
Heard a lot of good about the BT-16s, but not tried them myself.

Back to Mr KTM's comments, he's right about the chicken strips etc, but add to it that a stickier tyre will only make you a few seconds faster on a short-circuit lap, but will probably halve the tyre life. IMO, not worh it. There's also the heat cycles, if you're into that sorta stuff. Sticky road tyres, IMO, are good enough for most, even in the wet.
