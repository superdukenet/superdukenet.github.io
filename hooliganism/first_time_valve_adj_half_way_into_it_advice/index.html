It depends if your using KTM shims or HD shims ?

KTM shims go to half sizes - which will get you into spec but HD shims go to quarter sizes - which gives more scope.

Intakes are 0.10 - 0.15mm and exhaust are 0.25 - 0.30mm - so you just need to measure the shim you have and go up or down from that to get the correct clearance. Normally you can move some of the shims onto different valves but sometimes its easier to just buy the same size shim - as you may need to remove the cams again.

If this is your first valve shimming its worth noting down exactly what shims you put back in - as it makes the next valve shimming session alot easier.

I've found the opposite to Klinck that the valve clearance gets bigger over time and not smaller - having said that it hasn't moved much from the 10k to the 20k valve check.

Usually valve clearance tightens over time but not always the case.

Tighter spec clearances theoretically offer slightly better performance (could be 1 or 2 BHP) but usually run out of the right range faster.
Just put them in the middle of the range if possible, next time you probably need to check them only without shimming.
