I have a Christmas gift from maybe 5 years back that is my winter glove. A bud gave them out to his buds. Intended for the lumber yard. I believe they are Elk skin which is tougher than Deer skin. They have long gauntlets but no wrist strap, wish it did. We all seen "On Any Sunday" where the Datona rider gets off his ride and the second he puts his hand down to the tarmack, off flies his glove. Looks like I have plenty of years left on this pair. If I were to buy today I would pick up a pair of AEROstich Elkskin Ropers. They are made in Washintion, next state up from me in Oregon. Built for Cowboy and ag work. If you believe ad's, it says all Califorina Highway Patrol Officers wear Elkskin gloves 8-10 hours a day. I am going to stay clear from thick winter gloves. Lost my 1100 gsxr with thick winter gloves when I could not get the brake lever pulled in farther because of all the fluff. Aerostich is a great source for bike gear. Geared towards the mature rider

SD Nerd,

You have to move north about 150 miles. The last few nights, I've had to sleep with the windows open in the house because its too darn hot to sleep. Its been in the 80s during the day inland, but the coast is still in the 50s-60s (F of course). It was 74 at 11:00 PM last night. I rode Hwy 150 to Ojai on Sunday AM, and there was one patch about 2-3 miles long that was in the 30s or 40s, but the rest of the ride was in the 70s - low 80s (except along the AFOREMENTIONED coast.

Yes, we do get some weird thermals here. There is a spot on Malibu Canyon Road, in Agoura, that can cause the black ice sensor on may car to activate in the middle of summer. Its weird to see the outside temp gauge drop from the 70's to the 30's for no apparent reason.

Good luck on your quest though. The KTM heated grips are a pretty good solution. They work well until the temp dips into the 40's. Its not much fun riding when it gets that cold anyway.
