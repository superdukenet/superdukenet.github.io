Model differences and buying a used SD what to know.

Superdan

2012-02-14 11:31:00 UTC
Right people keep posting up this question so an official thread to be added to by owners with their thoughts and anything I have missed.

So your looking at buying a new or used superduke and want to be cool, lets have a look at the model history and what might be best for you, as well as what else to look out for.

First Superdukes came out in late 2005 the model remained unchanged until 2007 (when the US guys first got a glimpse so no pre 07 there). They had smaller tanks and do about 90 miles, they also have softer springs front and rear, so if your over 13 stone, springs might be a little soft, also if your into track days many 05-06 owners scrape the engine cases. The 05-06 bikes also had a sharper steering head angle making it a bit more flighty and fun (arguably) bottom line 05-06 SD'd were brutal and fun, reserved for true connaseur

Something you might want to look out for as milage creeps is wear to the wiring loom,might sound odd but I sold my SD with 28k miles on it, just prior to selling I had had a couple of problems,the first was where the three wires come out of the reg/rec and bend over the edge of the battery box the wires had worn through on two of them and was half way through the third,the second was where the wiring joins and runs up from the battery box area towards the ecu the loom sits against the frame just below the tank,it had worn through the outer heat shrink and broke the wire to my rear lambda sensor.The second one was a bitch to find.
I repaired them both with solder and heat shrink and also I cut back the edge of the battery box a little in an effort to stop it rubbing on the wires and I also fitted extra,thicker heat shrink to the wiring where it ran up against the frame,gotta love those V twin vibes
