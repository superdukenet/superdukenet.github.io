First, I have to agree with all the guys who said to ride it as you feel comfortable.

I rode mine in every configuration imaginable: bolt upright, full cheek off (never got a knee down, my legs are too short and stubby), upper body hanging off, pivoting around the tank and about a 1/2 cheek off. I found I was fastest on the track with just the 1/2 cheek off. On the street (and during the first few track sessions), I didn't trust the front and so I hung off more and that slowed me down some.

I've recently tested a GSXR 1000 and it was amazing how different it was to ride. It didn't like to be ridden bolt upright at all. It fell in corners, and I found myself hanging off as a natural result within a few miles.

Prior to that, I rode an '12 BMW S1000RR. It was the opposite of the GSXR. I never hung off it at all. It barely leaned in corners, but that wasn't a bad thing. It didn't run wide or do anything untoward. It went exactly where you wanted it to go, but required very little lean angle to accomplish this. I think you'd have to be going Mach Schnell before you'd have to lean it very far and Uber Schnell before you had to hang off. (As I recall, the first gen S1000RR was more like the GSXR).

So, the bottom line is ride it as you want. The SD allows many riding styles. Find one that's comfortable for you and do it.
