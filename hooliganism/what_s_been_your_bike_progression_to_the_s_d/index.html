weeksy

SO, where did you start ? What have you had ? and most importantly WHY did you go for a Superduke ?

Me, i passed my test in 2000 having already bought a ZXR400, and had that for only 6 months before moving to a Red/white ThunderCat, which i wrote off the week of my bike test LOL.

Then to a GSXR-600 which was stolen when away being painted..

THen moved onto the big boys... a 1998 Fireblade. I used this on track a few times and then decided to go club racing, which i promptly then destroyed and shattered more than 20 different bits of bone on my first lap of first race. I now have more metalwork than Steve Austin.

After recovery i went for a 916, which ended up being just a race bike... i still broke bones but actually finished plenty of meetings

That was sold to buy a 750K1 as the series i was running in was old WSB rules so the K1 was perfect. I then realised i was shit, the Ducati was OK LOL. So retirement came and i chose once again a sports bike in a ZX6R which i had for 2 months. That was replaced by another 916 and then a 2001 R1 and finally a 2005R1.

Finally moving onto the S-D.

Why then ? Well for me bikes were all about being pristine. All my bikes were truely immaculate. They were washed and polished after EVERY rideout. I ended up being 'scared' on the R1 of everything. Dirt, Water, white lines, cross hatchings etc etc. I was nervous on every corner, every ride...

So after a discussion last week with the lads, they said "superduke would be perfect" 20 mins later i'd been on Autotrader and had made an offer on a Superduke and was heading to collect.

I know after just a few short ride, i should have done this 2 years ago. It's transformed my riding, my confidence and my whole attitude to bikes. It's now a toy to be thrown about and abused in some ways, but i can't resist cleaning and polishing after every ride ::)

I've turned into a hooligan, trying wheelies every time i leave a standstill and ploughing through traffice like a kid.

Happy days.[/b]

SO for my story:

1. Started riding when I was 7 in 1986, with the help of my dad attached a motor to a bicycle - was fun and a starting point to getting to know the technical details how the bike works

2. Got many Soviet time mopeds

As with all Soviet technical equipment you have to reasemble it fully when you buy it new to make it run and I had to take out the engine at least once a week (I am very thankful for the poor quality of Soviet machinery 'couse I got to learn the technical things and could do a lot of stuff on my own) That was the first moment when I learned how to do wheelies by placing a big rear sprocket

3. Had many Soviet bikes - all 2stroke (Jawa; IZ; Minsk; Voshod etc)

4. My first foreign bike was a Honda 285cc 4-stroke trial bike


5. I also got an oldtimer bike at that time a 1956 EMW 350 single 4stroke (BMW built in the East Germany after the war, that is why it is an EMW - Eisenahe Motorishe Werk, Eisenahe for the region name, like Bavarishe for B). I still have the bike fully original


6. Then after some time I got my 1998 Honda CB1000 - first proper big bike. It was a real heavy machinery - 285kg, but was a reliable good cruiser, was not easy to do wheelies on it, but after this bike wheelies are peace of cake on any other


7. I am realy into the naked bikes - do not like the plastic selfdestruction rockets. Fell in love with the looks of 2004 Kawasaki Z1000 - a streetfighter look from the factory. Did a lot of stunt practicing on it. A very nice bike, super sound, super looks, reliable, but very bad suspension and brakes (understood that only when I got SD)


8. Saw it on the street on a vocation and understood that is definately my next bike - went on ebay and got it from Italy 2006 KTM SuperDuke. I love it a lot - this is the bike and the brand you bond together - 100% is my stile, hooligan driving, bike just loves to be thrashed around. I alsways liked the SD-R - now I am turning the bike into an R over the winter




Traveling to Kaliningrad - a photo by Lenin monument


9. I also have a motorcycle season prolonger - a Suzuki LTR450 QuadBike




Whats next........... definately KTM - I love the looks and concept of RC8 - that is my next bike. I am not into the superbike style bikes, but this one is a gem
