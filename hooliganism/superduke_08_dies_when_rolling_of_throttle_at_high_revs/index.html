To follow on a stream of consciousness; engine requires max fuel flow under WOT; then goes dead lean (20:1 or higher) on closed throttle. Plugs still firing on every cycle. Open the throttle back up, ECU kicks pump back in, takes a few moments for injectors to repressurize and send fuel to intake stream, intake to hit chamber, combustion to occur (snatch). now transitioning to stream of consciousness
Seems to have something to do with hard acceleration. What happens at WOT? Fuel pump demand spins up, plugs fire more frequently, engine rpm climbs, oil pressure rises (hopefully), electronics heat up from resistance/heat possibly leading to temporarily open condition, fuel tank temporarily pulls vacuum if demand for fuel volume exceeds vent capacity; let off throttle, bike goes into lean mode for pollution control, ECU cuts fuel to the injectors, bike is hot, tight parts sieze momentarily.

OK, question: you're on the throttle hard on a hot bike. You get off the throttle and the bike stalls. What effect does opening the throttle have? How do you have to restart the bike? Does it ever restart as in a bump start after the rear tyre locks up, or do you have to either by necessity or desire have to come to a stop and hit the starter button? Do you have to cycle the ignition?
