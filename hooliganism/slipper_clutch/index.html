Slipper clutchs will not effect accelaration, thats not what there designed for. A slipper clutch is designed to stop the rear wheel locking up under hard downshifts and alleviates the need to match engine revs with a lower gear.

There are 2 ways to find if you have a slipper, 1. stick bike on sidestand and pull the clutch cover off and take a look (no oil will drop out so dont worry),or 2. ride along at 60mph in 4th gear then smack the gearbox down into 2nd gear and dump the clutch, if the rear tyre locks up and the bike dumps you on your ass then you dont have a slipper

As for the tyre pressures, im assuming you set them at 30/30 when cold?!?!
