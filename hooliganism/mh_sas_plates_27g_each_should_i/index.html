MH SAS plates 27g each. Should I...?

TheJoker

2010-04-07 14:46:00 UTC
Just got my Motohooligan SAS plates today.

To my (extremely anal) surprise they're quite heavy. I stuck them on the scales and they're 27g each, or 54g together.



They're absolutely gorgeous though. Nicely machined with chamfers at every conceivable spot. *droooool*

However, I can't get over the weight and thickness. As I've got a milling machine, I've been intending to machine my own plates, but I took the lazy way out and bought these.

So, should I:

1) Stop being anal and not worry about a total of 54g... Just ride the thing. Put them on and be happy, forget about it.

2) Stick them in the milling machine and start shaving off weight. I'll lose all the beauty wherever my sh***y mill will touch the plates. Naturally the anodising will also be lost.

3) Revert to the original plan and fire up the milling machine and make my own, leaving the gorgeous Motohooligan plates for someone who'll appreciate their beauty. Targeting < 10g each...?!


What's the esteemed panel's opinion? Let the banter begin!

It's the small things, Joe. The weight is where you don't expect it to be. Like the side stand, who would have thought that?!
Bolts, clips, holders, stuff like that. Chain sprockets.

I've heard of race teams that take all clips/holders/etc off bikes and replace them with zip-ties. At the end of it you have a bucket of junk weighing 2-3kg.

I've got two subframes in my shed (not superduke), and I was thinking of saving weight by removing the pillion-carrying-capacity from them, welding new ones, etc. But you remove all that and there's not much weight there at all. They're engineered well... and then some muppet puts a 0.75kg side stand on the bike.
