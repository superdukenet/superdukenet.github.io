Any dyno guy worth his salt will spend at least 2 hours setting up a bike, making lots of runs at several different RPMs and throttle openings, as Shade stated, his fantastic work does not sound any better than any other dyno tuner!

IMHO what you should look for in a dyno guy is the following -

A recomendation from somebody you trust who has actually used them and has had good service/after service from them!

Speak to them on the phone/in person/email and make sure you happy with thier attitude and thier knowledge in engine/tuning/business, (i say business because some people will be amazing tuners but uselss at business, they dont understand the importance of after service etc). Also, if you feel like they are rushing you or cant be bothered to answer your questions then steer clear

Finally thier workshop, a clean, well equipped workshop speaks volumes, are you really gonna let a guy near your bike when he cant even tidy the shit off his shop floor?

If you are happy with the guy and happy with his pricing then go for it
