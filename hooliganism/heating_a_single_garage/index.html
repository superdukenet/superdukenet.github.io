Ive a concrete panel garage.
I lagged this everywhere with the fibre glass type insulation then cladded it all with half inch ply sheets.I did the same with the ceiling and covered the back of the garage door with 2 inch kingspan.I made sure i blocked all gaps up to prevent draughts.
I ran a 3 inch drainpipe underground across my garden from house and filled with insulation and my central heating pipes and Sky tv cable.
I now have a nice warm radiator on a TRV and TV to watch all the re run of this seasons races whilst working on my bikes in winter.
Snug as a bug in a rug.

The bestest ideas come in the form of insulation. Non-insulated structures will throw that heat and cost right through the roof. Litterally through the roof. Eighty percent of heat loss in a structure that is tight, is through the roof. Heat rises. This may sound corny but you could find an old wood burner and the radiant heat would work great. If mid PA is any thing like I suspect, you should have no problem finding wood at cheap costs. I have seen new chineese knock off pot bellies with catalytic converters that pump out some serious heat. I like the wood cause the charm of hanging in the garage is doin stuff and that would be a plus to have earned heat.
I think that electrical heating will cost a bundle. You could find a coke or coal from a remodel removal and get real dirty while heating your stead. Mostest important is insulation.
