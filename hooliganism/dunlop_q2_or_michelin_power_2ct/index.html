2011-03-04 21:35:00 UTC
I would never, ever run 2CTs. I am, admittedly, perhaps a bit more track focused than your average street rider, but if track riding is on your agenda, stay clear of 2CTs. They will, if you have a bit of speed, overheat and let go without warning. I've got numerous friends who have crashed on track with them on, and no newbies by any measure. The story is ALWAYS the same, the traction just disappeared. Looking at the rubber, they've overheated. I'm sure there are people with different experiences, but I wouldn't use them even if I got paid.

If you do, say, 70% street and 30% track, I'd go for the Dunlop Roadsmarts. If it's the other way around, i.e. 30% street, 70% track, go for the Dunlop Sportsmarts. The latter are the best tyres I've ever used alongside the Dunlop GP Racers.

The SDR has Q2's on it now. I did Monticello last fall on them. No problem. I ran the same track in the fall of 09 with Pilot Powers. No problems.
Did a spring track day last year on the 2CT's and toasted the rear and put significant ware on the front in 6 sessions.
I like the front end feel and feedback of the Michelin's better.
The Q2's rear has a stiffer carcass and works a bit better.
If you are having ware issues with the Q2's; The 2CT's will too.
You could try running the 2CT front and one compound Pilot power rear.
.....or adjust the suspension
.... or adjust tyre pressures.
just don't expect to get touring rubber mileage out of sport tyres.
...and the faster you go the quicker the tyres ware.

First off, thanks for all the feedback. As some of bought up other tyres, here are my additional thoughts on other tyres mentioned. Roadsmarts and Pilot Road both scare me. I don't care for the feel of either tyre when mounted on previous rides so I won't be trying those. The feel of the Q2s on my SD was fine and as intuitive as the Pilot Power's have been on previous rides, the wear a bit strange though, front end cupping primarily, perhaps because of suspension set up?

I have two days of the Keith Code school scheduled for the beginning of May. I am closer to the fast beginner end of the spectrum of track day riders than the anything else. I probably could do with proper setting up, sag, etc of the suspension and do intend to do so prior to the school. Per Trackday Junkies recommendation I've installed the Nichols upper triple clamp, but unfortunately the suspension guy I intended to use for a proper setup this spring is now closed (GMD Computrak in Connecticut). No other track days are planned, yet. As for pricing I could get both within about $30 per set of one another.

At this point I am leaning towards the Dunlops for the school and when those wear out I might give the Michelin's a whirl, as these seemed to have better wear, long term on the road where I ride primarily, over the Dunlops.
