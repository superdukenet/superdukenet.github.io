I have an 07 SDR too. Basically what you have bought is a standard SD with a nice orange frame, a couple of nice extras and a free sore arse thrown in.

I would be suprised if yours doesnt already have cans. So next to get is a Motobox and Tune ECU. I am waiting for Ash's bike to blow up, if it doesnt then raising the rev limit is worth doing.

Then, you have about 115bhp with no dips in the power curve. Depending on who you ask the 08 SDR's have about 10 - 15bhp more. All up the top end and I have never really seen one that looked quicker than a well sorted 05/06 SD. The 06 battle bikes were putting out 130bhp as I recall.

My plan next winter is to get my bike into Bob Farnham for blueprinting and a bit of top end work. That should give a usable 130bhp for less than the cost to change to a later SDR.

I am going to Bob Farnham's again next week so I will try to get a run down of what he was doing to the battle bikes.

I would suspect bueprinting helps. Get two big SD pistons and if they dont weigh the same the engine is fighting itself from the outset.

Raise compression a little, maybe a thinner headgasket?

Advance the ignition, I think Klincky is tinkering with this on Tune Ecu as we speak.

Schanuzer was saying the 08 SDR's were rumoured to have lighter valve springs (titanium?) It is unlikely they do but it certainly wouldnt hurt for an aftermarket tuner to use them on an 07 SDR.

Raising the rev limiter should give a bit more as every Dyno operator has told me the SD rev limiter cuts in too early.

Finally, keeping the bike upright is something we should all be working on. It is hard to evaluate performance gains on track when none of us can do a whole session without crashing.

I love the Motobox, so don't get me wrong.....
If you are going to ride hard on twisties or the track, you must get the Nichols triple and shock clevis.

My order of mods would be:
1. Exhaust-you need to shed the 20 lbs or so that monstrosity holds underneath the seat.
2. A competent dyno/map/TuneECU tuner because your bike WILL be running lean. Don't underestimate the effect of this!
3. 520 chain conversion using stock gearing. I lost almost 4 lbs doing this. You can feel it!
4. Motobox- Just because it sound KILLER! Also makes the bike breath better. Down side will be the need of a new map so maybe this should be #2?
5. Hi Tech battery like the Shorai or equivalent. This will shed over 3 kilos!

Now you can move on to comfort and bling.
