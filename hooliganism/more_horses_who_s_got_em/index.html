There are some horses to be found in the ignition curve. That is basically where James and I started out. If it is for racing I'm confident I can get a few extra horses out the SD.
Compression needs up.
Gasflow ports, get valve seats set on almost knife edge.
keep the cam profile and machine the lobe for a bit more lift.
Set cam timing
Superfinish the engine for quicker response.
Dyno with Tuneecu to get most out of ignition advance and fuel.

If money is no issue convert to dual lenght velocity stacks and injectors over stacks. The second butterfly motor can be used to lift the velocity stack up and down following RPM.

The RC8R is there at TriValleyMoto for the taking. I'm just not taking it. I've been around long enough to know what it would take to run that bike properly. The means are simply not there at TriValley, nor are they there in my bank account. Running that RC8R at the last round was eye opening. I got passed SOONER down the front straight than we got passed on the SDR. After two seasons running the SDR, we got along pretty good. My initial drive came far sooner than on the RC8R. The RC8R was foreign to me. And while we did lap faster on it, it just wasn't as fulfilling. The bike felt vague. For sure it's because we were not even close on set-up. I know that. Plus it had stock exhaust, and stock footpegs, and stock bodywork in places - and then there was our crew leaving the triple clamps loose for the race... The whole thing was a huge compromise, put together in a last minute dash to save the season after our SDR got pummeled trying not to kill the fallen rider from the previous round. Running the RC8R like that really painted a clear picture of just what it would take to get it to the same place we had already gotten on our SDR. It's a lot of time and it's a lot of money. Plus we wouldn't just run one class. It fits Open Twins how it comes, but on the RC8R our second class would be Formula Pacific. And Formula Pacific in the AFM is to club racing what MotoGP is to AMA Superbike. It's pretty fantastic, competitive as hell, and it's NOT a place for stock bikes - which is basically what our RC8R would be with no budget. Top runners in Formula Pacific have 200+ hp. A stock RC8R would be out of the top five consistently. What's the point in that..? If we run the SDR again, we are underdogs in Open Twins (which I kind of prefer) but we are competitive, and we are also actually competitive in Formula 1, and 750 Superbike (if we find another 20hp). Maybe I'm wrong but it seems like 20hp will be easier to find on an SDR that's already built, than 40hp will on an RC8R that still needs to be built.

It's all a matter of budget. Hell right now we don't even have the budget to run the SDR, never mind the RC8R. But I'm working on stuff in the background.. Fingers crossed something will click
