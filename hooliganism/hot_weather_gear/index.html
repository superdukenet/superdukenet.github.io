thewall67

2008-06-20 05:14:00 UTC
I have Vanson's Ventiitlator jacket and pants. 1000 denier Cordura with no polyurethane coating. From what I've read, it's the polyurethane coating used as a sealant in most nylon fabrics that melts, not the nylon (in this case Cordura) itself. It also makes the Cordura feel a little softer against the skin. Cordura itself won't melt at the temperatures caused by sliding down asphalt depending on your speed and how long your slide on the asphalt. I would not use textile gear of any kind at the track. But for the street I make the compromise with confidence (or else I wouldn't wear it).

A lot of mesh gears "mesh" part of the material will not be the same denier or even Cordura as the woven part. Vanson uses 1000 denier all around and it has leather (underneath the Cordura) in the impact zones. The outer arms and full back are not mesh but solid woven Cordura. Pants are the same deal with mesh only on the inner thigh area up to the package and behind the knees. I've used it in 95+ temps and it works well. It is the most comfortable gear I've ever put on. Armor is soft type and CE approved. This is some of my favorite gear but I have never crashed in it to truly test it. I trust the Vanson name though and I read a lot about textile gear and materials before making a decision.
