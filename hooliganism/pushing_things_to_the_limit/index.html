bic_bicknell

2011-01-17 06:56:00 UTC

So, I've been on crutches since November after I had my left foot screwed together for hopefully the last time. Been a real pain in the ass, no weight bearing for a month and then only partial. It's the inconvenience really, and not being able to do anything properly, and of course getting about. So back in December I gave up with cabs and tubes and experimented with riding my Yamaha 125 scooter thing which proved not too bad except it coincided with the worst snow and ice and weather for years. That was an interesting experience I can tell you all - but I rode in to work every day, through it all and I can report that in all this time I did not have to put my left foot down once (I should enter trials riding competitions now

bic_bicknell

2011-01-17 17:31:00 UTC

I have an appointment with my consultant at the end of this month and hopefully I will be able to trash the cast. I have a new type plastic one which you can remove so I have already been taking it off at home to practice walking a bit and getting some movement into the foot. I cheat all the time now with the crutches, well I should say "without" the crutches, because they totally prevent you doing anything.

It's been very bad timing - Amy is now 27 weeks pregnant with the twins so she is getting big and needs all the help she can get as she was in hospital last week, (we had a bit of a scare), so I need to help more and let her rest. It's hard with a 16 month old running about pulling everything of every cupboard and shelf from dawn till dusk.

Anyway, the foot is healed up where they put the screws in and the swelling has gone. So has all the blue bruising and the black toes! It feels weird when I put weight on it but I'm hoping that they will say that I can immediately start to walk on it and ride my push bike as I need to get fit again and build up my calf muscle which has wasted away a lot over seven weeks. I will not bother asking them if I can ride the SD as they will almost certainly say that it's not a good idea, (they always say this), but if they let me walk on it then I will be riding that same afternoon! My own personal concern is that because the SD is a tall bike I can only get my toes on the ground and this is a lot of weight to potentially place on a weak foot. We'll see, I don't want to end up falling off sideways at a traffic light because the foot gives out at a crucial moment. Gear changes I have no problem with I don't envisage, specially with the quick-shifter fitted.

So roll on the good weather in February. This is the longest period of time I have not been on a bike for 31 years.
