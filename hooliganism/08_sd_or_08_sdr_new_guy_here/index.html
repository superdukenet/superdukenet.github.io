08 SD or 08 SDR? - (New guy here)

weeksy

2008-11-03 04:41:00 UTC

I am new to this forum. I just started reading it the other day. Great info and it has made me more enthusiastic about getting a SupeDuke!
I currently have 3 bikes: 08 KTM EXCR, 08 Harley Nightrod Special, 1994 Ducati 888 LTD. (I used to have a Ducati Monster 900).
I am trying to decide about getting a SD or SDR. I read some other posts here but it still has me pretty confused.
I plan on doing mainly Sunday morning rides on twisty mountain roads and a few track days a year. (I race a Porsche 997 Cup car with the Porsche Club so my track day time is already busy). I am short 5'7" (at best) so the seat height is somewhat of a concern. Comfort is also somewhat of a concern because a typical Sunday morning ride is usually 1.5 to 2 hours before stopping for breakfast or a quick break and then 1.5 to 2 hours back home.
I currently ride the Ducati 888 for the Sunday morning rides. I am not a great rider but I am not horrible. I hope doing some track days/school will improve my ability. (On the mountain roads I ride on I am very close to dragging my knee even at relatively low speeds because of the turns being pretty tight radius - not quite there yet. But I also have 2 kids at home which I think about every Sunday morning ride - slows me down a bit).
I LOVE the look of the SDR! The standard SD is pretty cool looking but it doesn't get me as excited as the SDR. I have NOT riden either bike. I am not sure what to do. Please help me out before I miss out on some great deals on these bikes. (I can get a brand new 08 SD in either black or orange for $10,500 from a dealer 2 hours away from me or I can get a brand new SDR for $13,500 plus $400 shipping from a dealer 1200 miles away from me).

Hmmmmm tricky one really.

the SDR DOES in theory handle better... and has more power... However that power delivery is often open to debate.

Seat height won't be a problem on either at your height.

Comfort.... some don't mind the SDR, some hate it.... although i can't think of anyone who says "wow that's comfy"


Read your post... you want the SDR... whether ornot it's more suited doesn't matter... it's the bike you want.
