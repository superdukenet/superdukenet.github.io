yes, i got higher torque on my standard SD too, but you cannot compare Finland/Canada/Australia... fuel is very different, not to mention different dynos read very differently. all that matters is you got good A/F and bikes feels smooth and powerful. look at dyno as a tunning tool only, it allows the tunner to set the A/F for best performance. the numbers are almost arbitrary, all they tell you is the result of the mods you made (provided you have a before and after reading, and bike was properly mapped in both runs...haha)

if you're happy with the way the bike runs, don't change anything, just enjoy it, go ride the b1tch!

drswade

2011-04-27 19:18:00 UTC

I would never complain about those numbers if you are just using the old PC3 route.
Decent HP and damn near 100 pounds of torque!
I think I have posted here that my stock SD made 106 and 67 bone stock during it's dyno break in. I put on the MH air box and Leos plus PC3 and only saw less than 1hp and slight torque increase. With both race gas and pump fuel.
Huge difference in the feel of the bike, plus the killer MH box noise!!!!
At the beginning of the year I reinstalled the stock air box with BMC filter and had my maps rewritten. 107hp and 69 lbs of torque.
I have seen these hp numbers all over the place here and wonder what makes them so drastically different?
Do other bikes have such power discrepancies?

TuneECU is no doubt the ticket.
If I buy my totaled bike from the insurance company, I will be dicking around with the motor to see what improvements can be made. I don't remember seeing anyone here that has stripped down an LC8 from a SD and really worked the numbers for internal, top and bottom end changes.

The ability to change the ignition is huge!
A mechanic who works for my tuner got just over 30 hp from his stock Ninja 250 by tweaking the ignition and checking it with dyno runs.
(Why? There is a huge contingent of Ninja 250 racers in our club).
