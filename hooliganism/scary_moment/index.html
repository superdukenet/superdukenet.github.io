Your brake didn't stay on. Your mistakes were downshifting while turning, and using the rear brake while turning when not comfortable with the technique.

Do all your braking and shifting prior to entering the turn. This way you won't have anything else to think about other than your line, and you won't be upsetting the balance of the motorcycle while it is leaned over.

For what its worth, you can use the rear brake in a turn to tighten up your line... slowing the rear wheel down brings your front in if you're running wide. So there's definitely a place and a purpose for that brake, even while turning. But if you're not well seasoned, you need to practice this carefully and very gradually otherwise you have yourself a date with a ditch!

Like lots of people are saying here. Your experience sounds like a combination of rear braking and down changing into corners when things are not matched up properly.

This is all part of the joy and experience of riding big V twins

It's also worth remembering that all this rear wheel fun is also rear wheel engine braking and you have a LOT of this at you disposal also. It's a great advantage on the road and you can learn to ride with this advantage over bikes with engine configurations that don't give them this. Shitty road surfaces, gravel, potholes, wet patches, diesel spills, etc. etc. everytime you see this and need to brake you can opt to not use your front brake and simply whack it down a gear and use that reverse torque and slow without the risk of a front tyre moment.

And last but not least. It's good to get a feel for the back hopping and jumping and sliding and skipping and doing all sorts of strange stuff. The more the merrier. Off road guys have this all the time, it's second nature to them. Keeping it loose and letting the back end have a bit of freedom is maybe OK, you just have to start off by not being scared by it. I try and not be scared of it when it happens rear wheel stuff is usually progressive and you get a bit of warning. And isn't it better to be practiced and familiar with it when it happens?

Front end slides and shit is something else. I don't want to be familiar with them.
