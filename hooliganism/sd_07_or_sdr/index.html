These are the differences KTM claim! Klinck's SD may embarrass a few SDRs though.

SDR differences

The Engine
The Super Duke R's V2 sets the benchmark for free-revving twins. A lighter-weight crank drive than the Super Duke, lighter pistons, larger diameter titanium intake valves and larger throttle valves push the power up to 97 kW (130 hp). The engine impresses with explosiveness and spontaneous throttle response.

Cylinder Head
Aside from optimum combustion chamber design, the major factors behind the outstanding performance of the 990 Super Duke R's V-twin with 75° cylinder angle are the state-of-the-art four-valve cylinder heads, each with flow-optimised ducts and two overhead camshafts.

Crank Drive / Pistons
Particularly lightweight pistons and connecting rods bestow the 990 Super Duke's V2 engine with reduced reciprocating weight and hence a decidedly spontaneous responsiveness, underlining its extremely sporty engine characteristics.

Engine Management
The electronic Keihin engine management system regulates the modern fuel injection system on the 990 Super Duke R, focussing on maximum power and spontaneous responsiveness. The twin throttle valves have been enlarged by 2 mm (0.08 in) compared with the Super Duke, in order to increase performance.

Frame & Singing Arm
The 990 Super Duke R's orange, powder-coated tubular space frame made from chrome-molybdenum steel with a bolted-on, light alloy subframe provides a steeper steering head angle in the "R" setup than the Super Duke and less trail for even greater manoeuvrability. The 575 mm (22.64 in) swingarm ensures plenty of mechanical grip.

Suspension Elements
Thanks to a great variety of setting options, the upside-down telescopic fork with TiAIN-coated sliding surfaces and the directly connected shock absorber from WP Suspension with a TiAIN-coated piston rod allow optimum individual adjustment of the chassis to suit load, riding style and course.

Steering Damper
The Super Duke R sets itself apart from its Super Duke sister model not only with 9 kW (12 hp) more power, but also with so many prestigious components, such as the Akrapovic titanium manifold system, the WP steering damper and the double-butted, perfectly angled aluminium handlebars.

Worth remembering that the corporate speak mumbo jumbo spec that Schnauzer has posted is for the 08 and newer SDR.

The 07 SDR is, as stated earlier, exactly the same engine as the standard 07 SD. This is not automatically a bad thing though. When I bought my SDR I wanted the orange frame and single seat and liked the look of the SDR. I dont carry a pillion or commute, tour or pootle about so didnt need SD comfort. I liked the idea of a larger fuel tank and radial brakes although the brakes were fine on my 05 SD and I was used to the poor fuel range.

I bought my SDR in 2008, So would an 08 SDR have been better still than my 07 SDR? Not sure of that, firstly I couldnt get one. There were very few about and some shit dealers were actually taking deposits for bikes they had no hope of getting. Lagunas, THE WORST DEALER IN THE WESTERN WORLD, took deposits off Weeksey and me and then had to give them back when they couldnt supply. Also, some of the regular posters on here at the time that did get 08 SDR's experienced build quality issues with them. Couch Commando in particular was very vocal on here stating that he wished he had stuck with his 06 SD. Particularly when it dumped all its coolant on the rear tyre, which Schnauzer's 08 just did as well. My 07 SDR hasnt and neither has Sabre's as far as I know.

Maybe the extra power is worth a few build quality issues? I am not sure. All you are talking is about 10 bhp at the top end. Having ridden an 08 SDR It seemed a bit more powerful than my 07 SDR but I didnt feel it was any stronger than my thrashed 05 SD. Klincky's bike left my 07 SDR for dead along the straight at Cadwell and that was before he had all that TuneECU trickery. The 06 battle bikes, standard SD's were putting out about 130bhp at the top end so tuning a 5 grand 07 SDR may be cheaper than paying 7 grand plus for an 08/09 SDR.

For what it's worth, this is my experience/opinion.

I have an '07 SD. Over time I've done the usual mods - Full Akra system, Motobox etc. and I've been out riding with Klinks, (legendary '06 bike with very high milage and high dyno figures) and Shade, (SDR with way more power, (in his own mind and enthusiastic reprts ) than his previous SD. But everything all comes down to how fast you are willing to open the throttle. They ride fast. All the time. Me....I'm older and a bit lazier. I think that I worry about the consequences of crashing more. (I've had a few bad ones over thirty years, ha ha). I know that at times they leave me and I have to catch up. But that's not down to engine performance. It's me. Sometimes I have been able to catch them up on straights - just through hunching down more and nailing the throttle. Is that because I have a faster engine? Or just because I happened to nail it for 300 metres longer when they backed off a bit because of a rabbit running out of the hedge? I don't know. And don't think it matters that much. In the real world we are all limited by other factors rather than BHP and torque charts. Really.

And a few years ago my SD in standard suspension set up professionally with Racetec K1 tyres was brought back to the Bracken workshop by an enthusiastic Paul, (yes, the cynical, miserable Paul who is hard to get a good word out of), and I was told that I had the best handling, best set up SD he'd ever ridden. He was really impressed and told me to never change it. Must say, that period was when I was doing my first serious track days and I was really getting into it. Confidence very high.... and I never worried about set up and handling, just a few extra clicks of preload and rebound for the track. Then I thought I'd get more serious and get an SDR shock and start "improving" things by messing with the forks in the clamps and stuff. But ever since I'm not sure if things are better really. Still set up well by the guys at HMRacing for me but on track I have never felt the bike was a neutral and, well just effortless, to ride. Like all you lot...we want to believe that the mods we make always make a better bike. But maybe it's not so. Just because it's an 'R for racing" shock doesn't necessarily mean that you will like it better.

Anyway, rambling over.
