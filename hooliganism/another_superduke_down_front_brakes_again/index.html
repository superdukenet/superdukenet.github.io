I used to work on a fleet of Police Driving School bikes [Hondas] which were cleaned and stored over winter as the school did not run in the bad weather.
When we serviced the bikes after winter we found several brake linings that had become detatched from the back plates. Water had got in from power washing and then frozen over winter forcing the lining apart.
So power washing bikes was stopped and pads were changed for each new season.
I have also seen brake pad linings become detatched on several cars when i was a mechanic.
I am not saying there is not a fault with the Brembo's but damage to brakes from salt ,ice does happen on other vehicles.

Just like to add i have not come across one that has locked the brakes on.

I've just spent the best part of Sunday stripping the calipers, cleaning thoroughly and really carefully reassembling with new pads.

I had some corrosion and the pads were a 'sticky' fit in the caliper - but eventually came out OK.

Couple of the pads had a fair bit of 'cratering' on the surface - which I didn't like the look of.

What I did notice was that on one of the calipers only the diagonally opposed pistons were moving when operating the lever.

I eventually managed to 'massage' the pistons in and out for a bit and then was able to bleed some air out.

Can't believe how much air I've bleed from these brakes, as I've already vacuum bleed them a few months ago.

Can only think the air is trapped behind the pistons (when assembled) and its not until you fully retract the pistons a few times that it gets full dislodged into main brake hoses.

So - hopefully that will be the end of my rubbing brake problem - seems to be OK so far.

Not sure if it was the pads or air in the system - but it really isn't worth taking any chances with a bike that can build and loose speed as quick as the SD.
