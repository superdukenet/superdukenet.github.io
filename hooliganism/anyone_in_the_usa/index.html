2009-12-17 23:26:00 UTC
The TRC-D and Undertail TRC were new additions to our line over the last two years, with the TRC-D made for limited Japanese sport bikes, and the Undertail specifically setup for the R1 and the Ducati 848/1098/1198. Though the TRC-D is a popular item on numerous models including the 2009-2010 Yamaha YZF-R1, it did not fit the SD well and neither mufflers offered sufficient sound suppression and durability for the KTM SuperDuke's large twin. The large oval shape of the RS3-C was the best option with the short muffler length which still maintained the sound levels of the bike within our testing requirements without going to escalated costs.

I will vouch and put my name on the line to state for a fact that we do not purchase components or parts from other manufacturers. All of our components, other than carbon fiber components, are made in house and are 100% assembled by hand as well, all here in our Chino, California location. Even with that, we make all the tooling and molds for the carbon fiber parts in house in order to maintain QA.

Thanks again for the feedback and hope this helps to answer some questions!

cbecker33: The y-pipe will be available through our retail direct only for replacement of damaged or defective items. We will not be offering the y-pipe as an individual product at this time, sorry!

thewall67: Thanks for the comments on the pipe. Our first priority in developing a pipe is in making a good power band (note, not just peak power), followed by a weight reduction and then by aesthetics and sound. Though we've had exhaust systems which have generated minimal power gains on a stock fuel map, we strive to tailor our exhausts to perform well with modifications including fuel injection adjustment as well as other internal engine modifications. One thing to try to keep in mind is that even though many systems run well without fuel injection adjustments, tuning the fuel map is the ONLY way to ensure proper fuel injection after modifications are made.
