nampus

2012-02-26 18:06:00 UTC
Took the bike for a blast today up and down my local testtrack loop for the 1st time since the crash to try it all out.
Totally over the moon. The thing is mega.


Brakes are brilliant. Hardly any lever pressure needed and it bites hard without grabbing.

Suspention is firm but compliant. Feels nice..........really nice.

Geo surprised me the most. Still turns fast, but you can feel it's lost that twitchyness. Once it's leaned over its mega stable. Just sits there and holds a line but at the same time you can play with lean in the corner. Before it felt like it would tip in and want to keep on going over, but at the same time felt pretty vague. Feels real confidence inspiring now. Dont know if it's all the weight it's lost (and me!) but it feels light and flickable but at the same time has that stable planted feel at lean. Interacts have alot of feeling, dunno if they have a softer carcass or that but i like 'em. Looking forward to getting warmers on them and getting them working on track. Rearset plates help loads, feel alot more in control of it with the pegs jacked up.

Power is definitely up. I'm going to book the dyno tomorrow for next monday. Again i think the weight drop has helped, along with the lightweight battery at the front, but i've actively got to lean forward to try and hold the front end down. Lifts easily off the throttle at the top end of 3rd with a tiny tug on the bars. Honestly think it would clutch up at over 100 in 4th if you tried it.
Rottweiler airbox sounds the nuts. Nice deep throaty roar from it on wide open throttle. The box combined with the RC8 stacks have made a big difference. Motor feels loose. Power builds and just keep on getting better, almost like an I4 but with a good bottom end. There's definite more top end rush.

Slipper works great. I set it at 1.2mm. Still got all its engine braking but it just wont lock the rear on downshifts. Nice and subtle. It's weird, kinda like you cant feel it working unless you listen for it. I've never ridden a bike with a slipper before.............i'm impressed! Read you can go down the box with no clutch if your going in hard enough to get it working. Did and emergency stop from 130 right down the box to 1st with no clutch.....perfect....stayed in line, snicked into gear no probs, didnt lock or judder.

Only negaitve i found it it doesnt like being clutched up now. I'm a clutch up style wheelie guy...........when i try it in 2nd it lifts then kinda jolts. Best way to describe it is it feels like the chain was loose and it was jumping a tooth. I can feel the clutch lever clicking a bit sometimes..........i see this is a feature of a slipper......not sure why though.


It's on me now. There's no reason this bike cant run at the top 1/3 of the fast group. Not saying i'm the man to put it there, just gotta get out, ride it and build up slowly. Bike feels spot on though, great place to start the 2012 track season.
