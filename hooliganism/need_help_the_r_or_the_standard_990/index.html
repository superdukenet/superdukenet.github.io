Duke of Wrath,

One thing to remember that I didn't see you mention is that the R has a 12 mo/12000 mi warranty, while the standard is 24/24.

Getting what you paid for the std will be very unlikely, and with the extra money you'd put into the R combined with the loss you will take on the standard, you could probably get exhaust, motobox and at least an akra map if not a custom map. I don't know if the performance difference justifies the extra work necessary to get the R since you already have the standard.

But, the engine differences the R has (12.2 comp ratio vice 11.5, 51mm intake valves vice 48mm, titanium valves) would be expensive to do yourself unless you locate a wrecked R donor bike or get an R warranty replacement engine as someone else here has done. I think they are both great bikes, and I was also torn on which to get. The deciding factor for me was the fact that the R was only $1k more than the standard, and I loved the idea of the engine differences more than the actual performance difference. I also love how the R tail looks. And while both are uncommon, I would be less likely to see someone with the same bike if I had the R.

However, the increased comfort, better warranty, lower price, very small difference in performance and the fact you already have the standard would motivate me to keep it. You can adjust the suspension and add a steering damper if you want it to handle like the R, and you can still haul a passenger.
