RPM's are irrelevant, its the ability to flow sufficient air at those RPM's that really give you power. My RX8's engine can rev to 14K RPM's with no internal damage, it still begins to lose power after about 9K RPM. I would keep the red line at 10K or 9.8K personally. That's still pretty high for a big twin. Any more and I'd be concerned with putting too much pressure on the internals.

I clean my chain without removing the chain from the bike. I use a rear wheel stand. I still have all my fingers too. I do ride on some gravel roads and when I clean my chain I wear gloves and use a stiff brush to get all the dirt off I can. WD-40 will loosen some of the gunk. I lube the chain with motor oil from the inside and let the oil fling the remaining dirt out. This is not for everyone, but I do the flinging with the motor running as it is on the rear wheel stand and make sure the oil is not on the tyre before I ride. When I don't get in the dirt I use Tri-Flow Industrial Lubricant with Teflon. Make sure to shake the Teflon to mix the mix before lubing. I get 20K-30K from my street bikes chains. The key is to keep the chain from rusting. The rust will tear the O or X rings and will let the factory lube out of the chain.
