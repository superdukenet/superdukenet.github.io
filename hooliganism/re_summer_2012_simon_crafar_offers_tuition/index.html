Chatting with a number of people at the Crashfest there was considerable interest in doing a trip abroad with a track day(s) thrown in and because I organise a trip each year for a group of mates i was asked to start a thread to gauge interest.

What we might do is something similar to a trip a did a few years ago on my road SD:
Ferry to Caen.
Ride through central France, down through the Massif Central (mountains) and on to SE France and do a day at, say Ledenon.
(Ledenon has to be the best SD circuit in Europe)
Track time would be inexpensive as we could hire the whole place ourselves.
Spend a couple of days in the Pyrenees and then ride back.
Costs would be kept low by staying in inexpensive but interesting hotels etc , keeping off the tourist routes as much as possible and eating local food.
Routes would be SD friendly as much as possible - i.e. twisty and sub 110mph.
Target would be to cover 300 - 400 miles each day.
Normally these trips are for a long week (Friday to Sunday) but can be adjusted for the group.

Alternatively we can meet at Dover, cross the channel and get the car train down to SE France overnight and maximise our time in the mountains and on track.

Let me know if there is any interest.

OK.
I've done some preliminary work on this.
The autorail has been discontinued by SNCF because they are a bunch of assholes.
Brittany Ferries on the other hand are offering a much better service than they used to and much quicker sailings.
For example we could catch the ferry from Portsmouth to Bilbao at 16:30 on Friday 20th July which would get us in at 17:30 on Sunday 21st
We could then catch the 20:30 Ferry from Bilbao on Saturday 28th which would get us in at 19:30 on Sunday 29th
The cost for 4 people sharing, 4 bikes and an en-suite cabin would be a not unreasonable £182.75 each.

There are a number of circuits we could use but Ledenon is still looking like a great bet for, possibly, the Wednesday.
They are working on a quote for us right now.
If we ran 3 groups : 2 KTM groups assuming we open this trip to other KTM owners and 1 group for foreigners I think we might be able to break even on the track day (i.e. it would be free to Superduke.net)

This would give us 3 days of some of the most amazing roads to get there and 3 days to get back for the ferry.

I have been playing with routes already it might work something like this:
Day 1 Picos de Europa and Pyrenees
We would overnight outside Bilbao and make our way into the mountains traveling Eastwards 'against the grain' of the great valleys that run into the Pyrenees enjoying some fantastic ascents and descents as well as some superb runs up and down some of the valleys including one run in a tight limestone gorge with the Sds exhaust notes bouncing off the walls and caverns.
Overnight in a 9thCentrury farm house.

Day 2 Through the Pyrenees into France
Taking in one of the most amazing roads in Northern Spain: 50 miles of the best road in Northern Spain (for SDs anyway) where the bike doesn't get out of 3rd gear and is on its side almost all the time. Cross into France over one of the high summer passes and then a spectacular run down into France using some of the routes taken by the Tour de France. Overnight in Limoux - some would say spritual home of sparkling French wine staying in a C15th merchants palace.

etc.

For budgetary purposes I would suggest we allow £100 per person per day including fuel, food and accommodation plus a set of tyres and the ferry.
There would be maps, route cards and contact details provided.
I would assume that everyone going would be capable of following a route plan / navigating themselves if the group gets split up and every one would have insurance including medical and have break down cover to include repatriation of their motorcycle should the worst happen.

How does this sound?

I think we should be clear about the 'rules' up front: this is simply a trip organised by some people from the Superduke forum.
Can we agree that anyone is welcome provided that they are coming to have a good time?
You don't have to be fast and you don't have to ride a Superduke or, for that matter, a KTM.


I will say this, however, we are all grown ups so although maps, routes and locations will be provided, a support truck will accompany us, hotels and ferries will be pre booked and organised we, the organisers are not responsible for the members of the group. Whilst I'm sure we will all do our best to help our com-padres at the end of the day each individual is responsible for themselves - navigation, break down and recovery insurance, etc. It is not reasonable for someone to ride 200miles in the wrong direction and then expect someone to ride out in the dark, find them and guide them back to the hotel.
