Reasons not to buy an I4 bike:

Very little feedback from the engine. They're just too smooth and have no character, just efficiency.
Who doesn't have one? SD's are very rare where I live and it gets stares every day.
They're not really that fun unless insane high speed is your thing.
They're not comfy for most people.
Unless it's a track bike I've never known anyone to keep their rocket for several years. They're race replicas designed for the track. My good friend just bought an R6 about 5 months ago and he's already looking for his next bike.

I've always felt there was a connection between the small percentage of riders who do track days and the large number of supersports for sale (R6, GSX-R, etc). Those bikes aren't designed for public roads like street fighters are.
