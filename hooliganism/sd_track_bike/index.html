Hi,

My thoughts about SD as a track bike.

- It can do track quite fast, with couple mods
- There are somethings you cant change in the bike, what makes it not that good track bike (ground clearance)
- It's a great bike to learn how to RIDE, because it doesnt have any aids and its quite unstable compared to sportbikes
- It always crashes nicely
- It's not a smart move if you think it money vise
[youtube][/youtube]
I have been riding 2008 SD at track for 3 years now (first year it was 2-3 track days, second year 5-6 track days, this year 12-16 track days).. Now riding in a fast group and still learning quite fast I think.

Mods that I have done:

- R&G sliders everywhere
- Akra slipons
- MH airbox + SAS removed + the other basic stuff
- Custom tuned
- Changed brake rotor to powerparts rotor and installed EBC Extreme Pro pads
- Suspension totally rebuild and re-valved two times now
- Stiffer front and rear springs
- Puig windscreen
- LSL streering damper, dragbar, levers
- Stomp grip side panels
- PP-tuning rear sets
- HM quickshifter
- STM slipper clutch
- Dunlop D211 GP Soft in front and 190/55 medium rear
- 16/41 sprockets
- Sag with rider + riding gear on front 35mm rear 30 mm

And probably something else also.. Now the bike feels perfect.. except one f*ck*ng thing that annoys me alot.. ground clearance.. what have people done to that? Nichols TC seems to only mess the geometry up in standard SD bikes. I liked how the 190/55 rear tyre changed the behavior of the bike.. the only thing im missing is the ground clearance because engine is scraping on the ground

I will post a video from last weekend ride when its uploaded.

Looks something like that..

DGShannon

2012-08-10 11:39:00 UTC
Viking knows what he's talking about, especially the fact suspension is very personal and needs to suit your riding style.

For now I'm done setting up my 07 with front sags 27 and 39mm, rear sags 6 and 30mm and forks 1mm through the yoke.
I like a bike a bit tilted forward and I try to get a lot of drive out of corners so I had to use relatively much rebound to keep it from sliding front and rear.
It tends to steer a bit sharp and sometimes a little hard and unstable with normal riding but everything falls in place when riding balls-out. The ride gets smoother and grip seems to increase the more speed it carries.
I move my body around a lot to set the bike upright quickly and I'm sure someone with a completely different style wouldn't like my setup that much so it's a process you should find out for yourself.
