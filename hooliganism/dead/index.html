Th reason I asked about TuneECU cable is that it shows the error codes - a lot easier than trying to count
the flashing lights whilst you have a fist full of throttle. You don't have to change anything - you can use
it to just look. It may well point you at TPS - if so check other things first. Other members have had
similar problems cause by moisture in the Big Grey Plug (BGP).

With TuneECU you can see and set your rev limit. I assume the previous owner wouldn't have lowered it slightly.

15 minute reset won't do any harm but I'm dubious that it will help with your problem. Nobody really knows
how the 15 minute rest works - it's just recommended by KTM. Given that it doesn't change any map settings
or anything else that can be seen it's assumed that it must change some other values stored in the ECU. Those
values must be used by the ECU for fine tuning or something like that. I can't recall anyone ever reporting
radical changes with it but sure give it a go you never know your luck in the big city ...

i posted the question of if it's possible to disable the FI light after removing this Roll over sensor to an Electrical Engineer from another forum , here's his response ...


John,

I can't be sure without a schematic for the bike, and maybe even then I wouldn't
be positive. There are a number of possibilities. From easiest to hardest, here
they are.

1. The ECU lights the light once it detects a "rollover," which could either be
a real rollover or a bad sensor. After you removed the sensor, it no longer
detects the rollover, but it doesn't turn the light off until it's reset. You
can reset the alarm light if you have an OBD-II reader tool. If you don't, they
have them at AutoZone, and they'll reset your light for free if you bring the
bike in and show them where the diagnostic connector is (usually somewhere under
the seat). This is too easy.

2. The ECU is detecting an open circuit on the sensor wire, which means the
sensor is disconnected, which lights the light. You have to figure out what
voltage a good sensor sends to the ECU when the bike isn't tipped over, then
apply that voltage to the sensor wire. Then you might have to reset the light as
in #1. This is most likely the situation on your bike.

3. The sensor sends a digital message to the ECU over the CAN-BUS, say every
minute or so. The message is something like "Hi, this is the rollover switch,
and I'm OK." If the ECU doesn't get the message, it lights the light. It's
impossible to fake this system out without a lot of talent and effort. It's
unlikely your bike uses something like this, I think only BMW has engineering
OCD this bad.
