SuperDuke-DK

I've had my 08 SD for a few months now and love it. As said before the 1st gear is quite high and combined with the V twin torque shudder at low revs can feel a little aggresive on take off however give it more revs and a smooth lever action and it is not a problem at all. The SD is not like a jap bike that you can ride along sedately and expect a quiet smooth ride, it will punish you, but actually RIDE the bike with enthusiasm and it will reward you .. you wont get the grin off your face!.
I have the KTM touring screen and dont mind the look of it at all, it takes some of the wind off the chest area and still pretty buffet free over the helmet if you sit fairly upright ( I'm 5'9), if i lean foward to balance the wind draft at over 120 kmh or about 70 mph i think, then there is a little helmet buffeting, but they are pretty cheap and you can swap them in five miutes so give it a try.

Good luck

It sounds like someone has burned the clutch on that SD and now it only functions when it is completly released. I would try another SD. There is a tall 1st gear so you really do need to be able to slip the clutch when you ride in the city or traffic. I can tell you that I do not have that problem you are describing. I can engage my clutch slowly just like it needs to.
On the matter of windblast. You will get used to it. It takes a couple of rides and few more hairs on your chest but eventually you get used to it. I suggest you get the "touring" screen. It keeps the gust of winds from hitting your chest and pushing you back. The rest of the wind will relieve the pressure on your hands when you lean forward. Yeah how long could you ride your SV650? Try it on the SD and see how when you get off you will think... "hm.. that was nothing"
