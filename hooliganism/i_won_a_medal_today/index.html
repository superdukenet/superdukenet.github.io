I won a medal today!

Ducati Pete

2011-02-27 22:10:00 UTC
As some of you may know, back in the day I did a bit of Judo. Pretty much stopped when I left the Army though.
In the second week of Jan I decided to start training again.

After the first 2 or 3 sessions my body hated me, I've not ached like that since basic training or the all arms course. I also discovered that for me, Judo is not like riding a bike - you do forget.

So today after 6 or 7 training sessions I entered my first competition of the new era.
I've had a cold this weekend and that added to the fact that I was fighting guys around 20 years younger made life difficult.
I was far from impressed with my performance, only managed one Ippon throw but I still came away with a bronze medal.

So not all bad, but I know now how much I have to do in training to improve.

Ducati Pete

2011-03-01 00:16:00 UTC
Thanks for the encouragement guys, it’s been a pretty tough 2 months but at least I have an idea of where I need to improve.

I chose the nearest BJA club to where I live, it’s only 10 minutes drive away and they have their own place with a permanent mat area. What I didn’t realise is it’s a bit of an elite club, in that everyone there is a fairly high grade. I’m not sure that’s by design though as they have been quite happy for me to turn up and train.

There are several guys and girls training there who are on regional and / or national squads.

As I can’t find either my old BJA grading card or my AJA card I’ve started from white belt again. Just as well really as I have most of it to learn again.

Every time I’ve trained I’ve been covered in sweat and gassed for most of the Randori (sparring). At the end of the session we go into the fitness drills – shuttle runs with other exercises thrown in. We can take our jackets off for that, by that time mine is a sweat soaked rag anyway!

They said “Just do what you can and then have a rest.” For the first 2 weeks I did everything the younger guys and girls did in that phase, sometimes just a bit slower! (Apart form press-ups, I’m top dog at them – must be a leftover from the amount I did in the Army.) However, it took me 6 days to recover; the others were training twice a week at least!

The 3rd week I had to admit defeat, cramp in both calves! After a few shuttle “hobbles” I gave in!
