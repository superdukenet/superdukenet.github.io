I dont like the throttle up style myself and think its harder on the drivetrain than clutch ups. Both ways work though.

The problem i found with throttle up is you give it a good crack and as it come up you have to roll the throttle back and time it right to hit the start of the balance/light point, When learning i found this real hard to match it so would end up rolling off too soon and it went down, or not quite nipping it back soon enough and got that huge adrenaline spike as it came up way too high too fast.

On 1st gear clutch ups i only use about 1/4/ to 1/3rd throttle, just like your accelerating briskly, tinyest slip of the clutch, just enough to break the drive for a split second and it will come up nice and smooth. Its actually easier to just use your left index finger then you have a good secure hold of the bar and it means you cant give it too hard a ping. The beauty of that way is as it comes up you only have to roll the throttle back a tiny amount to keep it there.

2nd gear is easiest for me when driving hard out a corner and it will rise up, just keep it open and roll off a bit at the desired height. Or clutch up is the same as 1st but it needs a bigger handfull of throttle. Just use the same tiny clutch slip, but because you've got a load of throttle on the revs will rise quickly anyway. Also found you have to be pretty low in the revs for this. I tend to do it through sound but it's prob about 4k and when i clutch it it jumps to 6k for a split second.

Few little tricks i found is to relax and be loose.
Sit up with your ass cheecks just touching where it rises for the pillion pad. (i see you have an R so prob just slide back a bit).
Make sure rear sag an shock damping setting are right for you beign a big lad, too soft and it squats and absorbs the wheelie.
Try and sit up and kinda bring the bike to you. I had a bit of eureka moment when i couldn't get it up in 2rd but caught a glimpse of my shadow on the road to the side and saw everytime i clutched it i automatically leaned forward for some strange reason. Stopped that and it shot up easily

It's just a tiny flick, hardly any movement really. If you riding along all your looking to do is nip the lever in only just enough to make the revs rise, so only just nipping to the bite point. Your not disengageing the clutch totally and banging it back out. Think of it like a wheelie switch. If you are at 10mph in 1st and open the gas 1/3rd it will drive forward. All your aiming to do is just nip that clutch lever in enough to just hit the bite point for a split second and release.

I'm typing from my phone here, but if you jump onto YouTube and look for an MCN vid of how to wheelie it shows it there. It's harder to explain in text, once you hear it you'll get it everytime. It's like roll on braaaaaaa, wee tiny flick, brAAA, waa, then wee nips on the throttle to keep it there. I explain of in 1st coz that's the easiest and most gentle. In 2nd you have to give it a bigger handful of gas, same little clutch ping, bit coz its under a lot more load it will shoot up the revs quicker.

I'm still learning myself. Can only hold it up through a single gear just now. My slow mind is struggling a bit to match Tue gear change/ wee throttle snap to change up and keep it there. Always a bit nervous of getting back on the gas too hard once it's up a gear and flipping so it always just comes down after the gear change and tapers out into a long semi power wheelie.

Really though, if you can loft it high in 2nd and hold it there for a few hundred yards or so its gonna be good enough to get a good buzz and impress any bystanders. Some guys just have natural balance and can stunt easy. Guys like us have to take baby steps and build it up slowly. All you need to do is spent time in the seat and practice and it will come. Just take your time and build up slowly. Even if it takes a year then its a year of experience and you'll be better as time goes on.

If you check out the pics I posted earlier in the thread. When the headlamp is up in your line of sight that's the start of the balance point. You can still go way higher than that without flipping it.

Rather than put yourself under pressure, try practicing when your out on a run. Pull away from a quiet junction and flick it up etc. Then your warmed up and relaxed and not overthinking it and you'll find it'll all feel a lot more natural.

Love doing wheelies on my sdr.
I always think about shifting from 2 to 3.
It's gotta be super quick throttle control.
When riding, not on one wheel try shifting gears with no clutch.
Put a little bit of weight on the shift and super quick back off the throttle and get back on it.
When you do this the next gear will engage.
Don't put heaps of force into the gear lever. Just a little.
Practice this just for normal riding. You can get it pretty smooth.
Then when your confident. Try it on the back wheel. Awesome

As for me personally launching wheelies, I would always clutch it. These bikes have heaps of power and love it.
Or maybe I just love it. Either way. Go nuts. Look out for the cops.
