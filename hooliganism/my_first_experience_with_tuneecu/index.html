My first experience with TuneECU

BlueFlame

2010-12-23 01:54:00 UTC
I'll start by saying this is NOT baggin the software, just sharing to make myself feel better [beers aren't working] AND for a laugh.

I got my ECU cable from powercell, so i wanted to have a play and at a minimum get it connecting and save my stock map to my PC.

I don't have a laptop, so i informed the missus her laptop was needed in the shed for the night. I went through all the same BS other ppl have in getting it to connect. It would, then it wouldn't, then tilt your head to the left and it would connect etc:
Anyway, got a connection eventually, and long enough to read/save my stock map to the PC. I then disabled the O2 sensors and downloaded it to the ECU, all worked o.k, got the confirmation.

Time for a TPS re-set.
Started the bike, let it idle, 1 minute in, i notice my fuel light come on, 6minutes in the rev's start dropping and then dies. WTF
I knew the fuel was a bit low, but RUN OUT???

I started to think i could have damaged the ECU. So i give the missus her laptop back, and i drag my desktop rig into shed. Use the same drivers and all, same OS on the PC. It connects beautifully, and stays connected.

I bolt to the servo, get 10L Premium put it in the tank, crank for 15seconds, FLAT battery. WTF, it was connected to the battery tender the whole time. Oh well, charge the battery some more, cranking, cranking, nothing. I notice something missing, [the fuel pump whirr] just after turning on ignition. I test and relay is working, power to the pump and all, damn thing is dead.
I pulled it out and bench tested it NOTHING. How could it do this to me, dead i presume from overheating from running out of fuel while idling in my shed. It's only got 3500km's on the clock.

oh well..........
