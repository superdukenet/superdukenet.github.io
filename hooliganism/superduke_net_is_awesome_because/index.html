2012-02-08 20:19:00 UTC
I had my LC4 for 5 years and my SD for two before I even thought about looking for a forum that might bring me into contact with other bike owners, internet forums wasn't something I was familiar with or though about doing. I was just happy on my own doing stuff to the bike and minding my own business, doing track days and riding on the roads on my own at weekends. I thought the days of being in a biking community were over - not many of my friends had bikes, this was something I'd always done since I was 15 and I wasn't going to stop.

But I can't remember how I discovered this site. But I stumbled upon it one night and couldn't believe it. Here was a load of guys discussing the bike I loved - in great detail and with a load of passion and knowledge. I remember that I spent about a week sitting every evening with a bottle of wine reading back through many, many threads. Dr. F, The Thunder Pumpkin rider, etc. They inspired me. Amy wanted to know why I was often conversing with someone who's avatar was a cartoon Nazi called Klink My life changed a bit. For the better. My bank account changed for the worst as a two year orgy of modifications, fettling and general blinging took over my spare time.

I still get up every morning and have a coffee while I check out recent posts, and at lunch I usually click on to see what's up. Become a habit really. It's warming and comforting in a pretty hectic life. It's a bit of me time that no one else is invited into. Except you guys.

Thanks.

Crotchrockety

2012-02-08 22:20:00 UTC
Superduke.net is awesome because... without this forum I'm almost certain I'd be riding something else now.

I rushed into buying my first SD. My insurance money came through from my written off ZX6R (B1H) and I stumbled upon a second hand 2005 with 7,800 miles on it and a good few extras in a Yamaha stealer. I'd wanted a Superduke since I first saw a picture of one when I was 17 so I knew I had to have it. They started it up in the showroom and it scared the shit out of me. I took it for a ride and it scared the shit out of me. I then placed a deposit and later bought it.

I knew very little about them and their usual problems and had little to no mechanical knowledge. WP shaft and seal needed doing, had 2 failed clutch slaves and continous weeping after, seized front calipers, oil pressure sensor went, regulator/rectifier... there was more. Stealer helped very little, I'd spend a fortune on them looking at the bike and it would come out barely unchanged - lessons were learnt there also.

Some days I just wanted to leave it behind but when the bike was good, it was GOOD. Reading all the threads on here made me realise I wasn't alone with the problems I'd had.

Anyway that was then and this is now. I've learnt a lot from the wealth of knowledge on here and feel a part of something. I'm not going anywhere.
