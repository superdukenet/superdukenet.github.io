This is something that has always troubled me. I think it's just a problem waiting to occur - eventually an electrical plug will get corroded and fail. My prophylaxis has been to put each major cable connector in its own condom tied at both ends with cable ties. I make the condoms out of lengths of inner tubes from my kids old bikes. Occasionally I cut them off (the cable ties), make sure there is no moisture trapped inside and then put a new one on. Seems to work but even if it doesn't it makes me feel like I'm doing something to avoid a problem.

On many occasions I've thought about the wisdom of having the mesh at the front of the belly spoiler. Surely this just funnels water, oil and grime onto the cables and battery box Etc. I can't see that this area really needs air flow so perhaps a belly pan without the mesh is the way to go. Having the battery in that location means (of course) that it is really hard to give the engine a good wash without fear of creating an electrical fault. IMO an ideal design would have been to have the battery where it is for mass centralization but to have the cable connectors remote like under the seat. I guess it's all compromise...
