SDNerd

2011-11-18 20:19:00 UTC

The ST R gets maybe the most massive and universal compliments of any bike tested in the magazines: "The one I would actually buy."... Actually, I currently own a SMR--but close enough to the Duke (owned a std. and R there as well). And as I do every couple years, contemplating a new bike.

The deal is the SMR needs shocks/forks gone through; base gaskets done; new clutch pack; and tranny gone through and polished/burnished to fix notchy shifting as miles crept on (at 18,000 now). The local suspension wizard (who sponsers AHRMA racing) says he can do ALL for $2,000-2,400. Or I can trade for 2011 SMR (maybe best wait for the 2012) for $6,600 on a 2011.

I really like the SMR and it is the only bike I have ever seen upset the ST R in a comparo (Performance Bikes maybe?).

Thoughts and opinions on those who have ridden or owned the little ST R. The thought of enough power and really light bikes is appealing.

And then there is the new Tuono...... But man, I think it is ugly--and WAY beyond my performance needs--is it really more FUN? But same price as a SMR! C'mon KTM....

I used to have a triumph speed 4 (IL4 600), flat bars and all, free exhaust and played with tuneboys maps. I thought I really wanted a street triple and had many test rides (4 I think with 5-6 hours riding) because I couldn't believe what I felt; my old bike had better power and mid throttle torque, though the street triple was a little lighter and had a lower COG, brakes weren't better either though I had new disks and pads.

It is very comfy, everything naturally placed, nice to scoot around town, agile and nice handling (corners) bike, but doesn't make you laugh.

I almost bought a speed triple but one ride of a Sduke stopped that.

Anata

2011-11-18 23:51:00 UTC

I don't own a striple, but it came down to the stripleR versus the SD for me. A buddy bought the first stripleR that came into town, and I got to ride the piss out of that more than a few times in the twisty bits. Good fun. Such linear power - its like the throttle is a rheostat to an electric motor. Such a soft suspension ... so if you're not a big dude - good to go. Butt dyno told me that peak power (at the rear wheel) wasn't what the Daytona produces - I'm guessing like 95 rwhp. Makes good music at full song too, BTW.

Later, I had opportunity to flog an SD at a couple of different tracks (oh, yeah - on the street too), and I lost my mind and suddenly forgot that there was company called "Triumph" (even though I have one).

I was kind of 'meh' on the big bore S3 - heavy, soft - even the motor felt soft to me. Supposedly makes more power than the SD, but around town - the SD rules. All the torque in down low, slim chassis, quick turning, top shelf parts, stunning looks - sold!

Yeah, there were the Tuonos, Monsters, Brutales ... whatever ... If the Tuono was ever as handsome as a Brutale - and they just seem to get uglier with time - the SD might never had made it into the garage. No risk in that I suppose. I also don't need 130 to 160 HP to have a good time - especially on my everyday bike.

Anyway, I think least expensive route is to get your SMR sorted, flog that for another couple years, and then move on. But, I have to say that trade value would be pretty tempting.

My step brother has one and I've done a few hundred miles on it total. They're great bikes but there's no hooligan in it. The engine is too linear for any real excitement I find. Personally I found the brakes just as good as the SD and the suspension not too far behind. I am small mind you, only 5 ft 8 and 65kg but I didn't like the way the seat felt like it wanted me to sit on the tank with little room to adjust.

Anyway we swapped at Le Mans for 2 laps and now he's looking to sell the Triumph for a KTM so there you have it.

This year, I seriously considered trading my SD for an STR. I trailered it to the Triumph dealership. The owner/manager checked out my mint condition 2008 SD with 2300 miles and new rear tyre just installed. We agreed on a trade-in value but he didn't have a STR available. Seriously didn't think he could get his hands on one seeing they were sold out and waiting for them to be shipped. He wanted to sell me a plain ST he had in the show room. I wasn't interested in taking a step back for something that didn't have the components similar to the SD. It had to be a STR. We were going to stay in touch in case a STR surfaced. I was leaving and their sales guy came out to look at the SD knowing my interest in the STR. He looked at me and said, "Why would you want to trade this for a Street Triple R?", basically telling me I would be foolish to make such a deal. Needless to say, I put another 3300 miles on the SD this year, wore out a back tyre and was glad I didn't make that deal.
I do seriously think a KTM SM-R could be in my future. If I were you, I would trade that pre-enjoyed, high mileage SMR and for another $6600 have a new bike with a warranty.
Where's that dealer so I can trade in my 2008 SD (with existing factory warranty).
