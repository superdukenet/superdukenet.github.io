If it is the left hand side push rod seal, it is only necessary to remove the slave cylinder, hook out the seal (easier if you remove right hand side clutch cover and pull back the push rod)and push in a new one - I understand that there is a KTM tool to ensure correct alignment and depth and perhaps the need to glue the seal in - talk to Paul (ex-Bracken - 020 7232 1814) if you need help or want someone to do it for you - it is certainly not a three hour job, etc - I have a spare seal if you need it in a hurry and I'm due south of you in Kent but commute into London (past Paul's workshop just off the Old Kent Road) every day.
