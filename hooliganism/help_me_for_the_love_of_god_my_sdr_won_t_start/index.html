2011-05-13 13:58:00 UTC
To get to the BGC you do indeed need to lift the tank. You can also then access the rear coil pack (fires the spark through spark plug) and spark plug. You will need a thin walled socket to remove the spark plug. Its a very tight fit and a normal spark plug socket will not go down there. The front plug is a proper pain in the arse to get to. One of the advantages of fitting a Motobox is that it makes the whole of the top of the engine far easier to work on. That however is not want you want to hear right now. To get to the front plug the whole airbox has to come off. This is a pain in the arse so I would check other things first. If the rear spark is firing then she would at least try to start on turning over. I'd put fresh fuel in and a good squirt of WD40 into the BGC, dry out and dielectric grease that baby up.
