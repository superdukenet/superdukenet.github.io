i like them new, this way you know how they were treated, that they were broken in properly, and that noone but you had their hands in her. . but you do loose a lot of money, that's true. i only buy new what i think i'll keep for a long time.
with second hand, you'll never know - maybe she was crashed bad, and rebuilt? may look like new, but the frame is slightly bent, or maybe previous owner never changed oil, or run it with wrong/low oil overheating it for extended periods of time, maybe she was raced, or worse stunted, or who knows what else. checking compression in each cylinder and cutting open old oil filter will tell you alot, but not everything, and since my life rides on the bike, i want to be 99% sure i can trust her. peace of mind is worth extra money.
