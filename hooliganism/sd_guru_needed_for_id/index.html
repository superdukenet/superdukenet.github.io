BASH69

2010-02-12 11:44:00 UTC
Here in UK you rarely get any money for after market bling. Especially if you trade it in or sell it to a dealer.
What I'm saying is that buyers pay for the year model and the mileage, that's it. Any bling that's put on the bike is not worth paying for. It's better for you to take off all bling and sell it separately and put the bike back into original state when you sell it.

If the bling has got value for you, then consider paying for it. But don't assume that if someone puts on USD400 worth of rearsets the price of the bike should go up by USD400. And remember that when you come to sell it, nobody is going to pay for the bling.

If I were you, I'd offer the "standard value" for a bike of that year with that mileage. Then I would probably add a "good will" amount towards the bling and the nice condition - if I were willing to pay for it.

Buyers market! Good luck! Lovely bike regardless of price.

Don't be fooled, please.

Accept only hard evidence, if you're willing to pay for it.

A tuned bike, in this case with an alledged power hike of 50% (that's fifty percent), rarely commands more money than a standard bike. Even if you buy a tuned Hayabusa, you rarely pay very much for the extra tune.

Unless you're 100% sure of what's gone into this bike, and you're a collector, you'll be better off with a standard bike.

Just my thoughts - buying bikes isn't really about being sensible, though.

Anyone who went to the trouble of having a bike shipped over in bits from another country would surely have been equally as enthusiastic about having all the necessary paperwork to back up the alleged work/history that made the bike special enough to bring it over in the first place.
