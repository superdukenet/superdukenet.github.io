Bink

2010-01-24 21:43:00 UTC

Stratkat, the fuel tank leak is no more then a pain in the back side but easy to fix.

You might be able to fix this with the tank still on the bike. All I did is cover the edges on the metal screw 'socket' with epoxy resin which did the trick. As it is quite thick it should stick. Alternatively you could take the fuel tank off and fix it that way. I did take it off, poured some loc-tit 243 around the edges (to be sure, to be sure) and covered off any gaps with epxoy resin that way. No leaks since and this was done more then over a year ago.

Bink

2010-01-25 19:23:00 UTC

It would be the 44-45 bolts - the ones that go into the bottom of the tank at the back of the shrouds. I haven't heard of the 40's causing problems.
I did hear of leakages near the fuel pump due to overtightening.

It's fairly easy to do. I did not torque the bolts just had tightened them with a small allen key.
