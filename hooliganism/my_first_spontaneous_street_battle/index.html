Out for a ride today, sunny 32C, been sick in bed for 3 days, so needed some thrills.
Saw a bike ride the opposite direction, so i spun around and followed him.
It was all calm and casual, i didn't roar up behind him or anything, just pulled up slowly behind him at the round-about waiting for traffic.

He's riding a Honda CBR 1000RR
I had a chuckle to myself that he had shorts and t-shirt on, and i could see tape/dressing on his left elbow. [i was kitted out in my new Joe Rocket Speedmaster 5.0]

So, he goes right, i turn right. He slips around the car in front of us and opens the bitch up.
Hmmmmf..
I also realised i'm much more comfortable jumping 90' table tops on a motocross track than i am doing high speed on the road.

I Jap litre bike will do 160kph in first gear! From memory I think my 06 Gixxer would do 210kph in second so what chance have you got on a SD.
The trick is not to race one of these bikes on strait roads its on the bends that you will have these dudes thnking what the hell is going on.
On a sports bike Its feels easier to go around corners by getting off the seat like your on a race bike but on the SD I just stear the bike with my butt LOL!!
The SD just seems to go around corners easier than a sports bike. In the high performance bikes I have owned 05 R1, 06Gixxer, 07 Busa with mods and to me the SD is the best fun bike I have owned. I even owned a 08 Speed Triple and the SD is a better bike easy.
I used to think that sports bikes were great but where can you use the speed and H Power of these things. I had my Busa up to a indicated 297kph one time and you just cant stop thinking of things that might go wrong at those speeds.
Im running Yoshi's and they sound fantastic but make sure you do get the MotoBox and Mrz32 map as it makes a hole lot of difference to the SD.
Make yourself a cable for the Tuneecu and put MrZ32 map in your bike and you will be surprised at the difference for sure.
