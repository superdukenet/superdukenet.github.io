Point is Fairy Fellas; Stripping a faired bike into a nekkid bike is always stripping a faired bike into a nekkid bike.

The SuperDuke is one of the very few (if not the only one) 1000cc motorbikes that's built to be nekkid. (Which is one of the reasons why I bought it.)

Reason to go for a Jap 1000cc is exactly the same as going for one and leaving the fairies on; they're plentiful, reliable, fairly powerful and you can get bits and pieces for them from anywhere. But they're boring.

The S1000RR is the dogs danglers when it comes to bikes in 2010. Lately BMW has been building some truly awesome bikes, and they'll keep on doing that. S1000RR is 193bhp at the crank from the factory, someone dyno'd it to ~180 at the wheel. Show me a reasonably priced RC8 that does that. Besides, what's the point in calling the S1000RR ugly (which IMO it's not) when you're going to strip it off it's characteristic looks (a.k.a fairies).

How many nekkid RC8s...? Probably tons more than a nekkid S1000RR.

Final point; Starting a powerful-nekkid-project from a faired bike is easy and everybody does it. A 160-170bhp SuperDuke on the other hand is totally unique.

Live with it, gents, live with it.
