one time i was changing clutch on cbr600. to save myself dropping down oil i took 2 old car tyres, stack them and lay the bike on them, giving me a good access to the clutch without draining oil. everything went well, till one of the last nuts. i dropped it in the engine. pissed off, i drained the oil, took off the oil pan, took out the nut, put everything back... bike starts, but does not move when you put it in gear, unless you keep RPM very high. it feels like the plates are not catching.... put the bike down, figured i'll take the plates back out and have a closer look. i drop the same nut into the engine! quite pissed off, drain the oil again, take off the oil pan, take out the nut, put everything together, plates look fine, check the manual again, everything is in order... put the oil back in - and i crack the oil pan overtightening the drain bolt. FOOOOK!!!! i drain the oil again, it was Sunday, i ordered new oil pan and i have to leave everything till Mon. At the time i had no garage, so i was at my friends place, some 40km from home... i have to call my backthen girlfriend to come and take me home... Monady she has to drive me back out there.... put new oil pan on, put the oil back in, bike still will not move...WTF??? i have another, good look at the manual....
i've been lookin at the wrong diagram!!!! manual covers cbr600 and 1000 (87 to 91 i think?), and clutch for 1000 goes in opposite way then in 600! so my clutch is in backwards...
15 min later i'm riding her home, bike runs like it should.
30 min easy job turned into few hours of hell... + extra parts...
