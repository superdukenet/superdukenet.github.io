Single exhaust for Superduke?

icarve

2009-05-30 21:28:00 UTC
I'm looking for a better sound some weight loss and hopefully a little more HP from my 06 SD.
In a conversation with a custom exhaust builder he casually said that the best setup would be a single cannister exhaust. This got me thinking, and since I live in New Zealand where everything costs 3 times as much as the rest of the world, I thought that maybe there would be a savings here too. So I have got myself a new, single Two Brothers Racing M2 carbon fibre can (a slip on for a Suzi gsxr 1000) figure this can should be designed to cope with as much horesepower as any bike out there.
I am having a custom stainless connector pipe fabricated, and will have the single muffler exiting on the right hand side. I plan to do before and after dyno runs to make sure it works and also hope to see if I can get the dealer put in the Akro mapping for me.
Would like to find out if anyone has done or heard anything on this kind of setup?

2009-06-29 20:45:00 UTC
Well I found the answer to 2 into 1 versus twin mufflers , I'm pretty sure its just the noise factor as the two mufflers just split down the noise more than a 2into 1 can. I have installed my two brothers single exhaust after a bit of messing around, making my own bracket to mount in the end of the frame tube etc. The single exhaust is VERY loud on its own, so I installed a 2bros version of the db killer which they call apower tip and it is supposed to knock 5db off but in my opinion still too loud, so I did some experimenting with my own baffle and have added a 220mm section of stainless pipe, perforated with random number of holes drilled, into the end of the can which really reduces the noise and makes a much more pleasing throaty sound rather than the sound like a single with an open pipe. I am not sure about the space on the left where the can is missing but I am working on a small piece of bodywork made from flat black 3mm acrylic to fill the space and blend the tail section.
I hope to do a few dyno runs within the next couple of weeks so will try with a couple different versions of my baffle (drilled vs slotted) and with just the tip and then just open for comparison so will post results when I have them.
