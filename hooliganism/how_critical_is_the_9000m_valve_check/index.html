You can get cheap shim kits from the US and the gasket kit is not expensive also. (Harley ones have better choice)
As suggested above, do it yourself.
The main thing is to do a proper measurement and write things down in a neat order so you know what valve has which clearance.
Get the shims out and measure them or read what size they are.
Then either use a spreadsheet or a bit of math and paper to determine what shims need replacing and the size they need to be. It is just adding or subtracting.
Fit it all as per manual and check measure, and check again....
But the covers back on and all is fine.
A good practise is to take disconnect something like the plugs to the coils and give it a crank to see if all is smooth. If all fine reconnect and start it.
Also get a mate or a forum member who done it before or who is a mechanic to look over your shoulder to do it the first time.
Things you need besides the usual spanners:
Metric feeler gauges, 10 mm shim kit or get the needed shims from a friendly dealer. Rocker covers gasket kit, some long nose pliers, magnet, good verniers (pref electronic) or micrometer.

You can take a punt and leave it, open it up and measure if it needs done or bite the bullet and do it properly yourself. As mentioned above normally they don't need much adjusting, but hey you never know.
