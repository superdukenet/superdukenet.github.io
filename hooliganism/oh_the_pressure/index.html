I think the need to lower pressure on the track is due to the fact that you are riding a lot harder and faster and the tyres heat up more raising the pressure. Lighter wheels will help with handling making turn in easier as there is less rotating mass. Centripetal force makes the wheels want to stay upright and go straight. Less weight means they are easier to push over. The lower weight will also put less force on the tyres and possibly keep them from getting as hot during hard riding.

ktmguy

2011-04-26 07:25:00 UTC
Maybe we can explain correctly. It seems that this issue still is missunderstood.

On track lower pressure results in quicker warm up and higher temperature. There is a safe range as too hot will ruin the tyre and also produce hot scredding and you land on your backside. Some of you might have experienced that on a hot day at the tracks.
This lower pressure works also in winter as it makes your tyre warm up faster due to more flex in the carcass and bigger contact patch.
As you figured out by now this is not recommended for long excited rides in a hot climate as it will result in having to take a spare.
More pressure results in less flex in the carcass and slower warm up and less heat.

Idealy start wit a recommended pressure for track and go for a few rounds till the tyre is warm and you get to ride as expected. Then come in the pits and imediately check the pressure. The difference in between the two is the raise and the max should not exceed the manufectures recommendation!
Try to add air if the temp is too high and drop it if you need more heat in the tyre, again don't go lower than the recommended spec for that tyre!

Also make sure your suspension is set up properly as shitty damping will also stress the tyre and increase the temp, which will make your results useless. I could not believe the diffrence a proper suspension setup did for my bike and the tyre wear and temperature, recommended!

A bit of experimenting and keeping track of what the results are will help getting a ballpark setup for the next time.
And dribbel don't worry about the weight of the air, lay of the beer and burgers the night before this will make a bigger difference!
