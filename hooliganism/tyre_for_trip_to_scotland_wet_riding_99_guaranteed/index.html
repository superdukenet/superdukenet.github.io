Silly question - any idea where you're going? It's a (very) long time since I lived up there and I'm sure some of the locals on the site can point you in better directions but this is a few of my old favourites:

Aberfoyle (A821) to Strathyre (A84) to Crieff (A85) - the first part on the A821 was my Friday night special (it was a bit of a tourist trap but there was no bugger up there Friday afternoon/evening) - peg to peg fun.

A82 - pretty much all of it but Arden - Tarbet is big and fast. Tarbet - Ardlui is the real tight and twisty bit and then it is big sweepers to Crianlarich.

A83 (turn left at Tarbet, rather than right) and go all the way down to Lochgilpead and up to Oban - and then back round to Crianlarich. On the way, the A819 runs from Inveraray (south) to the A85 (north) - every time I rode it, I reckon I saw less than 1 bike or car each time - mostly it was empty and it used to be a classic "where the fook does all our EU money go?" road.

The road numbers might have changed but you should be able to work it out from the names - just be prepared for the sodding weather!
