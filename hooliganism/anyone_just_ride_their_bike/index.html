Dutch

2008-10-08 15:32:00 UTC
2 comments...

Bikes these days come restricted and modified to suit emmissions and noise rules, so they're often under powered, badly fuelled at certain rpm and all the bits in the cat weight a tonne,... plus manufacturers save money on the retail price by having some parts built to a lower standard, especially bits like exhausts, which many buyers see as an acceptabel mod cost.

Also, there are useful things you can buy, like crash mushrooms, or flip up screens, which add to the bike's useability.

Even vanity mods like tail tidies return the bike to the forms and shapes that the original designers had on thier drawing boards before the European equivalent of the DOT stepped in.

Therefore it makes sense to unleash the bikes full potential and return it to the way the designers intended...

...then you can "just ride it."

My second comment is that modding a bike is fun. We're all individuals ("i'm not" LOL) ... and some of us don't want to turn up at a bike meet, KTM dealership or track day just to see clones of our bikes in the car park or on track. I love every minute of tail tidy, mini indie, decal, screen, crash bung and zorst fitting agony.
