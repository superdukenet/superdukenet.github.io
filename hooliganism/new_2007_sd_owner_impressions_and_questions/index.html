New 2007 SD owner - impressions and questions.

Anata

2012-04-26 02:35:00 UTC
Hi everyone!
I bought my Superduke in November with only 1870 km on it. It had LeoVince SBK's on it and a PCIII, but otherwise stock. Given that it was winter, I wasn't able to actually ride it until last weekend. I was pretty disappointed with the bike initially, as the ride was very harsh, skittish handling, and way too loud! Power was only so-so, but with that brutal noisy exhaust, I doubt I ever got it to 7500 rpm. Then I decided to rip out the PCIII and put the stock exhaust back on.

Holy crap! It had double the power, and with the (too) quiet stock exhaust back on, and the PCIII off, I was able to play around at more reasonable (>5000 - 9000) rpms. The prior owner who set up the PCIII obviously didn't know what he was doing. Probably loaded the richest map he could find, then lost interest when there was no power. The suspension adjustments were way off as well - I counted 0 clicks of rebound on one fork, and 12 on the other. The rear adjustments were just screwed to the stops as well! I'm getting that sorted, but after going with "comfort" settings initially, I'm very pleased to see the level of actual adjustability. I'm hoping to find some quiet inserts for the LeoVinces, because the stock pipes are just too quiet.

So, other than the O2 eliminator I've left in, and the evap canister I've left off, the bike is stock. I have been reading posts on this board all winter, and now that this bike is EXACTLY what I want, I need to ask about one particular mod: The airbox.

I'm in Canada, and the Rottweiler and Motohooligan kits are similar costs (once you factor in SAS block off plates, etc.) It seems that they are both nice products, but the Rottweiler has seemed to gain favour with many members because it is supposedly much less $$ that the Motohooligan, carbon fiber base plate, etc. I'd have to guess that they are both similar with respect to noise levels, but are not too loud at cruise (like during the commute to work). I'd really like to know if these products are essentially equal, given how close they are in cost. I don't care about extracting every last bit of power (did that with my Hayabusa - now sold) but looking for rideability - keeping a solid mid range, etc, etc.

Any comments suggestions would be highly appreciated!

It must have been running really badly to gain power by putting the stock exhaust back on especially with adding another 7 or 8 kg!

You could even get some baffles made up for your Leos if you are really concerned about the noise. But I'd recommend putting them back on , along with the PC, and getting a custom map made at a good dyno place. Just for the benefits of loosing the weight of the stockers over the back wheel. They are seriously heavy! Do all the mods before the mapping though. Loose the SAS system along with the secondary butterflies and associated stuff. Latest thing is to also fit RC8 velocity stacks although they seem to increase top end power and torque - not midrange like you want. My bias is towards the MH airbox because Aaron, (aka Mr. MH), has spent a long time developing this for the SD - he's a true fan and deserves our support - the quality of the box is second to none.
