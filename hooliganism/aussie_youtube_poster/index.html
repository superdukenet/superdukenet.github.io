Is it fine sand or gravel pebbles?

Gravel and pebbles get everywhere - into each nook and cranny of the bike but I have found that this is a good way to ensure you go over the whole bike to check for damage. Just methodically work your way over the bike with a little brush and tweezers.

I would definitely raise the tank and have a look in the airbox to check it's absolutely clean. And pay special attention to the front brake calipers - I've had pebbles crushed in so hard that you have to remove the pads to get it all out - best be sure in that area, you don't want a bit of stone working its way into the pad material or piston area.

Emu, nice vid and thanks for posting it on the 'tube.

For cleaning the bike, I would say dont ride it until you've completely stripped and cleaned out all the brakes sand and stones can kill discs and they aint cheap!

Check your air box and all nooks crannies etc. Make sure your handlebars are on straight, loosen them off their rubber dampers and let them resettle then tighten back up again. Also make sure you havent damaged teh steerign head stops/bolts. Turn it to full lock in each direction and make sure that nothign fouls/touches the tank and also make sure it's the same amount of turn in each direction. You may have bent the stopper on teh headstock part of the frame. No big deal (as long as the frame is OK) but you may want to adjust the stop bolts to stop it hitting the tank if this does now happen.

Finally check your chain. Clean it thoroughly to make sure there's no sand in the seals/rings or that sucker is gonna wear out much faster as a result.
