I got it done at " Thermal edge coating" . He has sample headers and turbos coated in different colours/ finishes in his office.

He said there are a couple of different products used in the industry to ceramic coat. He prefers his of course, and I'm very happy. He does powder coating also.

The ceramic apparently gets sprayed on like paint ( without the +ve charge like powder coat) and baked at a much higher temp. Mine have a satin/ polished alloy appearance.

jambox

2011-03-03 20:57:00 UTC
Word is they will never discolor, but you need to occasionally give them a polish with a non abrasive mag wheel cleaner to keep them looking good. Tar comes off with thinners.

My mate has had it on his 1098 headers for two years....still looks like new.

The guy who did it said he does a lot of speedway race cars. He's seen headers crumpled up after a crash and the coating has not flaked or split.....so tough stuff!

I did polish mine to remove hand prints after fitting them. Used Maguires wheel cleaner, no effort and they look great.
