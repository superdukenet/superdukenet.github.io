The day has come... Selling the SDR

Dutch

2011-10-16 19:07:00 UTC
Hey fellas,

Well, the day has finally come where I have decided to sell my beloved SDR.

As you know I'm a 6x KTM owner, and on my second superduke and third LC8-engined bike. I love these machines, and I love the KTM brand... but I've been hanging on to this bike for all the wrong reasons, almost out of nostalgia and not being able to bring myself to leave the Superduke fold. It's such a great bike to own, even if you're not on it. LOL.

Reasons are complex, but basically my riding is changing.

The SDR is the best bike for the old me. Aggressive, loud, in a fooking hurry, and grinning like the devil on PCP... but as time goes by I'm becoming less angry and prefer to ride smooth instead of fast. As some of you noticed, I picked up a (cough, splutter) for slow summer rides (85-bhp at the wheel, LOL) and to my horror, I've discovered that not only am I faster round Brands Hatch on it, but that it's also better through town, and it's even easier and less demanding to ride as an every day bike, despite being an air-cooled, twin-shock retro roadster. It corners faster too.

This isn't about the bike tho, it's about me. When faced with both sets of keys I opt for jeans, piss-pot lid and the Ducati keys. Also, I'm 45, with 2 kids, and my biking tastes are becoming more about lifestyle than the latest power commander, TuneECU, or which widget will give me another 3 bhp. I want timeless biking, not this year's latest bike. I don't really care about a 1200 SDR, and that's weird.

Also, the Dutchess was knocked off her Buell a few weeks ago, and though she's on the mend, it reminded more how mortal we all are, and while any bike is dangerous, I'm well aware that the super duke has always made me ride like a cnut. Time to chill a little, I think, and you just can't chill on an SDR.


So... I still love my Superduke, and if I was a little more financially stable I'd keep it, just to hang on to a modern classic, but it's crazy to hang on to such a high spec machine as a "second bike" while I spend my bike-money on an old cafe racer.

I'm not in a rush to get rid of it and I want the right price (the mods and spec are ridiculous), so as this isn't the best time of year to sell, I may still have it through winter. ...and if you'll let me, I'd love to hang out here still, and keep meeting up with the mates I've made on road and track.


One final comment: Out of all the biker-types out there, Superduke riders are the maddest, quickest, and most opinionated, individualistic, loonies I have ever met.
So many good riders, so many decent fellas, so much knowledge, trust, passion...
I'm more distressed about leaving a brotherhood of like-minded nutters than I am about losing the bike. It's a very exclusive, very special club. I will definitely have regrets, and I can barely bring myself to press the Submit button.

Love this thread. It's great to see a proper debate raging - and about bikes too...

I knew my decision would be polarising. Bike ownership can get pretty tribal and for a while I could't even hear anyone say anything about KTMs or Superdukes that was negative...

If had a big garage and unlimited funds I would absolutely keep the Superduke, and I'm lucky that I can have two bikes and a car, but I am sticking with the European trellis-framed V-twins, and in many ways the Ducati Cafe Racer has the same heart and soul as the Superduke, it just does it in a timeless way... with no sell-by date.

Speaking of sell-by dates, what I realised is that I'm NOT getting twitchy about the 1200 Supeduke. That was the wake-up call. I just wasn't interested.
