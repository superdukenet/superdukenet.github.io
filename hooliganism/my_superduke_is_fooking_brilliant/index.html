Best. Bike. Ever.

If you want brownie-points service & a hand to hold, call Honda.

We've all got to remember, dealers are in the biz to SELL BIKES, not service them or make you love KTM (or whatever brand they are pushing). Service is something they have to do, and once they have your $$, 95% of them could give a rat's @ss what you think. KTM has always been a serious racing/off road bike company, and expects it's owners will do a certain amount of DIY maintenance. KTM isn't going to roll out the BMW/Porsche/M-B/Cadillac-style " service maitre d' " and give you a loaner while some white-glove guy cleans the windshield. There are good & bad dealers, just like there are good & bad customers - which are you?
With all it's imperfections, the SD is more fun, more capable, and more forgiving than any other bike available. There are faster track bikes, faster (and cheaper) sport bikes, and prettier ones. This KTM has soul, guts, the best components on a stock bike, and incredible real-life (usable) power. What's not to love?
