I havent had a KTM dirtbike as such, but I kind of have.. (03 exc)

I sold this a couple years ago now. Got a txt from a mate last week, said he saw the bike around town. It looked a bit (more) ragged, but was still doin wheelies everywhere, lol.
Never had any problems with it. Bike was rock solid. Build quality was fantastic, bike was punchy and very torquey, and maintenance wasnt any where near as bad as I was expecting. Suspension wasnt on par with my CRF, but it definately did the job.
From what Ive read, the old gen RFS motors (as pictured) were a bit more reliable than the RC4/XC4/RF4 (2008+ I think?) generation. Ive seen heaps of peeps put a rebuilt RFS motor into their newer frame after their engine blew up, but I cant comment on that from experience.

However, Ive laid down a deposit on a new sx 350, cant wait to get my hands on it

I ride a 2008 300XC. I have owned nearly all KTM offroad bikes and this one is the bomb. I guess if I weighed 150lbs and was 20 again I'd go for the 200...but at 6'2" and 220lbs it's a big bore for me. Until you have tried the 300 you just haven't lived. It's referred to as a 3-stroke...for good reason...all the best of 4 stroke and 2 stroke benefits rolled into one bike. But it is not street legal. For that you must go to the 4 stroke EXC's...that is if you don't mind a heavy bike. I'll take the 3-hunny all day long!
