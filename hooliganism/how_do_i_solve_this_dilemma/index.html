Hello Dukers,

At the moment i'm in Canada (Fernie, BC to be exact) spending the season snowboarding, I saved up and have spent the last 4 months here just playing about snowboarding. However when i return home in about a months time i will be pretty much broke, i'm going to start working again as soon as i can, possibly two jobs but i loved it out here so much i have decided to come back again this November but this time for a year and i will be working while i'm out here (find out if i get my visa in 2 weeks) sooo here is the dilemma....

My SDR has been sleeping all winter while i have been away and i think i'm going to have to sell it when i get back, i wasn't planning too but i'm going to need the money to get me started for the first few months when i get to Canada again as the pay is crap (or the jobs i will be able to get will be anyway) I could ride the SDR through the summer and try selling it just before i go but it would be a risk and i think i could sell it easier and probably for more before i go. However i don't want to be left without a bike all summer as i'm gagging to get back on a bike already!

Also i keep getting this funny fascination with a new bike the bike taking my fancy is a Duke 690 (60mpg and it looks great) but how much will i miss the extra power of the SDR if i was to get a nearly new Duke 690 when i got back from canada in a year and halfish?

Ideally i'd never sell the SDR its the best bike i've ever owned and i love it to bits but its already sat for 6 months now and i can't leave her for a year again just depreciating
