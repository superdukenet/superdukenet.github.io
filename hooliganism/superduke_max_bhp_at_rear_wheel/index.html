Oh Christ, what did I start? Dyno wars? Yes, yes, I know all dynos are different. My point was not that an SD can't be tuned to make rwhp 148HP, its that it shouldn't be tuned to give 148 HP - at least not if its intended to be used on the street.

There are only two ways to increase HP. You either have to increase the torque or the rpms. The rest is simply math. Increasing either of these variables is going to introduce additional stresses in an engine. The issue is not can it be done, its can it be done and still have an engine that won't grenade? There are plenty of examples on this very site where folks have tried to hotrod the LC8 engine only to end up with weeping cylinder gaskets and grenaded pistons. So unless there was a hell of a lot more work done to this motor besides porting and polishing, i.e., lightening the pistons and thicker cylinder bolts, and increased cooling, one is asking for a catastrophic failure. As I said, caveat emptor.

i think that you mistook me for what i was saying... without increasing the compression ratio, the displacement or significantly increasing the octane and ignition advance

by allowing the airflow to travel faster (without restricting the volume of the airflow) (maximising the air that can flow in/out), the torque curve should not drop by much.

what is currently happening is once you get to around 7000 rpm, the the airflow is getting past its efficiency for and that is why you see the toque curve dropping off.

140hp only requires 76.5 (roughly) ft lb of torque at 9600 rpm (200 below that of the redline of an 07 model).. we are seeing bikes currently produce well over 80ft lb of torque... so it is not inconceivable for an sd to make 140hp at the wheel.

how can this be done? by allowing the airflow to flow as efficiently as possible... flowed heads (just cutting out material wont do it).. maybe a 5 angle cut on the valves.

im sticking with the viewpoint that on pump fuel, without raising compression, cubic capacity or going for custom cams (06 cams should be enough).. 140hp will be seen on the back wheel of an SD let alone an SDR

I have ported quire a few motors over the years & have found the best results is using a flow benck & to CNC the ports to get equal dimensiond for both heads, then there is no guessing, the throttle bodies need to be attached as well as the stacks to get the exact flow of the intack system.

I have done 2 heads with a 5050 Raptor high lift cams on my XT660X Yamaha.

Stage1 flows 339@ 500 lift, flow only = 10 HP from head only US$700 all dyno proven
Stage2 flows 369@ 500 lift, +2mm inlet valves = 14 HP from head only US$1200 all dyno proven

The standard bikes hp went form 45 Hp to 74 rear wheel HP, big bore kit 686cc to 734cc, compression ratio from 10:1 to 12.5:1, head & cam work, +2mm bored throttle body with bigger throttle plate, DNA stage 3 air box, & full exaust system PCII with custom map. We now have a stroker crank on one whick produces 80 HP which is almost doulbe the standard HP, we will see how long this one will last before the big bang.
