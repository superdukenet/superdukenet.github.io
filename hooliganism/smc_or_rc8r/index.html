I can only speak from a viewed experiance and very little from a on seat place. I think that a person that has a good road course style will be perfectly capable of getting every last mph out a a corner on a sumo bike.
I went to ButtonWillow race track to watch Chris Fillmore and the Boz Broz practice on the cart course. The were preparing for the X-Games and Ben Bostrom and Chris Fillmore know a few things about putting together good corner speed on a thumper.
Both riding styles were completly differant than each other. Ben left his inside foot on the pegs and Chris the classic dirt track style. Ben rode the bike more nuetral with very little weight shifted inward and Chris rode off the outside letting the bike do the leaning.
All directly related to comfort and both looked like they were riding on a Sunday cruise.
I owned and rode a Duke and a DRZ 400 and never felt like I could ride the bikes in the classic style due to muscle memory forcing me into my comfort zone. My guess is that you will find tgat a foot remaining on the peg will fit you very well and you will get that scooter sliding just fine, foot firmly pegged. Larry Pegram and Max Biaggi and Nicky Hayden all have the road racer stance and I have seen them in SuperMoto racing perform just as well as thier dirt breathren.
My best guess is all about comfort.

2011-07-18 12:57:00 UTC
That is a great video. What a laugh.

As for the SMC, I had one before my SD, and after I got the SD I felt there was quite a bit of overlap. I mean, there is less than 100 pounds difference between each with both fully fueled. I had to double check that, I was surprised. It was getting so little use after SD, and it felt so much heavier than my dirt bikes (which was a feeling of lightness that I thought would be fun on the street) that I tried the Aprilia SXV 550 and the Husky 510 SMR (both real supermotos; the SMC is not). The SXV is manic, a barrel of laughs, and anyone who thinks they are badass on an SD but wants something more badass needs to try this. It's the most manic, exciting bike I've been on for the street.

However, at that time they had not fully straightened out their serious problems of oil mixing with water (that was indeed sorted with 08 models, as it turns out) so I bought the Husky, which itself is far more manic, far more "throw aroundable" than the SMC. I weighed the Husky with 1/3 tank of gas: 272. SMC with same gas: 330. I'm not Bashing KTM or the SMC, the SMC is an all-rounder, not a supermoto. If you already have an SD you have a great all-rounder, you might want to try a totally different beast with the next hell-raiser. The only warning, you are not going to want to sit on the highway for 50 miles on either the Aprilia or the Husky.

Lightness is everything in this kind of bike. I never was tempted to come up to a stop sideways on the SMC, or to lock the brake in a turn. As soon as I had a couple of hundred miles on the Husky I was sliding down my driveway sideways when my kids would come out to see me coming home, stoppies at lights, sideways in turns. Yes, the SMC will do all of that, but the point is I just never felt like it. I know this is off topic, since you were asking about the RC8R. Just thought I should throw this out there....
