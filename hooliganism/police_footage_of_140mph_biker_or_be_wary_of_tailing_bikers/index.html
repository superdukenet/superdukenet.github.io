I ride the roads shown on that video and remember the case in question.

I agree the guy was riding too fast for the conditions also his road positioning was lacking in certain areas. Overtaking on solid white lines is always asking to get nicked etc etc

BUT

I fail to see any justification in putting police riders / photographers out on unmarked sports bikes (black 06 fireblade and a blue / white GSXR 1000) in the hope of entrapping bikers that are breaking the law.

Furthermore, once they have succeeded in their entrapment and have enough to ban the guy why not break off pursuit? This would have reduced 10 mins of footage down to less than 1 minute. It could also have prevented either the rider or the copper from being involved in a fatal collision.

I have seen these police riders around. They are hardly paragons of safe riding and that doesnt mean they are fast. The guy that rides the fireblade has shit machine control. I followed him after he went all secret squirrel around the block trying to entrap me and a few guys i ride with into speeding.

If it is the case you refer to, the guy was 22 years old and stupid. But werent we all at 22 (some of us still are). What did he learn from a prison sentence, loss of his job and a long ban? The bike was on finance so he is probably still paying it off and what do you think his views on the law and its enforcement are now?

I too was riding fast bikes bought on credit when I was 22. When I got nicked, which was often, I respected the Policeman and accepted his judgement of my riding. 20 years on I have zero respect for the police and no acceptance of trial by video in these cases. This change in attitude has come about in the last year and purely from these underhand revenue raising tactics. The police today are nothing more than stealth tax collectors. They penalise the working classes whilst giving real criminals, who wont pay fines, cautions and ASBO's.
