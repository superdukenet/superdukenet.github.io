Hi All,

Lots of good ideas in the posts above. Not wanting to diss anyone but here are my thoughts on a few of them.....

An 'ignore' button? Hmmm. Not one in this version of PHPBB but we may migrate onto V3.0 soon (maybe) and there may be more options on that??

Other models. I understand where these comments come from and agree in part. The other models were added as a separate section for those who own/want to know about them. Yes I agree they water down the site a bit. However they allow SD owners the option to talk about other KTMs without having to register on other forums. Maybe I'm wrong on this but I think this is preferable to not having them.

More stickies.... Good idea. Maybe we could go one further and have a FAQ section?? We would need a volunteer to keep a list of subjects and link to the various threads from there. The reason I say this is that stickies are just that...they stay at the top. too many and the new posts would become lost. Just my opinion, though.

Attaching documents is a do-able. Save the document in HTML, Word of PDf format (etc) on a hosting site and pop in a link to it in your threads.

Adding pix. Again this is easy (if you know how). You can either link to an image on t'interweb or save an image on a hosting site (e.g. photobucket) and link to that. Remember that images bigger than about 800x600 will not be easy to view on posts so please try to resize them (ditto, file size as there are still members on dial up and a 2 MB photo can take ages to download). Unfortunately there is no way in this version of PHPBB to link a picture from the gallery into a thread. Why?? who knows!!!

Lots of good ideas as I said. As DR F rightly says no-one owns the site (well actually Someone owns it - member #1) so let's try to please as many as we can and not piss people off too much.

That said I like the forum just the way it is and hope nobody takes offense at others' posts. If they do then we as moderators are here to delete offensive material (porn, personal attacks, racism, etc), but we try to do so without infringing members' freedom of speech.

Have a great Xmas and New Year everyone.

Rick

My feelings on this forum are somewhat bi-polar.

On the positive side, the owner reviews and feedback were instrumental in getting me to quit waiting on my HM-S and go for the SD instead, and for that I'm very grateful! The pics, technical resources, and helpfulness of everyone here is much appreciated as well.

On the negative side, I could do w/o ever reading about the fastest color again, it's old and played out by now. I suspect at least one frequent poster (I won't mention names) is wasted &/or too busy trying to write like a brit - all too often I find myself scratching my head trying to interpret what he's trying to convey. Lastly, even though English is the primary language here, there are enough regional differences in vocabulary and humor that much of it can be lost if you're non-local to the poster's country.

Here's what I'd like to see:
- keep topics in the right forum, and hopefully limit the threadjacks
- create a user control panel so you can subcribe to and view only the forums you're interested in viewing - this way, you could have an RC8 forum, and I could hide it. (I'm all for KTM dirt bike discussions, though)
- create regional forums, which can be used for rides, get togethers, and for sale listings - I'm not likely to buy something from 1/2 way around the world.
- as mentioned, create stickies or an FAQ for the most popular topics, i.e. cannister removal, PC III maps, etc. Maybe even a library of how to articles (idea stolen from caymanclub.net)

Thanks for asking, this is my $.02. I'll still be around either way, but the forums would be more usable to me w/ the above enhancements.
