Well to be honest Rob. It was me making that wrong road choice. Yet again!!!

The road I was on The B6355 is a road that you shouldn't be taking a sportbike along. It is more for an enduro bike and not a hard setup SDR
The 3 pics are from the very place I lost it.

First pic shows a Blind approach. (I was kindof aware of this)

Second shows the bends fairly steep downhill. I'm over to the left to see if its clear to cut. Bikes ends up on the worn away gravel 3rd pic. I end up going straight on and heading for a ditch. It's Unfortuanately to much front and down for me.
It is common practice for councils up here, just to throw loads of gravel down on backroads during the summer, in the forelorn hope that the tar melting in the Summer Heat?? and car tyres will force the gravel into the tar. This ridiculous method causes mounds of gravel in the centre of lanes, which us on bikes have to be aware of. Also through constantly laying the gravel The Gravel Tar mix becomes unstable and with less bonding material the road then becomes a Hit or miss dirt track.

BTW. That's the bit I came off at was a lot better than some of the bits I managed to get through. More fool me? Should've stuck to the fast 'A' road.
