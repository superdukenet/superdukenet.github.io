When I lived in London it was in a big old factory building and there were 20 units. We sacked the management company, (because they were ripping us off and not using the service charge cash they received in a good way), and took on the job ourselves. It was necessary to have a sort of management committee to run things and a few people stepped up to the job in roles that suited them and a few others just because they were keen to help build a great team to do things better.

For about 5 glorious years everything was pretty good, the feeling of what we'd done and achieved kept us going and things all around improved, we had more cash to spend on the building, all the occupants felt that they were getting value for money and any disputes were handled in a fair and democratic way. There's always some things that cause friction and there were some politics with personalities and egos but generally things were fine.

As the years went by some of the key people sold and moved on and others were brought in to replace them. The dynamics changed at times, sometimes for the worse, but there was always enough of the old team and memories of what it was all done for and it all hung together. It was really up to the new recruits to learn the way of the management committee and to be inspired to continue in the same spirit.

And also of course, more importantly, for all the rest of the occupants to help support the team in their efforts to run the show. Because after all these were the people who were spending their own time and effort in making it happen for the whole and the people who were not doing anything personally - except paying their service charge - should have been always appreciative of the effort it took. But this was not always so and being part of the management team was often a thankless task.

One of my friends who was on the original management team with me came to stay the other night. We pondered on the past and talked about what we had heard of the building and what was happening there. One of the key things is that the management committee had broken down in a vitriolic war of power, greed, selfish thinking and actions. It had got so bad that the whole building had more or less decided to revert to hiring an expensive management company to do the job because no one trusted or supported the way it was now being done. Despite being a bad option they thought it better than a warring, bickering self-govenment.

Lets hope we never see the same thing happen here. Superduke.net is probably one of the best run, best designed, best supported forums I've ever subscribed to on the internet. The moderators are all volunteers and deserve respect and all our support in doing what they do. I think one should only bitch and moan if they are willing to step up and offer to do the job better themselves.
