Least destructive KTM PP LED blinker install on '06 SD Pics

bruders8146

2015-05-07 10:29:00 UTC
Hello,

So when I bought the SD used, it came with RR peg sliders and KTM Power Parts LED turn signals that were not installed.

It turns out the rear peg pins are knurled and pushed in.
I was able to get them out and install the sliders using a clamp and socket.

The LED Turns were another story, no easy fix.
I do not want to cut the Factory harness.

The Problems:

1. The LED turns have JST micro connectors.
2. The accessory power feed on the '06 is under the seat not in the mask.
3. The rear turns are hard wired and combine in a Molex 4 Pin connector under the seat.
4. The front turns are hard wired, and terminate in a Molex 12 Pin, under the speedo.

My Options:

1. Rear Turns, Make a jumper in the rear with 4-Pin Molex on one end and 2 JST connectors on the other end. (plug and play)
2. Power feed, Make a feed jumper wire that feeds from under the seat up into the mask ( Spade connectors that match the KTM harness) (plug and play)
3. Front turns (Opt 1) Make another Molex jumper for the front, pull the Molex pins from the 12pin and remove with old turns. Put jumper pins directly into 12 pin Molex and have 2 JST on the ends. (Rearrange, plug and play)
4. Front turns (Opt 2) Cut the factory harness and install JST waterproof connectors on the factory harness. (plug and play) However I will add JST connectors to the old turns, thereby making them plug and play should I decide to remove the LED turns.

Additional comment: I chose option 2 on the front turns, despite my comment not wanting to cut the factory harness, here is why.
The wires coming out of the front turns splice into the main harness and go up to the speedo and down to the 12 pin main connector, probably part of the indicator lamp system or the turn signal switch itself. As a result option 1 would have required splicing as well as swapping out pins.
The next owner probably would have come after me with a lynch mob.
I say next owner because I always approach my mods and customization so that I can restore the bike to factory when I move on, I have yet to move on, I seem to collect instead.

The execution:

First I needed an assortment of connectors and tools and supplies.

Being that I have just moved to France and did not bring much in the way of tools or supplies, I looked online.
I found cycleterminal.com in the USA, he had the Molex, 6.3mm Spade and JST connectors and pins.
He also has an assortment of crimp tools.

Upon discussion with Joe, he offered to help reduce my cost,(buying 3 different crimp tools would be needed) by assembling the Jumper harness and Molex Jumper.
I agreed to have the Molex to JST and 6.3mm Spade to 6.3mm double barrel jumper made.
At the last minute I had him add another 6.3mm Double Barrel on the spade end of the jumper, so that after using the factory Acc. 1 connector, I would still have an Acc. 1 under the seat.
This now meant that I only needed one crimp tool and the JST male and female connectors for the front turns (opt 2) cut and install waterproof JST Micro connectors.

I am tracking the package and will update with pictures and How to instruction when I do the install.
