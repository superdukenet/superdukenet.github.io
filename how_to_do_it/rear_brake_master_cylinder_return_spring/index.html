Rear Brake Master Cylinder Return Spring

KronosMotorsports

2013-01-15 08:19:00 UTC
Not sure what you guys are running with your rearsets but here's what I came up with using Fasst Co's brake spring kit as a starting point...



Fasst Co. KTM rear brake spring #BC-1RS-6K



Start by loosening the 10mm jam nut on the brake rod, removing the 8mm mounting bolt on the back of the brake pedal, and removing the rod end completely (note location for reassembly). Pull off the stock master cylinder dust boot and replace with a slightly shortened "V-brake" mountain bike dust boot (available at any local bicycle shop).



Next you need to make a spring locator between the spring and the master cylinder body. A modified 5/8" plastic heater hose connector from PepBoys Auto Parts #47094 did the job.



You need to trim the tube a little longer on the spring side of the flange, and a little shorter on the master cylinder side.



Insert the longer side of the tube into the small diameter of the spring.



Slide the assembly over the brake rod and make sure the flange of the spring locator sits flush against the body of the master cylinder. If not, remove and shorten accordingly. Now is a good time to put a little anti-seize on the threads of the brake rod. Thread the spring bucket completely onto the brake rod till it bottoms out on the threads. Thread the rod end back onto the brake rod to the same location as it was (do NOT re-use the stock jam nut). Replace the 8mm bolt on the brake pedal with some blue Loctite.



Now thread the spring bucket back against the rod end and snug using a 19mm and 11mm wrench. Check function and location of brake pedal prior to riding.

Let me know if anyone has any questions but pretty simple mod and works great!

Chris
