Single exhaust superduke??

icarve

2009-05-30 21:30:00 UTC
I'm looking for a better sound some weight loss and hopefully a little more HP from my 06 SD.
In a conversation with a custom exhaust builder he casually said that the best setup would be a single cannister exhaust. This got me thinking, and since I live in New Zealand where everything costs 3 times as much as the rest of the world, I thought that maybe there would be a savings here too. So I have got myself a new, single Two Brothers Racing M2 carbon fibre can (a slip on for a Suzi gsxr 1000) figure this can should be designed to cope with as much horesepower as any bike out there.
I am having a custom stainless connector pipe fabricated, and will have the single muffler exiting on the right hand side. I plan to do before and after dyno runs to make sure it works and also hope to see if I can get the dealer put in the Akro mapping for me.
Would like to find out if anyone has done or heard anything on this kind of setup?

2011-09-25 10:59:00 UTC
A lot of 990 Adventure riders have 2 into 1 to make room for a tool tube or aux fuel tank. I purchased my set up from HT Racing and they supply the y pipe and a FMF slip on for a Yamaha R6. It usually results in improved mid range power with no improvement to top end. I had a 04 Tuono with 2 into 2 exhaust and a factory 2 into 1 set up with a Akro slip on. When I sold the bike I parted out the 2 into 2 set up and reinstalled the factory with 2 into 1 and was surprised at the improved mid range power.
