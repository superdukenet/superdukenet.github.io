Seat Wont Fit?....Errrr Tank Problem Solved...

DukeKook

2011-09-17 15:30:00 UTC
Hello all,


I searched the forum and did not find anyone having a problem getting the seat to fit the bike. (2008 base US Super Duke) so I thought I would reach out to this great community and ask for HELP!

This is my 4th Super Duke so I am not a total newb, in fact I think my issue is self inflicted. I just bought a super clean 2008 and had some goodies sitting around the shop so yesterday we gave her a makeover. One of the items we installed was a set of MH tank brackets, despite the brackets and the bike having only 900 miles (as new condition) the tank was a major PIA to get the front bolt in, more so than most I would say. We did finaly get it in but now it appears the tank is sitting so far back on the brackets that the seat cant slide forward enough to get the pin behind the lock down in the sub. Any one had this issue? Solution without going through the tank dance again? I have thought about removing the pin from the seat or making the opening in the sub a little larger. I think both of these get the seat to latch however i know the J hooks are not sliding forward and locking into place so the thought of it not being totally locked in has me a bit nervous... I suspect the solution is to get the tank further forward on the MH brackets and wrestle the tank (again) but before I did so want some expert advice.


It's funny we did a significant amount of work/upgrades to the bike yesterday and about half way through I pointed out it was all going to well and certainly we would run into something that would slow us down.... Thought the tank was it but no instead the final and last thing (before test ride) turned out to be the deal breaker! Cant fit the fooking seat!!@!@ All day under the knife and the seat prevents a ride sad sad day....

Ok all paitence is not my strong suit, especially when I have a tricked out Super Duke that just needs a seat and I can go for a little spin! After creating this post I sat for a moment and realised I didnt have a seat problem... I had a tank problem! Drilling subframe or molesting the seat pan seemed pretty silly even though I have spares... I just bit the bullet and with a big THANK YOU to Collyer and KTM666 it was really not that bad. I cut the bushings like KTM666 showed in the tank bolt thread, and used Collyers method of saving the prying for the pivot pins.. So 86 the MH brackets and 30 minutes later the results.....
