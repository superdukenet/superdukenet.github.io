My take on this situation is that the SD is pretty bulletproof. If it's the right price you just have to take a punt on the previous three owners and and give it a really good service and draw a line under it's past. If there are no obvious signs of neglect or misuse then it's probably OK. I think that we are in an age where we are all paranoid about service history and how a bike is maintained. If you think about it you could buy a bike that has had one owner who was a prick and didn't have any mechanical sympathy and damaged his bike during the first year through neglect and ignorance. Or you could find a bike that has had three really caring owners who, for each of their terms as owner, has cherished their investment and done the right thing every time. And you must remember that "service history" even done by an authorised dealer is not really worth the paper it's written on. Some dealers and their mechanics do not know what they are doing. Seriously. It all comes down to how the bike is.....and that's down to you to check it out before buying it.

In my youth I bought bikes that had over 15 previous owners. And they were fantastic bikes that I loved to own and add to their care and longevity.
