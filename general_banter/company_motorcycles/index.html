Hi Mate,

Rates will differ to cars but principle is the same.

Usually the company pays a deposit (typically between three and six months lease charges up front) and then a monthly charge at an agreed rate. There is an agreed annual mileage allowance and a pence per mile charge should you go over that level which you pay when the agreement terminates.

There will be a charge to your income tax based on the assessed benefit. The company can claim 50% of the VAT on the lease payments back. (or at least they can for car leases)

My last leased car was worth about 35K and cost the company about £250 + VAT. I had a two year lease period and returned the car at the end of that.

There are insurers out there happy to insure leased cars, presumably the same is the case for bikes. Google is probably your friend. My mate had a company bike for years so you are not alone.

Hope that helps
