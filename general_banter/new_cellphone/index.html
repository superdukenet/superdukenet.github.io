I love my iPhone. I didn't think I'd get on with it as well as I do. It doesn't come with MMS, but O2 in UK has got a website where you can see MMS' sent to you. You can download an app that allows you to, at least, send MMS. However, I don't miss MMS as I can as well send emails with pictures. The data is free (read; included).
I was also afraid of the paltry camera, but despite the low megapixels, the pictures come out better than I thought. Another thing I was worried about was the lack of physcial buttons, but the on-screen keyboard is very clever and is designed to correct your mis-taps. Works fairly well.
If you push the technology to its max it'll drain the power of it, but I've had smartphones that have emptied batteries quicker.
Naturally the iPod part is as good as any other iPod if not better due to the screen.
I don't like using iTunes to sync it, but I can live with it.
The web browser is absolutely divine, the user interface works really well (unlike anything else on the market).

It's not perfect, but what it does, it does really well. Worst part is that you're going to have to live with being an iPhone owner.
