Searching for Pythagoras

DribbleDuke

2012-07-18 21:46:00 UTC
I just got back from five days of riding-camping. I played on the, getting to be familiar, roads in Kern and Tulare counties. I crossed over Sherman Pass twice, as it was sooo good the first time I had to do it again.
As long as I was in the six thousand foot and better elevations the weather was perfect.
I ended up in a campground totally deserted. Fifty eight million people in this state and I get an absolutly empty and fine BLM campsite.
I laid on a picnic table ans stared up into a gorgeous night sky. No city lights to counteract the heavonly glow.
I found myself looking for three star configurations (triangles) that would as close as possible represent the Pythagorian theorem. I would look for equalatteral and Isosceles also.
Mostly right though. The closer I could get it to the three four five ratio the better.
Hiway 155 from Issabella to Glennville is never to be missed if I am near.
A carpenter lives with Pythagoras on a daily bases and it is nice to see him shining down from the heaven above in such dominance.
The SD is a really good motorcycle.
