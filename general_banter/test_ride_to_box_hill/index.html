bic_bicknell

2013-08-18 19:42:00 UTC
I think Chelsea is still a thing still but it is full of London posers on Harleys and Ducatis that they don't really ride - just cruise about on wearing cool gear.

There were almost as many police riders at Box Hill today as bikers. And yellow hi-vis safety jackets. (bikers and police) And so many patrol cars cruising all the approach roads. Don't think there is any room for hooliganism anymore. Also the average age of bikers there was about 97. I felt young. Everyone was drinking cocoa and I was the only one smoking.

Since moving down to West Sussex and Surrey I can't say I'm impressed with the roads or the biking atmosphere. Never thought I'd say it but I really miss my old biking roads up in Essex where I used to go hooning with Klinky and Shade a few years ago.

When I got home and posted on Facebook I saw there were a load of old mates up in Lancashire posting about blasts round the Trough of Bowland in Lancashire, up to Devils Bridge and over to Settle on the Yorkshire border. I was well jealous.

Chelsea Bridge meets ended in 2009 when Westminster and Wandsworth Councils installed CCTV and 24hr parking restrictions. The councils then changed the T Huts lease so he couldn't open evenings. You'll occasionally find a couple of bikes there, anymore and the police move in spouting section 59 (vehicles used in a manner causing alarm, distress or annoyance).

The Ace Cafe is going a similar way, with the local council closing nearby roads to motorcycles due to the weekend mayhem.
