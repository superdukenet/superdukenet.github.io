It doesn't matter how much the governments try to claim that they are only there for safety, we all know its for revenue.

I'm all for putting them up on dangerous roads/dangerous sections of roads, but when the are put up on a a nice straight, wide section of road a couple of miles away from an accident black spot, that's when they are blatantly a revenue raiser.

There's a road near me called the A9, which is the main trunk road from Inverness to Perth. It's mostly single carriageway, about 120 mile long and is Scotlands most dangerous road. Rather than duel carriageway it more than it is, to stop car drivers getting frustrated behind slow moving lorries (this road goes through the Scottish Cairngorm mountains) and doing stupid overtakes, the government have decided, in the "eyes of safety", to install average speed cameras the whole distance. So rather than getting stuck for a bit then overtaking when safe to do so, you're now going to be stuck behind a lorry doing 45mph for miles on end.
