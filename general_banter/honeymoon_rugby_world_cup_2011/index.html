I've been to Tahiti, but not New Zealand yet. If you're still looking for a hotel/resort Les Tipaniers on Moorea was a great deal. Excellent restaurant, rooms on the beach at reasonable rates. It's not Bora Bora glamorous, but I was there for diving and surfing so most of my time was spent in the ocean. Plus, from Moorea it's a quick express boat back to the main island of Tahiti for night life, museum, ancient monuments and stuff. The resort has access to all beach facilities like diving/snorkeling, kite/wind surfing, canoeing, but for normal surf you need to have guide take you on a boat out to the outer reefs. The main island of Tahiti has some beach breaks that are fun. The diving/snorkeling is much better than Hawaii and Mexico. You don't even need tanks to see live reef. I remember one dude asked me how far away are the fish, I was like dude "they are right there, just swim 10 feet from the beach and you run into live reef". The water is ridiculously warm too, and you must wear sunscreen even if you tan well since the sun is so close. You can buy black pearls super cheap too, just don't buy from the first dealer you meet. There are so many pearl farmers that you can get super good deals by asking around especially the natives (non-french looking).
