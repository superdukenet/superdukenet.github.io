Recent fall from SD on the track has me wanting to push speed on a dedicated track bike, so I picked this up a few days ago!


Pros:
2002 sv650, gsxr front end, slipper clutch, quickshift, light engine work. Was crashed with dent on R side of tank, some fiberglass rash on tail section but seems to all work fine! Tyres are ancient so will be swapping for fresh rubber before upcoming track day. Price was a (non-literal) steal. Really excited to ride it, actually

Cons:
Owner must've thought (or didn't care) to register the bike with DMV since 2010!! Claimed it was reg'd as non-op but when I took it in to transfer title today, what was to be $150...I got SLAMMED with $700 of back-fees and penalties . The guy did offer to pay up some but not all... but I'm rather bitter he declined to pay it all...guess i'm lucky he bothered to reply instead of telling me to f* off!?

He wants me to ask about getting the bike "off the grid" from DMV making it a gray-market vehicle....I don't think it's wise nor even possible. Have you guys ever heard of this?

Anyway, bitter sweet purchase, not sure how I could have prevented it before handing over the cash, insight welcome, but hoping this will last me a while

Just got off phone with DMV. Same answer but more detailed: cannot transfer ownership without registering vehicle. Registering vehicle means I must pay up all owed fees associated with vehicle. Declaring plan non-op status is only possible during renewal period (in this case near April) so I am forced to register it FOR THE ROAD (ridiculous). Affadavit of non-use form exists, but merely lets you waive the need for insurance coverage for a registered vehicle, not circumvent the need to register in the first place.

If I don't register the vehicle, I can't obtain the title transfer...end of story apparently...makes me wonder how folks find, buy, restore, and use that ole '56 Chevy without paying $1M in a half century+ of late fees
