I have owned over 20 Somthing bikes, Now in 2008 I purchased a brand new Triumph Speed Triple this was my first non Jap bike I thought I have heard good things about Triumph so why not get one.
That bike was a piece of crap! I hated it! Also the dealer seemed like he didn't give a rats turd about the problems what got me is you don't know how good or how bad a bike is buy reviews in magazines or talking to other owners of the same bike if you do ask about problems they will just say This bike is great just as good or better than a Jap bike. Then you purchase the crap and find out on a Forum that most dudes are having the same problems as you are and it's not the great none Jap bike you thought you were getting
So I sold in with in 3 months and got a 2006 Gsxr 1000.
I had that bike for about 12 months but I was getting bored
With it and you can't use the thing on public roads without speeding so I started looking around for Somthing different again I also swore I would never buy a non Jap bike again. I was thinking of getting a MT01 but then I saw the Super Duke 2009 model black.
Now before the purchase I got on this forum and did some home work I read about the problems and how to rectify the problems. I thought ok this thing can't be as bad as the SPEED TRIPLE and went ahead with the purchase.
I have now owned the SD for 2 years the only problems I have had was the dash started to mist up in the first month of ownership this was replaced with a 2010 dash and the battery went within 18 months.
The best thing for me was all the info on this forum I thought also instead of waiting for the problems to raise their ugly head on the SD I went ahead and did the preventative matainence.
Flush the radiator and cooling system grease BGC and other connectors ETC....
The main thing for me to fix was the on off throttle my bike was not that bad but the best fix is to just buy a PCV with auto tune or PCV and get dyno tuned. My bike is smooth as and as good as any jap bike even with the throttle sped up.
So to me the SD is a great bike light years ahead of the old 2008 Speed Triple.
The SD has given me more faith in euro bikes than I had in 2008 and I just can't see myself going back to a jap hell I even think the Moto Guzzi V7 is a great bike

Im only new here but I have been on forums for the last 18 months since I got my bike license. Some are more active than others, and obviously the state based or model based are quieter than others due to the smaller target market. Don't know what this place was like before but Ive taken a lot of good info from here and appreciate the members contributions.

And on the topic of the "can't use the power of a thou" on the road argument. I used to buy into it too but I’m now in the "who cares camp". I mean you can’t use the power of a Ferrari, Porsche, M3 or the new Golf R for that matter but they still sell. Why, because they are just beautiful machines, a credit to the human civilisations excellence in engineering. I rode a mate’s new ZX10R and another’s R1 the other day and loved them both. Sure you have to be a little more diligent with the throttle but they were simply awesome. Stick them in a lower gear and hang on, but you all know that. Here in Brisbane with the ridiculously low posted speeds I can be breaking the law in first gear and in license losing territory in second on my SD. I understand the torque/acceleration is in the lower range on a twin but I love winding out the SD’s throttle to hear that growl from the Yoshis and that puts me at risk all the time! I used to do the same on my Daytona and sure I’d do the same on a thou, I just like going fast

I love my Superduke and get heaps of comments. It’s a tuff ass beast and makes me ride like an idiot, but shit damn do I have fun doing it. I do however miss my Daytona dearly. And after riding the two thou's last week I’ve decided I’m going to do everything I can to get another Daytona as well as the Duke. Shit if I had the money I'd have one of everything, why, because there is something special about all of them and they can offer the rider a unique experience/buzz no matter what the engine capacity/style. I figure life’s too short to only own 1 bike at a time.

Anyway that’s my two cents. Hope it doesn’t sound like a rant cause its not. In fact I’m not sure where that all came from, my fingers just kept moving. I guess I’m just expressing my passion (read obsession) with all things motorbikes. Twins, Triples, and Fours, I love them all
