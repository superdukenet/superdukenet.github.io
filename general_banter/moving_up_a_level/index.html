Woah there guys. I think we are looking at the wood and bumping into the trees somewhat.

Thousands of racers are as fit as Rossi. Fitness will give you an edge but 80% of riding is psychological.

We can all ride a bike, we all know how to change gears, get around corners, brake etc. None of us can be Rossi but we could all be as quick as the guy 2 secs a lap faster than us, the only difference is mental,

Think back to your first time on track, all of that was hard to do because you were so tense. Then after a couple of sessions you began to relax and things started to get a bit smoother.

We have all had rides where everything seemed to flow. You drifted into the first corner and it went well, which set you up for the second corner and so on. These always turn out to be our fastest laps.

Equally we have all gone out and nailed it up to the first corner, hit the limiter, braked til our eyeball exploded, chucked the bike in, survived and passed 6 people on the exit, nailed it to the next corner repeat to finish. Then we pull in and punch the guy that has timed us 4 secs slower than our earlier laps.

Same on the road. Smooth = fast. If you are riding well it will all come together. If you arent then no amount of fitness or effort will help you. If it did Rueben Xaus would be world champion.

Like you say you have reached the next level. Like when you first got a knee down. You couldnt do it and your mind built it up to be a big thing. Then you did it almost by accident and it scared you a bit. Then it happened a couple more times and you got used to it. Now, you barely notice as your mind is elsewhere.

Freestyler you should stay off that Redbull shit. I have driven all night to trackdays and done them on caffeine and Red bull. I just end up feeling wired. When I was a kid we used to do a line of coke then race around a road circuit at night. I knew of a lot of good riders then and several of them are dead now. None died when we were doing this but you get used to pushing on on the roads and end up riding like it all the time.

Now I drink a couple of litres of water on a trackday. Unless Ducati Pete is there then we drink 40 cups of tea
