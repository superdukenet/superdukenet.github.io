You won't be touching down the clutch cover unless on some ridiculous off camber that would be atypical for a public thoroughfare (even in CA), or crashing (and no, Motovation's lower sliders won't protect the clutch cover either).

On un-modified suspension (e.g. stock springs and valving) - and I'm a helluva lot heavier than you are - at the track, could grind up knee pucks all day (OE pegs and lower cowl as well) , but the clutch cover never got close.

Touching down with OE pegs is an SD norm. Rearsets, adjustable OE rearset risers, shorter pegs, etc. can all aid in alleviating this - as on any bike.

There are aftermarket clutch covers for the SD with replaceable (sacrificial) bits, but given the cost of these covers compared to the OE replacement (= to the cost of a few of them), difficult for me to justify.

Sure, you could raise the bike via the suspension, but I'm guessing you're aware that will affect other things. All of which gets expensive and complicated in a hurry.

Search - try it.
