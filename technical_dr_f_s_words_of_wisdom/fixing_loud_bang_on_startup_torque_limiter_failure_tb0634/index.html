Fixing Loud Bang on startup - Torque Limiter Failure TB0634

Gregz

2008-03-30 01:35:00 UTC
If your 2005 or 2006 model Superduke is Banging or slapping loudly when you start it - you are experiencing a known issue. Here are the details of what is going on and how to fix it:

In brief - this is a known problem with the starter motor's torque limiter failing. It has been identified as a warranty issue, but has only been replaced if the customers bike fails and is returned for warranty. So far not all starter torque limiters have failed, but these will most probably fail eventaully due to the design fault.

Affected Models:
2005 - 2006 LC8 Models 950 Adventurer 950 Adventurer-S 950 SuperMoto 990 Superduke

What happens:
As the LC8 engine does not run auto-decompression, the starter motor must push the engine past TDC compression on both pistons; and may have trouble and stall. This is just like moving your bike off from tandstill; which we do by slipping the clutch until the rev's match, if you just release the clutch it will stall.
The starting mechanism is designed in the same way - it is fitted with a clutch mechanism on the starter. As the engine reaches TDC the clutch slips slightly until the compression is released and the starter motor can continue spinning without stalling.

When the torque limiter fails, it does not allow the slip and stops with a loud clacking noise when starter button is pushed. When the starter "kicks-back" during starting under certain conditions, it is possible to either damage the starter motor or break the wheels of the starter gearing; or worse - both.

The Fix:
The original starter torque limiter has been known to failue due to poor design and KTM replaced ith with a new design for 2007 models. The fix is significant and requires the change of various other parts including this torque gear - so you cannot just change the part just by itself. Lucky enough, KTM supply the complete kit to do the job worth approx US$210.00


Replace the original torque limiter part #'s
60040016000 Supreceeded - % TORQUE LIMITER 10/49-T CPL
60040022119 IDLER GEAR 19-T 03
60030002044 IGNITION COVER CPL. 06

The replacement kit part #'s
60040016044 MODIFICATION KIT TORQUE LIMIT

Individual parts:
60040017000 TORQUE LIMITER
60040022119 IDLER GEAR 1 19Z
60030002144 IGNITION COVER CPL. 06

How do you know if this has been fixed:
You will have a larger torque gear fitted and thus the Ignition cover will be larger at the top to fit the wider torque limiter.
You will also have a 19 tooth idler gear. The replacement torque limiter will look like this:



The Tech buletin:
Quotation from Techincal bulletin # TB 0634 - Torque Limiter Conversion Kit

On some of the current torque limiters, repeated activiation lowers the pre-tension of the spring washer. Thus the limiter can become activated at a lower torque than was designed. This can be heard through a very loud banging noise coming from inside the engine.

If the loud banging noise appears permanently while starting the engine without applying any throttle exchange the existing torque limiter (60040016000) to the new friction based torque limiter (60040017000)

The new friction based torque limiter requires more space when mounted, therefore a new ignition cover (60030002044) and the idler gear 19-T03 (60040022119) must also be replaced. All parts are supplied in a special conversion kit (60040016044).
