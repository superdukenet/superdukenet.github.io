SD Cards - Buying, Checking and spotting Fakes!

Schnauzer

2011-03-07 19:49:00 UTC

With more equipment and more of us using camera's such as GoPro's there's an increasing number of people being ripped off when buying SD Cards from online courses including eBay and others. When you consider that a 32mb Class 10 cards sell for over Â£30/$50, its easy to see that motivates the crooks.

Here's some of the things to watch out for:
- Fakes - cheap cards with expansive brand labels
- False capacity - card which have been altered to over report storage capacity (easy to do)
- False Class - Class 2 cards claiming to be Class 10 (which you should have when recording 1080p 30fps video)

The Fakes ads are relatively easy to spot, they come without packaging, there are no serial numbers on the cards (all quality cards have serial numbers, its a basic quality control mechanism), cheaply printed labels which are ill fitting and use cheap paper (quality cards are tending to use metallic base labels). Typical fake ads feature pics like:


Spoting cards which have been altered to over report is slightly more involved, you need to install a free (Windows) program such as: H2testw () or CrystalDiskMark ().

False Class cards:
I recently purchased such a fake Class card, a 32GB Transend Class 10 card from eBay, however I always test the cards on receipt using a simple card reader. This is the result of the CrystalDiskMark test on the card:

Ignore everything except the Seq test, (the others test small files which never happen with video), the Read rate is 7MB/s and Write is 3 MB/s. This is a Class 2 card, not a Class 10 as claimed. The write is the most important to us as HD video is large and needs to be written quickly. In order for this card to be a Class 10 it should be reading and writing WELL in excess of 10MB/s. The same test below on a genuine card shows a Read rate of 19 MB/s and a Write rate of 18MB/s, a genuine Class 10 card.


When I contacted the seller they put up some BS argument about different readers, PCs, cables etc etc. Don't accept those arguments, SD is a hard specification, as is the speed Class classification. I have worked with semi-conductor manufacturers since the mid 80s, If a card does not perform then its not built to the specification (make sure you have a good reader of course). You will get a little variation of course, not 18 MB/s versus 3 MB/s.

Fake Capacity cards:
These are cards which report double their actual capacity, 32MB instead of 16MB. Its a very trivial task to modify a card to do this, very. For a fraudster, who's already selling false Class cards its a no brianer, split the casing, do a few mods & your done. To check for these cards use H2testw, it simply fills the card with data up to the reported capacity, if it cannot do that the card has been modified to report a Fake Capacity. Both windows are displayed during testing, ignore the Warning on the first line in the lower window, the report also displays the Read speed and the Write speed. The second line reports that the card successfully wrote 15271 MByte (15.2 MB/s) to the card.

A fake card would report the test as unsuccessful and that it could NOT write xxxx MByte to the card. I don't have a fake Capacity card therefore cannot show a screen shot. Ebay have a good section on spotting fake cards, and there are many other tools out there to test cards with. Here's the eBay
