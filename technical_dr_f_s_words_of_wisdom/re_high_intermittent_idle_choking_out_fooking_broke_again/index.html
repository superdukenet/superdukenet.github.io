The erratic idle shooting up and down is defo the tps sensor, this doesn't mean it's faulty as it needs to be spot on to work.
I get you to check a few other things: What is the idle set at in tuneecu?

Are the throttle bodies been synchronised?

No. 47

2013-01-29 23:07:00 UTC
Between the throttle body and cylinder intake shafts - if they're poorly seated and/or split, tickover, A/F, etc are completely thrown out.

500 miles ain't that many and I've had a fair few fuelling problems that appeared to be solved for many more miles, but weren't.

Assuming that all that's been done has been done correctly and that all the replacement parts are good, and not faulty which I've also experienced, I would go through all the basics again eg even if the battery/earth connections are good, are the cables themselves good and not broken 'internally'', and check TPS, steeper motors, sensors etc as per the workshop manual.

You really need to hook up to TuneECU even if you have no error codes on the speedo - for example, I've found a faulty coil, despite no error codes or visible problems, by checking O2 sensor voltages, ie exhaust gas profiles for each cylinder - happened twice, once only showing up when hot and the other only at tickover.

ECU could be faulty but can't recall anyone on here having had a proven problem with this.

When I bought my 04 KTM 950 Adventure it had a miss. It would fall on it face, then pick up. It did not rev up like I think you are telling us. The 950 is a carb bike so it might act different than your 990 FI motor. The fix for me was to clean the contact pads inside the key switch. It is not a servicable item, but I opened my up, cleaned it, and relubed it with delectric greese. Caution if you try this. It's not for everyone. Springs fly out..small parts find the darkest places in your shop to hide. Bumb your buddies for theres if you can for a test.

This key switch is a long shot quess. but it sounds like you are running out of options. Our SD are getting old e-nuf to have this stuff happen to them. My 08 SD key switch looks a lot like my 04 Adventure. It was a free fix to me, except for my time.

Next time it happens, try turning the key back and forth to see is it makes any difference.

Good luck.
