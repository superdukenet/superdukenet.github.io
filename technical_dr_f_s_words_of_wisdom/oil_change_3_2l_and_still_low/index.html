Guys some advice please, just done an oil change as recommended 2.5l then run it up and check, i've now put in 3.2l when warm the level is to the max mark when on the sidestand but drops below min when level, does this sound right? I also noticed that the level fluctuates a little with revs when warming it up.

You'll find just adding 100ml or so will make quite a difference at this point. Mine always takes about 3.2/4 to get it perfect. I always warm the bike up before dropping the oil and leave it around an hour with sump plug and filter out to try and drain as much oil as possible out. Remember it's a dry sump so slightly under or overfilled won't do the bike any harm.

You should be fine, checking oil can be weird, especially on bikes. Remember the details of the oil level sightings (temp, running, side stand, etc.). As long as these are consistent you're fine. What you need to be concerned with is when the oil level begins to rise, at which point you also need to be aware of your coolant level. Make it a habit of checking coolant IN THE RADIATOR regularly along with oil level. Don't rely on the coolant overflow tank.

As stated above, overfilling a dry sump is fine, overfilling a wet sump is bad. The crank can splash into the oil if it's too high causing it to froth up and introduce air.

My rule of thumb for oil and coolant, cold on side stand, oil in sight tube. 3 quarts every time, no more, no less (unless I spill it when filling or forget to put the plug in (never happened yet...)). Coolant level on side stand is always near the neck but never below the fins of the radiator. When I see fins it means water pump seal...
