Cheers Graham,

I see. We each modify for a purpose, and that is more important to understand the limitations of your particular use. If you are running the SD as a track bike - then I understand the nature of your modifications and in most respects the rule of thumb you can use is "the bigger the better".

No I have no relation to Tuneboy. More so, I have attempted to contact Wayne in Aus via quite a few emails and he has not responded.

As a side not - have you riden the SD after removing the ECU butterflies? I am quite interested in how it effects to delivery. This has been done of the Kawasaki 1400 engines with success.

I have 2 thoughts on this mod:
1) is that the ECU might use its butterfly to control a safe AR. As most SD's run rich; and your's now runs lean - this hypothesis may be correct.
2) is that your engine will fuel unexpectantly at throttle release. This is due to the anti-hop function programmed into the ECU (to mimic a slipper clutch). You may find you need to turn off the anti-hop or re-tune fueling for off throttle; in this case you will need the Tuneboy.

Regarding intake, personally I would bring the engine to final tune and find the worst dip in the power curve. Then I would make my calculations on total intake runner length (btw - this is really easy on the SD as it has straight intakes to the valves), and fit appropriate length runner extentions tuned for that dip. Then fit some Unifilter's on the ends like these:


This will bolster the dip at that particular rpm range and retune on the dyno. In your particular case, you may choose the secondary dip at the high rpm range for racing.

So the process would be something like this:
- remove AI, ECU butterfiles, airbox (as you have done)
- fit large exhaust and headers (custom make a stepped header with uneven length runners)
- head work and polish
- higher compression ratio
- lighten crack / clutch / balance
- piston or stroker kit if necessary
- larger valves if necessary
- fit Tuneboy or PC or Rapid module
- dyno tune
- then tune intake length with runners

Failing this, if you still do not see the max HP and have a dip in the top end and you have the shortest intake runners... then you will need to fit larger throttle body set.

There is my view of setting up a racing twin (as opposed to a 4cyl).
