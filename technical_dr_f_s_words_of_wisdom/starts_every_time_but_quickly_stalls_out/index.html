Starts every time, but quickly stalls out.

990 WFO

2015-09-14 17:48:00 UTC
Hey all. I'm steadily getting my life back together (in a good way) and finally got around to getting my bike moved from NC to TN where I live now. The bike's plates are crazy expired but I'm working on fixing that. I would still ride it down the road a couple miles once or twice a week. Well one day I went out for a ride and everything was fine. Bike had its valves adjusted while in NC and a suspected small exhaust leak (popping on decel, no change it the tune it's been running for years) but aside from that everything normal. I park it in the garage and call it a day. The next morning I go out for another quick ride and the bike starts up...but the idle drops until it stalls out and dies after just a couple seconds. Weird. I start it and the bike fires back to life but again after just a couple seconds the idle droops down and stalls. Started it twice more and the same thing happens. Zero issues starting but won't hold idle. I even rev it to 3 or 4K just to see what happens, no dice. Idle still falls until it's off.

I suspect a fuel issue? After some thinking I realise the same tank of gas has been in the there since probably February. My dad started the bike and let it warm up while I was away, and he said he might have just added a gallon to it but yeah probably the same for over half a year. So I emptied it out and while I was at it I pulled the pump out. I can post some pics once I'm home, but the little bag on the side of the pump looks kinda dirty (don't know specifically what it does) and my fuel filter looks brown and in tact. Bad tank of gas? Clogged injector? Bad filter? I'm hesitant to drop $180 on a fuel pump rebuild kit and I hate dropping my bike off at the dealership without knowing the general problem, otherwise I feel like I'm just writing them a blank check.

Things just don't appear overnight for no reason!

You might be correct about the dodgy fuel. Just try that first.
The pump is normally easy, if you can't hear it when turning the key on there is an issue.

Also check the BGC, it controls a lot of things including idle. Some moisture might have seeped in during a coolish night.

Tilt sensor is another one as mentioned above, but then it would keep stalling after a few seconds regardless what you do.
