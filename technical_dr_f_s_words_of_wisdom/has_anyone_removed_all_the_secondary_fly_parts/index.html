MrZ32

2011-04-02 11:13:00 UTC
all done... will post any pics if you want.
obviously the tank has been lifted for ease of access and the coolant bottle moved out of the way.

then take all the mechanism off the rear cylinder only (this is the secondary throttle body gear), or in tune ECU talk, cyl 2 and disconnect the two plugs

to get the shafts out you have to lift the throttle bodies off the engine, take the e clip off the front cylinder (holds the shaft for the front cyl in on the 2ndry flies)

slide the shafts out, requires a bit of fidling (be sure not to let the springs shoot off (the two shafts are attached with the 'arm', this can be what you hold onto to pul them out evenly

once out put the throttle bodies back on.

now blanking plugs

parts:
2.5 mm internal, 7mm external fuel line
pliers
sharp knife
6gauge 1/2 inch tapered head (counter sink syle thing) 304SS screws

Method:
-get the fuel line (too big to fit normally), and cut at least half of it off down the length of it
-thread the thin cut end through the new holes until it sticks out the other end, use the pliers to then pull it through until you get to the complete full size line coming through (the cut bit is just to make it easy to thread through the hole)
-cut the inside and outside flush
-screw in the screw from the oustide (expands the rubber for tight fit, tapered head to expand outer side more so it can never be sucked into the engine.

done... now put it all back together, but while its all of check your oil and coolant levels

oh and forgot, important part if you dont want error codes, turn it off in tuneECU

MrZ32

2011-04-12 08:24:00 UTC
Washer sits on the outside and must have a bigger external diameter than the 6mm opening.

The washer is held in place with the screw.

The screw has two roles, to block the hole in the fuel line and to expand the external diameter of the fuel line... Acting like a dyna bolt in concrete but is air tight.

I didn't bother with the washer as the tapered head of the screw expands the line even more... Essentially impossible to come loose... As the fuel line was 7mm external diameter to begin with, then compressed into the hole, then game another mm diameter added with the screw (was measured before put in hole to see what effect the screw would have).

Essentially forcing the fuel line diameter to be compressed by 2mm... Remember area of a circle is pi*r^2 So a notable change in area occurs and hence force being exherted to the hole causing grip is great.

Thought I'd chime in on this subject as, due to my recent unemployment, I've spent a ton of time on the bike lately experiencing with different TuneECU maps and secondary fly positions.

First off here's my setup for reference:

-07 SD 990
-Stock airbox and filter
-No SAI, EVAP, or O2 sensors
-FMF Apex w/o baffles
-Stock Akro map with FL switchover lowered
-G2 Ergo throttle w/200 cam installed

I first tried the secondaries 100% open all the time. Bike bogs a tiny bit on the bottom end especially in tight stuff but EXCELLENT!!! throttle control, both on and off the power.

Next I tried the secondaries +10% from the stock settings to replicate roughly the oval ones. MUCH better low/mid torque with a slightly twitchy/jumpy on/off throttle (better than stock settings though). Top end power is about the same. Prob limited by the stock airbox at this point.

Third I tried the secondaries +15% from the stock settings. Slight reduction in low/mid torque and slight reduction in twitchy on/off throttle.

So at this point I have to say even though it was a little down on low/midrange power, I was actually "faster" on the bike with the 100% open settings. Due to it's confidence and ability to roll-on earlier, corner exit speed was higher and the bike more manageable at the edge of traction. My test rides consist of a mixture of tight canyon roads to roads with long high-speed (track style) turns.

Here's my question:

Has anyone played with the secondary opening % further? Or even a mixture of ovals combined with greater opening %? Or is it a waste of time and just rip those damn things out?
