SDR running like sh*t after refuelling

RRR

2011-09-06 14:51:00 UTC
Hopefully not too ‘wordy’, but trying to provide as much info as possible..........

Issue with bike which happened about 6 or & miles after refuelling, so seems a bit too coincidental not to be related to duff fuel (Shell V Power)?

Was riding bike and starting to accelerate out corner when it suddenly lost power, had to keep blipping the throttle with the clutch in an rolled it into a layby. Managed to keep it running albeit it was rough as anything as if it had drawn up a load of dirt, shit fuel or similar.

At the roadside, had it running again a few times but not smoothly (no misfires) and cut out again. Sometimes I could hold it at constant revs then it would dip dramatically or cut out even. It didn’t want to fire up again after 2 or 3 attempts, so rather than hammer the battery too much, gave up and vanned the bike home.

Afterwards I siphoned the tank and put a gallon of fresh fuel in, and it started first turn of the key, seemed to run smooth for a 5 mins then had a repeat of same issue. Had it running again today, started no probs and left it idling ok for about 15 mins. Once Fan kicked in ran ok for a few more mins, then started to skip a beat occasionally and run a bit rough when revved. I could rev it quite freely but not a smooth as normal and had the odd misfire/flame when shutting throttle. I’ve confirmed it’s firing on both cylinders by holding the downpipes when starting from cold – both very hot within a few seconds of starting

In the circumstances I was wondering if I should keep trying to run the bike until the ‘suspect’ fuel clears the filter and is used up or just go ahead take off the tank, remove the fuel filter plugs etc then retry. I gather from my Local KTM dealer there’s fuel filter kit for about £60 (not sure if this is a bit steep or a cheaper alternative can be had elsewhere?) Is it worth trying to clean the existing filter (assuming that’s feasable) and having another go or just replace it ?

Obviously I don’t want to spend £60 for nothing, but given the bike’s done just over 5k miles and will no doubt be on the original filter, maybe I should just replace it anyway. I’m aware this could be caused by a host of other issues, but in the circumstances fuelling seems like the first thing to eliminate.

Thanks for any help / advice
