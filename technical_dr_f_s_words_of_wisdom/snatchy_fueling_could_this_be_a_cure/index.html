Hi folks

I have been reading here for a while now thus decided to buy myself an 08 SDR, picked her up last Thurday - crazy machine!, seat is no problems for me, power is great, still running her in only 250 miles so far and they have all been very good I am still getting used to a V twin after an 07 Z1000 which was very smooth but not radical in any way, strange having to keep the revs higher than normal > 4000 otherwise engine feels a bit luggy

I have suffered the dreaded snatchy fueling and found it is very distracting thus fitted a G2 throttle cam (no free play etc) as many others have done, found it to mask the problem as opposed to cure it so I begun to research the net and what I found is written below.

I have tried it and found the bike has massively improved and now feels like a sharp revvy V twin, although I still have 3.5k to 4.5 k approx hunting, however I believe the reason for that is something different.

For me anyway I can say I NOW have smooth SDR except for the 3.5 to 4.5 k issue - so well happy.

Your thoughts on the info below would be much appreciated


Two bar warm-up

All of this talk about the snatchy throttle makes me wonder if you all are allowing the ECU to remap during cold starts. Do you allow your engine to warm to two bars before touching the throttle? Are you aware that the EFI is adjusted for barometric pressure and ambient temperature setting each time the engine is allowed to idle when cold?

Try it. You can hear the ECU remap during the idle phase. You will hear an occassional hiccup. This is the EFI adjusting to ambient conditions. I have not had the jerky throttle"ness" as many have described. At first I thought it might just be many having not ridden other modern EFI bikes, and the throttle response of EFI can take some getting used to. But, I'm beginning to wonder if the problem could be related to not allowing the ECU to do it's job.
