Just had my 07 standard SD checked on a G.M.D. Alignment/ geometry measuring device. I just wanted to see what the real numbers were.

Rake; 23.7 degrees
Trail: 97 MM
Swingarm angle: 7.9 degrees
Offset: 28MM

Measurements taken with Bridgstone BT016 tyres, (34psi F&R) rear sag at 35mm, front sag at 37mm, and the fork tubes flush with the top of the triple clamp.

These numbers suggest a very quick handling bike. ie easy turn in, a little nervous at high speed/ hard accelerating over bumpy tarmac.( All the reasons we love this bike!)

I guess that is why KTM went with a shallow swingarm angle (most fast track bikes run about a 12 degree angle)

FWIW the SDR has a 6mm longer shock and a slighty longer forks to get a very steep 23.0 degress of rake, mabey thats why the R comes standard with a steering damper?
Just my 02 cents
