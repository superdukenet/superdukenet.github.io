That additional $4K won't get you anything near what you could get in terms of performance from another bike if you sold the SD and added that $4K in to the purchase of said other bike.

What you're asking, is whether or not the addition of a set of wheels that cost more than 50% of what the bike is worth, will provide substantial gains in performance. No, no they won't. If this is just about cost-no-object bling, go for it - and someone please hand this man a Rizoma catalog.

BSTs are light, strong, and approved for road use in all markets I'm aware of - but please be aware that most racing orgs don't allow them (don't know if that's your objective).

I've ridden a 990 with them, and anything I can describe about them in terms of performance would be purely anecdotal. Even if cost were of no concern, I still wouldn't put a set on my own SD. It's possible (likely?) your suspension will need some adjustment when they are installed.

Then, when you go to sell the bike, you'll either get little to nothing extra for the wheels if on the bike ... If sold separately, you are dealing with a fairly small market, from which no one will be kicking down your door for them. Yes, I'm saying you'd be throwing money away, and they won't make you a faster/better rider. Of course, if you want them and don't care about any of this - I can only encourage you to buy a set.

FWIW, I would have a different answer for the 1290, particularly given that the cast wheels on those bikes appear to be made of sintered fecal matter, and there aren't really any other aftermarket alternatives (yet).

DFauvre.

Mate, I've had my '07 since new and as everyone knows on here I'm a bit of a fan for carbon look. I'm not into racing or extreme weight saving, I just love the material and the way it looks, (especially when it is matt and purposeful looking rather than shiny and bling).

I've replaced just about everything on my bike with carbon alternatives - most of it not bought from new but carefully accumulated from ebay over the years from people who bought the bling then sold it on when they got bored three weeks later.

Anyway, as you can imagine I have been after a set of carbon wheels for ever but they are so expensive I would never even think about buying new. Over the last six years only three sets of carbon wheels have come onto the market for the SD through ebay or this forum. Two sets of Dymags and one BST set. I wish I had bought the first because the guy only wanted a grand for both wheels but this was in the early days and I didn't realise what a great buy this would have been. Viking ran a set of Dymags during his racing career but took them off after not too much time as he didn't particularly think they helped him. They were offered to me and I just did not have the cash at the time so they went elsewhere. More recently the BST were up but the guy wanted more like a grand a wheel I think and that's more than I am willing to pay.

As Nerd says, although carbon wheels do make steering quicker and if you were a racer they maybe make a one second a lap difference, they are not really worth the £4K new price as a performance enhancer and as a bling accessory that is a lot of money to pay for show! Myself I will continue to wait until a set come up and if I have £800-£1000 spare at that moment I might be tempted but if the seller tells me they are worth twice that then I'd just say, "Well they're not worth that much to me".
