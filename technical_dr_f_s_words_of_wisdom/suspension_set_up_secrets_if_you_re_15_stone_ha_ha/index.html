SUSPENSION set-up secrets, (if you're 15 stone + ha ha)

bic_bicknell

2010-03-24 14:21:00 UTC
Just had HMRacing set up my suspension on the SD so thought that I'd post the specs. for others to try if you are about the same weight as me. I'm about 15 stone, give or take a few pounds plus full leathers and kit I had on yesterday. Bike was with standard OE shock and forks set to factory settings. Straight away they said sag was way too much for my weight.

They set up the standard stuff for me so that everything was right, front and back. Standard spring is on the soft side for my weight with the minimum sag just at the edge of acceptable limits. If you're over 15 stone be warned, don't eat any more pies.

Then we swapped over the rear shock for a KTM race shock, (that I won from Ebay which had been raced in the Superduke Battle series a couple of seasons ago - Â£120 bargain). This is longer in length, nitride coated rod and stronger spring, 180 rather than the 170 of standard spring. This is of course stiffer and when that was all set up the sag was perfect for my fat arse.

I'm no suspension expert by any means, but I got the impression that the static sag was set for the kitted up rider and would be right for that rider and bike for most riding situations. The difference for road or track would be made with the rebound and compression settings. But maybe that's for my amateur ability and of course a good set up for any individual track would probably benefit from some additional preload etc. depending on bumps, track layout, surface, tyres.

It's a bit of a black art isn't it?

There seems to be quite a few SD owners looking for a 180/185 spring. This is the one fitted to the KTM Hardparts race shock, (orange) but I don't know if it's available as a separate part. Or if not, I can't believe White Power don't just have a whole range of springs for their shocks available as replacements.
