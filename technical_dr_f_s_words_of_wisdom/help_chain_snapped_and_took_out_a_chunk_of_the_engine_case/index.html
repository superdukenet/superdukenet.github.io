Help! Chain snapped and took out a chunk of the engine case!

AngKuGuay

2009-04-11 14:04:00 UTC
Two weeks after collecting my 07SD, my chain snapped on the highway and almost killed me! It was by a stroke of luck that the locked rear wheel managed to free itself of the broken chain that I managed to keep the rubber side down.

At the side of the road, I noticed that a portion of the engine (that the front sprocket cover is attached to) case was broken off. Bits of the bolts were broken inside and there were lash marks on my tyre, rims, engine casing and frame.

Have it towed to the dealer and they are now saying that its a result of my own fecking negligence! Fact is that I got the SD for LESS than 2 weeks and was only halfway through my second tankful of fuel.

When I collected the bike, I checked the chain and sprockets, they looked new and I was told by the dealer himself that it is new! And when I checked this morning before my ride, the freeplay on the chain was not excessive.

Can anyone here help as to what could have caused the damage it did? Could it really be a case of rider fault? I had hit a top of close to 190kmh the first day I got the bike, subsequently it was nearly legal speeds of around 100kmh on the local roads with no wheelies or stoppies. I did a few hard accelerations off the lights though. But then again, a bike like the SD should be ridden that way right?

Freestyler is right on that one. The headlight cowl comes from a '07 SuperDuke Battle Replica, which is very rare indeed. It was called a '07 model but was in fact a modified '06 model.

I doubt very much that originally your bike was a Battle Replica because it was only done in black and with a single seater. Check it out:



I agree with Freestyler, your bike is a modified '06 (maybe '05 ?) Titanium SuperDuke.

Looking at the pictures that you posted it looks like it is the same bike used for the stunts. I doubt there are 2 SD with the same strange combination of colours, headlight and cans.

Did the stunts affect the strenght of the chain that snapped? No, professional stuntmen always use huge rear sprockets and much longer than standard chains, so it is not the same one that snapped on your bike.

From what you told us the chain looked new and correctly tensioned, but are you sure it was an original KTM part? I lived for many years in Southeast Asia and I've seen a lot of really poor quality parts being sold and mounted on bikes.

I think it's time for you to have a talk with your dealer...

Good luck mate
