Toys out the pram on fuelling issues

sdrk

2008-06-17 15:14:00 UTC
Tell me I don`t know what I`m talking about, but is it just me or is there anyone else out there who doesn`t have a problem with the Sd`s fuelling. Maybe I`ve been lucky with the SDs I`ve owned (early 05 and now 07 SDR) and not had a significant problem but there seem to be an inordinate number of posts on this issue. OK there is a degree of surging at low revs and the throttle response is sharp, but so what? It`s easily ridden around and isn`t detrimental to my enjoyment of the bike. Yep, you may have to be smoother with the throttle,use the gearbox more,even ride the clutch and use more back brake than it`s competitors,but again is this really a problem? Maybe it`s my background of big Brit twins in the 70`s, followed by loads of Ducatis but I like the KTM`s engine characteristics. If I wanted something anodyne, even uninvolving or a bit characterless there`s loads of IL 4`s out there to choose from. I don`t want a CAD/CAM generated identibike with minimal human input in desgn and development--that`s one of the reasons I bought the SD. Seem to remember a post saying the SD`s fuelling was potentially dangerous in the wet---I just can`t agree with that . Granted the throttle response is sharp ,but it`s linear and predictable. You always know how it will react--or I do on my SD. Try an early MV F4 1000 if you really want to know what`s potentially dangerous in the wet.
Just felt like defending KTM from a load of what seems to me to be questions that mainly don`t need answering........
Well I`ll go and pick up my toys now and don the flak jacket......

I've posted quite a bit about how bad mine is (or how bad I perceive it to be).

Just come back from a quick ride, and it really is getting on my tits now. I have the G2 throttle with 300 cam fitted, and my biggest problem is getting on the throttle at low(ish) revs from zero.

I can live with the surge, it doesn't bother me that much, its the abruptness from zero to any amount of throttle (which is usually only just a little bit for town riding). Its that initial on/off lurch thats really pissing me off.

Before you all say take it to the dealer, my local KTM dealers are a bunch of monkeys and I wouldn't trust them to wash the bike, let alone attempt anything more serious.

I am envious of you guys who say your bikes are sweet, straight out of the crate, but I am speaking to Sinex and trying to resolve the issue.

A quick note: Sinex was kind enough to send me the Power Commander installation manual, and the most interesting part of it (for me) was the following paragraph:

"Note: The installation of the O2 eliminators
will allow you full control over your entire
fuel curve. Without the eliminators installed
you will not be able to adjust the fuel curve
under 3000rpm and under 10% throttle."

This does describe the area of problem I'm experiencing, but its also a bit touchy higher in the rev range, like when you've closed the throttle going into a corner, and try to power back on, its the transition between on and off that tries to buck me out of my seat!
