Breaking a new bike in....what do you do ?

overthetop.ca

2008-04-19 13:47:00 UTC
As there are many that follow manufacturer suggested break in rules, I'm interested to know what you think and what you do for a good clean running reliable motor ?

Myself,since I can remember,I have used the short break in theory(get it warm and flog it like it's gunna be ridden for the rest of it's life !) I know many of the top US teams break in motors on dyno's , running them 2-3 times in under a half hour for a complete and true break in.

I know many won't agree with this and some are just plain scared to go above what the mfg says, but a motor is a motor is a motor, and they all have the same characteristics, except for the Wankel rotary.

Here is some good reading on breaking in a motor, but please for the love of God READ THE WHOLE DAMN THING before passing judgment .

http://www.mototuneusa.com/break_in_secrets.htm

I have used this theory for my last 4 bikes and they have all been GREAT motors with very little use of oil between changes.

My last road bike I bought brand new was a 03 GSXR 1000 and was bought home in the crate assembled and ran hard right off the hop, my buddy bought the same bike and followed the mfg suggested break in, they were both great bikes , but mine NEVER used any oil,while my buddies would use about a 1/2 litre between oil changes, and mine , buy the time it was down on the sight glass,it was also time to change the oil .

I can't say that there were any horse power gains on mine over the other bike as they seemed to be neck and neck every time we did a roll on drag race, but I did get better gas mileage than my buddy on long rides despite he is about 80 lbs lighter than myself ?

Anyway,the breakin secrets is a good read if you read it from start to finish and take in all that is discussed.
