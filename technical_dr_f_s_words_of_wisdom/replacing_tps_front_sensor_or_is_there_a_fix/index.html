Replacing TPS front sensor or is there a fix?

larryf8

Hi guys, apparently my problem with high idle then stalling or rough low throttle running was not the fuel filter. On another thread, i posted pics of my fix for a practical way of replacing the fuel pump filter assuming this was the cause of my irregular throttle response at low throttle openings. TuneEcu was used to determine the proper voltages on the TPS. Got mine dialed in almost spot on. However, the symptoms came up again in a short city ride. Plugging the TuneEcu showed an error of PO122 Throttle Position Sensor Low Voltage (short to ground or open circuit). This happened too in the past but i ignored it and just did the adjustment and do the 15min idle reset.

Any advice against an alternative expensive replacement of the TPS? Has anybody opened up the front TPS for cleaning. I assume it's a resistor type with a wiper contact. My constant city rides may have resulted in a worn area of the resistor plate on low throttle use.

My similar symptoms were cured by adjusting the throttle cable slack on the TB's behind the left airbox triangle cover. The dealership had replaced the TPS, synced the TB's, and replaced the fuel filter in an effort to solve the issue of high idle/no idle. In reading on a BMW F800 forum where similar symptoms occur, this fix was found to be only remedy so I gave it a go.

Have a look inside to see if one TB opens before the other from a closed throttle.
