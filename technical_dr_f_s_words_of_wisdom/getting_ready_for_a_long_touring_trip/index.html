getting ready for a long touring trip

Doon

2013-02-11 03:04:00 UTC
Hi Guys, I bought a 07 SD last fall with the intentions of prepping it over this winter so I can take a nice long road trip on it come spring. I haven't decided how long of a trip this is going to be yet, but lets assume its going to be about 3000-5000 miles.

Thankfully the bike seems to be in pretty good shape as the last owner kept up on the maintenance. Right now it has about 8000 miles on it. I'm trying to put together a list of things to address on the bike. Since it seems like its halfway to the next service interval I'm thinking I might as well just do some preventative work.

The bike is stock except for a set of yoshi slip ons. The seller says that the dealer reflashed the ecu but I am leaning towards getting the tune ecu cable to be on the safe side. The only other issue the bike has is a bit of brake shudder thats noticeable at lower speeds. I'm going to scuff up the rotors and rebuild the calipers to see if that helps. I need some suggestions at what else to tackle:


-oil change
-air filter
-tires
-chain/sprockets? (I think these will be ok with a good cleaning/lube)
-valve check?
-water pump seal?
-grease headset bearings?
-anything else?


Also would like a list of recommended mods to perform on the bike. I already have the ktm tank bag and a tail bag, touring windscreen and gel seat. Thinking about:
-heated grips
-handguards
-better lighting?
-frame sliders (which ones are good?)
-spare brake/clutch and shift levers

Yeah so I took mine out 6k miles over a 6 week period.
From SF-Yosemite-sierras-death valley-AZ-all through UT, zion etc-West Slope Colorado-Bonneville Salt flats(!)Ut-Oregon-SF.

Camping all the way. Minimal hotel. Had valve job service in St. George Ut. Changed oil in Oregon. All KTM dealers
The dealer in Or left the oil and seals in his mailbox so when I got to town, I could just do it myself. Even left an oil pan out for me:) I used the online KTM dealer map online and called to make sure they know what a Superduke is, you'd be surprised!

Ran a powerlet from the ascc port, the port that's switched(think #2)
iPhone/GPS(GPS was unreliable, do real maps)
Ran Gerbings from battery to plug in jacket and glove liners( liners: use a larger glove so it all fits )
-tall windshield-tools and tyre repair-wet wipes-padded cycling shorts under the pants-fork pressure release screws-oh shit: listen, be sure to bring and keep an extra KEY!!! Don't ask)- check the life of your tyres don't hesitate to get freshys-frame sliders: I bought a pair for every available spot on the bike.
Be sure to get large, long ones above and I front of the engine: this doubles as highway pegs to stretch your les out on. Totally required-your lighting is fine however I upgraded the bulbs-your battery should be fine-foam grip sleeves and foam donuts for the bars-buy an all access nat'l parks pass pays for itself in 4 uses and good for the year-MSR 22oz. Fuel bottle for gas-JB weld- zip ties-duct tape-rotor lock-long cable lock for gear, helmet, piece of mind while day hiking etc.- padlock for your zippers, luggage.-I knew I was going through Death Valley so I bought a new thermo radiator plug: ask your dealer or online: get the thermo switch that kicks the fan on earlier, at a lower temp. This helps prevent overheating-flush the radiator system before you go, but learn how to do it properly: search for the how to here on the forum.- get a Spot device a d subscribe or at least roadside assistance form your insurance-extra duplicate fuses-get good gear, I'm using Rukka and glad I spent the money.

That's all I can think of off the top, but Im sure to have add on's
But feel free to PM me for details or ? On anything.

BTW: when I packed up the bike with all the gear, suited up: I tuned the suspension.
So load the bike up and adjust the comp, rebound, provided you set the sag.
If not then have a pro do it, this made a big diff.

Finally: remember: it's a motorcycle and you are on an adventure,
Get prepped as best you can and then put back at least 20% of the shit you packed. Trust me you won't need it.

If you're going to be riding in cooler temps then the SMT handguards would be a nice addition to your setup. For years I avoided them because I didn't think they looked "streetfighter" enough for the SD but after one winter riding season with them I think they are the best $70 I ever spent on the SD.
