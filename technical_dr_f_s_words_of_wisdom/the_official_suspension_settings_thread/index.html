Ya see, I don't agree with internet suspension threads. Suspension is very personal and will vary greatly with bike age, mileage, rider weight and most importantly, rider style. There are a lot of guys on here who do talk the talk real well, then you see a pic of the bike with chicken strips or a track vid that's like driving miss daisy. That's not intended as a dig as there are a lot of good good guys on here, but also a lot of chaff and typical biker bullshit talk. Just the way it is with bikes and forums. It is def one of the better biking forums out there though.

I would always say if the guy can set rider sag correctly, may need to change the springs to hit this, but if he can get it in range, then set damping to standard and go out and ride it and adjust damping as its needed then they will find the setting that suits them.

Personally I've had to change to 420ml of 10wt oil in front forks to get enough rebound damping range for my liking. Front and rear sag set ok with std springs. Rear shock had enough damping range to work with. Dropped forks 5mm through yokes and did oil and bike is now perfect for me. It does exactly what I want without having to think about it. If another guy at 14stone kitted with an 8k miles 05 model set up the same as me it might totally work against his riding style and mess his handling up totally.

Set rider sag. Learn how damping works. Set everything the standard and get out and ride then adjust from there.

Just my 2p worth. .

2008 SDR, Stock front fork springs, stock rear shock with standard 180 weight spring. I currently weigh 200lbs. Bike is used mainly for mountain roads and 2-4 trackdays per season.

Suspension setup:

Front:
Fork tops flush with top clamps.
Rider sag - 40mm
Rebound – 4/5 clicks
Compression - 18 clicks
Streeing Damper: 8 clicks

Rear:
Rider sag - 35mm
Rebound - 18 clicks
Low speed compression - 16/15 clicks
High speed compression – 2.5/2.25turns

These setting give the SDR and very tight feeling with little to no bounce in the front (rebound). If I want a little smoother ride for the street I’ll open the front rebound to 5 clicks out. For the track I’ll sometimes stiffen-up the preload for the front and rear to 35mm front and 30mm rear depending on track surface conditions.
