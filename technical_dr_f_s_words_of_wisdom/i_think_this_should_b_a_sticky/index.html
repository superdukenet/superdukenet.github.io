Hello All. have to say this . first after hours and hours of reading past threads ( and no where near ALL of course ) multiple up till 5 am and 6am reads. There already may be a post of this somewhere however i need to say:

I have an 07 SD i got with 8 thou miles on it 8 weeks ago. As already mentioned now has MH Box - sas off etc etc with Akra's , it's in the shop w/ the dyno guy . To cut the story short after what my dyno guy felt was a smart resetting of the TPS voltage amts he came up with the 3,000 rpm then settling to idle issue. He was frusted to put it frankly.

He having the manual out used the manual recommendation for voltage settings for the TPS. ( i don't have the numerical value in my head - but just to say it's listed in the manual under fueling ) --- PROBLEM: If looking elsewhere in the manual particularly the ELECTRICAL section the TPS recommendation there is different than the recommendation in the fueling section . Makes sense to go to the fueling section to look for TPS settings no ? The problem is the voltage recommendation in fueling is what causes the 3,000 auto revving problem --- Turns out that fueling recommendation is for Carburetors and not Throttle Bodies w/ injectors.

tps 3.8V cold at full throttle opening (engine off).

should make the voltage when hot and running (after 15min throttle reset) at 0% throttle around .63 to .66 (although KTM says .56 - .6V.)

this is what i have found the most effective... I have played with a lot of the different sensors... after enough calibration i have the thing running silky smooth.

BTW... 3000rpm bit lasts for about 5 to 10 seconds when during tps reset.

reason for 3k rpm is the tps talks directly to the idle speed controller.

Too lower a voltage will cause the idle speed controller to cut in too much and not get anywhere near 100% throttle opening (more like 92%),

too high a voltage will give true 100% throttle opening but Idle speed controller will not kick in and bike will stall almost every time that the throttle is lifted off(or if only a lil off every now and then under heavy braking)
