that's lucky, as I plum forgot! Good luck with it. From what I understand, a custom tune (with a decent tuner) adjusts for all the variances in your individual machine (probably mostly related to TPS and actual butterfly positions relative to these, which don't seem to be all that consistent, especially with the new bikes). Unfortunately, these seem to be most evident down low in the SD. Otherwise you'd have to think the factory tune would be better (neglecting emissions related allowances, and that the engines aren't run in at the factory).

I'm stoked with my PCIII tune, and can't see how a PCV with autotune could possibly improve on it. So might be worth a trip to get it in the hands of someone capable!

p.s. mine was done by Procycle dyno, whose shop was full of Harleys, but they still did a great job.
