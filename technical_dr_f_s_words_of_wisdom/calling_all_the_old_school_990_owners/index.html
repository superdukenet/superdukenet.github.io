Scotty

2016-05-14 23:07:00 UTC

I own a 2008 Super Duke Black Edition. It has trumpet tipped Akrapovic cans; all the MotoHooligan intake system mods; 16 tooth front and 42 tooth rear sprockets; Throttlemeister cruise control/bar ends; cheapo aftermarket levers (stock brake lever ran afoul of Gymkhana and a clutch that needed bleeding, see next); and an Oberon clutch slave cylinder. There are 27,000 plus miles on the bike and other than the failed slave cylinder (they all go eventually, Ducks too!) and a broken rear header pipe (probably due to poor dealer install of the Akrapovic cans) it has been a totally reliable bike. The 990 has also been an absolute blast to ride up in the "mountains" of Alabama, Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, Kentucky, and Tennessee and I love it to death! However, I recently bought a new 1290 R. I got a killer end of the year deal on the 1290 and the new version has most of the performance and safety related tweaks I wished the 2008 SD 990 had (ABS, slipper clutch, TC, etc...). The new SD only has about 2,500 miles on it, but it is a very competent machine and I am enjoying it immensely. Was it worth the additional monetary outlay? Only time will tell. The main differences I notice are GOBS of torque, smoother motor, much smoother throttle response, more roll around weight (gotta work on that), even stronger brakes, and increased high speed cornering stability. The more I ride the 1290, the more I like it. It has been to the North Carolina and Virginia mountains and rode and handled very well on some VERY tight roads. I have never logged in to the forum very often, but many of the posts have been helpful. I am a pretty fair and experienced automobile and motorcycle mechanic, so I don't usually need a lot of advice. However, when I have needed advice, information, or recommendations for the KTM twins in my life; I've found help here. Thanks!
