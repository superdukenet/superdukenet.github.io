Have you screwed your forks lately? (Fork air bleed screws)

Hotbrakes

2011-07-21 04:03:00 UTC
Coming up on 2 years of ownership of my beloved 06 SD and I guess about a year ago I began to notice that the bitch just doesn't seem to handle like she used to. I've done a few trackdays and while getting a knee down wasn't a problem, really pushing the lean angle always felt weird and unsettled. Riding on some bumpy back roads has also been feeling unusually harsh. The other night I cracked those little phillips head air bleed screws in the forks and both released a slight hiss. Took her out for a couple hundred miles today on varying roads and it is back to the girl I fell in love with nearly 2 years ago. Unfortunately while trying to coax some more plush action out of the forks I moved the compression and rebound adjusters a bit far to the open side so the bike was a bit bouncy like an old sedan with blown shocks. Shouldn't be hard to get them back on track though.

Gonna do the 450EXCSM next as it is ailing from similar handling, rear squats in turns as it should, front is harsh and stands its ground making turns very difficult. Works Connection air bleed caps are on their way to my house. Now if I could just rewind those trackdays and all the BS passes made on me for slugging in turns...

Thought I'd share this superb information as it has helped me a great deal, even if it's already been discussed. This basic maintenance should be a sticky...

My forks were on fork seal change 2 weeks ago.. The dealer said (who is a motocross racer with a KTM + repairman too) I could have to take apart the fork at the lower end too at last oil change, because it has an inner tube with oil wich can be very dity and slimy from the water (mine was). The lower screw was very loosy he said.

He also said that I could have to grease the fork between the fork seal and dust seal with a very expensive factory grease at the last fork oil change.. Luckily he has had a self made grease for cheap (maybe with glycerine, I didn't ask). Greasing have to be often on dirtbikes as he has experienced.

We discussed the KTM fork bleeders too: they are crap with the almost unsealed rubber end he said.
