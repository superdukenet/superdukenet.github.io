Not quite.

I think (and I stand to be corrected if I'm wrong on any of this) that KTM has laws to obey in order to make and sell bikes. I believe production bikes can be sampled at the factory gate by Eurocrats and tested for conformity, so all new bikes have to be within the specified limits.

Dynojet, on the other hand, does not have such limitations and can remap the ECU to run better and/or produce more power. AFAIK, the emissions for a new bike are far stricter than, say, those for an MoT test so once sold, a bike can be fettled but not before.

I suspect KTM (and others) can't be seen to provide maps for bikes that would fail the emissions laws for new bikes.
