Hotbrakes

2012-12-27 18:17:00 UTC

I run 190's on the SD all the time with no issues or rubbing. In fact my avatar was with a 190/55, bike handled fine...

Why there is /50, /55, or /60 I don't know. Seems like most street tyres are /50 (except 180's) perhaps inspired by drag racing for a bigger contact patch straight up and down. /55 is pretty standard for DOT race tyres although Dunlop has gone to 190/60 for their 211's with no option for 180. Their 190/60 is designed for 5.5" rims (600's, SD's, etc.) and 200 is for 6" rims (litre bikes). Another fact is that the measurement stamped on the side is measured by each manufacturer's ruler and they vary. So "190/55" might in fact be 181mm. There's a lot of science involved including carcass, carcass shape, carcass flex, carcass design, etc.

I run the 190/69 Michelin slick and there is no rubbing of the swing arm at normal chain lengths.
I'm running my chain as short as possible ATM and it has rubbed some of the paint away.

A fellow SD racer is running 180 pirrelli's and he has rubbed through his swingarm, on the disc side, due to running the wheel base as short as possible.
So a 180 will rub if you run it short enough and ride it hard enough.
A 190 will be fine at lengths of 70mm to the back of the axle block (right way round) with the Michelin 190's (on my bike)
