8 hours of DOT-E Enginering the SD airbox

Dr F

2008-05-23 18:49:00 UTC
Today IÂ´ve been 8 hours on a Dyno 250i with a DOT-E engineer (the european supporter for dot-e, he is the one that develops dot-e airbox and filter kits to bikes, the one and only





We are not finished yet And I dont have the charts yet (Ill post them ass soon as I get them) All I got is pictures from all setups.

1. run was with DNA, Akra race cans with DB inserts removed, stock airbox
This run was used as comparison for rest of the day

We tried remove the snorkel = lack of power (compared to 1. run )~ 1-3hp from 2000-9500rpm
Remove the triangeled sideflaps, snorkel installed = +1-2hp (compared to 1. run) from 2000-8000rpm

The absolute best result was to remove the airbox LID, so the throttle bodies can breathe free.
Result:
2000rpm ~ +3hp
5000rpm ~ +4hp
9000rpm ~ +6hp
torgue 2000-9500rpm ALOT better ~ +8Nm. All compared to 1. run

Problem is that without airbox lid the air is not filtered. Next step was to filter the air, and still get same conditions like when lid was off.

So we tried clamp on from pipercross and removed the airbox lid. The clampon was fitted directly to the throttlebodies with bellmouths.



Up to 6000rpm it gave same gain as the result with lid removed . But from 6000-9500 it lacked 2hp compared to the 1. run. That was a difference on 6hp (compared to the run without lid) from 6000-9500rpm just by adding the pipercross clampons. The filter area was to small and couldt not deliver the needed air. Maybe bigger pipercross elements would have been better, but almost impossible because of lack of space beneath the tank.

Then we tried manualy to remove the sides in the airbox. This was the last we tried today.


Same gain from 2000-7500rpm as the run without lid, but from 7500-9500 no gain compared to 1. run.
Next step is to remove more plastic from airbox (as much as possible) and add filter were we have removed th eplastic. That should give same as removing the lid.

Just to add to this thread

Spent 4 hours on the dyno with my 08 SDR trying various things with very similar results

First run was PCIII with zero map, air filter and snorkel removed and penny slots in the triangle filter covers, full acro evo system

We got 125 bhp and was running at about 12:1 air/fuel throughout

We then did a full custom map on each cylinder taking about 15% on average fuel out of the front cylinder and about 8% on the rear

We re-ran the power runs and got 123bhp ! We had been running the engine for a few hours and therefore probablly lost a few horses due to the temperature etc, so basically getting the fueling spot on made no difference to the top end power ? Very disapointing and not your usual result.

We then refitted the filter and snorkel and got back up to about 124

We then fitted the modified filter top as shown below along with a 10mm spacer under the front tank mount and gained another 2-3 bhp taking it up to 127 bhp and about 5-6 ft/lb in the mid range.

Overall I was very disapointed when this SDR battle bike was track dyno'd at 129-131 bhp and the general consensus was that a PCIII and filter mods would be getting me another 8 or so bhp

I know dyno's vary and I can only go on the figures of this particular dyno, but it's generally been very accurate with my TL1000S

The power just tails off at 8k and looks to be struggling to breath, better cams are needed in my opinion, but who does them..........
