Well, the Big Grey Connector wasn't it...(actually two BGCs ...because of the PCIII) all the WD-40, the two days hosing it in and draining it out, the electric grease,...made no difference...
so...I took everything apart on top of the motor again...the complete airbox, fuel injectors, all those electric connections around them, etc...got down to the plugs, made sure everything was secure and all connectors were correctly plugged in...put it all back together...still made no difference...
so...took out the fuel pump again...noticed the top cap (that goes over the round filter) was slightly "off", not correctly clipped down...re-fastened that..put it all back togethr...put in fresh gas...charged the battery...
..fired it up...everything seems fine...I haven't taken it for a ride yet (it's pouring rain) but it idles and revs up smoothly....
so...what was it?...my guess is the fuel pump being not correctly assembled (by me)...probably looked okay when I put it in but the metal side cl;ips were not fully engaged on one side...ran fine for awhile and then the plastic bit slipped past the metal clip and ....that caused intermittent fuel pumping...or some possible combination of all the above....
