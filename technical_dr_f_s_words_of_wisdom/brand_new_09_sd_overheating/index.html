Search is your friend. Although I started my own thread too!

On the 09 model there are 12 bars for the radiator temp. When it gets to 11 you should worry, when it hits twelve and eventually flashes and has a nice red light flashing worry some more. This is bad.

The twelve bars equates to about 120 DegC (based on the manual and my memory). Thus 10 bars is about 100DegC seeing as the fan only turns on at 105DegC it is possible you will see 10 bars before the fan comes on (taking inaccuracies in thermostats etc into account ie +-5 DegC etc).

My bike regularly sits at 9 bars due to stop start riding at lower speed. Usually dropping when the light goes green!

So if it hit 10 bars at a stoplight I wouldnt be worrying too much, assuming you still have the right amount of coolant. I've been told they run hotter while they are running in and then loosen up. I think this has happened on mine but it took a while due to other issues with my bikes cooling system.

Heres the link for some good reading and suggestions:

My first guess would be air in the cooling loop. You'll need to burp the system. Easiest way is to take it to a shop so they can lift the front end and make sure "most" of the bubbles are gone.

I would check the level in the radiator as the level should be up to the cap ie just about over the lip the cap seals on. If it's below this you most likely had an air bubble and just need to top it up.

While you're there check there is no "gunk" on the rubber seal of the radiator cap as this may allow air in and stop the cooling system working as wel by using the overflow tank (needs seal to suck it back from the overflow tank).

Check your overflow tank as well it should be between min and max at least.

Does your radiator fan come on? If not then get it checked out. The fan will come on at about 9-10bars depending on your thermostat (as the fan has it's own thermostat and doesnt turn on based on the radiator temperature.

Mines all good now. Just see how it goes. 11 bars and up means there is something wrong or you are riding slowly in the sahara dessert.

Let us know what you find out.
