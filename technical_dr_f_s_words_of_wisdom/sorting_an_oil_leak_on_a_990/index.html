Sorting an oil leak on a 990

Scotty

2016-08-15 16:18:00 UTC
When I serviced my 990 road bike a few months back I found evidence of previous intervention by someone not familiar with the use of a torque wrench - when refitting the oil tank drain plug I experienced that horrible feeling of "shit, it's home but it isn't tightening" and realised that some ham-fisted idiot had over-tightened it previously and yielded the threads. I left it suitably "tightened" and decided to keep an eye on it.

Recently I was getting an oil weep on the left side of the tank but couldn't positively identify the source, considering maybe the copper crush washer on the drain plug or the green rubber seal on the strainer and I decided to do something about it. As the oil is relatively new I wanted to re-use it so I drained the tank into a recently-emptied oil can, but wasn't pleased to see fragments of the thread come out with the drain plug. I also noticed oil around the lower banjo bolt on the level sight tube so figured that maybe it was leaking from the copper washers so I decided to replace those on the lower bolt. As it was impossible to get a torque on the drain plug and the threads were disintegrating I decided to lock wire it in place so while it was out I drilled the head and drilled through the tank mounting bolt flange nearby and refitted it with some tidy lockwiring to prevent it coming undone. Oil back in the tank, fire up, no leaks apparent.

The following morning I noticed that the lower banjo bolt on the sight lube was still wet with oil, and looking closely I saw a split at either end of the tube - how on earth had I missed those the day before?


Bollocks, cut off the lockwire and drain the tank once more, not liking seeing more bits of disintegrating thread coming out on the drain plug. Fortunately, when I bought the 990 track bike, a spare oil tank was amongst the box of spares that Shadowman gave me with it, so I went up to the attic and removed the sight tube and fitted it to the tank, then refitting the drain plug and re-wiring it in position, oil back in. Job done, so I thought, and did a couple more small jobs on the bike before packing everything away. I'd put an old newspaper inside the big tray that I put beneath the bike to soak up any drips and as I pulled it out to the right side of the bike I noticed fresh oil drips on it. Eh, where have they come from? A quick look round to the left and I found that the drain plug was no longer oil tight, such was the damage to the threads. Double bollocks, but at least I had a spare tank, so it was back up to the attic to retrieve it and I found that swapping it was relatively straightforward. My main concern was being able to detach and re-attach the three oil pipes to the top of the tank, and it would have been easier had I already undone the three mounting bolts to allow it to move and get space beneath the fuel tank and radiator (the rad was already detached from frame and oil tank). The sight tube was shifted back over to its original home, and I put the new green seal round the tank strainer... all back together, oil in once more, fire up and no sign of any leaks...
