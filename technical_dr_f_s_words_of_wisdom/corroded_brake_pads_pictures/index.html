2013-01-06 19:36:00 UTC
Sorry, but without a minimum of maintenance it is not possible to use the Brembos in steady rain and winter climate. Seeing the amount of dirt build up on your calipers, you should push the pistons out of their housings 2-3 times a year, so that they stick out for maybe 8 mm and then clean the whole caliper in warm water with dishwasching gel and a toothbrush.

After that, dry them and apply ATE or similar "Brake piston and caliper grease" to the pistons and push them back in. Maybe you should coat the edges of the pads backplates too with some grease, i know that in VW and AUDI garages they use such a brown grease for the pads guidings.

If you ride throughout winter in the UK the brakes will end up like this unless you wash them regularly to keep the corrosion at bay. A few years ago I stripped my brakes and the Brembo OE pads had completely de-laminated and were only being held together by the callipers and pins.

I think it's the type of salt that is npw being used in the UK. I suspect it's cheap and highly corrosive.

There has been a lot written about the KTM/Brembo brakes. The bottom line is they are high performance, racing kit and need more maintenance than normal. This is the price you pay for having brick wall performance braking.
