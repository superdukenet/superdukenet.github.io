Not that difficult a job to do yourself, detailed information on the forks is shown in here

losbol.mine.nu/Superduke/RA_WP_Fork_LC8-SD_SM_2005-E.pdf

You can use the triple clamp on the bike in place of a vice to undo the necessary nuts, you can even leave the cartridge intact and just undo the bolt at the bottom to remove the whole unit as one although it's not as easy to pump the oil through the cartridge this way.

There's a guy on ebay that sells the tool to undo the fork cap and also a driver for the fork seal, the driver is just a couple of bits of plastic pipe and a jubilee clip, easy enough to make yourself but he only wanted a couple of quid on top of the cost of the tool so I thought, sod it, saves me the hassle of making it myself.

Just a thought for the future. Often a leak, weep or seep can be cured by cleaning the seal from the outside. In the bad old days we would use a piece of 35mm film as a cleaning tool. Today there is a $5.00 tool called a seal saver or something like that. It consists of a piece of flexible plastic with a hooked end. You could make one but for the price I couldn't be bothered to source the proper material. Drop the dust boot and follow the instructions, basically to insert the plastic tool (or film) into the seal from the bottom and run around the seal and remove, hopefully taking the debris with it. I have had many seals either permanently repaired or go thousands of miles before another problem occurred. A danger of using 35mm film (assuming you can find any these days) is that it is brittle and could break off inside the seal. Use something more flexible,like the dedicated tool.
