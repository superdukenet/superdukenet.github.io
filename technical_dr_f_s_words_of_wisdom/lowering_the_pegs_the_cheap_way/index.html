One of the things with the first part was that I filed it down from the 25 mm I needed for the peg diameter to the 22 mm it is at the pivoting point. This was an easier way of doing that.
You will notice the kind of rough surface on the inside - got surprised by an overzealous milling cutter - but you don't see that when it's on the bike. The spring needs a cavity to fall into, hence the milling there.

Befbever

2008-05-10 09:22:00 UTC

These should lower the pegs by a further 5 mm compared to the first one. I also increased the space between the threaded holes so it looks cleaner.
I made a huge mistake by sawing off 3 mm on its twin part - lapse of concentration I guess - but that doesn't change the space between the various holes so it'll still fit. The only thing you'll notice is that the spring hook on the top sits 3 mm above the surface....
The parts are otherwise identical and interchangeable.

I'm done with this for now. Time to ride!
