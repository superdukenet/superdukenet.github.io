I've .

Getting the watertemp sensor out with the engine in place is a real PITA!
Here is a picture of the tool I made:

I had to saw and file until I could fit it between the frame and over the watertemp sensor. I only managed to get it far enough over the sensor by gently tapping it on with a screwdriver.
The sensor was stuck pretty good. I had to apply quite some force before I heard a little 'crack' and it started to turn.
Now... a word of caution. If you want to minimise the spillage of coolant, ask for an assistant that can put his/her thumb on the hole or keep the new sensor handy in your other hand and blazingly fast put it in. The old sensor will probably be stuck in the tool (mine was) so you will need something to push it out.

The connector of the new sensor needs an extra wire. Just run a wire along the frame to the headlight mask. I put mine in some wire protection tube that I tie-wrapped to the frame.

The plug of the 06 watertemp sensor can be opened! With a small screwdriver pop out the yellow plastic bit on the inside. Then use a watchmakers screwdriver or other fine tool to pry open the plastic retainer clip of the pin. This way you can pull the pins out. These pins fit in the 07 plug! Remember what color goes where (there are little numbers on the plug). The new wire goes in position #3. The new wire needs to be connected to position 9 (blue/brown in the 06 loom) in the 12pin plug of the 07 loom.
