There is truth in the 1/3 setting's that is about where the ride should be with the rider no matter what the style or speed,you just need to adjust springs and valves to accomodate .Suspension IS a bit of a black art sometimes, but when you do fully understand it ,it's like a light comming on? I am an agressive track rider and weigh 250 lbs and LOVE to brake late, I used to think that the stiffer the suspension, the faster I would be able to go.....NOT ! as it turns out, the faster you go , the more suspension you need to keep it STABLE (not skittering and chattering in corners) so the settings need to be more soft edged, and use greater travel to fully absorbe bumps.


I was taught this way.
take an egg......drop it from 5 feet and catch it in your hands, in order for the egg to not break or crack, you moved your hand downward when catching the egg in order not to break it......right ?

now take that same egg and drop it from 20 feet ,you will have to move your hand in the downward motion for longer and faster to meet the speed of the egg.....no?

well friends,suspension works the same way, to go faster ,you need to use more or ALL of the available travel you have in the suspension, now let's go break some eggs !

CAR'S Suspension is totally the opposite ! it needs to be tight andd move next to none to go fast
