There's a couple of different "rattling" at-idle sounds I've experienced.

There's the clutch chatter: When the transmission is in neutral and the clutch is engaged. I consider this normal.

There's the occasional rattle, when coming to a stop from high speed, there's a bit of a rattle, that sounds almost as if there's at little starvation at the cams (?). The noise seems to come from the top of the engine somewhere. If I rev the engine a little (say ~1000 rpm over idle), the sound goes away. It really sounds more like valve clatter than anything else. I've done nothing about it - but I do know that there are others here who have suffered premature cam-lobe surface wear/failure.

When my water pump seal was starting to go - and perhaps I was fortunate in that I caught it early - there was no additional sound/noise associated with it. That's not to say that it doesn't occur for others.

2013-12-30 18:16:00 UTC
I have a couple more suggestions as to what your engine rattle just might be:

Check that your O2 sensors have not loosend in their threaded sockets into their respective header pipe. If they have loosened up, be careful when tightening them, they are a bit delicate (not as strong as a solid nut or blt of that size).

There is an exhaust bracket down low on the bottom of the SD's engine, It is there for supporting the front header pipe's long run to the back junction. Make sure that has not loosened up, or the bolt missing altogether. That could be causing a rattle.

Lastly, if your exhaust is VERY loud and backfiring A LOT (not just typical SAS stuff), Check for a broken weld on the header pipes to the cylinder head mounting flange.

All of these have happened with my '08.

I think the original culprit was the missing lower header support bolt (possibly from the factory or dealer) which overstressed the rear header's mounting joint/flange and broke its spot welds. When I was resolving all of this I took the O2 sensor out of the front header to drop the whole freaking exhaust system to replace the broken rear header. When I put the O2 sensor back in the front header, I just did not tighten it quite enough and it loosened and began rattling around a bit. Hope you find your problem and it is not a major one!
