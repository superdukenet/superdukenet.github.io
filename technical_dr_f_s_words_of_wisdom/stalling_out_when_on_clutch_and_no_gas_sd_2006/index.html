Sounds like your map sensor vacuum ports (at the cylinder head may be plugged. First disconnect the map sensors electrical connectors at each map sensor (under the fuel tank on each side), ride the bike and see if it is any better. If so then unscrew the brass vacuum ports (where the hose from the map sensors connect to the cylinder heads, again on each side) and use a 1.0 mm drill bit and clean the brass (jet) nozzle. Also check the idle speed rpm and if you have an aftermarket exhaust you may need another map than the stock one.

No. 47

2016-03-06 11:05:00 UTC

Set the TPS with throttle closed, not fully open - the 0.66V throttle closed value shown by TuneECU is probably/usually too high (that's why the fault code can't be erased) and 0.55V too low - aim for/closer to 0.6V - then try rest adaption followed by 15 min reset.

Also, TuneECU does give slightly different readings (+/- 0.2V) than the (probably) more KTM diagnostic tool - so your 0.55V could actually be 0.53V and your 0.66V, 0.68V.

As Aphex says, this may just confirm that the TPS needs replacement - they seem to fail by erratic performance rather than simply stop working - my spare TPS is out on loan at the moment but, once returned, happy to sent it to you, on loan also, to try before you buy.

Apologies - mis-read the error code number - P0223 (or P0222), blink code 07, is for the sensor for the 2nd throttle valve, the rear unit and not the TPS, P0123 (or PO122, blink code 06 - my comments re the TPS are still valid though.

Did you download the repair manual? Page 7-38 explains purpose of 2nd throttle valve, page 7-43 what happens if it is faulty (closes the butterflies which would/may explain stalling) and page 7-47 the checks and right voltages you should have with throttle fully closed (1.0V) and fully open (4.3V) - page 7-56 repeats these required settings - adjustment is as per the TPS.

If the voltage isn't changing as you open and close throttle, the wiring, connector or sensor itself is damaged/faulty.

I experienced the exact same problem with my '05 SD and was able to get to the bottom of it thanks to this thread.
So thanks for everybody also from side!

To provide some contribution on the subject, here is how my "repair" went:

1. Checked TPS volts with TuneECU (bike cold, not running): 0,79V (= way off the spec!)
2. Did "reset adaptation" with TuneECU
3. Started the bike and ran the 15min adaptation (idle, bike straight, no throttle)
4. Rechecked TPS volts with TuneECU (bike warm, not running): 0,65V
--> Test drive, NO MORE PROBLEMS!

So my fix was somewhat simpler since I did not have to touch the TPS it self.
The 15min calibration was able to adjust it automatically.

I know the TPS voltage is still not exactly at the spec (0,6V) but the bike works fine so this is OK for me.
