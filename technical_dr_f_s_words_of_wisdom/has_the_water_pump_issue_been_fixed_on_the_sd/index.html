As far as I'm concerned there was never an issue with the pumps. The problem originates from left over sand from the casting process in the water ways of the engine.
This will go through the pump and ruin it.
When you go and inspect an SD check the overflow reservoir for black particles (sand). Have a look if the waterpump has been changed if it is an older model and try to find out if the owner ever flushed the engine and replaced the coolant.
I have an 08 and I never had dirt in the resrvoir and no problems with the pump.

The pump is not that hard to change and if you flush the whole thing out properly at the same time you are pretty much safe for a long time. The hardest part is probably to bleed the radiator as the front of the bike needs to be raised to do it.
So don't be scared of this and if you find sand you might be able to negotiate a good price and replace the pump yourself. There are how to's on the forum how to do it and how to flush and bleed the system.

Take care when you remove the LC8 water pump from the engine block. The LC8 motor has a sand pit built into the top side of the engine case right above the water pump that collect all kinds of road grime. If you don't clean this sand pit before you remove your water pump....that road grime is going to fall right in the inside of your motor.....
