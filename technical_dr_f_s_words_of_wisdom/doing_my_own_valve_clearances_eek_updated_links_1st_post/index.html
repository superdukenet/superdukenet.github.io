With the cam timing, don't worry about locking the crank etc. With both cam covers off and plugs out (I used the plug spanner that comes in your toolkit as it was the only one I had that was small enough) You'll see 2 timing marks on opposite sides of each camwheel. You want to have these marks lined up horizontally meaning the camlobes will be facing outwards and with no load on your buckets. You can turn the engine over just on the starter to bring your marks into place. Then use your feeler gauges to measure your clearances, take your shims out and measure them with a set of verniers and work out what shims you need to order. Once the first cylinder is done re-assemble and repeat with the rear one.

I got away with leaving the oil tank in but pulled the radiator which was ok as I was doing coolant, oil, filters as well anyway. I hope this helps.
