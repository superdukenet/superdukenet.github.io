working out a new fueling map

jcozine

2013-12-30 03:11:00 UTC
I haven't been all that happy with the different maps out there for my '08 USA superduke (w/ stock airbox, and slip-ons with dB baffles), so I've been working on making a new one. I don't have access to a dyno, but I'm an engineer so I know what I'm doing (ha!). So like a good engineer, I had to take some data on my current set up first. I was surprised that the 'stumble' I've been experiencing at cruise is actually at VERY small throttle openings, on the order of 5% or less. I figured this by cruising until I got to the stumble, then pulling the clutch and seeing what the RPM stabilized to. Usually it was around 5000 rpm, so back in the garage I plugged in TuneECU, started the bike in neutral, and gave it enough throttle to get to the same RPM. I recorded the vacuum numbers on the way up while I was at it:

rpm throttle% cyl1vac cyl2vac
1600 0 395 400
2000 1 442 450
2500 1 441 442
3000 2 475 475
3700 3 501 503
4100 3 481 484
4900 3 491 495
5200 4 460 475

Surprising also that there's a reversal in the vacuum trend on the way up. There must be all kinds of standing-wave badness going on in that airbox, like I've heard others say.

I don't like the idea of turning off the o2 sensors, since from my experience with dirtbike carbs very small jetting changes on pilot circuits (the low-throttle ones) make huge differences in throttle 'feel' and off-idle performance. Also pretty bummed on the unburned fuel smell on my clothes and the shitty gas mileage I get when I turn them off using, say, the 'smoothened' map that's out there.

I also don't like the idea of setting the F-L switch to 0 across the board, since then the throttle bodies will have to be perfectly balanced, because it would be fueling based on throttle% and not individual cylinder vacuums so it wouldn't be able to fuel differently between the two cylinders to compensate.

And finally I don't like setting the 2nd throttle to 100% (or removing it) since it seems like it would run leaner by letting in more air at the same throttle% (but the same amount of fuel since you're on the F map, which meters by throttle%).

So I'm using '08_SDGuysCustom_possibly_the_best_map_for_aftermarket_pipes_.hex' map as a starting point, and going from there. It looks like it was developed from a US map, because it has the characteristic odd secondary throttle mapping as the factory US map, KM611US0704501Map.hex. Still not sure I believe that there's a fundamental difference between the euro and US maps, since the factory-sponsored Akra slip-on map is literally identical in all visible ways to the stock euro map. I think the first strategy will be to just set the F-L switchover to a really small value, like 3% across the board. Let's hope I don't fook it up. If anyone actually read all the way thru this, I'd be curious to hear your thoughts.
