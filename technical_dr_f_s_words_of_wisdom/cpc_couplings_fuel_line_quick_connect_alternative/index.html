A few more notes on this:

- One possible explanation for KTM's use of black-colored (acetal?) plastic, is that they are probably of a UV-resistant polymer(via added stabilizers). Sorry for not recalling this possibility earlier. As they are, acetal polymers are NOT UV resistant (but can be with additives), and will degrade in the sun over time. As such, it would then be a semi-custom item from CPC for KTM, for which they still rob us. The inexpensive plain white acetal quick disconnect tube fittings would be fine short term (assuming you dispose of the EPDM o-rings in favor of something like Viton - an absolute must-do) - as a temporary spare - but probably not a good idea for permanent use.

- Given that the Jiffy-tite products are about ~$50 or so (USD) for both ends, are designed for use with gasoline (including race fuels with high percentages of aromatics - which acetal doesn't tolerate well ...), there is almost no reason not to switch. This is about 1/2 the cost of OE bits. The company has a serious dedication to motorsport (http://motorsports.jiffy-tite.com/)- they clearly understand our requirements. The last things you'll have to worry about are their UV resistance (the anodizing WILL fade however), temperature range, and strength. They come with fluoropolymer (e.g. Viton) sealing components. The ONLY downside I can imagine is that if the connectors were poorly placed such that they would conduct a lot of heat ... But would still be better than plastics placed in an similarly poor placement.
