No oil in sight tube and high idle

BassAgent

2014-05-12 04:31:00 UTC
Two questions, the first one is... probably pretty dumb.

Can anyone think of a reason why I would have no oil in the sight tube on my '08 990? I did an oil and filter change a week ago and replaced the sight tube while I was at it, but the tube is always empty when checking it by following the KTM procedure. Am I just losing my mind, or is there some silly possible issue?

I drained the tank and crank case, replaced all filters, re-installed the drain plugs and new sight tube then measured up 2.5L of 10w50 Motorex and filled it. Started, ran to 4 bars while holding the bike vertical and on both wheels. Shut off, waited 20 seconds, nothing. No oil. Added 500ml and repeated the steps, still nothing. I get oil in the sight tube at varying levels when I check it on the side stand, and the tube quickly fills when the bike is running at idle either on its side stand or held vertical. I have zero oil leaks and no smoke or smell from the exhaust that would indicate burning.


Second issue started today on the ride home from grabbing lunch. Bike started fine and the idle sat at about 2500 for a few seconds before dropping to ~1800. Strange, but oh well. Started riding and found the usual high idle symptoms, very little engine braking, sitting around 2000rpm when clutching in as I came to a stop. I will plug it into TuneECU tomorrow as I needed to remap for my Akra pipes I installed last week anyways, so I'll do the 15minute idle trick and check my setpoint. Is there much else to look into? There are two items that I found in searching since it happened
1: I used the kill switch to stop the bike when I parked at the restaurant. I believe this was the first time I used it before. (Someone said this can cause idle issues... I don't see how but maybe?)
2: Signs of a dying battery or reg/rec issues. I have never had starting issues but I will put it on a tender overnight to see if it makes a difference.

When you drain the oil tank there is a ball valve on the line out of the tank to the pump/crank case ( you know the 2 screws you undone to drain it).
If that is fitted incorrectly or leaks, the oil runs in to the sump instead of staying in the tank when you switch off the engine.
Check this!
