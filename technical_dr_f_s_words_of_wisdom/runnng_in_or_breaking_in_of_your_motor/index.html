I'm of the opinion the SD's are using more oil than normal were broken in too gently.

In 37 years of riding and 20+ new bikes I've never followed the word of the manual, more like a 300 mile compromise. I used hard throttle under a load without exceeding rpm recommendation and never held constant speed. I've never owned a bike that had an engine failure or used excessive oil.

FWIW - I've been closely associated with a KTM dealer for many years, in the last 8 years typical break in for off-road competition bikes (2 or 4 stroke) has been 4 10-15 minute heat cycles under aggressive throttle with restricted rpm, then 20 or so miles using some restraint, then enjoy the bike. This has resulted in no failures with 10 close friends who race full off-road seasons in AA and Expert classes. Accurate jetting and frequent oil changes were the norm in all cases.

Remember Shane Watt's season with KTM when they launched their "Ready to Race" makreting program? Shane raced the "GNCC" circuit on one of every KTM model that season. KTM shipped the crated bikes to the race site, Shane did the uncrate/assembly on site, heat cycled the bikes and ran the event the next day. He won a staggering number of events with no engine failures.

blah, blah, blah.............it will be a never ending debate

I'll stick with initial heat cycling, hard throttle under load, and restricted rpm methods.
