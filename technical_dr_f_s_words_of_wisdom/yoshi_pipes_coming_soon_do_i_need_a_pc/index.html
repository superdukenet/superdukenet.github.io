Akra Map is very rich, I've seen up to about -27 figures in PCIII maps to negate the richness of the akra map in certan parts of it.

Tuneboy vs PCIII is a different sort of argument as they're both quite different.

The PCIII only connects after the ECU and so it just intercepts the signal and adds to the values for the fuel before it hits the injectors.

A Tuneboy actually changes the map in the ECU itself and can also make other changes, I believe this includes adjusting your rev limiter, ignition advance, turning o2 sensors on or off and maybe some other bits and pieces.

There are some benefits to the extra options in the Tuneboy but there are less tuning centers that know how to use them and support hasn't been the best for some members. They are cheaper than PCIIIs as Tuneboy is essetially just a piece of software that runs off your own laptop with an appropriate cable to connect to your ecu.

PCIIIs are known by all tuners and also gives you other options that are not possible with the tuneboy such as the ability to connect a quickshifter straight into it so you don't need to purchase a seperate ignition module or additional wiring loom, there are some other accessories available too depending on how far you want to go.

Both devices allow you to swap any predefined maps you may have depending on what you require at the time, this requires a laptop (or a long cable from your PC)

PCIII is a simple easy solution to just optimise your fuelling but if you want to play with other options like ingnition timing and have a tuner that is willing to work with it then Tuneboy could be the way to go. Decision is yours really.

You can get rid of the SAS to reduce popping and backfiring, it's not recommended for std exhaust as it also helps the cat but for slip ons it can be removed, no problem.

Most exhausts will still pop occassionally but the SAS does it intentionally as a result of its function so removing it will reduce them considerably.

So fit your slip ons, block off the sas and do the 15 min reset if that's how you want to do it.

I preferred the akra map but you can always try that later if you want to.
