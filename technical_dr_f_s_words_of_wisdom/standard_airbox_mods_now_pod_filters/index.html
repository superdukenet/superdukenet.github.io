tripoddave

2011-10-22 21:48:00 UTC
It will make more power but by opening the side pods you are letting unfiltered air into the airbox.
If you aren't bothered about filtering the air: remove the airbox altogether and gain as much as a DNA Stage III.
Alternatively the cheap method is to fit a 2nd air filter into the top of the existing airbox: worked just fine for me and gave me about 5bph.
Buy a flat air filter: cut suitable hole in top of airbox and attach using self tapping screws.
Don't forget to put the covers back in place!

I think this could be a problem unless you fabricate a rain shield in front of the filter stacks.
A lot of people used to put Ramair foam filters on Guzzi s and they always stuck out slightly so got wet in the rain.

This only seemed a problem if the bike was parked out so the filter got soaked. I never saw any engine water damage or evidence of liquid water in the carbs BUT the filters seemed to block up with the water droplets on the oil so it would let enough air through for starting in the cold then falter and die as soon as the revs rose. Took the filters off and they started straight away; had 2 or 3 brought in like that.

2011-11-09 17:06:00 UTC
Dyno session done and dusted.

Pretty interesting day, learned a few things. The guy at FBM Motorsport was superb. Let me in for a few power runs and show'd me how it all goes down.
He preps the Triumphs that won this years superstock cup championship.

The bike had been running like a bag of shit with the pods on, zero PC3 map, and the Akra KM610AK050E002M (no 02, no baffles) map in the ecu. KTM666 kindly hooked me up with a decent map from his bike from when it was mapped with the DNA mk3. Turns out this map wasn't far off at all.

Bike went in and ran 113.78bhp and 71.97lbs as it was. After the dyno work it's now 117.60 and 73.51.

We did a run with the baffles in and it lost 3bhp

He mapped it at 5% throttle openings right through the rev range. Revving the bike now it feels like it revs super quick.

Double checked with him and this is RWBHP

We had a root around the pods when it was on the dyno with the fans blowing and my side panel mods have worked. When we put our hand up under the tank we felt real good airflow directly to each pod from either side. And it flows dwon the tank over the engine so should help cooling a bit.

Very happy with this TBH. On initial impression it doesn't seem that great compared the 130bhp that everyone uses as a target. But we compared it to two other Superdukes that had been in previously. Both 05-06 models. One bike ran at 104bhp and the other at 106bhp. So with the differences between dynos etc i can only compare like for like on the same dyno. KTM quote 120bhp for a standard bike. That will be at the flywheel, so the bikes dynoing at about 105RWBHP works out correct to me.

The flies and shafts give about 3-4bhp once mapped. And it'll have gained another 3-4bhp with the pods and mapping, and the same with the cans.

What i cant understand is how the non SDR bikes are hitting 130RWBHP. Thats 25bhp over standard with only an exhaust system and flies/airbox. Cant get my head around it. To get 25bhp out a motor it would need head work and cams.

What was interesting was the map the KTM66 gave me was from a DNA MK3 session. In the air/fuel chart it shows the bike goes lean as soon at the throttle is cracked wide open, and then goes rich at the top. Might be way off but this suggests to me the pods have a little less flow than the DNA MK3, whick we know has prob the best airflow.

Pondering aside. Happy with the results. I've not had a chance to ride it yet as it's getting dark here at 4pm and the bike doesn't have any lights. It had good power as standard with just cans. The flies n shafts made it better, really noticable better, and on the pre dyno test run with the pods it felt superb,like a different beast altogether.

Best part is it's not about 100% throttle top end power runs with the bike, its the way it makes it power. I felt good on track and road before. The graphs look excellent, good torque curve from 6k-9k, but look at that linear power curve making power all the way to the 9950rmp limiter. Cant help but think we might have got a bit more with a 10100rpm limit but thats a bit too high i feel. Good safe power and reliability as it is. Cannot wait to get it out on hot sticky tarmac next year

So for 30 pods, 2 breather filter, 1 y piece, and whip out the flies and shafts it gains about 10bhp. Worth it for anyone on a buget i'd say.
