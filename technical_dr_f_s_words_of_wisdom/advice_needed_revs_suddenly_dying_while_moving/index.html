Advice needed: Revs suddenly dying while moving.

cjc

2009-06-28 11:24:00 UTC
Hi guys,

A bit of a worrying problem reared it's head today while I was riding into town today on my 06 SD (17000km, round akras, akra map).

Basically, I was riding down a stretch of 80 kmh (50mph) main road in fairly heavy traffic, sitting on fairly constant revs. Probably around 3500rpm or so, not 100% sure. Anyway, all of a sudden, I felt the bike loose power and heard the revs dropping, almost like the feeling when you're running out of fuel (have never done this on the SD before). I accelerated, and the bike hesitated for a second, before it 'cleared out' and began running fine again.

I had just filled up with fuel (BP Ultimate 98)about 20km ago, so it definitely wasnt running low on fuel. It worried me quite a bit, but I continued on, as it felt fine after that.

Probably about 10 minutes later, I was pulling away from being stationary at a traffic light, when the bike hesitated and the revs dropped again, just like before. I clutched it and gave it a decent amount of throttle, and it felt like it 'cleared out' again. I told my riding mates that if it happened again, I was pulling over. I tried keeping the revs up higher than I normally do in town riding (gear lower than normal), I guess from habit from the old days of fouled plugs in 2 stroke MX bikes.

About another 10 minutes later, pulling away from a traffic light like before, it did it again. Same thing, I clutched, dialled in revs and it kept going. I pulled into the BP servo across the intersection and stopped. Wasn't really sure where to begin looking, being in a carpark, short on tools and time, and was about 60km from home. Bike still idled fine, free-revved cleanly etc.

We decided to continue on into the city, and in the next 20 minutes it took us to reach where we were going, it happened one more time.

The bike cooled down to zero bars on the temp gauge while we were in the city, and we then decided to head home via the twisties (I was a bit nervous about riding up there with this problem, but hoped getting out and away from the stop/start/constant speed traffic might help). We stopped one more time to have lunch before we hit the twisties, and the bike had not faulted again since leaving the city thankfully.

We hit the hills roads, I rode like normal and the bike did not miss a beat...in fact it loved it, like normal We snaked our way North through the hills for about 80km or so, before hitting the highway for home. The problem had not re-occured since leaving the city thankfully, but now I am racking my brain and searching these forums for possible causes/remedies in case it happens again.

So far (I'm not all that mechanically-minded unfortunately) I am thinking it could be one of the following:

1. Dirty fuel filter
2. Fuel Pump playing funny buggers
3. My battery is on it's last legs (extremely sluggish to turn over when I left to go on the ride). I read somewhere on the forum that a battery that is low on charge can cause the sensors etc. to play up/give false readings to the ECU. I will be getting a new battery and trickle charger soon.
4. Spark plugs or coils stuffed. My mate who I was riding with today (Diesel Mechanic) said that at low revs when the engine needs a strong spark, if the coils or plugs were on their way out it may stutter/hesitate for a bit.
5. The fact that I was riding with reasonably constant revs/speed in traffic and it was just a 'character fault' with the SD. Eg. Many of the fellas on this site reporting stalling at lights, under hard brakes etc.


Many apologies for the long post, just wanted to make sure I included as much information as I could that might help someone to help me out.

If anyone has had the same problem, or if anyone knows what I should do, your help would be greatly appreciated.

Many thanks,

Ben

Running the ECU, fuel pump, lights and all the other stuff takes a fair bit of power.

So if you suspect your battery is on last legs then I would start with that.

If the battery isn't taking and holding its charge, then running at low revs puts alot of strain on the alternator; which is trying its best to charge a duff battery and running all the other stuff.

For the cost of a battery (especially if its a few years old) I would change sooner rather than later.
