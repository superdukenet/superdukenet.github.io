Hi All,

I find that if I gas it (ie: a quick rev), then it is more prone to stall. Unlike other
bikes (like Gixxers), the SD doesn't need to be gased up anyway. It has the
power there when you need it, and there is no need for any sort of "engine
preparation". For me, it took a few months to break the habit

The starter motor problem. Yes, I find that very embarassing. It always likes to do
it when there is a large group of people standing around my bike. It's a real
passion killer ;(... however, it's only an aesthesic problem. When starting the
motor (only when it's hot -- as it doesn't do it when it's cold), I find that if I quickly
press the starter motor button (ie: hold it down for less than a 1/4 or a second), then
problem is almost elliminated.

The above two problems don't worry me. It's power and superior handling more
than make up for it's short falls. However, I can understand how it might "get" to
some people. Other than that,.. as the owner of this site once told me -- "stop sook'in
bitch, and ride it" (or words to that effect).
