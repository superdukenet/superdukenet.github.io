A few months ago I noticed the idle on my '06 was hanging out around 900rpm and would occasionally stall when coming to a stop. This has become progressively worse over the last 5k miles. The idle also intermittently increases to 3-5k rpm's but if I kill the ignition and restart it stops. Now even when I hold the rev's to 2k rpm while stopped it frequently dies.

Yesterday she was consistently surging while maintaining low to mid road speeds with the throttle only slightly opened. Cracking into it feels normal and the bike seems to have full power (4th gear wheelies no prob). When trying to maintain slower speeds below 45mph the bike bucks and surges. Fuel mileage has decreased to 25mpg and a significant amount of soot appeared on the rear fender near the exhaust, obviously running very rich. The FI light blinks occasionally. At one point the FI light stayed on for a few minutes and the bike seemed normal, idling at 1400rpm, no surging. FI light went out and problems immediately returned.

What I've tried since first experiencing the idle problems to no avail-
-Researching the forum
-Leo Vince exhaust added
-Remap at dealer
-15 minute reset
-Cleaned BGC and all other connectors under chin fairing
-Reinstalled stock exhaust
-15 minute reset
-Reinstalled Leo's
-Cleaned BGC again and all other connectors under the chin fairing
-Yesterday ran a tank of Seafoam through

In a nutshell the symptoms are
-idles at 900rpm
-stalls coming to a stop
-intermittent high idling
And yesterday in addition to above
-surging at small throttle openings
-excessive soot and rich exhaust gas
-25mpg
-FI light flashes occasionally

WTF is wrong with my beloved bike?

Awaiting SAS block off plates and O2 sensor eliminator plugs.
Considering fuel filter, balancing TB's, adjusting TPS, fire bombing.

This is definitely a BGC issue. Thus is the exact thing my bike was doing down to the very last little detail. It was moisture in the BGC.

The only other connectors that could possibly cause this exact senario are the injector connectors as Mrz32 has suggested or the connector that plugs straight into the ECU. The BGC is the interface between the ECU and injectors so is my bet as the culprit but moisture in either of the other two connectors will cause the same issue. The BGC is the wonkiest of the three connector types so again is the most likely culprit (especially if PCIII is mounted).

Do the search and follow the instructions I've laid out. Think my original post on the subject was back in late 08 or early 09.

Not sure if your SD is the same as my SMR.

1/ O2 sensors don't normally run at idle, the SAS system does from cold start for emission control.
2/ Using tune ECU what codes are listed?

UPDATE

Poor girl has been stuffed in the corner til a few days ago when I pulled her out to do a water pump seal and starter back torque limiter replacement with new engine cover since the old one had split and was leaking oil profusely. Likely caused because the limiter was no longer limiting since I had it welded together so the torque was transferring to the engine case cover which gave.

My gf recently got a BMW F800S and while doing some research on these fine machines it seems they are susceptible to similar idle woes with no known fix. After reading hundreds of posts and several threads I found one where a guy had adjusted the throttle body stop screw to increase the idle just a hair, curing the problem.

So this evening I popped the left airbox cover off and low and behold, there lies the throttle body stop screws (or whatever you call them). I watched as I opened the throttle and the forward linkage moved a couple millimeters off the screw before the rear linkage picked up. This means the butterfly is closing too far with the throttle closed. So with great difficulty I squeezed a wrench and 2.5mm allen key in and turned the screw up by moving the lock nut about 3/4 of a turn down. FIred her up and she idled up to 2k (hasn't done that in a while) and slowly came back down to around 1400 rpm. Flicked the throttle several times where it would normally die upon returning to idle yet it did not. Turned it off and tried again. Same thing. Buttoned everything back up, tinkered with some other stuff and did it again. No more idle problems! So far...Need to ride it still but for the last several months idling longer than a minute is not something it has done til now.

This is the screw with yellow paint on it.
