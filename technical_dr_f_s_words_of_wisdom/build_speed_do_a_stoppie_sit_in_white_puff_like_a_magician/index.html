Fazer, received your email and have gone over this thread. Thanks for reaching out to us and giving us a link to this. Honestly there is nothing at all that the Rottweiler Intake System would do to cause this. We have approximately 1200+ in current use and not one return ever so if this were common we would cease to exist. Seeing that you had a mechanic perform the installation, I can't ask you details about what was done but I can tell you if anyone doesn't read the instructions, it is usually (we have found) the bike shops because they are on a time clock and it's not their bike. (Assuming this was in your case) Usually the owners doing the install seem to take more time although it is not very complicated.

Here is what we are thinking:
1. They did not install the breather tube correctly. The check valve, should allow air out, but not in, meaning the white ball should be on the bottom with the spring on top. If it is puking that bad they may have left the ball and spring out altogether by accident. conversely, if it is installed backwards, in any case with or without our intake, it will blow the rubber gaskets on the valve cover and puke oil as well. So, the bottom line is, the hose must be shortened about 36 to 50 mm so it doesn't kink in its new location and the check valve should be from bottom to top: Bottom aluminium piece with seal, white check ball, spring (cone pointing down), top aluminium piece with lines machined in it all sandwiched in the nylon plate. Seeing this there is a high chance they did it wrong. This happens from time to time and it is almost always when a dealer installs it for whatever reason. Maybe the one above.

2. You blew a balancer seal. This is the second most common problem and if you still had your stock box on it would take you longer to notice it as it would consume the oil and fill the stock filter with the rest.

In our instructions here http://www.rottweilerperformance.com/Downloads.html we recommend that you route a piece of the unused hose inside the filter pointing into the intake. This gets the breather line back where it was intended so the engine will consume any oil that mists into there. On most bike the customers leave them off and just mists a tiny bit of oil into the underside of the filter. The filter has a fire proof liner and it's harmless, but in your case, I still think you need to inspect the valve. Fortunately, with our intake it is super easy. Just pop the tank, take off the filter with the 1/4 turn fasteners, remove the 1/4 turn trumpets, unplug the map sensor and lift off the base plate. Take two 19mm open end wrenches and make sure the check valve is as explained above. If it's not, you have other issues that have nothing at all to do with the addition of the filter element I can promise you that.

Please let us know what you find and sorry about the chicks. We have no warranties in place to cover the loss of possible booty unfortunately...
