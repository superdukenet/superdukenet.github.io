Ok does it start? I assume not. And also assume the battery is Ok. I'm a bit worried that it has never turned over since the barrels were off.
When you say it only turns over with the clutch in, I assume you mean turns over normally. If that is so then the resistance must be coming from the drive train. As you say it is not showing that it's in neutral. As you know the acid test for neutral is to roll the bike without the clutch in. No resistance is good, resistance of any sort is bad. Assuming all is Ok then the question is what's up with the neutral indicator (indeed is this your only real concern). There is no easy check here. It could be bad contact on the gear position sensor down under the clutch slave cylinder (it has a sliding copper contact) or it could be a bad connector on the way up to the ECU. Given that the gear position sensor is prone to get rubbish from the clutch and chain down there it might be a good place to look first. It only has an O-ring to keep crap out. If theres a problem on the line to the ECU it probably goes via the BGC so I'd check there next.

Just looked at the wiring diagram for you. At the 2-3, neutral switch (which I have called the gear position sensor) the neutral wire is green and black. It works by earthing the ECU on that line. You could just try earthing that line at the connector and see if the light comes on. If it doesn't it proves nothing but if it does then the sliding contact is not working properly. Hope this helps.

If you can send more info.

Edit: When you say it only turns over with the clutch in I assume you mean that the clutch switch is disabling the start because the ECU believes the bike is in gear. If you earth that green and black line I bet the thing will turn over (even if it is in gear so be careful).
