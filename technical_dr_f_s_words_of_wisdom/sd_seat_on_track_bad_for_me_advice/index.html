This is obviously déjà vu all over again, but anyway..

You don't want a seat that's sticky as it restricts your movement. If anything, you want the tank to be (to a certain extent). Fit proper help. I prefer the TechSpec Snakeskin over StompGrips, but either one works. TankTredz notsomuch for me.

As for your arms, they are a sure sign you're not using your lower body/core properly. After a track session, you should feel fatigue in your legs. Your arms should be very well rested. Essentially, what they are for is counter steering, counter counter steering (or whatever it might be called in Ye Olde English, i.e. picking the bike up again), throttle control, clutch control and braking. CSS should have taught you this (it is, if I can remember correctly, one of the first things they teach you day one).
