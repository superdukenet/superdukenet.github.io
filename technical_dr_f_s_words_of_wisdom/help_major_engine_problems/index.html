UPDATE:


I just got off the phone with Todd Jones who is the manager of customer service for KTM N.A. Some of you will be stunned by some of the answers I got; others won't.

First, KTM is waiting for a repair estimate on my engine to determine if they're going to repair it or replace it. That is, they do not routinely replace defective engines on bikes a tad over 2 months old and with only 1700 miles on them.

Second, with regard to extending my warranty given my trust issues about the reliability of this bike, they don't do this either. They might extend the warranty some if the bike is in the shop for an extended period of time but only in relationship to time spent in the shop. However, should my engine failout of warranty, "they will consider the service history".

So, exactly where is this great customer sevice and support I've been hearing about?! Based upon my experience thus far, it appears to be a myth. Therefore, I won't be buying an Adventure from my dealer as I had planned to do this Fall. Clearly, KTM is not looking for me to be a repeat customer. I find this rather strange in that I paid $14,000 for the bike and probably have another $4500 in KTM accessories on it.

I suspect that it going to get very ugly before it gets resolved to my satisfaction. I'll keep y'all posted on how this plays itself out...[/u]
