What dipstick?

Just keep an eye on your silicon tube and see if it stays ok. The point of having it's so you check it,...more likely than you'd check a dipstick?

Have a look here:

Is it what they call OIL RISING PIPE CPL. ? (33)
And the filler cap is on the other side?
The filler cap is PLUG CPL. M24X3 ...so maybe possible to find a dipstick from another bike that fits...but the dipstick probably wouldn't be long enough.
You could epoxy an aluminium rod into the filler cap. ?

PS: I looked at a few other bikes there, clutch covers, even 1995 250/300, it seems like all of them have the same M24x3 filler plug, but no dipsticks - I think dipsticks died out in the 70s, or 80s, they used sight glasses instead. btw, searching for "oil sight" on advrider turned up a lot of results about sight glasses blowing out, mostly on BMW GS's I think.

Silicone isn't a good choice for an oil line ... even if under little-to-know pressure. A different material, silicon is 100% gasoline resistant (see remark above about using glass). http://www.mcmaster.com/#=f7edbk

Extremely high temperature resistance, and few materials are as oil resistant.
