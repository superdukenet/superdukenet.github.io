The only problem with HID bulbs is they have a very high initial current draw, which then gradually drops off as the arc is struck to then use less than a normal bulb.

Like this >

However, with 07/08 > SD's the dipped bulb is on all the time (ie you can't turn it off).

So could this cause something bad to happen !? ie drawing too much current just when you need it to start the engine, especially on a cold morning.

Some sort of delay like 30seconds would be good, to allow you to start the engine then turn the lights on.

This would give the added benefit of 4amps less draw when starting compared to a std set-up.

Yes, but the biggest part of that initial draw will have already happened as soon as you turn the ignition on while the bike is still doing its start up sequence before you even hit the power button and then the draw is only small so wouldn't really make much difference, 6 amps is nothing compared to everything else on the bike that's running at the same time and the brief amount of time it draws high shouldn't make a difference either on a properly maintained battery.
