who besides me also like the Yamaha mt-01

BadBikerBob

2014-07-16 00:28:00 UTC
Unfortunately Yamaha never brought the Yamaha mt-01 to the states (we are too unsophisticated) but I have always longed to own one. Very much my style of looks and performance. I love the idea of tons of torque on a machine that handles well. An engine that makes all the power where I used it 98% of the time. Plus being air cooled and never having to adjust the valves is the s#$t to me. But I must be a rare bread since that bike wasn't very popular. Too many guys read specs and have to have the most power and lightest weight with highest top speed. I want a bike that works with the type of riding I do. Found out long ago that I'm no Nicky Hayden and all the accessories and bling in the world doesn't make me a better rider. And I ride, I don't meet up at coffee shops or at the pub and cruise down the street to show off my bike with all the stuff I put on it so everyone knows how "bad ass" I must be.

AGRO!

2014-07-16 04:56:00 UTC
Yes I liked the looks of the MT-1. But the high center of mass and overall bike weight are drawback to the performance levels wanted by any customer. Although the bike has a beam perimeter frame, upgraded suspension and brakes compared to the V-max, it still missed it's mark. Since my 84' Ninja, I have never paid for, or felt the need, to do a valve adjustment for any four valve vehicle I've owned, car or bike. The MT-01 is still in demand in UK on the resale market, there is a passion for them. MT-01 is one for the wall, in poster form.
I also wouldn't consider an air cooled bike again. The reliability and performance of all of my water cooled, fuel injected machines have proven themselves to me.
