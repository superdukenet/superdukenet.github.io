For anyone not wanting to listen to my KTM woes, stop reading now !

I have just got my 07 SD back in my hands after 7 weeks in the dealers.

This follows problems since October when the bike would cut out on one cylinder for no apparant reason.

1st time it happened it was collected under the KTM recovery scheme and send back to the dealer. They couldn't find anything wrong but thought it was the fuel pump, so that was replace.

I got the bike back, then a coupld of weeks later the same problem, cuts out on one cylinder (just like slamming your brakes on in the outside lane of the motorway, or overtaking, not recommended!).

So it gets picked up for the 2nd time, this time the dealer manages to recreate the problem but they don't know why, they think its the Alarm, so that comes off and I get the bike back.

Another 2 or 3 weeks later it happens again, once again KTM recovery pick it up but this time they say rhey won't pick it up if this happens again ! The dealer this time thiks its the ECU thats cracked but after some discussion between them and the Main Office (and me, by now getting extremely pissed off) they decide to replace the main wiring loom as well !

After waiting the best part of 7 weeks I got the bike back yesterday.

I am hoping and praying that this is now sorted but I am not particularly happy with a new bike needing this sort of attention before its got 3000 miles on it.

Put it this way, I won't be buying an RC8 any time soon !!!

Intermittent problems can be very hard to diagnose and add to the fact that it is (or could be) an electrical intermittent problem can make a technician pull his hair out. These types of problems you have to be very patient with, 7 weeks is totally unacceptable for them to replace a fuel pump. I would place most blame on your dealers inability to take corrective action to make sure that they fix the problem before returning the bike to you in the first go around. Remember the Superduke is engineered and assembled by humans and humans make mistakes. This is why we have warranties. While working for KTM, (technical and warranty) I experienced first hand every single 950/990 warranty problem ever submitted by a US dealer and yet I still went out and purchased my own. Do I wish any problems onto my bike? No, of course not but I know if I would go out and buy any other brand of motorcycle the potential is always there for problems.

I hpoe it turns out ok for you but intermittent faults are the worst kind for everyone involved.
I was a mechanic on a large fleet of cars for years and every so often you would get a fault like that. It can be something so minor or a complete one off fault, the car would often end up getting passed around hoping someone would stumble on it.
The trouble is if the fault does not re appear then you have to give the vehicle back as you can't just keep hold of it and use it.
It is sometimes good to have a doner vehicle so the shop can swap parts over and see if the fault appears on the doner bike
