Hey bic,

Better off mounting the PCIII yourself as you will do a better job simply because it's your bike and you'll take pride in what you're doing more than anyone else.

I would sggest you pick up some dilectric grease (Halfords maybe) from a good auto parts store and apply it to each female contact of the connector (stock and PCIII side) to avoid the problems I've encountered. You'll thank me later.

Side note: You're gonna love the Motobox (even with the stock map). Your bike is gonna rip! It'll run smoother as well below 3K RPM.

Also, the resistor value you need for the SAS removal is a 1K ohm 1/4 watt resistor. Any good electronics store (not Curries) should be able to get you sorted.

Good luck, John

I agree with Bigash (If the pc111 is brand new then it will come with all fuel perameters set to zero so the bike will not see any change in the fueling values and will run as the standard ecu is set!). I recently installed a PCIII and Motobox following SAS, canister and O2 sensor removal and resistor install. My PCIII came with a map with all cells set to zero (I checked using a PC). So nothing should be added or subtracted from your ECU fuel settings. Install the PCIII and ride it to the dyno.

Battery is so you can access the PCIII without a key, without turning on the ignition.

You need a PC or a Mac running BootCamp with PC software installed to load PCIII maps into the PC module.

Used 1/4 watt 22,000 ohm resistors. Worked fine. Bought resistors online in USA http://www.vetco.net/index.php and Eagle Day for connectors to make it look factory http://www.eagleday.com/ampconnectors.html

Good luck with the custom map!
