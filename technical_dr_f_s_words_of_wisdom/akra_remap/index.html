As said, it's important to get the Akra map with the cans.

A dealer asking that kind of money to load a fuel map should be de-authorised by KTM immediately. Or at least get both his arms broken in a dark alley... This is a 5 minute job and the fuel maps are free to download from the KTM dealer website.

I would complain to KTM in your country about this. I don't think KTM wants their dealers to treat their customers like this. But where did you buy the Akra cans? Remapping comes together with the cans.

After loading a new fuel map it is CRITICAL to do the reset/calibrate procedure on the ECU. This is done by letting the bike idle for 16 minutes standing UPRIGHT (not on the side stand, not sitting on it) and WITHOUT touching the throttle. With a cold engine this can be done anywhere. With a warm or hot engine this should be done with cooling, for instance on a dyno with the blower on.

This procedure is optional
