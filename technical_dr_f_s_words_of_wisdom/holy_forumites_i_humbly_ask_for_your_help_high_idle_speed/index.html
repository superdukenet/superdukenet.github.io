don't dismiss the line too easily, once (a long while ago) i thought my clutch was going, turned out to be bad cable. it would stick sometimes, not always, and if i pulled the clutch few times, it would be OK for a day or two... There was a small crack where the line bent , and just a bit of rust inside...moving it few times might bring enough grease to stop the sticking for a bit... eventually it comes back, and with time gets worse.

if the line is stuck, the servo on the throttle boddy cannot move while the bike is doing the 'self test', which could produce the sound you're hearing. the servo's there are not that strong, you might not feel much resistence with your hand, but too much for the servo?

Keep in mind it's all just a guesses from my side, not being able to see the bike and all. This is - to me - the simplest possible situation, and more often then not, it is the simple stuff. There are plenty of other possibilities, but it's a good bet that it will be something on your throttle bodies - loose/lost nut, twig or bug lodged between the stop and the screw, or in the spring, etc; it could be more serious, like burnt/damaged servo or servo gear, but i doubt it is the ECU. It would not explain why the sound around the throttle bodies doesnt stop as it should. Same with temp sensor - i'm not even sure there is one, but all the self test sounds should stop even if the engine was not started.

Hope my rant is of some help, cheers!
