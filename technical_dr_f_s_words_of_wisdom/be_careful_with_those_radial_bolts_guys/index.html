roamer

2012-09-27 21:56:00 UTC
No, it's not true.
Any fastener that is in aircraft assembly is traceable to the foundry that made the bar stock.
All steps to produce that fastener are inspected, approved and certified by the FAA.
From the day that material was forged to the minute it was placed in service on the aircraft it is all traceable.
I'm not trying to jack this thread, just trying to correct mis information.
If you were referring to my use of the term aircraft parts, I never said aircraft quality.
The aviation industry and military just seem to be the ones who take the time and expense to do it right the first time, not add it later when the first hole fails.
Even the Ohlins Moto GP forks have a Cromolly guide bushing and thread insert for the radial mount brakes.
