Lower airbox left in place. Original screws are used to fix aluminium base plate, additional nuts and washers are added to have enough thickness screwing them in.


Temperature sensor is placed into a transparent polycarbonate snorkel closing plate.


Air filter seal is cut out from KTM original paper air filter. Shafts and secondary butterflies are removed.


RC8 stacks. They fit perfectly but do not lock completely (razor blade correction on locking edges might help).


The filter and base plate is glued together, no screws. Filter itself is rated for 350 bhp, so is more than enough for breathing this engine.
