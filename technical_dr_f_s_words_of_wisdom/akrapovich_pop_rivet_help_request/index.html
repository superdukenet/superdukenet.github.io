2010-01-30 12:33:00 UTC
I'm taking apart my Akra silencers so I can have a look at the packing and change the titanium sleeves over for carbon ones.

They are fabricated with pop rivets which are easy to drill out and replace. But there are three fasteners, one on the rear band and two on the front, which are different - see photo. I think they are used sometimes to attach the heat shields. They are turned steel plugs with an internal thread.

Does anyone know how to get them out without damaging them? Or how they are fixed in? I've tried unscrewing them by putting in a bolt but it just reaches the bottom and the whole thing turns - it's neither a left or right handed thread.

I can drill them out but will have to find another way to put the cans back together - the hole is much bigger than a pop rivet shaft.

Thanks.

So I rang Rivetnut Technology Systems Ltd and found out that this other fixing is called a Rivetnut and the specific part number is RFCA-05030-G. It is aluminium with an M5 thread and a 7mm diameter shaft.

Unit 5 Bridgegate Business Park
Gatehouse Way
Aylesbury
Buckinghamshire
HP19 8XN

http://www.rivetnut.co.uk/contact.htm

They sold me 100 for about a fiver and I bought a hand tool for fixing them in for Â£28. I thought that it was something worth having - now I can go around my house drilling holes through stuff and mounting M5 fixing points everywhere.

Big Ash, if you want to repack your cans I could post you the tool and some rivets if you want.

Update on the CF sleeves is that I talked to PJ Engineering who are now sending me two lengths of 115.75mm internal diameter tube which is apparently what the Akras require. I've got them to cut the tubes to length because it's not easy to do neatly without a band saw and the ends of the samples I have are a bastard for injecting micro shards of carbon into your hands and fingers. Decided to shorten the silencers by 35mm so they don't stick out so much at the back but probably not increase the sound much.
