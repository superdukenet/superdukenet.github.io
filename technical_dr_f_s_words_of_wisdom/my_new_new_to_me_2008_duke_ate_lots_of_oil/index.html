Mate it's a dry sump get your head arround it and ride it instead of checking it every 10 minutes and loosing sleep over it.
oil level needs to be checked after engine is well warmed up and not too long after you shut it down. Sometimes a sticky check valve (the screwed on thingy on the left side of the oil tank which connect the hose out of it) leaks a bit and lets the oil run back in to the sump when the engine is not running.
That is why that needs to be cleaned and checked when you do an oil change.
If you find out later that indeed it eats a bit of oil you can change to another brand of synthetic as it makes a difference and also take it out for a good trash, this normally stops it. (yes it does )
