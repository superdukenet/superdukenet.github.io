Don't know what the regulations are in Aus but here in the UK noise is not something that many MOT tests cover. It's a grey issue. I've got to taking my bike to the MOT and sticking the Akro baffles on the seat with a bit of tape and saying, "Hey guys, if you need to fit these to pass the bike here they are". But I always pick the bike up and they haven't fitted them and they shrug and say, "No worries, don't need to do that". I also used to have a Ducati with home made carbon silencers....they had no EU certificate, no street legal status and I always passed the test. One guy who passed it said, "They are meant to sound like that aren't they!"

So... maybe you should just take the bike in and get it passed.

Here in Denmark they're going nuts about noise... I had an zx9r with an Akrapovic can, and the first two places wouldn't accept it, they told me it was too noisy and would only accept the bike if it had an original silencer on.. I even put in a DB killerand it was quieter, but they test the bike at 6000RPM, wtf.. everything is noisy up there... one guy just reved the engine once, till about 9000rpm, and then said "yeah its too loud, FAIL!" and failed the bike... shithead

Another guy even told me I could had borrowed his silencer from the zx6r he had, mount it and then he would let the bike pass.. wtf arghh..

In the end, I looked up the Akrapovic website that had some other end-pipe than mine, but on the zx9r bike.. and I put in another picture, changed the text a bit so the model years were correct.. and I got the papers from the initial year my model was introduced in Denmark etc.. lot of paper work, but in the end, the papers said that the Akra silencer was 97db, while the bike was allowed to do 98db (with a 3db overhead, because of "tear and wear on the exhaust")... I arrived 3 minutes in closing time on a friday, and he didnt want to spend too much time with the papers.. I just convinced him that the stuff was right, he looked briefly at it, and said they were okay.. damn what a stupid hassle
