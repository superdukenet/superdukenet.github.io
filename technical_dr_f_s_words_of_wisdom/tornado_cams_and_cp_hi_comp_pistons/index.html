OK, if were chasing HP here lets be sensible.

We want a decent hike in HP but also reliablity and longativity so any tuning is a 1 off cost and doesnt involve expensive rebuilds.

The way i see it and the order of doing things (and i could be wrong) is as follows -

1. High flow air filter element or box, depending on bike between 1 & 4HP
2. High flow exhaust system and silencers, depending on original design between 2 & 8HP
3. Port & polish cylinder heads, possible skim, again depending on engine, between 3 & 6HP
4. Blue print, between 1 & 3HP
5. Compression changes, CC hikes, cams etc should all come at the end of a list and only when chasing top HP.

All the above of course will require fuel injection/timing changes at each stage of tune.

The real question ishow big is your wallet
