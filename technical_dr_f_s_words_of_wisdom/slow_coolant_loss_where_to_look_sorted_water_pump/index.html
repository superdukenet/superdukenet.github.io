Hi
My (new to me) 990 is loosing coolant, I didn't notice at first as the rate of loss is slow, it becomes more obvious as the level gets nearer to bottom of the catch tank.

In the picture below is the amount that has been lost after a 55 mile ride.



Strange thing is the level stayed the same throughout the ride and only dropped once the bike had cooled down.

So does this behaviour point to anything in particular?

I've looked at all the hoses I can see and although some look a bit crusty up inside the pipe, there is no obvious signs of coolant leaks.
Coolant looks clean in radiator, oil looks clear in the sight tube.
Rad cap looked ok - rubber seals are intact. A little bit of build up there (like Calcium) but nothing much. The inner metal face of the rad filler neck could be better (some deposits on the mating face) so I guess I'll give that a clean up with some scotch bright but I think if it was leaking it would start to fill the catch tank?, which it doesn't.
What should I do next?

Bike has about 15,000 miles on the clock and it looks like the water pump has been off at some point, also some hoses have non-standard jubilee clips on them so I'd say the water pump has at least been inspected at some point and maybe had the seals replaced.

Should I take the clutch cover off to check for cream cheese?

More than likely waterpump seal; worth changing the whole lot ie seal, bearings and shaft (shaft was updated at some point).
I would also give the system a really good flush, as mine had casting sand (fine silt) in and took 20k miles before the header tank stopped filling with sand. I think this is what knackered the first seal.

I would guess the correct way would be to do all at the same time, but last time I changed the seal / shaft / bearings and then checked all was good before changing oil. I think if you had a lot of coolant going into oil I would change all at the same time as the danger is the coolant causes the oil filter to deform with shit consequences.

Also, not sure how familiar you are with bleeding the system on this bike, worth looking up as its not like a normal bike.
