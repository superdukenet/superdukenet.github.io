2005 UK model (Orange, of course - shut it, Dr F ).
Sold with 3300 miles on it last week.

15/20 minute idle trick would always make the snatchiness go away for a few miles but it always came back again!

Did 2 days at Jerez (300+ miles) Oct 07. Since then it got better and only stalled twice (incidentally the 1st was after I picked it up after a month lay up and the battery barely started the engine so alternator must have been working over time). 2nd stall was out of the blue.

Last month added G2 300 cam and snatchiness got a lot better.

Now sold

OK, looks like the new KTM map is a dead end. After reviewing the thread, there are some commonalities:

1) The bike suffers from a lean condition between roughly 3000 and 5000 rpm, characterized by surging or inability to maintain steady throttle setting. This is common on twin butterfly EFI VTwins, one that V-Strom owners know all too well. According to the responses, owners who have added cans and richened the feuling with KTM or PCIII/Tuneboy maps report improvement in the condition.

The responsibility for this problemis lies squarely at the feet of the EPA and Euro Emissions and noise standards; motorcycles are tested for emissions and noise in this range, hence the necessity for bone lean fueling which leads to surging and other aforementioned problems. Let's also not forget the second set of butterflies...

2) The severity of the condition likely is related to the atmospheric conditions of temperature, elevation, and pressure of the owner.

3) Without an ECU hack or piggyback black box, the problem of injector shutoff on closed throttle at low rpms isn't going to go away. BTW, the injector shutoff is usually programmed into the aforementioned rev range to further help a good motor pass ridiculous emissions and noise standards

4) Let's get back to the secondary throttle body plate. Anyone doing datalogging or dyno testing, it would be helpful if you could pay attention to when the ECU begins to open the secondaries. Secondary plates are used as noise and power limiters. I'd speculate these plates don't start to fully open until at least 5000 rpm if not later. Also note that in V-Stroms, the secondary plates were also programmed to close at high rpm to limit power.

5) Gears 1, 2, and 3. Whatever EFI programming that contributes to snatch problems seems to be only active in the first three gears, also a common finding among EFI bikes. Unfortunately, these are the gears where delicate throttle response is most desireable, i.e. when heeled over in a fast sweeper at or near the apex and modulating traction requires a delicate hand on the throttle and hopefully a delicate response by the bike.

6) Best results so far seem to revolve around balancing the TBs, an Akra or Vince exhaust system with either KTM, PCIII, or Tuneboy remap, and changing to a smoother throttle cam. Things are also looking good for the Mar airbox; however, make no mistake, these changes won't rid the bike of any issues related to the programming of the ECU by KTM, but it may make them more bearable, much in the way that a handful of ibuprofen and a couple of espressos make a migraine tolerable.

Zenbiker, see the post in technical titled 2nd/3rd gear sensor. I connected and disconnected the sensor 4 times and rode the bike. no discernable differences felt by my ass or brain. No one responded.
This forum is great and drastically reduced the time it took me to sort my bike. The big problem is too many members approach problems with an unscientific approach-many variables and changes before any testing occurs.
I did this gear sensor work before I changed anything on a completely stock bike. IT MAKES NO DIFFERNCE TO THE WAY THIS BIKE RUNS.
the next thing I did was replace the O2 sensors with dynojets O2 eliminators. yes, that's right, I did not originally purchase a PCIII, you can purchase the eliminators for $15.00 each. This made a difference in the throttle snatch and the surging but did not cure either. I could feel the difference in the way the bike ran. it was slightly smoother.
Next, it was time for my first service. the dealer loaded the updated stock map. It also made a slight difference initially but then after about 2 runs the bike ran exactly as it had before the updated map. This temporary improvement was probably due to the ecu reset.
My next step was to balance the throttle bodies. This completely eliminated the surging between 3-5K rpms and made the snatchy throttle better as well.
I felt like the snatchy throttle was due to low fueling so I purchased the PCIII. I loaded map 925-001. This is an advanced map for a stock us bike. This improved the snatchy throttle but again didnt entirely eliminate it.
I added 2% more fuel in the low and mid rpm ranges using the power commander and this also made a small improvement in the snatchy throttle. I added another 2% and this also improved it but I believe the fueling was a bit too rich at this point. The richer the fueling the less abrupt the throttle and the opposite is also true. The leaner the fuel map the worse the throttle abruptness.
I really don't think anything is wrong with the bike at this point. I have eliminated 95% of the problems with snatchiness and surging and am now very happy with the bike.
I believe if everyone out there having problems with their machines would take a step by step approach, changing one variable at a time they may just find the answers to their individual problems.
I understand that we all may experience the problems differently but you must approach your situation scientifically or you just create frustration.
This bike is always going to have the abrupt throttle(have you read any of the stories of the 06 FZ1, that bike was unridable) but I think you can tune it to an acceptable level.
