I didn't get round to posting about my chain.... It was due for replacement along with the sprockets (original - 17000 miles) it had an annoying tight spot. But I had a weekend trip away to the North East planned (Berwick on Tweed and Lindisfarne - which was most pleasant) and decided to get the chain replaced on my return.
Anyway, at 2pm on the Friday afternoon, heading out of Haydon Bridge, whilst giving it a handful of throttle the chain snapped. Fortunately there was no damage apart from a small gouge on the rear wheel. The chain actually snapped the side plates of the link.
I called the AA who arranged recovery which arrived within 30 minutes. I phoned a couple of localish bike shops and found M&S - a KTM dealer in Newcastle (about 20 miles away), they said they had chain and sprockets in stock. So the recovery guy took me there.
Bike wheeled in to workshop at 3pm. I was offered a 525 DID chain and original KTM sprockets (17, 40), the rear being a fancy orange one. I wasn't in a position to argue about cheap alternatives - I was just glad they could fix it.
At 5.30pm I rode away with a nice shiny gold heavy duty DID chain and KTM sprockets, £220 lighter in the pocket (only charged £35 labour and £90-odd for the chain - it was those bastard sprockets that cost me).
I was very impressed both by the AA and M&S for getting me back on the road - Stranded in the middle of nowhere at 2pm on a Friday afternoon I wasn't hopeful of being back on the road so soon.
So whilst the snapped chain was undoubtedly down to high mileage and a tight spot on a chain of dubious quality, I feel far more comfortable with a decent quality heavy duty chain on it now.
