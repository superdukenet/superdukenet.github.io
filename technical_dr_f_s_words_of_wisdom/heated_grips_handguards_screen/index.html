Russ

Off Start Run is the right order. Start is meant to get the grips up to temp quickly and run keeps 'em there without using too much battery power. I'll be fitting my old Daytonas at weekend for another Scottish autumn. Om my last bike, I wired them straight to the battery and then used a relay to only keep them on whilst the engine was running. Means you get max oomph without the danger of flattening the battery.
