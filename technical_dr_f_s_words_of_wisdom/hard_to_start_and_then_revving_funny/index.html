Hard to start and then revving funny....

Linga

2013-06-04 14:26:00 UTC
The bike is taking ages to start (occasional misfire) and then when it fires it jumps into life.
Will then start easy after that.
Idles fine and warms up, once warm revving causes surging of 500-1000 revs even in neutral, worse in gear.
Water leaking out the exhaust on start up.

My first thought... Head gasket.

Done leak down, compression test and have checked coolant for hydrocarbons (coolant just changed so may not work)

Have made new loom for it, but has run for three days fine with no dramas.
Slight hesitation twice on the end of day 3, but just thought it may be a mysterious fuel surge,

I've had to give it to a mechanic as I'm going back to work, but any help I can get would be welcome.

Thoughts....?

ktmguy

2013-06-04 23:30:00 UTC
If it was me I would start with draining the tank and pour some new fuel in it.
You might just have a shit batch like bottom of the fuel station tank.
If not and it's this bad you should see "mayonaise" at the breather/ filter soon.
Pull of the breather hose and have a look while its running, if steamy smell you have a leak somewhere. Either waterpump seal or head.
Pull out the plugs, if water makes it in to the cylinders you pick it up there too, look for very clean (steamed) plugs or crystals on it.
If water spits out the exhaust I'll suspect head gasket.
Hopefully nothing too bad.

There are 3 pressure sensors, 1 each vacuum for the front and rear cylinder and 1 in the headlight mask for ambient air pressure. The headlight isn't so important and the bike will run ok without it. However if you knock a hose off or they are leaking on either the cylinder end or sensor end for these it will run like shit. Won't want to start, you'll need to give it gas to fire up and will be lumpy as fook. It might not be this looking at all your symptoms but worth a look. I ziptie the hoses on at both ends to avoid this.
