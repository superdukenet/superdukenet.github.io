Oil Pressure light.....as relates to temp

DukeHD

2014-08-02 20:56:00 UTC

I have read many posts relating to oil pressure light coming on. Seen solutions from coolant leak down to shorting at temp sensor etc.
Didn't see any comments on Oil Pressure Bypass Valve. (or I couldn't find one). There were different pistons installed on some valve bodies
and needed to be updated/changed. 5-8 in manual. Anybody had this being a fix to oil pressure problems?
My light comes on when into slower traffic AND the the temp must reach 8 - 9+ bars on scale.
Blipping the throttle 2 - 3 times will bring up pressure and light out or just get up some speed again. If bike is cool. below say 7 bars, light will not appear.
Bike is fine on back roads and hi-way riding when temp drops.
Going to run #s by KTM next week and check about piston in valve change out. Stopped riding until I resolve out issue. don't need bigger problems.
Checked Sensor wire fouling issue......looks ok there. Coolant levels are consistent and just did oil and filter service last week.

Seems to be a lot of knowledge on this site so if you have any info to offer it will be appreciated.

This bike is '08 SDR, less than (2000 km)1300 miles, has sat for about 3-4 years. Apparently started once a year while in storage. Very nice other than this *&%@# issue.

My '08 SDR did the same thing as yours back when I first bought it. I wrapped the oil pressure sensor wire with vacuum hose, zip tied the hose in place, and rerouted it slightly because it was pressing against a hot coolant hose. My bike had about the same mileage as yours when I fixed this problem back in '09, and almost 5 years and 9,000 miles later the problem hasn't returned. The oil pressure light would stay on until about 4500 rpm only when the engine was warm, but there was no unusual noise or overheating, and this was with Motorex 10w50 full synthetic oil. When under warranty, replacing the oil pressure switch was common, but now that it isn't under warranty, save yourself some money and figure out ways to keep that oil pressure sensor wire cooler. The sensor probably is not the culprit although it seemed to fix many bikes when replaced; I have a hunch dealers moved the wire off the coolant line at the same time as switch replacement. My bike still has stock fans, temp switch and all other cooling-system related items and has not had the oil pressure light problem return. This is a common problem with R model 08 Superdukes in particular. They have many subtle differences that aren't always mentioned in KTM literature and sometimes they manifest themselves with little nuisances like you are experiencing.
