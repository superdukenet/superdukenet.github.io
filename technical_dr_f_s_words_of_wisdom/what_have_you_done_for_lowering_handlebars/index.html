Just curious if you gave the bikes design a chance before you try to change it? How many miles have you put on you SD? The fact that you are looking for a lowering kit tells me you don't own a dirt bike, which this bike is based from.. or know of the different bars out there. Visit your local dirt bike shop and look at and hold the different bars avaiable..They can be adapted to the SD by a qualitified person. The stock bar height allow you to move your body weight front and back and lets you move around on the bike to ease fatigue. If you get bars that are low you have less of an option to move around if needed.
I just bought a 04 KTM 950 Adventure with a custom seat. It has a bucket shaped seat that prohibits any front to back movement and I hate it. Did 180 miles on it yesterday and I thought my ass was on fire.
The designers / engineers spent a lot of time, thought, money and experience on bikes...............just my experience and 2 cents worth....KISS for at least 3Kmiles. (Keep It Stock Stupid)

But then again...hard telling what you are going to do with your bike...Road race..yea, I can see lower bars...Street bike..stock. As others have noted..there is adjustment for free you can make.

If yours is an SDR,(or you have SDR top yolk), then there is a 'forward' and 'rear' position you can bolt on your handlebars.

I fitted a LSL bars which aren't as flat as the ones mentioned by tripoddave but are about an inch lower than standard. Better in my opinion all round, especially on track. You have to watch going lower because the tank is not designed to clear the switchgear, any lower and you have to start going out wider to get clearance and I don't like too wide bars either because of filtering through traffic.
