Bike is finally fixed after 4 months... need advice

Davo-Singapore

2013-02-26 22:18:00 UTC
I will try not to make a very long story too long.

The bike in question is a SDR 2010. The local KTM dealership had sold a lot of SMTs, a couple of SMRs and 4-5 RC8s, 5 SDs and a total of just one SDR, which I'm the not-so-proud owner at the moment.

About 9 or so months after the bike was at the dealership for its large 15K km treatment, I started having jump idle revs, which sometimes caused the bike to cut off and die when going to idle revs. Especially gliding to or standing at a traffic light.

The bike went in and out of the dealership almost every month. Even though sometimes they told me I'm imagining things, idle revs and cutting off felt way to wrong for me
The dealership tried resetting the bike computer, replacing TPS, battery, testing R/R, disassembling fuel pump and filter, nothing was found.
About 3.5-4 months ago the bike ended with dying on me 4 times in the space of 10 minutes. The bike was sent to the dealership with a "WTF is going on? Please fix it" because not only I'm a crap techy and have two left feet instead of arms apparently, I just don't have time and space to take the bike apart on my own, nor know wtf I'm doing.

Took them 1.5 weeks to make the bike even worse, another 1.5 weeks to say that the ECU is toast, and another 1.5 months waiting for a new ECU to arrive and 2 weeks to replace it. And still the problem was there.
A bit fast forward, the bike was completely stipped down, the local ktm office tech team took the bike, examined it inside and out, and they ended up deciding it might be the plug heads (where checked at the dealership and marked as OK by them). Ordered new ones, 3 weeks later, now the bike is finally fixed according to the team.

I didn't pick up the bike yet. Nor I know how much they plan to ask from me for a 4 months job where they could not even find the problem and left me hanging (if I wouldn't call to ask whats up every day or two, the bike would have been there twice as long). Nor I know if they are just telling me or the problem has not gone fully away (as said, didn't check the bike yet because I'm not sure I even want to anymore, which is a different story).

I sat today with the ktm office rep, the main technical adviser and the ktm media rep. One of the things the tech guy let slip, is that the ECU might have been fried because of the dealership attempts to find the problem (I have everything recorded).

Now my question is this (and the reason I'm writing it here):

* Can they actually screwed up the ECU when they started to replace parts or disconnect plug heads or play with them trying to find the problem?
* Also from what I could see on the bike earlier, checked the fiche, and looked at pictures of "how to"s here, except the fact that the plug heads have a screw on them to keep it in place, the plug head, throttle bodies and injectors are not overly complicated to reach and take apart and are similar to the normal bike, but they made it sound like its so overly complicated that you need a PHD to find your way around the bike.
Is this problem can be that complicated?

I'm just trying to find out what actually had gone wrong with the bike.

Cheers.

Mate. Others here might have a different opinion but to me it sounds like a classic BGC problem that's at the heart of your troubles. Big Grey Connector. If if gets wet it creates havoc. Find it. Open it up. Dry all the conns. Stuff it all with dialectic grease and wrap it up so it doesn't happen again. You'll find lots of related threads here. Good luck.
