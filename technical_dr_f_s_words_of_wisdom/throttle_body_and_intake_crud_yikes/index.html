2016-10-05 17:21:00 UTC
So I was pulling my 2007 990 SD intake and emissions apart during a Rottweiler install, got the throttle bodies off, and got a nasty surprise when I turned them over. I'm pretty sure it's a combination of settled dust over the years and/or the old air filter eroding. It didn't seem too badly stuck on the metal, but I haven't started cleaning in earnest yet. The throttle bodies I'm not too worried about, some TB spray and a soft rag should do the trick. Am more concerned about the intakes and whatever has settled inside the head. I can spray and wipe the intakes of course, but what about the head? Am thinking about things like too much liquid hydro-locking, crud scraping the cylinder walls, etc. Any advice or suggestions?

FYI- the bike sat inside a climate controlled dealership for almost 10 years- I got it with about 20 miles on the odo. Thanks!
