Any updates from riders running CMC rotors/brake pads?

DukeMonster

2011-09-10 05:36:00 UTC

So I was at a local dealer a few months ago when I was shopping for a SD, and noticed a SD on cosignment with CMC rotors/brake pads on Dynamag wheels...which I thought looked pretty trick, but was out of my price range. I wanted to get input from riders running the CMC rotor set up. I found a few vague posts on how it felt on the street back from 2008. So fast forward to today...besides the advertised advantages...what did the SD community experience? How did the rotors wear over the years? Durability? How did it perform on the track/racing environment? Brake pad wear? Is it worth the $$? etc..etc.. Considering they are about half the price they were in 2008, but still more $$ than Galfer or EBC.

One of my rotors is slightly bent, and I measured about .100 runout. I tweeked it to about .025, and I am considering getting new rotors. Stock, EBC, Galfer, or CMC rotors Are the aftermarket rotors cast? or stainless steel? The benifits from the reduced reciprocating mass when running CMC sounds like it might be worth it...if it performs and wears better than stock.

You forgot Braking rotors. I have these. https://www.motomummy.com/store/product ... 475&page=1

I think they look pretty good.



I've only had them on for 2-3K miles, so I cannot comment on longevity. I can say that I wasn't impressed with the stock SD's braking and was looking for better initial bite and better feel. These rotors, with the Carbonne Lorraine pads I'm using, provide that. The only thing I don't like is that they still squeal occasionally. They are not as noisy as the stockers, but still squeak sometimes.

I spent the extra $100 and got the rear rotor as well. That was probably the best money I've spent on the bike. The original rotor and pads provided no braking power whatsoever. I like to use a touch of rear brake to settle the chassis and the stock set up was useless - no power or feeling. The Braking rotor and CL pads work beautifully.
