Diablo Corsas???? Had a cracker of a slide today

Viking

2011-07-04 19:16:00 UTC
Whats the general opinion of these tyres. I ran std Diablos on a previous bike and hated them. I always run Dunlops, 208rr's then moved to the Qualifier II's.

Was out a run today, 100 miles in, had been going reasonably hard but a good margain for error left,, odd kneedown etc but not full on attack mode, then 20mins slowish short shifting with a few squirts now and them through some good corners. Had been 18deg and sunny all day. Bike is set up spot on, all sag is set, i've spent alot of time recently sorting out settings to suit me and my riding style. Tyre pressures are 30psi cold

Came to a nice right sweeper that i've done 100's of times before over the years, passed a car just before,no visible debris r fluids on the road, 4th gear, brake, blip down to 3rd, tip in slight trail brake, good lean, rolling on, corner opened and got hard on the gas. Had glanced down just as it was tipping in and bike was in showing 6500rpm and 70mph in 3rd, just as i was picking it up and getting hard on the gas , bbbbbbbraaaaaaaaaaaaaaa, it spun up and stepped out at the rear , instinctivly nipped the throttle back and it came back. Pulled over and checked for oil leaks etc and got a few snaps of the tyre. Road surface isn't the best but i've ran it through there quicker in the past with no issues.

Peeled off to a tight twisty b road and tried driving hard out some tight hairpins. Same again, road surface aint too great on this road but 2nd gear, driving out and it stepped out and snapped back in harder this time. This is the corner........ (have a look at the that full road if you get time. It's like a rollercoaster, absolutley blinding fun


You can see in the pics the big lines down the side where it slid.

Whats the crack with these tyres?? Anyone else had any moments with them.

With the Dunlops they feel like they have a nice wide margin. Like they go all squidgey and you can feel them distorting and digging in, push harder and they will start to move ever so slightly like the grip is really linear as it runs out. These Diablos seem to just go grip grip grip, whooosh, and it's sliding. I'm a bit nervous about going on track with them if this is the characteristics of the tyre.

shadowman

I used to run Corsa 111 on my track bike gixer 1000K5. I ran in fast group, many many hundreds of laps and never had anything but praise for the tyres.

I think the superstock boys used to race on these with impresive lap times.

I did thousands of road miles with never a tyre problem.

My SD came with them and I had no issues. Now I have fitted Rossa Corsa's and they are better still. In fact they are the best tyres I have ever owned and I'm content that they offer more grip than anybody can use on the road.

A lot of the tyre thing is confidence. If you have lost it in the Corsa set up it's probably best to change to something you trust.

Good luck.

Are they Corsa 3's or just standard Corsa's. The standard Corsa would be a bit long in the tooth now but I used to use them on my Gsxr750. They used to slide quite often but reasonably predictably. Also are the OEM Corsa 3's that came fitted to some bikes as new, or aftermarket ones which have a different compound.

Finally, just my opinion but I reckon 30psi cold is too low for road riding. Fine for a trackday but, without knowing the roads you ride, I dont think you can keep enough heat in on the roads. Road pressures are 2.5 bar (34 psi) as I recall. Could be that 20 mins of slower riding have let the tyre cool more than you expected?
