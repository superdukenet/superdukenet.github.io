Try not to build up force on the bolt by slowly adding more force to the wrench but do this instantly, that's the reason a pneumatic wrench get bolt loose easily.

Hi,

I had a similar problem a while back, the adjuster bolt tightened up when I wanted to adjust the chain tension. Normal water dispersing oil/fluid and heat did not help. Then I used some proper penetrating oil. (Brand: Q5 graphite-based penetrating oil). It made a huge difference. I sprayed some of the penetrating oil on and worked it back and forth a bit, and it came out easily. There was some aluminium on the bolt thread. Cleaned it up with a thread file and put the bolt back in with lots of copperslip. Put some coperslip on the other adjuster bolt as well.

Apparently a mixture of kerosene and ATF also works for seized bolts.

There is still hope.
Get a welder and a bag of nuts.
Weld nut on the bolt in the center. Undo and it will probably break. Try again and again. Eventually the heat will loosen the bolt and it will come out.
If not, use a left turning drill to drill it out. Normally the heat of the drilling process is enough to loosen it and it will spin out.
If not, good luck...
