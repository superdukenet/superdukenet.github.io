Colonel_Klinck

2011-05-23 09:52:00 UTC
the bigger valves, higher compression and freer flowing exhaust would all assist with making the engine breathe easier,exploiting the higher rev limit.
seeing as horsepower is nothing more than a mathematical calculation of torque x rpm divided by 5252 moving peak torque higher in the rev range AND increasing the maximum rpm will give a larger horsepower number.
obviously any increase in torque will result in an increase in horsepower, but with an increase in max rpm you get an increase in the multiplication factor as well.
grand prix machines make insane horsepower because they're incredibly well engineered, the 18,000 rpm isn't the lowest factor in how they get their 240+ hp.

From what I've read, the SDRs have titanium exhaust valves, enlarged ports, lighter flywheel (but it could have been the crank), bored-out throttle bodies, increased compression and the higher rev limit along with a revised ECU. My SDR also came with a 525 x-ring from the factory (not sure what the 'normal' SDs use). Other differences include the front brake calipers, the front fork, the rear shock (which shortens the wheelbase which steepens the rake and it also raises the ground clearence), the mono-seat setup, and of course that orange frame. Mine also came with the Akropovic Ti header but maybe that was a dealer add-on.
In reality, it doesn't really matter; those are just parts or bullets on a tech sheet. It's how the whole damn thing comes together, how it comes alive...
