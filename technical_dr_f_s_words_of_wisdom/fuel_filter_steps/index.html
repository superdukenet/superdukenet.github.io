Hi There. Just changed my fuel filters. My bike had The following symptoms: it would refuse to go above 8000 rpm. It would hesitate and feel rough. It would also stall just after braking hard. The symptoms would get worst as the fuel level went down.

Solution: replace the fuel filters. It comes as a kit and is a bit on the pricey side. 150$C.

There are two filters, both situated in the tank. It takes about 1 hour to replace the kit.

The first filter is like a small bag made of wire mesh with some kind of filtering element inside. The gas has to go through it before entering the pump. This is the one causing my problems. After going through the pump, it goes through a small cartridge filter.

The job implies removing the tank, taking out the pump/filter assembly,replacing the filters and putting everything back together. To Remove the tank, you need to undo the fasteners that attach the side panels to the tank and the bolt at the front of the tank.

You can then lift tue front of the tank. There's a vent tube at the front of the tank, on the left side that will prevent the tank from going up. Unplug it and lift the tank. At the back of the tank there are two electrical connectors to undo. There's also a pipe for the overflow (for when you overfilled) you need to undo.

There also is the gas outlet pipe. If you follow it, you'll see a connector 2 or 3 inches down. You squeeze the upper part (the metal part) and pull it appart. Don't worry, gas will not flow.

Now you can undo the little shock that holds the tank up and the two Allen bolts at the back. It's a good idea to do this when the tank is not too full so it's not too heavy.

Take the tank off, flip it over and rest it on something that won't scratch it.
Now you have four bolt to undo on the pump/filter kit. You will have to pry it out gently because there are o rings resisting.

Now that the assembly is in your hands, flip it over to let gas out. It's a good idea at this point to take a few pictures for when you're trying to put everything back together.

There is a white filter cartridge at the end. There's a filter in there. It's a little hard to get but it's feasable. The other one you can see attached to the pump. You need to undo the whole assembly to replace that one. You will need to undo 2 Phillips screws for this. There's also a smal Phillips screw to undo (electrical ground) also.

The rest is pretty much everything in the reverse order.

Be careful when you flip the tank back uplight. Some gas will come out of the vent. I have tried to clean the "screen filter" at first (in a motel parking lot). It helped a lot but only replacing it cured my problems completely.

I hope this helps. I have pictures of the kit if you'd like.

Hope this helps.
