How do I repair cracked carbon fibre....?

Razy

2012-02-17 21:58:00 UTC
I've got a could of my CF parts that the luggs are starting to crack along.

Can I fix it by first drilling a small hole at the tip of the split to stop it spreading further, then fibre glass the inside of the parts to restore its strength?

bic_bicknell

2012-02-17 22:23:00 UTC
Mate, this is a common problem with light weight carbon. Especially where the item is under heat conditions - the belly pan mounts on the right hand side are especially prone to cracking up and anywhere that vibration occurs eventually degrades the material.

What I do is a mixture of things depending on what's going on.

First off get it all degreased and clean so you can see the damage and what not. Then get some really good superglue, not the high street stuff but proper cyanoacrylate with the accelerator to make it set off. Fix the tear or split with this first. Then I use the two part filler you can get from Halfords that comes in a roll . It's like chemical metal and sets into a hard metallic grey resin. I use this to fill in any gaps and use it on back of the unseen surfaces to make the repair stronger. You can sand it down after if you need to. I sometimes then give the outer surface a light coat of the superglue and then set it off with the accelerator - this gives a gloss and hard finish to the surface.

On the carbon mounting points - which is where I find most damage occurs - I often have to make a new structural fixing point out or some hard material like nylon or a metal washer or something. Then I bond it onto the carbon part. The place where I have to repair the most is the very lowest points on the belly pan because they get damaged riding over curbs and get chipped away with stones from the road.

Really big damage you can do with the whole resin and fiberglass treatment but I find that this is hardly ever necessary.
