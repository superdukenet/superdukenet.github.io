I'm with Joe, after looking at the microfiche it looks very likely to be the shift linkage behind the clutch, I would be amazed if it is the actual selector barrel or forks themselves.

Check the sliding spring loaded fork on the end of the selector shaft that enguages with the star on the end of the selector barrel and I'm pretty sure you will find the cause of your woes.....

btw There was a similar gearbox problem on the RC8's which only took the dealers 70 odd minutes to fix, so that certainly wasn't for splitting the crank cases to replace selector barrel, more likely to remove the clutch basket to allow the shift rod assembly to be pulled through the cases from that side and replaced.
