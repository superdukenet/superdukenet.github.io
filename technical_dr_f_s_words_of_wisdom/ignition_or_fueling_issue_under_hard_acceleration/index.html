Could it be that you are creating a vacuum in the fuel tank? Clogged filters and such should give issues no matter how warm or cold.
If the bike stalls and you open the gas filler cap, does it suck in air?
At cold start the o2 sensors are not functional yet, maybe something wrong there? As soon as the o2 sensors run hot the fuelling changes. Unless you have switched off/terminated the O2 sensors of course
Did you check the voltage when bike is warm? Rectifier might be on its way out and over power the coils when the it gets hot. Any light bulbs blowing lately?

Sounds like a nasty one, good luck,

marc lenssen

2012-04-27 06:45:00 UTC
Regulator and coils both respond badly to getting hot once they start to get hairline cracks....
Regulators can also go off if there are issues with the 3 yellow wire feeds or their connectors. I have seen this happening an Hondas where the connectors started building up resistance, sometimes even melt. At the Hondas many would just take out any connector and solder the 3 yellow wires together directly. This means that it is less easy to change the part but the bike would run. But I guess it would mean that you could measure a change in charging voltage when it gets at the 7 bars...
