SD Dyno'd/PCIII-Some Observations ETC !!!

Captain Pugwash

2007-09-03 20:46:00 UTC
I've just got back from having my PCIII fitted and then having had the bike set up on the dyno by Nick at PDQ,one of the leading tuning people in the country.Nick has been running the dyno for 15 years and has done every bike you can think of,including some recent SDs used in the race series that supports MotoGP.




Just a recap on my bike-it's an 07 SD fitted with a full Akra Evo titanium system and DNA filter.


Nick strapped the bike up on the dyno and did a base run,and got 112.35 bhp and 70.21 lb ft of torque.

He said that was on a par with the race bikes he had done,that were just fitted with Akra slip ons.

He showed me the graph and noted where he thought he could improve on it,once the PCIII was fitted and ready for fiddling.

He thought that the fuelling was quite rich in places (which ties in with previous thoughts here on the forum) and said that he should be able to liberate about 4 to 5 bhp and improve on the fuelling in general.

Three hours later and he was done-and the result was 114.93 bhp and 70.64 torque.



So only a gain of 2.6 bhp !.

He explained that where he thought the standard Akra map was quite rich,infact wasn't bad at all,and he struggled to make much gain.

The main improvement is where the PCIII overrides the lamda sensors and the map is smoothed out low down between 2-4000 rpm.

He reckoned that the Akra map which was downloaded by my dealer when the full system was installed,was actually very good,and only lacking a tad where needed to meet emission regs-this would make sense as the maps were supposedly done by Sommer KTM-the biggest dealer/KTM perf specialist in Germany.


I left PDQ and went for a 600 mile spin,taking in some cracking roads in Wales/west country :





Riding the bike on the road,I can certainly notice it is slightly smoother low down,which suits me as most of my riding is done at home in Guernsey and is very much 'town riding'.

It also pulls like buggery,and goes bananas above 7000.

I've owned 8 or 9 KTMs but this goes like nothing else !!!

Was the PCIII/dyno time worth it ?

To be honest it is very marginal-I wanted to do it to finish the bike off,having spent a fair bit on the full system,I felt another Â£400+ was worth it to make the bike as 'sorted' as possible.

Purely on powergains,it's not worth the money-I reckon just fitting the Â£88 DNA filter gave me as much of a gain as the PCIII !!!

The Akra maps which are downloadable by your dealer when you buy Akra stuff are very good (certainly for the full system,so I assume the map for just the Evo slip ons is also good).This would explain why the Tuneboy guys have struggled to better it !!!

There is very limited benefit,performance wise,in fitting a full Evo system over just the slip-ons.The OE KTM headers are very good-the main benefit is weight reduction,which could be achieved by dieting for a week or 2 !!!


As an aside,I was amazed how comfy the bike was.

I did 270 miles today,of which about 150 of that was motorway.

Sitting at a steady 90,the riding position was spot on with the touring screen.

Fuel consumption was also good.

I did 110 miles before fllling,with most of that at 90+ mph and I only put in 12 litres so could easily have done 130 miles before hitting Reserve.

I was using Total Excellium (97 ron) and it certainly seems to increase range.

That's about it.

Why it would be smoother on a duel cylinder map rather than a single one for both?

Well v twins for one generally need different mapping for front and rear cylinders due to the rear one running hotter than the front as its losing much of the cooling air that runs into the front of the bike,and therefore the front cylinder,as its hotter theres a chance it can run weaker and cause running issues ,one of which is throttle responce and pick up.... my buell monitored engine temps from a sensor mounted in the rear cylinder head,the hottest one obviously being the most important one to monitor,optimising the fuel and ignition maps for each cylinder individually will give a smoother running engine,maybe as described above minimal horsepower increase over a single map fits all,but a more efficient engine.

Ktm release the bikes with a single map as its cheaper and easier to do so.
