Hi guys,

In the last few weeks I've been trying to set up the suspension on my SuperDuke R 2008. I've made very good progress but I'm not there yet. I'm posting this thread because I've been speaking to the-bunker quire a bit about it and I'm hoping more people will chime in. I've also noticed that spartaro87 has posted a .

Either way. My set up is for British back roads. These roads are sometimes quite awful with loads of roots, changing surfaces, patches, horse pats, etc. IMO, the stock set up is too hard, both compression and damping.

Check that your sag is correct before you start this all. There's plenty of resources out there on how to do it. Here's one from . I'm 90kg with all my gadgets, leathers and helmet on, so I'm probably slightly above average. Fact is I've not bothered setting this as it feels OK and the zip tie at the front fork leg kind of confirms it.
Setting the sag first is because you want the suspension to use all available movement.

The next step is to set the suspension back to standard. You do this by reading the manual. The SDR requires a flat screwdriver and a 17mm spanner. All available in the tool kit.
Then, I recommend that you print my , print it double sided. Then you get a short pencil, and a screwdriver that won't puncture your lungs if you have an off. Grind a coin if you need to improvise.
Keep this with you at all times.

The PDF contains one column for every setting (on the SDR). On one side you have the default settings and some information. On the other side there's the settings. First thing to do with the settings side is to jot down the starting points (which you get from the manual or the sheet).

This is how you get to your suspension setting.
You ride your bike (d'Oh!!!) and then you stop and make an adjustment, and it's absolutely vital that you write down what setting you've changed. Also write down a comment (typically on the previous line) about what you thought the bike felt like.
Every setting has got a "Change" and a "Result" column. In the "Change" column you set + or - and the number of clicks (or turns for the Hi Speed Compression). Then you sum it up into the "Result". For example, I started out with Front Compression at 18 (in the "Result" column), I then decreased the damping, which means I screwed the screw out 2 clicks. In my notes I thus entered +2 in the "Change" and in the "Result" column 20.
Try to adjust as little as possible every time you're adjusting something. The more you fiddle with, the more difficult it is to "feel the change". I.e you can't easily put down which turn of which knob made the difference. Constrain yourself. Finally I want to say that I'm no expert, and that these settings should be considered guidelines only. Don't stick the settings on your bike and get on the bike and ride it as a dork. The bike's characteristics will have changed and might catch you out resulting in an accident. That's bad. One step at a time.

Have fun!

SAG -

Rider sag is the distance from full extension (wheel off floor) to the amount the suspension drops (sags) with the rider on the bike (full riding gear on)

Static sag is the distance from full extension to the amount the suspension drops under the weight of the bike only.

rider sag aim for 30-35mm rear and 35-45mm front.
static sag aim for 5-10mm rear and 25-35mm front.

Just as examples my statics are 9-10mm rear, 29-30mm front. Rider sags are 30mm rear and 45mm front.

This web site here is as good as any to fully expalin setting sags -


http://www.moto-racing.co.uk/Guides/mot ... nSetup.htm

With the rear shock just spray some WD40 on the top of the adjuster and it will soak into the threads. Leave it to soak for a while then undo the collar locking screw, grab the collar and spring by hand and you should be able to turn it. Failing this use a peice of wood and a hammer to turn it.

OK, the sport settings are too hard for the road, (infact the rear shock sports settings are too hard for anything including track). Make sure you read the manuel and your turning the adjusters the correct direction!!!!!!!!

Try these -
Road settings -
Fork
rebound - 8 clicks from full hard
Comp - 14 clicks from full hard

Shock
High speed compression 1.5 turns anti clockwise (1.5 turns for everything, this is 1 adjustment youl never need to touch!!!!!)
Comp 15 - 17 clicks from full hard
Rebound 15 - 17 clicks from full hard

Track settings -
Fork
rebound - 6 clicks from full hard
Comp - 10 clicks from full hard

Shock
High speed compression 1.5 turns anti clockwise (1.5 turns for everything, this is 1 adjustment youl never need to touch!!!!!)
Comp 12 clicks from full hard
Rebound 11 clicks from full hard

The above should give you a good base setting for road and track. Set it for road and give it a try, just remember its going to feel stiffer now anyway as you have set your rear sag correctly. KTM set them up real soft at the rear as standard

if you are used to a hard setup in the front (giving lots of feel), you may not be used to how the sd's suspension works best... as the sd has much more travel and a dirt bike style needle in the fork valves.... not the long fine ones used in most sport bike suspension like showa or ohlins. it works better when it feels softer.

it may not feel right at first (especially coming from a sportbike) but you will find that the suspension can now work properly and maintain grip with the road even over b and c grade surfaces

oh and bridgestone bt016's are horrible... but apparently they pilot pures are far worse
