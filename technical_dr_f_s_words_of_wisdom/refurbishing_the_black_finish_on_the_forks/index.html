Refurbishing The Black Finish On The Forks

shadowman

Does anybody here know what the finish is on the front forks and what would be the best method or refreshing them? To avoid any confusion I'm referring to the black coating on the upper tube outers.

I have a slight scuff through to the uncoated metal on mine and some general pitting to the forward facing elements from contact with the atmosphere and its contents!

I could just use a black marker to patch over the scuff and rotate the fork in the clamps to hide the worst of it but it would be nice to make a proper job and do it right if that is a practical option. The thing is they have that nice fine machined finish which paint of powder coating would likely cover and I don't want that.

Has anybody done this and if so how did it go? Even if you have no direct experience I am open to thoughts / ideas.

They are soft black anodised.

There are suspension houses In the UK and US that can take care of this for you. Some offer "Hard Black" anodizing as well. This adds some toughness to the surface finish - but as with ANY anodization color - the black will eventually fade as well (although there are some excellent solar-resistant dyes available these days), to a somewhat dark-gray color (versus silver for a soft-anodize).

It may be possible to "spot anodize" the nasty-looking areas, but it will appear inhomogenous, and if they are rash/scratches - the rash and scratches will still be there, only anodised.

The existing finish will be "stripped" (chemically) before the new anodization is done. A good shop will try to clean-up rash/scratches if reasonable (e.g. not much material to remove) Worth noting, that there may be a slight softening of the appearance of the tool marks ("machined finish") from stripping and/or clean-up. A good house, won't strip any more than absolutely necessary.

Why go through a suspension house that does this? Because they know how best to prep the pieces and have arrangements with platers/anodizing shops, who are already familiar with the unique masking and hanging required to anodize fork tubes. They will likely be happy as not, if you pulled off the tubes from the forks yourself (probably saving you money).

That "nice fine machined finish", well, isn't. It's a least-cost finish, where they turned those tubes as fast as possible - which leaves the tool marks you see as the "machined finish". People seem to like it visually (or at least, not complain about it). Definitely not a high-grade aluminium finish.

You could get them done in orange if you liked!

There's a reason USD forks are generally not painted/powdercoated ... You can probably guess why that is: TC's would be gripping onto paint ... loss or failure of these-types of finish could be disastrous. You can probably also see the issues if they attempted to mask the areas for the TCs.
