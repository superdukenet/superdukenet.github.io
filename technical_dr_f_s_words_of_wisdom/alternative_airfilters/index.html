I think it's great people are still, after 6 years of the SD, prepared to try new ideas.

One or two things to bear in mind though.....

The stock airbox set up allied with the V twin config and offset firing pattern means that in standard form one cylinder is trying to suck air soon after its neighbour. Because of the restrictive snorkel that 2nd cylinder will be sucking on a slightly reduced air volume (a partial vacuum) hence the timing and fuelling differences between cylinders.

In the setup you describe you will remove the bulk of that part of the intake difference (there will be some differences due to one filter being behind the other, the shape of the tank, exhaust etc). So,to get the best out of the engine - indeed to make sure one cylinder is going to run very lean - I would advise a full custom remap including timing. You may well be able to set both cylinders to almost identical advance in your setup which should boost output by maybe as much as 5%. This is not an area for the amateur to play with so be sure to do it right.

Good luck with it and I look forward to seeing the before and after dyno graphs
