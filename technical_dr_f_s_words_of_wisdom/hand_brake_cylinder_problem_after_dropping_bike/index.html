Hand brake cylinder problem after dropping bike

samba

2010-04-20 08:30:00 UTC
Guys,

After dropping my bike at the weekend I've got a problem with the brake cylinder's mounting onto the handlebar.

A little history - after fitting my awesome orange handlebars I couldn't get the top bolt back in so I've been running the bike with the front brake assembly clamped on with only one bolt. Has been fine if not totally a good idea...

In the pic below you can see the missing top bolt.

Now, after taking an impact on the brake lever (snapped the end off, bent the rest of the lever), the clamp was wobbly and loose. I took out my trusty KTM toolkit and a quick turn of the lower bolt clamped the cylinder on nice and tight for my ride back home with my tail between my legs.

After a bit of further investigation - I'm worried. The two halves of the clamp do not line up properly (shown in the pic), which if they were tight and sound wouldn't worry me too much, only I've got the following problem;

screwing the lower bolt anti clockwise tightens the clamp. Then screwing the bolt clockwise loosens it briefly before tightening the clamp again. I think my bolt is bent/buggered/fooked.

I reckon my only option is to hacksaw the bolt in half and try to get it out, but what if it's not only the bolt that's fooked? Will I need a new cylinder assembly? The parts manual doesn't have a part no. for the clampy bit, just the whole fooking assembly.

worried....

Oh dear, Oh dear. At least you didn't leave a bolt off something important - like your brakes or something............

This is the problem with non standard bars, (and the OE bars IMO) the bend prevents the controls moving in to where I want them so the overall bar width is very wide - good for a motocrosser - not good for london traffic.

Anyway, I know what I'd do. Like TJ says - you have to get the bottom bolt out - either by unscrewing it or hacksawing it in two. Then if it's bent throw it away. If you really want your controls where they are then I would use a dremmel, (or use emery paper wrapped round the slightly curved bit of the bar) to profile the inside of the clamp so that it fits without wobbling and the two clamps meet up properly.

Or course, you've probably bent the clamp as well so you might need to evaluate and take a view on whether to try and straighten it or just enlarge the holes slightly on the non threaded side of the clamp. The cast material is not strong stuff - it bends in a crash but then has a habit of snapping if you try and straighten it. Go easy.

Either way make sure you get two bolts back in and that it's all nice and secure.
