I have produced an Excel sheet of the costs break down and added a little formula where you can toggle on/off costs. Get the Excel sheet .

The service cost me £136 more than I had budgeted, luckily I get the VAT back otherwise it would have been £160. All this has got me thinking about Dealer Service strategies. It's not an MOT therefore there are loads of items which you should do rather than the dealer as their labour charge multiplies by an order the total cost.

You should do items such as Brake pads, Air filter and Spark Plugs yourself. The labour costs would be reduced by 1 to 1.5 hours and then the total falls in the £450 range. £30 for 4 Ltrs of oil is expensive, do it yourself. If your bike goes in and they find shims need adjusting (swapping) then because of labour your costs are going to mount as the cams have to come out etc.

Think about giving the dealer a total Hour Budget i,e. I will pay you for 3.5 hours work, no more.
