Frame Sliders that extend beyond the tank spoilers?

SDNerd

2011-11-22 22:30:00 UTC

I currently have Motovation frame sliders on my bike and they worked well in protecting the essential mechanical components during a low side a year ago.

However, I think we all know how expensive the tank spoilers are. I'd like to have some that can extend far enough to protect more of the plastic.

Suggestions?

MADDOG53

2011-11-23 00:02:00 UTC

LOL - I recently, ahem, "tested" my Motovation sliders as well (have both upper and lower frame, front and rear axle, and bar ends). The lower frame and bar end slider took the brunt of the damage. I'm not sure if the upper frame slider even touched down.

Yes, the tank spoiler was creamed. And, I'm OK with that. The tank was virtually unscathed - and the spoiler is cheap compared to even a tank respray. I gotta hand it to KTM - whether they intended this or not - its a smart way to have gone IMO. I've always known the tank spoilers were going to be sacrificial if it went down. I like that idea of having sacrificial on the tank, that is easily replaced.

Given that some of the larger Motovation sliders are upwards of $50 each to replace, the spoiler at $170 doesn't seem unreasonable painted, decaled, and ready to bolt on.

Regarding a longer (presumably upper) frame slider, I'm pretty certain that isn't going to save the spoiler. And much depends on the crash/drop itself. And by making a frame slider longer, can cause other problems. Even if you had sliders that saved the spoiler, it just means that there will be another contact point somewhere else, likely something at least as expensive.

The one thing I'd want perhaps, is better clutch cover protection. Mine got pretty worn ... fortunately, not a super expensive part. I know there are some super spendy aftermarket ones - and at that point, $61 for an OEM replacement also seems a bargain to me. Its trick looking and all, but what's a Rizoma cost, $250 - $300+ ? And when you go down, its plastic "buffer" gets eaten - which is fine until you try to get the replacement part (in the US).

I didn't like the look of all the frame sliders out there, and as a corner marshall working over the years have seen "frame " damage done by some even low speed crashes with the sliders snapping right off,,,most manufactures recomend replacement after a crash,,not just the slider puck but the whole unit,,MH say's his won't snap off,,,like I said I did not like even the look of the things,,,,like a highway peg off a cruiser,,,they will protect most if not all the time, but the right crash can cause damage by snapping off or twisting the frame up,,,it can happen,,,sometimes,,,,I went with the Top-Blocks,,,never tested them but a member here did and his damage was limited to the bar end and the slider puck itself,,,they are beefier than all the others IMO, with a steel frame with "three point" mounting,,the puck is mounted slightly higher than the lower mounting point on conventional units,, don't know about them sticking out farther than the others,,,IMO well worth the extra dosh,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,
