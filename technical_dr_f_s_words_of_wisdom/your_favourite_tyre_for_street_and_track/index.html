More useful/useless tyre info for tyre nerds

MOTORRAD do things fairly scientific (they are germans after all...though this is the Swedish version) and have compared Michelin Pilot Road 2, Pilot Power 2CT and Power One, here's some figures (sorry about the quality).

Lap time dry track:
Pilot Road 2: 1.38.28
Pilot Power 2CT: 1.34.10
Power One: 1.31.17
Power One Race: 1 second faster than Power One



Lap time wet track: (not same track...obviously)
Pilot Road 2: 1.18
Pilot Power 2CT: 1.18
Power One: 1.29
Power One Race: 1.41



The magazine conclude that it's no point using Power One on the road as you won't get the neccessary heat into the tyres for them to perform well. Also they conclude that Pilot Road is not suitable for track riding. And they compare the Road and 2CT with rain tyres, very impressing on wet. So no surprises really

Read in Fast Bikes from the Dunlop Sportsmart launch and the journalist was very impressed with the tyre even though it was very cold. Also performed very well on wet track. Dunlop even had bikes with Power Pure and BT-016 for comparison. So emailed the journalist (Simon Roots):

"Read your article from the Dunlop SportSmart launch. I’m out for a tyre performing equally well in warm, cold and wet conditions as we are going to North Cape (Norway) this summer and expect both low temperatures and/or rain (and hopefully some nice weather as well). I know you haven’t done full back-to-back test with competitors or have a lot of experience with the SportSmart yet, but would you consider it the best option out there for my trip? Wear is less important (though doesn’t hurt), it’s mainly the cold/wet I’m worried about. But still want sport tyre performance when dry and warm (of course).

Other options I have considered:
· Michelin Pilot Power 2CT (not sure how good when cold, but know they are very good in wet)
· Michelin Pilot Road 2 (know they are very good in wet, better than Power in cold?)
· Pirelli Angel (always liked Pirellis but not sure how well performs in wet/cold. Assume better than Diablo Rosso in wet/cold though?)

I know there are plenty of other options as well. But the SmartSport really sounds convincing. Riding a KTM Superduke BTW."

And his reply was:

"From my experience on the launch (I’ve not tried them yet here in the UK), I’d be very happy to recommend the Sport Smart, although Benjamin reckons that the Interact M5 was very good in difficult conditions at its launch in Portugal. To be honest, either one would be a good choice and I’d take that above the Michelin options, although the SportSmart should really suit a SuperDuke."
