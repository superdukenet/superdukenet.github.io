Comrade DFauvre: This doesn't sound right. Is it running on only one cylinder? For somebody coming from IL4's it should feel at low revs as though it'll make you shit yourself if you crack it open. Ignoring vibration, which is a V-twin thing, it should have plenty or torque even at low revs and a pretty good spread of power throughout the rev range. In fact, the spread is so good that you should be able to not change too much around town. Your statement that you have to keep changing gears is a worry. So what is wrong? My thoughts:

- It is not really a low milage bike but one that has a substitute speedo. Does the bike really look low milage?
- The bike has been worked on or reassembled by somebody that doesn't know what they are doing.
- The bike has a really bad map installed.
- The bike really has stood for a long time and is showing signs of this. Possible problems are bad fuel, fuel gunk in the injectors or in-line filter, mice in the air box, brakes dragging, engine seized, wheel bearings Etc.
- The bike is being ridden at very low revs (unlikely).

Perhaps get on somebody else's 990 and have a ride and see if it is similar.

BTW didn't you test ride this bike?

Big twins do vibrate and to some degree it is inevitable that your hands will become numb however this can be reduced by carefully tuning the bar-end weights and/or by using vibration reducing hand-grips. There are threads regarding this on this forum (use the Search button) and many other forums such as the HD forums.

Hi, Interesting post.

I came to the SD from a jap sports bike background and have had all the litre bikes you mentioned + ZZR's etc.

After first ride on my previous SD (One of the first Models) I thought it was a bit clunky and agricultural compared with the jap IL4's and I kept hitting the rev limiter but the brakes and suspension / handling were great and one thing it wasn't was slow. There was a little vibration but I didn't find it intrusive at all even on long runs which included 650 miles in a couple of days one time.

The bike before it had been a track spec K5 gixer 1000 and my 06 SD felt like it would easily keep up with that in rollons only losing out at the top of the rev range and or as speeds got into the 120 MPH+ range.

My bike was fairly standard just with round Arka's and a bigger back sprocket. It would wheelie off the throttle in second and anybody going faster than me in any given situation was doing so because they had more talent not because the bike was miles better.

Personally I doubt yours is running on one cylinder as it wouldn't go well enough to be fast when revved hard but it doesn't sound like it's running right either! If there is a dyno near you get it there for a run, should only cost a few dollars. Most std bikes put out over 100 HP at the rear wheel, if it's under this something is wrong. 110 is probably more like it but Dyno's do vary a lot.

If yours is in this range and the torque line looks good it's fine and the bike just doesn't suit you. If it's well under there is an issue which once sorted will let you have a lot of two wheeled fun.

Honestly a sweetly running SD is about as much fun as you can have on two wheels on the road, especially if you like honing about. Hope yours is sorted soon.
