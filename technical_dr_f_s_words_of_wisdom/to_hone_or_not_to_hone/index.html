Definitely hone the bores. It will put the crosshatch back onto the cylinder surface allowing the rings to bed in and seal properly (as others have mentioned). I would highly recommend replacing the pistons as well. 42K miles of hard road and track use will definitely weaken the piston structure (possibly to the point of failure..I've had a piston crack in half along the gudgion pin (small end/piston pin for us Yanks) in one of my YZ450Fs because I cheaped out and didn't replace the piston...it looked perfect. Another weak area will be the ring lands. They take a pounding.

Cheap insurance if you're going in there already.

KTM used Nikasil coated cylinders as well as for example Ducati does. Your dealer told you complete bullshit.
First of all 42K is nothing for a Nikasil coated cylinder. How can he advise to hone the cylinder without having them
inspected first ? If they do not show serious marks which i would not expect there is no reason to work
on them. You can reapply the Nicasil coating in case there are any marks, but there are only few
companies which can do that. Nikasil is a trademark of the German company Mahle.
Also balancing the crack shaft is only needed if you change the pistons, i would not see any reason why
you want to do that. Headporting and flowbench makes sense for the engine also inspecting the inlet valves
which ususally show lots of carbon debris sticking to them ...

Colonel_Klinck

2012-02-06 09:36:00 UTC
Plated bores should not be honed, clean them up with a soft scourer and muratic acid . If the plating has been damage the bores will need re-plating.
I would go right through the heads checking valves ,seats , springs, shims,cams and replace the timing chains (also check the tensioners and guides).

If it were my bike I would check the bores and mic the pistons ,if all within spec put new rings and slap it back together.

If you have been diligent with oil changes I think you will be surprised at how little wear you will find.
