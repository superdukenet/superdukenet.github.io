Your on it Cheesie.

They do use all the rear travel.

GoGo's settings are useless to us coz he ran a 190 rear and played with rear height with the clevis. And from re-reading all his posts on here and old blog posts that bike was pretty much built to wrok around the 190 Michelin. Might be wrong. But thats def how it looked.

Weird thing i found was i could get rear sags identical with a 160 and a 180 spring. Static and rider. Would never beleived it if i hadnt done it myself. Bike was way better with the 180 fitted though.

All this is going to make a difference if its a std bike or an R or a stdbike with an R shock. The R shock being longer and the bike having no linkage means it affects the swingarm angle. But no so much on the R with the longer forks. Looking at all that rake and trail stuff...the R has the same geo as an 05 but has 07 yokes! The R shock is 10m longer and the forks are 6mm. But an R shock raises the rear 20mm coz of the pivot points of the axle. So by raising the rear of the bike 14mm(through preload/tyre or other means) can change the chassis geo from the nice 07 planted to the tucky floppy 05 trail numbers

Then what wheelbase you run will affect how the shock moves through its stroke and the weight distribution front to rear. Longer wheelbase allows the axle to push the shock up easier as it has more leverage. But it also takes weight off the rear wheel. So what i'm getting at is there's no set rules. It's like pushing bubbles in a carpet, you push one and another pops up elsewhere. Change the gearing..it totally changes the weight distribution and how the shock works through its travel whick then affects how the forks work.

Then you add damping rates which have to match so as the bike is at lean and compresses on hits a bump it reacts as a whole chassis. With guys throwing this an that shock and forks on it the bike can be at lean at the apex and hit a bump then forks and shock go through their stroke at different rates and completely change the chassis geo mid corner. Thats where you get those mysterious crashes where it just goes down and you never figure out what happened. Watch the ariel shots of the TT bikes over the mountain. The bikes are at lean and reacting to the bumps as a whole chassis. Both front and rear move at the same rate.

It is a black art. The damping and spring stuff is hard enough. Then we add chassis geo and weight distribution into it. Holy fook. Even Bugress, Ducati and Rossi cant get that right!
