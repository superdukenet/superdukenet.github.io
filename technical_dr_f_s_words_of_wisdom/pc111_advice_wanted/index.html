Dude if you are good on the spanners you'll piss this. I'm no mechanic but I fitted mine in about 40 mins. The instructions in the box are good enough.

Make sure you download the right map for your bike and upload from your PC via USB cable to PCIII (software CD included and easy to follow) What SD do yo have? There might be a Motobox custom map for your SD already doing the rounds here. Mine is 06 SD, Akra round slip-ons (baffles out) Motobox, SAS removed. If your bike matches you are welcome to try it, if not there are others. Post up bike specs and someone will sort you out I'm sure.

As for the O2 sensor eliminators all they do is plug the already existing cable from the O2 Sensors to the ECU. They are not in the PCIII loom and are just stand alone plugs.

You are going to love what the Motobox does to your SD It puts a big smile on my face just thinking about it. Takes something bad ass and gives it that bit more attitude

I'm pretty sure I read the same article as you on the 15 min reset thing but came away with a different opinion, although the thread started out as if it made sense, by the time I got to the end the guy had pretty much been shown out as not having a clue what he was talking about although it was kind of agreed that ECU reset is probably not the right word for what it actually does, either way it seems it does still take readings during that 15 min run time with regards to various stuff and is free so might as well do it anyway, the idea being that you'll have a nice fresh base to start from before you disconnect the o2 sensors.

Unfortunately you can't plug the PCIII into a PC without a power adapter as it won't run off the usb connection, I don't think it has a std type power inlet so you can't use a generic universal power supply and I think although I never looked into it more closely that you have to use the specific Dynojet power supply at additional cost, so you're gonna need a mate with a PC laptop (or a macbook running windows via parralels or bootcamp) or a really long usb cable to run from his desktop.


The PCIII map doesn't override the ECU map it is in addition to, it adds or subtracts fuel from it, ie; if the ECU gives a fuel value of 5 and this is too rich, the PCIII map will have a negative value of lets say -1 to lean it out according to what the tuner decides is needed. This is why if you have the akra map installed you need to pick a PCIII map that is designed around the akra map as the base values will be different than the std ecu map

The dynojet supplied maps are created on customer bikes at the dynojet tuning center so what's available depends on the bikes they had at the time.

You can always confirm with the supplier to make sure it is sent out with a zero map to start with and so it doesn't make any adjustments until you can get to a mates and upload something more suitable.

Hi Luis, Greetings from NZ.
You can buy the motobox from a member, look in the parts for sale tread his link is in there as a sticky. Motohooligan parts
SAS valve bleeds air in to the exhaust to clean the cat on overun, no need for this when you changed the original exhaust unless you like the popping noise.
The PCIII you can use with anything and any map in the ecu you like, the best thing to do is get a custom map made by a dynojet centre or on a dyno with an operator who knows what he is doing. All the PCIII does is modify the existing map by adding or substracting fuel for a particular rpm-trottle position.
On my bike I found the maps way to rich for cruising speed/trottle openings and a bit weak when twisting the trottle. Since I adjusted that, it goes like stink when needed and uses less petrol when at steady speed.
