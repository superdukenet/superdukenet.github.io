ALL: You do know that you can test your rectifier/regulator before just chucking it. Not that difficult ...

Regardless, fazer_1000 - stop right there: How cold was it when you first tried to start the bike?

Do you know how LiFe batteries work? If it was cooler, to get the most out of them (in terms of starting current), they work best by being allowed to internally heat some - before hitting the starter button. This can be accomplished by turning on the lights for a bit (or high-beam to accelerate this further).

Take note: This explains well why it started on the 4th try, and not the first. A lead-acid battery - at any temperature would not behave like this. If a lead-acid battery is flat - you will never get it to turn the starter, no matter how may times you try. "Soft 32 Dat 101" is the principal reason I went to a LiFe battery (12-cell Ballistic).

Do you know whether you have the older OE rectifier/regulator (discontinued), or the the subsequent replacement version: Part number from yours please.

Also, if you don't have one already, I can only encourage you to obtain, and occasionally use Shorai's load-balancing charger. It will not only balance the charge in the cells which will maximize performance, it will also extend the battery's life.
