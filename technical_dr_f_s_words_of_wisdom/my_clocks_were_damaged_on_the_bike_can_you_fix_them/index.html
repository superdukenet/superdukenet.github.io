Mileage cannot be reset on the clocks.
A dealer can only set initial mileage on new clocks.

I haven't seen new style clocks up close so I don't know if they can be stripped of their internals without damage.
If they can be opened properly it shouldn't be a problem moving everything from the broken housing to the good one.
If you are lucky the wiring to the screen is detachable, if it's not you'll have to find someone with a soldering iron and steady hands.
Good luck.

PS: if you aren't up to it, I'd be interested in the clocks you bought. My bike almost has 13000km and I'd like to do a mask upgrade

I have swopped out clock internals on my '08 SDR - four screws to remove clear plastic cover, (carefully) pull off the tacho needle, lift off thin display plate, remove the visible screws to middle right hand side and lower left hand bottom and lift out box of tricks - the only tricky rebuild part is the tacho needle as I was told that you need to pre-tension/turn the spindle and refit the needle at max turn - I just put mine back on at the ('off')vertically down position and it seems to work fine - suggest you silicone seal the clear plastic cover joint to reduce risk of water ingress.

Would you be able to do the old trick of putting it into an oven (bare with me) to soften the glue like if you want to colour the back of a car headlight?

If it was possible it would then just be a case of doing it to both your old a new one, then placing the good top on your old clock and put it back in the oven to soften the glue again so it seals.

Obviously there's the risk of melting stuff, so if you do this, do so at your own risk
