Hi Guys, Thanks for the positive comments. I did not get a chance to take apart the SD to investigating the installation of the battery/wires, but I started it and it was a little hesitant to do so, after a minute the red battery light came on. After another minute I shut down and now tried to restart and now dim headlight and completely dead. Sounds like I may have a bad one like you had HitMike.

I am glad you all like the video, I could not imagine there being a better motorbike for me to rip away the miles on the Start Routes of America. The cop was a local in New Hampshire and if there is one thing I learned growing up in Massachusetts, don’t even try to talk your way out of a ticket in New England. I should also add that I really do not mind getting pulled over if I am breaking the law. I respect Law Enforcement and First Responders for being able to do a difficult job and treat them as such.
It was the 4th of July, which as New Englanders know, it is the annual migration of the overpriced heavy gleaming showboats. After a particularly difficult situation involving a few Harley owners at a local eatery, nothing that has not been said before, I heading north to Vermont. With pity in my heart that they will never understand what it is like to really do a motorbike ride I quietly enjoyed the freedom of the road through the woods of turns and abrupt elevation changes. I became distracted when the fuel light came on, playing with the GPS to see when the next gas station is on my route and the road straighten as I caught up to traffic.
It was then I noticed the Officer in the mirrors. He was all business.
Cop said as I was taking off my gear “Do you know why I pulled you over?”
Me “Umm, probably speeding?”
Cop “Yes, any idea how fast you were going?”
Me “umm, sixty-seven?”
Cop being very serious “No, eighty-nine.”
I made a little humming noise as I was digging through my gear for my wallet
Cop “You know what the speed limit is here?”
Me “umm, sixty-seven?”
He looked at me for a second then laughed “nope it is fifty-five.” He then just became curious about my trip, how long I have been on the road and why I was traveling alone across the country. We talked about the non-profit that I founded which has Chapters all around the country and that I spend a couple of days here and there with the volunteers so I am really not alone. He asked nicely to see my License so he could run me, as he was doing that I poured myself a cup of coffee from my thermos and sat on the guard rail and watched the chrome go by in the light rain.
Once he was done he wished me safe travels and to take it a little easier. Nice guy.
